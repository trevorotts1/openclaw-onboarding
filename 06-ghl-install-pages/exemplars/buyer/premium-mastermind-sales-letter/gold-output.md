# Exemplar — buyer / premium-mastermind-sales-letter

Fictional brand: **Northbeam Advisory Group** (illustrative only — no real
client, person, or company). Deliverable: a sales-letter page for a premium
mastermind offer (BUYER-stage: the reader has already raised a hand).

---

## Headline

**You Already Know Your Business Can Handle More. The Question Is Whether
Your Calendar Can.**

## Opening

You did not get here by accident. Revenue is up. The team is real. The
problem nobody warned you about is the one sitting in front of you right
now: every decision still runs through you, and the business has quietly
become a very well-paid job.

The Northbeam Mastermind exists for exactly this stage — not the "how do I
get my first client" stage, and not the "I have a team of two hundred"
stage. The stage where the business is proven and the founder is the
bottleneck.

## The offer, plainly

Twelve operators. Four live quarters. One closed room where the questions
you cannot ask your own team get asked out loud, and answered by people who
have already stood exactly where you are standing.

## What's in the room

- **A quarterly two-day strategy sprint** where you leave with the next
  ninety days mapped against real numbers, not a vision board.
- **Monthly small-pod calls** — six operators, one facilitator, the
  specific decision in front of you this month, worked through in real
  time.
- **Direct access between sessions** for the handful of decisions that
  cannot wait for the next call.

## Risk reversal

**The First-Quarter Guarantee.** Attend the first sprint and both pod calls.
If you do not leave with at least one decision that pays for the entire
year, we will refund the year and you keep everything you built inside the
room. We would rather lose the fee than keep a seat that isn't earning it.

## Why now

The room closes at twelve. Not as a scarcity trick — a twelve-operator room
is the size at which everyone still gets known, still gets pushed, and
still gets heard in the same quarter they raise the question. Past twelve,
that stops being true, so the room stays at twelve.

## CTA

**Apply for one of the twelve seats.** A short application, then a call
with our team to confirm fit both ways — this is not the room for every
stage of business, and we would rather tell you that on the call than after
you've paid.
