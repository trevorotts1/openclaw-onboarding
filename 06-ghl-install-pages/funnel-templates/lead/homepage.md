# Homepage Funnel

**id:** `homepage`  |  **category:** Lead Funnels  |  **skill:** Skill 6  |  **length:** medium-form

**Aliases:** Homepage-Style Lead Funnel, Funnel Hub Home, Branded Home Funnel

**Source (master library entry #19):** The Funnel Hacker's Cookbook

## When To Use / Trigger Criteria

Gives the feel of a traditional website homepage while functioning as a lead funnel. Highlights the business origin story and features top products, routing visitors into individual product funnels or onto the email list.

**Goals it matches:**
- Look like a legitimate website (PR/credibility)
- Route traffic into individual product funnels
- Capture email
- Serve as a central brand hub

**Keywords / request shapes:**
`homepage`, `website`, `brand site`, `main site`, `hub`, `looks like a real website`, `feature my products`, `professional presence`

**Signals:**
- You need a legitimate-looking home base
- You have multiple products/funnels to route to
- You want a browsing feel plus capture

**Do NOT use when:**
- You have a single-offer direct-response play (use Squeeze/Lead Magnet)
- It's purely a personal brand (use Hero)

## Page Structure

### Page 1: Homepage (single long page, StoryBrand wireframe)
*Purpose:* Deliver the feel of a real website while routing to funnels and capturing email.

Key blocks:
- Header: clear one-liner + primary CTA + light branded nav stubs
- Stakes: what's at risk / the problem
- Value proposition
- Guide: empathy + authority (brief origin story)
- Plan: 3 simple steps
- Featured products/funnels grid (each card routes into its own funnel)
- Explanatory paragraph
- Lead-capture / email section ('junk drawer' + opt-in)
- Footer

### Page 2: Routed Individual Product Funnels
*Purpose:* Each featured product links to its dedicated funnel (Lead Magnet, VSL, Webinar, etc.).

Key blocks:
- Product card link -> dedicated funnel URL per featured offer

## Copy Framework

**Primary persona (writes the copy):** The Story Brander (Donald Miller, Building a StoryBrand (StoryBrand SB7 / BrandScript))

**Supporting personas:** The Funnel Architect (Russell Brunson, DotCom Secrets)

**Script:** StoryBrand SB7 BrandScript mapped to the StoryBrand homepage wireframe; supported by Brunson's Funnel Hub concept.

**Copy structure / beats:**
- Character (the customer is the hero)
- Problem (external/internal/philosophical)
- Guide (you - empathy + authority)
- Plan
- Call to Action (direct + transitional)
- Success
- Failure / Stakes

**Notes:** Customer is the hero; the brand is the guide. Every section drives to a CTA or a product funnel - no dead ends, avoiding the brochure-site trap.
