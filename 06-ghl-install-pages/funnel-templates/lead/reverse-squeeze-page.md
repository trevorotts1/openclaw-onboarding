# Reverse Squeeze Page Funnel

**id:** `reverse-squeeze-page`  |  **category:** Lead Funnels  |  **skill:** Skill 6  |  **length:** short-form

**Aliases:** Give-Before-You-Take Funnel, Content-First Opt-in

**Source (master library entry #2):** The Funnel Hacker's Cookbook

## When To Use / Trigger Criteria

Give valuable content (a free video or article) BEFORE asking for the opt-in - 'give before you take.' Lower raw conversion than a standard squeeze, but produces higher-quality, pre-sold leads because the visitor's walls drop first.

**Goals it matches:**
- Qualify leads / raise lead quality
- Build trust before capture
- Warm a skeptical audience
- Prioritize quality of leads over raw volume

**Keywords / request shapes:**
`give value first`, `free training video`, `watch then opt in`, `qualified leads`, `demonstrate expertise`, `content-first`

**Signals:**
- Audience is skeptical of opt-ins
- You have a strong teaching/demo asset
- Brand-building is a priority
- Quality matters more than volume

**Do NOT use when:**
- You are optimizing for pure list volume (use Squeeze)
- Traffic is so cold it won't watch a video (use the article Bridge variant)

## Page Structure

### Page 1: Content-First Landing Page
*Purpose:* Deliver an epiphany/value upfront with no gate, lowering the visitor's resistance.

Key blocks:
- Headline naming the result the free video/article delivers
- Embedded free video OR full article
- Brief credibility intro
- Soft 'want more?' transitional CTA placed BELOW the content

### Page 2: Gated Opt-in / Bonus Page
*Purpose:* Capture the email to unlock a bonus or continuation of the content.

Key blocks:
- Bonus value-stack (what they unlock)
- Opt-in form
- Submit button
- Redirect to bonus delivery / thank-you

## Copy Framework

**Primary persona (writes the copy):** The Expert Guide (Russell Brunson, Expert Secrets)

**Supporting personas:** The Story Brander (Donald Miller, Building a StoryBrand (StoryBrand SB7 / BrandScript))

**Script:** Epiphany Bridge delivered IN the free content (so the viewer adopts the new belief), followed by a StoryBrand transitional Call-to-Action to opt in for more.

**Copy structure / beats:**
- Backstory
- Desire
- External + Internal struggle
- The Wall
- The Epiphany
- New Opportunity
- Opt-in to go deeper

**Notes:** The content must be genuinely valuable; the opt-in is the natural next step after the epiphany, not a gate slammed in the visitor's face.
