## When To Use / Trigger Criteria
**Goals (request shapes that should match):**
- Generate a continuous flow of new clients/patients for a LOCAL service business or professional via paid ads to a funnel (the chiropractor example).
- Replace word-of-mouth dependence with 24/7 automated client generation.
- Turn ad clicks into booked appointments/consultations.

**Keywords:** `local`, `clients`, `patients`, `appointments`, `leads for my business`, `chiropractor`, `dentist`, `med spa`, `contractor`, `agency clients`, `booked calls`, `near me`, `geo-targeted`, `consultation`, `service business`

**Signals:**
- Client is a local service provider or professional (medical, trades, agency, coaching with consults).
- Goal is booked appointments / qualified leads, not product sales.
- Client runs (or wants to run) geo-targeted paid ads.

**Anti-signals (do NOT match / route elsewhere):**
- Pure ecommerce/product sale -> use a tripwire/SLO/VSL funnel.
- High-ticket take-away sale requiring an application + closer -> use the Application Funnel instead.

## Page Structure
### 1. Geo-Targeted Landing Page
*Purpose:* Match the local ad promise, establish trust fast, and drive to booking with one clear plan (StoryBrand).

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Location + service headline ('[Service] in [City] — [clear outcome]')
- StoryBrand: customer-as-hero, name the problem, position the business as the guide with a plan
- Social proof: reviews, star ratings, before/after results, trust badges
- The offer (free consult / exam / quote / first-visit special)
- Primary CTA -> booking
- Map / hours / NAP for local trust

### 2. Booking / Contact Page
*Purpose:* Convert the lead into a booked appointment or a contact request with minimum friction.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Calendar booking widget (self-schedule) OR short contact/quote form
- Reassurance copy ('what happens on your visit', no-pressure)
- Lightweight qualification fields only

### 3. Confirmation / Thank-You Page
*Purpose:* Confirm the appointment, set expectations, and start the show-up + nurture automation.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Appointment summary + add-to-calendar
- What-to-expect / directions / prep instructions
- Reminder-sequence trigger (reduce no-shows)
- Optional referral or review ask

## Copy Framework
**Primary persona:**
- **storybrand** — Donald Miller — StoryBrand persona (The Brand Clarifier): the SB7 framework — (1) a Character who (2) has a Problem, (3) meets a Guide, (4) who gives them a Plan, (5) and calls them to Action, (6) that ends in Success and (7) helps them avoid Failure — plus the one-liner and the grunt test.

**Supporting personas:**
- **architect** — Russell Brunson — DotCom Secrets persona (The Funnel Architect): Star/Story/Solution sales letter script, the Value Ladder, Who/What/Why/How lead logic, Soap Opera Sequence + Daily Seinfeld follow-up.
- **edwards** — Jim Edwards — Copywriting Secrets persona (The Copy Mechanic): the Big Promise headline formulas, fascination/curiosity bullets, the order-bump and OTO close formulas, the 'one-sentence' hook.

**Scripts:**
- StoryBrand SB7 — make the local customer the hero, the business the guide, give a 3-step plan, show success vs the cost of inaction.
- DotCom Secrets lead-funnel Who/What/Why/How logic for the landing structure.
- Jim Edwards local Big-Promise headline + offer copy (free consult/exam/quote).

*Notes:* Clarity beats cleverness for local buyers. Lead with the city + outcome, prove it with local reviews/before-after, and make booking a single obvious action. The plan must feel safe and easy.
