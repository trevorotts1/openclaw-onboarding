## When To Use / Trigger Criteria
**Goals (request shapes that should match):**
- Warm up COLD, non-problem-aware traffic with a native article before any opt-in or offer.
- Bridge the cold audience's existing beliefs to your solution using stories written in THEIR language, then capture the email.
- Feed leads into a 30-60 day cold-to-warm nurture before any direct purchase ask.

**Keywords:** `cold traffic`, `advertorial`, `article`, `presell`, `pre-frame`, `non problem aware`, `native ad`, `warm up cold audience`, `story-based landing page`, `bridge beliefs`, `long nurture`, `content ad`

**Signals:**
- Traffic is cold and unaware of the problem/solution (e.g. broad interest-targeted ads, content/native placements).
- Direct-response sales pages convert poorly because the audience isn't ready.
- Client has a longer follow-up sequence and patience for a multi-week nurture.

**Anti-signals (do NOT match / route elsewhere):**
- Warm/hot traffic that already knows the problem — send them straight to a sales/VSL funnel.
- No nurture sequence in place — the article captures leads that then go cold.

## Page Structure
### 1. Advertorial / Pre-Frame Article Page
*Purpose:* Read as native editorial content (not an ad); meet the cold reader at their awareness level, tell relevant stories, and bridge their beliefs toward the solution.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Editorial-style headline in the audience's own language (curiosity, not pitch)
- Story lead (Epiphany Bridge) that the cold reader sees themselves in
- Belief-bridging body: reframe the vehicle/internal/external false beliefs
- Native formatting (byline, article layout, images, subheads)
- Soft in-content CTAs to the opt-in (no hard sell)
- Author/credibility box

### 2. Lead Capture / Opt-In Page
*Purpose:* Convert the warmed reader with a lead magnet directly related to the article's promise.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Lead magnet headline continuing the article's thread
- Curiosity bullets (Jim Edwards fascinations)
- Single email opt-in form
- Minimal friction, zero nav

### 3. Thank-You / Delivery Page
*Purpose:* Deliver the lead magnet and explicitly enter the lead into the long cold-to-warm nurture.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Download/access of the lead magnet
- Set expectations for the email relationship (what's coming)
- Optional low-friction next step (follow socials / watch a bridge video)
- Nurture-sequence trigger

## Copy Framework
**Primary persona:**
- **expert** — Russell Brunson — Expert Secrets persona (The Attractive Character / Expert): Epiphany Bridge story script, the Big Domino, false-belief patterns (vehicle/internal/external), Perfect Webinar, The Stack and Close, Hook-Story-Offer.

**Supporting personas:**
- **edwards** — Jim Edwards — Copywriting Secrets persona (The Copy Mechanic): the Big Promise headline formulas, fascination/curiosity bullets, the order-bump and OTO close formulas, the 'one-sentence' hook.
- **storybrand** — Donald Miller — StoryBrand persona (The Brand Clarifier): the SB7 framework — (1) a Character who (2) has a Problem, (3) meets a Guide, (4) who gives them a Plan, (5) and calls them to Action, (6) that ends in Success and (7) helps them avoid Failure — plus the one-liner and the grunt test.
- **architect** — Russell Brunson — DotCom Secrets persona (The Funnel Architect): Star/Story/Solution sales letter script, the Value Ladder, Who/What/Why/How lead logic, Soap Opera Sequence + Daily Seinfeld follow-up.

**Scripts:**
- Epiphany Bridge (Expert Secrets) written at the COLD audience's awareness level — their words, their world.
- Advertorial / story-lead formula (Jim Edwards) so the page reads as editorial, not as an ad.
- StoryBrand problem framing to name the villain/problem for non-problem-aware readers.
- Soap Opera Sequence (DotCom Secrets) for the 30-60 day cold-to-warm nurture that follows.

*Notes:* Do NOT pitch on the article. The job is belief-shift + opt-in. Write in the reader's vernacular and let stories do the persuading; the offer comes weeks later inside the nurture.

*Build notes:* Skill 6 builds the 3-step funnel and applies the editorial template to the article step; Skill 44 builds the opt-in form and the mid-article inline form. The nurture workflow is the heart of this funnel — budget the most effort there.
