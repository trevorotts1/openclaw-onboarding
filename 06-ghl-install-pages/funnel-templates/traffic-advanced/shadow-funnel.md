## When To Use / Trigger Criteria
**Goals (request shapes that should match):**
- Ensure that when a prospect researches the brand/person/product after seeing an ad, every search result is an owned property with a clear opt-in and no dead ends.
- Convert the large share of buyers who do NOT click the ad but instead Google the name later.
- Dominate the branded SERP and review surfaces so the research journey funnels back into the value ladder.

**Keywords:** `shadow funnel`, `people google my name`, `branded search`, `reputation`, `what comes up when you search me`, `research journey`, `capture the lookers`, `own the search results`, `review management funnel`

**Signals:**
- Client runs ads but conversion attribution is murky — many buyers arrive via 'how did you hear about us: searched your name'.
- Searching the brand returns third-party pages, dead ends, or nothing the brand controls.
- Client has a Funnel Hub or social profiles that are not optimized as capture points.

**Anti-signals (do NOT match / route elsewhere):**
- No brand awareness / no ad traffic yet — there is no shadow to capture; build front-end traffic first.

## Page Structure
### 1. Node: Funnel Hub (anchor property)
*Purpose:* Be the #1 organic result for the brand name; the central capture point for all shadow traffic.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Optimized title/meta for brand + founder name
- Clear primary opt-in path
- Links to every front-end funnel

### 2. Node: Owned social profiles (Dream 100 anchors)
*Purpose:* Rank for branded searches and route profile visitors to the Hub / a lead funnel.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Consistent handle, bio one-liner, and profile imagery across platforms
- Bio link -> lead magnet or Hub
- Pinned post/story highlight that pre-frames the core offer

### 3. Node: Reviews & reputation surfaces
*Purpose:* Make sure third-party results (Google Business, YouTube, podcasts, press) reinforce trust and link back to owned properties.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Google Business profile / review requests
- Authority placements (podcast guesting, press) pointing to the Hub
- Branded YouTube channel optimized for name search

### 4. Node: Branded-search SERP coverage
*Purpose:* Occupy the first page of search for the brand, founder, product, and '[brand] reviews/scam' queries.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Hub + blog content targeting brand-name and '[brand] review' queries
- FAQ / about content answering common research questions
- No dead-end pages — every owned result has an opt-in

### 5. Node: Retargeting recapture layer
*Purpose:* Re-present the front-end offer to people who researched but did not opt in.

*Key elements / blocks:*
- Pixels on Hub + all owned properties
- Retargeting ad audiences segmented by page visited
- Bridge ad creative back to the #1 front-end funnel

## Copy Framework
**Primary persona:**
- **strategist** — Russell Brunson — Traffic Secrets persona (The Traffic Strategist): Dream 100, Hook-Story-Offer for native content, the Funnel Hub + Shadow Funnel capture doctrine.

**Supporting personas:**
- **storybrand** — Donald Miller — StoryBrand persona (The Brand Clarifier): the SB7 framework — (1) a Character who (2) has a Problem, (3) meets a Guide, (4) who gives them a Plan, (5) and calls them to Action, (6) that ends in Success and (7) helps them avoid Failure — plus the one-liner and the grunt test.
- **expert** — Russell Brunson — Expert Secrets persona (The Attractive Character / Expert): Epiphany Bridge story script, the Big Domino, false-belief patterns (vehicle/internal/external), Perfect Webinar, The Stack and Close, Hook-Story-Offer.

**Scripts:**
- One consistent StoryBrand one-liner used as the bio/meta description across EVERY owned property (consistency is the whole game).
- Attractive Character bio (Expert Secrets) reused on each profile so the researcher meets the same person everywhere.
- Hook-Story-Offer retargeting ad copy to recapture researchers who didn't opt in.

*Notes:* There is no new sales copy to write here — the work is consistency and coverage. Audit every place the brand name appears and make sure the message, one-liner, and opt-in path are identical and unbroken.
