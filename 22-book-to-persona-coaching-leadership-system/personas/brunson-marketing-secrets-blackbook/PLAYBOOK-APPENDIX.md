## A — Headline, Hook & Subject-Line Formula Bank

The book teaches *message/hook* formulas more than literal headline templates. Each below is a reusable opener for a page headline, ad, video first-3-seconds, or subject line.

### The Food-Court-Proof Plain-Language Hook
- **When to use:** Cold-traffic landing-page headline, ad headline, or webinar registration headline where the audience does NOT yet know your jargon.
- **Pattern:** `Who here wants [the end result, in words a stranger already understands] — [free / for free]?` — strip every insider term ("funnel", "sequence", "automation") until a stranger in a food court would raise their hand.
- **Worked example:** "Hey, who here wants a free money-making website?" (the winner — after "learn how to build a sales funnel?" and "see my sales funnel that makes $17,947/day?" both failed because nobody knew what a funnel was).
- **Source:** Secret 70 (Food-Court-Proof). Iteration ladder lives in Section C.

### The Prolific-Zone Curiosity Hook
- **When to use:** Any hook that must be both believable AND shareable (social posts, ad hooks, video titles).
- **Pattern:** `[Surprising everyday action] to [achieve a big desired result]` — land between the SANE zone (boring/mainstream, unsellable) and the CRAZY zone (clickbait/wacko, few buyers).
- **Worked example:** "Putting butter in your coffee to lose weight." (Prolific zone. SANE = "the food pyramid"; CRAZY = "get your nutrients from the sun.")
- **Source:** Secret 9 (The Prolific Zone). Full model in Section E.

### The "Secret" Curiosity / Exclusivity Hook
- **When to use:** Pre-launch invite, DM, beta list, anything you want people to feel "in the know" about.
- **Pattern:** `Hey, this is [the thing]. It's going to be really cool and you may want to be part of it. I'm doing a secret [webinar/drop]. If you want to come, show up at [link]. Do NOT tell anybody else about this. This is just for you.`
- **Worked example:** Pre-launch app text sent to ~50 people → 100 registered → 85 showed. Verbatim in Section C.
- **Source:** Secret 46 (The "Secret" Referral). Brand spine: "I've built a $100 million business by leading with secrets — DotComSecrets, Expert Secrets, Marketing Secrets."

### The New-Opportunity Reframe Headline
- **When to use:** When a competitor category already exists and you'd otherwise be "a better version of X." Kills competition / opens blue ocean.
- **Pattern:** `[Product] isn't a better [old category]. It's [a brand-new category/vehicle] that [does the new thing the old one never could].`
- **Worked example:** Don't sell Actionetics as "a better email autoresponder than Mailchimp." Reframe: "the tool that creates customized FOLLOW-UP FUNNELS" (email + text + direct-mail + Messenger, customized by purchases/influence/actions). "If your prospect is driving a Volvo, present her with an Audi."
- **Source:** Secret 42 (New Opportunity vs Improvement). Full model in Section E.

### The Sexiest-Offer FREE-Reframe Headline
- **When to use:** Front-end offer headline / order page hero when you want the SAME deliverables to suddenly sell.
- **Pattern:** `When you buy [the thing they were going to resist paying for], you get [the thing they want MOST] FREE.` — make the most-wanted part free; do not change the products, only the structure.
- **Worked example:** BEFORE (failed): "Buy a six-month pass to ClickFunnels and get this course free." AFTER (sold like crazy, $997): "When you buy this course Funnel Hacks, you'll get ClickFunnels FREE for six months!"
- **Source:** Secret 52 (Sexiest Offer). Full model in Section E.

### The Double-Reframe "Half-Off AND Free" Hook
- **When to use:** Front-end course/info-product offer that needs an extra shove.
- **Pattern:** `Get [core product] half off — AND also get [this physical/bonus thing] for free!`
- **Worked example:** Expert Secrets funnel: Perfect Webinar Secrets course at half price PLUS a free MP3 player. "Get it half off and ALSO get this thing over here for free!"
- **Source:** Secret 52.

### The One-Gold-Nugget Headline
- **When to use:** Selling to a HOT market that thinks it already knows your topic. Lead with the single transformational nugget, not a feature list.
- **Pattern:** `If [one specific result the nugget delivers], then [the whole purchase] pays for itself.` Highlight the ONE thing the market responds to best.
- **Worked example:** Teleseminar Secrets ($1,000): "If one thing earns me $1,000 on my next teleseminar, the course pays for itself."
- **Source:** Secret 54 (One Gold Nugget). Full model in Section E.

### The Hook-of-Discovery Event Invite
- **When to use:** Registration headline/subject line for a free live event that you'll later use to sell continuity (Section C/D).
- **Pattern:** `You'll never believe it. I just [discovered/learned] this cool new thing and I want to show you. I'm going to walk through it LIVE and take you behind the scenes.`
- **Worked example:** Verbatim in Section C (Subscription-Selling, Secret 87, Step 1).
- **Source:** Secret 87.

### The Indoctrination Story Hook
- **When to use:** First video/email of a pre-sell sequence — bond before you pitch.
- **Pattern:** `How I [started / did the thing] [in a humble place] and [got in trouble / did everything WRONG].` Lead with hardship, not credentials.
- **Worked example:** List Hacking sequence video 1: "How he started his business in his duplex and got in trouble doing everything WRONG."
- **Source:** Secret 65 (The Sequence Is What Sells). Full sequence in Section D.

### The Polarity / Line-in-the-Sand Hook
- **When to use:** Manifesto, About page, opinionated post — to repel non-market and bond fans 10x harder.
- **Pattern:** `[The industry/enemy] tells you [X]. I believe [the opposite stand]. If that offends you, we're probably not for each other.`
- **Worked example:** Pattern derived from Secret 19 (Polarity) + Secret 12 (Manifesto). "If you haven't offended someone by noon each day, you're not doing it right" (Dan Kennedy). Note: book defers the full Manifesto SCRIPT to Expert Secrets.
- **Source:** Secret 19 + Secret 12.

### The Decoy-Anchor Price Line
- **When to use:** Headline/CTA where a target price needs to feel like a steal.
- **Pattern:** `It's only [decoy: $X/month], or you can get [target: lifetime/full] for [target price].` — present the option you WANT bought as the obvious bargain.
- **Worked example:** "It's only $297 a month, or you can get lifetime access for $2000." Verbatim/full in Section C.
- **Source:** Secret 50 (Decoy Pricing).

### The 2-for-1 / Bonus-Expiry Urgency Hook
- **When to use:** Event or product offer that needs a NOW.
- **Pattern:** `[Get 2 for the price of 1 / Get these bonuses] — for the first [N] buyers / for the next [hours] only.`
- **Worked example:** Funnel Hacking LIVE: "2 tickets for the price of 1, short time only" (sold hundreds in hours); previous year's recordings as a 24-hour bonus sold hundreds more.
- **Source:** Secret 44 (Power of the Bonus) + Secret 51 (Urgency & Scarcity).

### The Speedy-Premium Ballpark Hook
- **When to use:** High-ticket service/agency close while excitement is at its peak (don't wait for a formal quote).
- **Pattern:** `This is going to be around [$ballpark] — I'm going to nail down the details right now, but I want to lock this in today. What day can we begin?`
- **Worked example:** Verbatim in Section C (Secret 75).
- **Source:** Secret 75 (Speedy Premium).

### The Curiosity-Number Hook (caution-flagged)
- **When to use:** Warm/expert audiences who DO know your category. (For cold traffic it fails — see Food-Court ladder.)
- **Pattern:** `Who wants to see my [specific asset] that makes [$specific number per day/week]?`
- **Worked example:** "Who here wants to see my sales funnel that makes $17,947 per day?" — book flags this as a FAIL on cold traffic (they don't know what a funnel is); use only when the term is already understood.
- **Source:** Secret 70.

## B — Funnel & Page Recipes (page-by-page)

The full recipe set the book teaches. Each is a build brief for a funnel-builder / landing-page specialist (Skill 6).

### The Core Funnel Math (the architecture every recipe rests on)
- **When to use:** Justify/architect ANY paid-traffic funnel; the governing law.
- **Pattern:** Pay `$[ad cost]` for a click → ask ONLY for an email in exchange for something free → present an INSANE complementary deal they can't resist → on purchase, before the receipt, present a one-click bump → reinvest the profit into acquiring more customers. Goal: net customer cost = $0, so "whoever CAN spend the most money to acquire a customer wins."
- **Worked example:** Company B: $5 ad → buys $15 (tripwire + one-click bump) → $10 profit → buys 2 more customers → compounds into a brute-force ad budget. (Company A — traditional store with 100s of products and a cart — spent $5, made $0, couldn't follow up.)
- **Source:** Secret 56 (Why Funnels Win).

### The Free-Book / Free-Plus-Shipping Funnel (the flagship page-by-page)
- **When to use:** Front-end customer acquisition with a physical or digital book/report. The default ClickFunnels-style funnel.
- **Pattern (block/page order):**
  1. **PAGE 1 — Shipping form:** headline + free-book hero + enter SHIPPING address only (micro-commitment; word "FREE" everywhere). Job: get the yes.
  2. **PAGE 2 — Payment + Order Bump:** enter PAYMENT info; a 30–40-word ORDER BUMP checkbox (e.g., add-on training/Black Box) right above Submit. Job: raise average cart value to ~$70 on these two pages.
  3. **OTO 1 — Extension upsell:** the AUDIO version of the book (feels like an extension of what they just bought, not a new pitch). Job: lift cart value with low resistance.
  4. **OTO 2 — Core ascension upsell:** a masterclass / next-level program. No additional downsells (keep it SHORT — long funnels make buyers hate you).
  5. **ORDER CONFIRMATION PAGE — Value + next step:** a 60–90-min video training (one of your best); CTA = opt in to a web class.
  6. **Backend web class:** 2 hrs 20 min of training over the next few days → pushes into a high-ticket bundle (e.g., $997).
- **Worked example:** The Expert Secrets book funnel — exactly the six steps above; principle = "shrink the front-end, extend the value on the backend."
- **Source:** Secret 59 (Shrink the Funnel, Extend the Value).

### The Tripwire (front-end no-brainer)
- **When to use:** Flip a cold prospect into a buyer cheaply; the first sale is the hardest, so make it idiotic to refuse.
- **Pattern:** Offer something so cheap/amazing that "no" is irrational. Two set-ups: (a) give something FREE, then offer the tripwire on the THANK-YOU page; or (b) make the tripwire a FREE + SHIPPING offer (the word "free" makes paying shipping feel like getting something for nothing). Price it to BREAK EVEN, not profit. Immediately follow with 2–3 upsells while they're in buying mood.
- **Worked example:** Credit-card knife at $2.95 free+shipping → instantly hit with 3 upsells (instant-light match, magic gadget card, etc.) → bought all. Math: $5/day ad → 1 customer who spends $7.95 → covers ~$3 product + $5 ad → customer acquired FREE → everything after is profit.
- **Source:** Secret 63 (The Tripwire).

### The Order Bump (checkout add-on)
- **When to use:** Every order form. The single highest-ROI 30–40 words in a funnel.
- **Pattern:** A 30–40-word description + optional image + a CHECKBOX placed RIGHT BEFORE the Submit button, offering a complementary add-on. Target take rate: 40–50% of buyers when paired with an irresistible tripwire.
- **Worked example:** Flashlight tripwire → bump = batteries/case. Strategic math: free+shipping ($7.95) + a $20 bump beats a $27 thank-you-page product — far more people buy $7.95 than $27, and ~half take the bump → ~$27.95 average with less funnel-pushing.
- **Source:** Secret 64 (The Order Bump).

### The Pre-Funnel (native social content)
- **When to use:** The content layer that feeds the funnel — IG stories/posts, FB posts/Lives, Pinterest pins → blog. (Skill 35 territory.)
- **Pattern:** Create content NATIVE to the platform, consumable in-platform, matching the platform's vibe — that draws people toward the opt-in. Don't cold-link your "party invitation" on your own front door; go where people already gather, work the room, casually mention you're new, exchange contact, follow up.
- **Worked example:** Pinterest pins → blog post → opt-in; FB Live → retarget viewers into the funnel. (Analogy: town hotspots on Fri/Sat nights.)
- **Source:** Secret 62 (The Pre-Funnel).

### The Indoctrination Pre-Frame Sequence (the part that actually sells)
- **When to use:** Before the sales video/page — pre-frame so they buy "whether your pitch sucks or not."
- **Pattern:** A choreographed 4-piece STORY sequence (not hard pitches): (1) origin/hardship, (2) the obsession that taught you the craft, (3) a bit of real teaching, (4) values/mission. Then the sales piece is "the cherry on top."
- **Worked example:** List Hacking sequence — full four steps in Section D.
- **Source:** Secret 65 (The Sequence Is What Sells).

### Switching Sales Modalities (multi-format + exit-intent)
- **When to use:** Maximize conversion across people who read vs watch vs listen.
- **Pattern:** Build the funnel to lead with your STRONGEST modality, but capture the rest: on EXIT-INTENT, pop an alternate modality (video page → sales-letter version for people with no sound). Repurpose every asset (webinar → sales letter → audio podcast).
- **Worked example:** Video sales page → exit-intent → text sales-letter version for the no-sound crowd.
- **Source:** Secret 66 (Switching Sales Modalities).

### Funnel Fridays / Demo-Selling Replay Page
- **When to use:** "Sell by doing" — a live demo (FB/IG/Zoom) of the product in action, with a replay page that keeps selling.
- **Pattern:** Demo the product LIVE on a real example (not a hard sell — watching it used makes viewers want it). Keep the REPLAY on a page with buy buttons + product images BELOW the video, with a plain CTA: "If you're interested in purchasing [product], go here."
- **Worked example:** Funnel Fridays — built a NEW funnel live in <30 min every Friday using the software on a real business; replay page sold Funnel Scripts. Meta-principle: "Almost every funnel online is built on the principles of real-life marketing" (model direct mail, in-store, QVC, home parties).
- **Source:** Secret 69 (Funnel Fridays).

### The Bridge Funnel (list-build with NO product yet)
- **When to use:** Build a list cheaply before your product exists, by piggybacking on already-viral content.
- **Pattern:**
  1. Use BuzzSumo to find articles/videos already TRENDING that attract your future buyer.
  2. Build an opt-in page with a curiosity hook/headline → capture email.
  3. Redirect to a branded thank-you page that embeds that trending video / links the article.
  Benefit: zero content-creation cost, proven-viral material, cheap cost-per-lead.
- **Worked example:** Curiosity opt-in → thank-you page embedding a trending YouTube video on the topic; leads captured while the real product is still being built.
- **Source:** Secret 84 (List-Building Without a Product).

### The Microcontinuity Funnel (recurring revenue)
- **When to use:** Turn a front-end buyer into a monthly subscriber with a low-barrier, scarce-each-month membership.
- **Pattern:** FREE + SHIPPING box of free books/content (~$19.95) → starts a 2-week trial of the membership → option to skip trial and pay yearly in full (extra savings bonus) → each month the member gets (a) something NEW + SCARCE (a mailed newsletter on a freshly "hacked" funnel) and (b) a pre-built share-funnel in the members area. Three requirements: NEW+SCARCE monthly, LOW entry barrier, great onboarding to survive trial-end.
- **Worked example:** FunnelU ($67/mo) — drives "I wonder what they'll send next?"; the monthly share-funnels "made thousands if not millions."
- **Source:** Secret 45 (Power of Continuity / Microcontinuity).

### The Webinar-A-Week Funnel Cadence
- **When to use:** The default weekly sell-and-traffic rhythm for any early-stage business. "The apple in marketing is the WEBINAR."
- **Pattern (7-day loop):** Sun→Thu = push hard for REGISTRATIONS; **Thursday = SELL** (live webinar + offer); Fri→Sun = REPLAY + emails pushing the special offer; **Sunday = offer ENDS** → reset and repeat. Each new audience perfects the webinar.
- **Worked example:** One live webinar/week; offer with a Sunday-night deadline. Companion: re-promote the SAME offer as an EVENT (a "last ever" ClickFunnels webinar drew 7,500 registrations and ~$750k).
- **Source:** Secret 4 (Webinar cadence) + Secret 14 (Promote Often). Email layer in Section D.

### The Subscription-Selling Live-Event Funnel
- **When to use:** Add hundreds of members fast by combining a free live event + scarcity + framing.
- **Pattern:** (1) Hook-of-Discovery invite to a free live event → registrations; (2) a 45-min event that tells the WHAT/STORY but NOT the HOW (they leave WANTING it, not knowing how); (3) close into a members-only live "how" training that is one-time-only and not recorded; non-members get a 15-minute scarcity window to start the trial.
- **Worked example:** Full verbatim 3-step script in Section C (Secret 87).
- **Source:** Secret 87 (Subscription-Selling Secret).

### The Assess-Your-Funnel Optimization Loop (the diagnostic recipe)
- **When to use:** AFTER launch — never critique a funnel with theory before it ships; "let people vote with their credit cards." ~90% of campaigns fail on the first go.
- **Pattern:** Launch (~$100/day; initial data budget $500–$1,000) → break the funnel into pieces (like reviewing wrestling tape) → read the 4 KPIs → fix the weakest piece → re-test. The 4 KPIs: **Cost to Acquire a Customer · Average Cart Value · Landing-Page Conversion Rate · Upsell Conversion Rate.** Goal = break even on the front end, profit on the back.
- **Worked example:** Biohacking Secrets funnel — CAC $20, cart value $17 → −$13/sale. Fix 1: replace ads with Facebook Live videos as ads → CAC dropped $20→$6. Landing page converting fine → left alone. Order bump only 11% + weak upsell → simplified copy + changed pricing → funnel broke even. "It's not IF, it's WHEN."
- **Source:** Secrets 67–68 (Assess Your Funnel).

## C — Script Bank (sales / objection / follow-up / discovery / close)

Word-for-word say-this scripts. The ones marked **VERBATIM** are reproduced from the book and may be swiped directly.

### Objection-Handling: "Agree First, Then Close" (VERBATIM)
- **When to use:** Any time a prospect/customer tells you WHY they won't buy or wants to quit. Never argue or defend.
- **Pattern:** PART 1 — agree/validate the feeling. PART 2 — fix it and go for the sale.
- **Worked example (response to "ClickFunnels is too hard, I want to quit"):**
  - Part 1: "I totally understand how you feel. It can be frustrating when something isn't as intuitive as you expect."
  - Part 2: "How about we schedule a call with one of our coaches who can guide you step-by-step through your first funnel? You'll be able to ask questions, keep the recordings, and best of all… get it up and running the right way the first time."
- **Source:** Secret 47 (Grant Cardone's "first agree with your prospect"). Anti-example: the Boise State coach who fought a $600k donor on every reason and lost the money.

### Decoy-Pricing Close (VERBATIM)
- **When to use:** You have one target price ($2,000) and want it to feel like a steal — while still capturing people who can't afford it.
- **Pattern:** Acknowledge the payment-plan request → reframe as a "monthly option" decoy → present the target price as the bargain.
- **Worked example:** "Hey, a lot of people are asking for a payment plan. We don't have a payment plan, but I have decided to offer a monthly option. It's only $297 a month, or you can get lifetime access for $2000." ($2,000 looks like a steal vs $3,564/yr; non-buyers of the $2k still enter at $297/mo.)
- **Source:** Secret 50 (Decoy Pricing).

### The Street-Performer 7-Step Live-Sale Framework (VERBATIM components)
- **When to use:** Any live or recorded sell (stage, webinar, VSL, live demo). The universal sales-process template.
- **Pattern (7 steps):** 1) Get attention. 2) Build rapport. 3) Train the audience on what you want them to do. 4) Justify the price (or pre-frame it). 5) Do the show. 6) Give a call to action. 7) Follow up with those who don't act.
- **Worked example — price justification (VERBATIM):** "Look, I'm a street performer and this is how I make my living. If you were to go to the bar right now and buy a beer, it would cost five pounds, and that's going to take maybe a minute to drink… I've been performing for almost 45 minutes so far, and I would assume this is worth at least the same as a quick beer in actual entertainment value. So the minimum donation accepted is five pounds. The maximum is a hundred."
- **Worked example — follow-up call-out for non-buyers (VERBATIM):** "What are you going to be, a freebie seeker?" (social pressure that closed the wallet-leavers).
- **Source:** Secret 73 (Street-Performer Sales Sequence). Tactics: 5-min fake cough to gather a crowd ("a show without attention is just an accident"); chalk-square micro-commitments; sync the crowd's breathing for fastest rapport.

### The Speedy-Premium Close (VERBATIM)
- **When to use:** High-ticket service where the traditional quote/proposal wait would kill the excitement (and turn you into a comparison-shopped commodity).
- **Pattern:** Position as the best/most expensive → give a ballpark → ask to lock it in TODAY with a deposit.
- **Worked example:** "Look, this project's going to be pretty big. It's going to be around $25,000 — that's the ballpark number. I'm going to go find those things out for you right now, but I want to lock this in today. I know you're excited to get started, as are we. What day can we begin?" → take a deposit on the spot. Add-on: be so busy clients must APPLY — "do it on YOUR terms, not theirs."
- **Source:** Secret 75 (Speedy Premium).

### The Subscription-Selling Live-Event Close (VERBATIM)
- **When to use:** Converting free-event attendees into trial members with maximum scarcity + framing.
- **Pattern:** Member frame (you already have it) → non-member scarcity window (you'll lose it forever).
- **Worked example — the close:** "Okay, so I'm going to be doing a 90-minute breakdown of this training in my membership group, where I'll show you exactly how to do this. I'll go step-by-step through each detail so you can replicate it exactly in your business."
- **Worked example — member frame (VERBATIM):** "If you're a member, congratulations! Log in to the members area, and the link for the live training will be right there. I'm not recording it, and I'm not going to share it ever again. It's happening one time and one time only. If you want it, now is the time!"
- **Worked example — non-member scarcity close (VERBATIM):** "If you're not a member yet, you've got exactly 15 minutes before this training starts. In 15 minutes, I pull it off the page, and if you wait even 5 more minutes, you will miss your shot. It's gone forever. The only way to ever understand how to do this is to be on that live training. In fact, we have three other experts — the people you need to meet who will help you get this thing started — are going to be there on the call. But this is a one-time-only offer, it's not being recorded, and it starts in exactly 14 minutes. So you better hurry. Go sign up right now for the trial at…"
- **Source:** Secret 87 (Subscription-Selling Secret).

### The Hook-of-Discovery Event Invite (VERBATIM)
- **When to use:** Step 1 of the subscription-selling funnel — get registrations for the free event.
- **Pattern:** Excitement + discovery + "behind the scenes" + live.
- **Worked example:** "Hey, guys! You'll never believe it. I just learned this cool new thing and I want to show you. I'm going to walk through it live and take you behind the scenes." (Then 2 emails + social to drive registrations.)
- **Source:** Secret 87, Step 1.

### The "Secret" Referral / Pre-Launch Text (VERBATIM)
- **When to use:** Seed a launch through people who love feeling "in the know."
- **Pattern:** Tease the thing → invite to a "secret" event → tell them to keep it secret.
- **Worked example:** "Hey, this is this thing I'm doing. We're rolling out this new app. It's going to be really cool, and you may want to be part of it. I'm doing a secret webinar. If you want to come to it, show up." + registration link + "Do NOT tell anybody else about this. This is just for you." (~50 invites → 100 registered → 85 showed.)
- **Source:** Secret 46.

### The Backdoor / Sell-the-Bonus Close
- **When to use:** Things awkward or hard to sell directly (a mastermind you'd have to brag about; a service that feels salesy).
- **Pattern:** Find the ONE component people truly want → SELL THAT → close with "and it's FREE when you join [the hard-to-sell thing]."
- **Worked example:** (a) Wrote a 4-page sales letter on the "Black Box Program," sold the Black Box, then: "It's FREE when you join our mastermind." (b) Chiropractor teaches a free 30-min morning routine, then gives a foam roller + special oil + meditation CD FREE "only if you come get adjusted" — the sale doesn't feel like a sale.
- **Source:** Secret 77 (Backdoor Method).

### The Hail-Mary Support-Request Close
- **When to use:** End of a revenue period when you need a fast spike; mine inbound support requests for a hidden offer.
- **Pattern:** Notice a recurring request → package it as a limited-time offer → pop it up to the people already asking.
- **Worked example:** People kept asking to pre-pay 12 months for tax relief → pop-up: "Buy a year + get 2 months free, right now." (One of five Hail-Mary sources that tripled ClickFunnels revenue in a week needing +$100k/day; full list in Section E.)
- **Source:** Secret 86 (Hail Marys).

### The Frame + Button-Copy Test (CTA microcopy)
- **When to use:** Choosing button/CTA text on any opt-in or order page.
- **Pattern:** Keep the buying LOOP OPEN ("what's next?") rather than closing it. A button that signals completion can lift opt-ins but crush sales.
- **Worked example:** "Get Instant Access" vs "Click Here to Finish." "Click Here to Finish" RAISED opt-in rate but CRASHED sales 50% (it "closed the loop"). Use "Get Instant Access" — it keeps them in a buying state.
- **Source:** Secret 82 (The Frame). Proof: identical substitute-teacher bio to 900 students with one word changed ("warm" vs "cold") flipped the entire rating.

### The Food-Court Message-Iteration Script (discovery/test)
- **When to use:** Pressure-testing a cold-traffic value proposition before you spend on ads.
- **Pattern:** Imagine 100 strangers in a food court; say "Who here wants [your message]?" Rewrite until hands would actually raise.
- **Worked example:** (1) "Hey, how would you like to learn how to build a sales funnel?" → FAIL. (2) "Who here wants to see my sales funnel that makes $17,947 per day?" → FAIL. (3) "Hey, who here wants a free money-making website?" → WINNER.
- **Source:** Secret 70 (Food-Court-Proof).

### Sequence 1 — The 4-Step Indoctrination Pre-Sell Sequence (complete, in order)
- **When to use:** The drip (email or video) that runs BEFORE the sales asset so the prospect is already sold.
- **Pattern:** Four story pieces, escalating from hardship → craft → teaching → values; THEN the sales piece is "the cherry on top." Not hard pitches — stories.
- **Worked example (List Hacking sequence, the book's actual four pieces):**
  1. **Origin/Hardship** — "How I started my business in my duplex and got in trouble doing everything WRONG." Role: relatability + the start of the hero's journey. Subject pattern: `How I [did the thing] and got it all WRONG`.
  2. **The Obsession** — "My obsession with junk mail and how it taught me marketing." Role: establish craft/expertise through an unexpected obsession. Subject pattern: `My weird obsession with [thing] (and what it taught me about [skill])`.
  3. **A Bit of Real Teaching** — list-building, with some actual how-to. Role: deliver value, prove competence. Subject pattern: `The [#] things I learned about [topic]`.
  4. **Values/Mission** — "Me in Kenya helping build orphanages; a % of profits goes to the mission." Role: bond on values; people buy from movements. Subject pattern: `Why a piece of every sale goes to [mission]`.
  - **Then:** the sales video/page — already pre-framed by story, hardship, success, expertise, and values.
- **Timing:** one piece per day (or per touch) leading into the offer open.
- **Source:** Secret 65 (The Sequence Is What Sells).

### Sequence 2 — The Webinar-A-Week 7-Day Email Sequence (cadence-complete)
- **When to use:** The weekly email layer wrapped around the Webinar-A-Week funnel (Section B). Cadence is from the book; bodies are skeletons in the book's voice (THIN in source).
- **Pattern + worked example (day-by-day, every email's role):**
  - **Sun — Registration #1:** Subject pattern: `[Curiosity hook] — free training Thursday`. Body skeleton: tease the ONE discovery + register link. (Hook-of-Discovery voice, Section C.)
  - **Mon — Registration #2 (story):** Subject pattern: `The day [origin/struggle moment]`. Body: indoctrination story #1 → register.
  - **Tue — Registration #3 (value):** Subject pattern: `[One useful thing] (more on the training)`. Body: a teaching nugget → register.
  - **Wed — Registration #4 (urgency):** Subject pattern: `Tomorrow — don't miss this`. Body: recap + last call to register.
  - **Thu — SELL (live + replay):** Subject pattern: `We're LIVE — [offer] inside`. Body: the offer, the stack, the deadline (Sunday).
  - **Fri/Sat — Replay + Offer push:** Subject patterns: `Missed it? Replay (expires Sunday)` / `Why [objection] isn't true`. Body: replay link + offer + objection handling (Section C "agree first").
  - **Sun — Deadline close:** Subject pattern: `Last chance — [offer] ends tonight`. Body: real urgency (offer/bonuses expire) → buy.
- **Timing:** Sun→Thu drive registrations, Thu sell, Fri→Sun replay, Sun close. Reset weekly.
- **Source:** Secret 4 (Webinar cadence) + Secret 14 (Promote Often; Daegan Smith emailed 9x/day to stay top-of-inbox when pain hits).

### Sequence 3 — The Hail-Mary Re-Engagement Blast (to non-buyers)
- **When to use:** End-of-period revenue spike; email everyone who watched but didn't buy.
- **Pattern:** One targeted campaign to the warm-but-didn't-buy segment with a fresh reason/urgency to act now.
- **Worked example:** 14,000 webinar-watchers who didn't buy → a dedicated email campaign re-presenting the offer with a new deadline (one of the five Hail-Mary sources, Section E).
- **Timing:** burst (days, not weeks) when you're close to a goal.
- **Source:** Secret 86 (Hail Marys).

## E — Frameworks, Models & Templates (with all steps)

The full framework set, each reproduced executable + one worked example. (Scripts living in C and recipes in B are cross-referenced, not duplicated.)

### The Underachiever Method (market selection)
- **When to use:** Before building anything — choose the market, not the product.
- **Pattern:** 1) Find a HOT market. 2) Ask them what they want. 3) Give it to them. Rule: competition is EVIDENCE of a market; if you can say "no one is tapping this," BE AFRAID.
- **Worked example:** Idaho roadside waterslide (great product, no market, no highway exit) went bankrupt; a product guy paid $10k to be talked OUT of his "untapped" idea.
- **Source:** Secret 17.

### The Prolific Zone (message positioning)
- **When to use:** Calibrate every hook/message to be sellable AND shareable.
- **Pattern:** Position between the SANE zone (mainstream/boring → unsellable) and the CRAZY zone (clickbait/wacko → few buyers). The PROLIFIC ZONE in between is sellable + viral.
- **Worked example:** "Butter in your coffee to lose weight" (prolific) vs "the food pyramid" (sane) vs "nutrients from the sun" (crazy).
- **Source:** Secret 9.

### The Value Ladder (offer architecture)
- **When to use:** Organizing ALL offers so customers ascend and you maximize lifetime value.
- **Pattern:** Ascending priced steps; each step's VALUE slightly exceeds its PRICE so they come back; price + depth rise each rung; each offer "solves one problem and agitates another." Build it: find your most loyal customer, map what they bought 1st/2nd/3rd, draw the ladder, build marketing around it.
- **Worked example:** Brunson's ladder: free/low-cost books → ClickFunnels ($97–297/mo) → FunnelU → coaching → $25,000 Inner Circle → $1,000,000 done-for-you funnel. (Apparel: shirt→pants→shoes→belt→jacket→cufflinks/cologne.)
- **Source:** Secret 39.

### The One Big Domino (sales-message focus)
- **When to use:** Every sales page/VSL — sell ONE thing, not twelve features.
- **Pattern:** Find the ONE belief/result that, if the prospect accepts it, makes everything else fall into place. Sell THAT with EMOTION; all other features become the buyer's LOGICAL justification. (Deeper Big Domino chapter = Expert Secrets.)
- **Worked example:** Selling FunnelU, the one domino = "free million-dollar funnels" (proven funnels that cost thousands to build); every other feature became justification.
- **Source:** Secret 40.

### The Attractive Character + 2-Step Deployment (bonding)
- **When to use:** Building rapport so people buy from someone they aspire to be like.
- **Pattern:** Step 1 — LEAD with what they want / the bait (use what converts, even if off-brand) to get them in. Step 2 — THEN introduce your Attractive Character story to bond.
- **Worked example:** Lindsey Stirling plays 2–3 songs first (gives them what they came for), then runs a 3-min "I don't think we've officially been introduced yet…" life-story video → bonds harder.
- **Source:** Secret 23.

### The Hero's Two Journeys (story model)
- **When to use:** Writing an origin/sales story.
- **Pattern:** Story = Character + Desire + Conflict, told as TWO journeys: (1) Journey of DESIRE (external goal) and (2) Journey of TRANSFORMATION (internal change — the more powerful one that gives the goal meaning). People buy on emotion (conflict), justify with logic.
- **Worked example:** Little Red Riding Hood — character (Red), desire (cookies to Grandma), conflict (Wolf); external = deliver cookies, internal = learns to face fears. Application: surface the transformation behind your "dream house/car" so prospects see themselves.
- **Source:** Secret 24 (credited to Mike Hauge).

### The Five Turning Points of Conflict (narrative beat-sheet)
- **When to use:** Structuring the beats of your story so it deepens trust.
- **Pattern:** 1) The opportunity they take. 2) The change in plan (the map changes). 3) The point of no return. 4) The major setback / glimmer of hope. 5) The climax (everything on the line).
- **Worked example:** "I could make money online → sold a DVD on potato guns" (1) → "Google slapped us, lost our rankings" (2) → must-choose moment (3) → "Nemo's dad in the belly of the whale" setback (4) → final battle (5). (Deeper in Expert Secrets.)
- **Source:** Secret 25.

### State Control / Epiphany Bridge + the 4 Retrace Questions
- **When to use:** Telling a story that puts the prospect into the same emotional STATE as your aha moment, then closing.
- **Pattern:** A story lands by HOW it made you FEEL (your state) — built from PHYSIOLOGY + FOCUS + MEANING. Recreate it with 4 retrace questions: • What were you thinking about? • What was your body doing? • What were you focusing on? • What things led up to that aha moment? Close: lead with the Epiphany Bridge STORY; use features + bonuses to JUSTIFY. "People buy with emotions and justify with logic." (Full Epiphany Bridge script = Expert Secrets.)
- **Worked example:** Retrace your own "I finally figured out funnels" moment with the 4 questions, then have the prospect relive it before you present the offer.
- **Source:** Secret 37 (from Tony Robbins).

### The Dream 100 (relationship/distribution)
- **When to use:** Growth via other people's audiences — "people do business with people, not companies."
- **Pattern:** Pick 100 Attractive Characters with built-in audiences; build real relationships; one connection can 10x a business overnight.
- **Worked example:** Expert Secrets launch (goal 50,000 books/30 days): (a) sent THREE physical gift boxes to the Dream 100 pre-launch (Batman lights, figurines, first 3 chapters); (b) goal = a Facebook Live virtual book-tour interview during launch; (c) the hack — logged into each influencer's own FB ad account, put Brunson's card on file, paid to drive ads to the influencer's OWN affiliate link, so the influencer just showed up, got the commission, and Brunson paid for all the traffic. (Dream 100 detail = "lost chapter" on the DotComSecrets audiobook.)
- **Source:** Secret 29.

### Polarity (attention/loyalty)
- **When to use:** Branding/positioning — neutrality is invisible.
- **Pattern:** Take a real stand and back it up; making one group dislike you makes the lovers love you 10x more and spend more. (Wrong way: smear a competitor for noise.)
- **Worked example:** Howard Stern — fans listened 35–40 min, HATERS listened a full HOUR. "If you haven't offended someone by noon each day, you're not doing it right" (Dan Kennedy).
- **Source:** Secret 19.

### The Manifesto (movement asset)
- **When to use:** Stake your "line in the sand" — who you are, who you're NOT — to repel non-market and attract your market.
- **Pattern:** "Pretend you are Jerry [Maguire] and you're fed up with your industry. Announce to the world who you are, what you stand for, and why your movement is better than your enemy's." (Detailed manifesto SCRIPT = Expert Secrets / ExpertSecrets.com.)
- **Worked example:** Jerry Maguire's mission statement as the turning point.
- **Source:** Secret 12.

### Building Cult-ure (community/identity)
- **When to use:** Turning customers into a movement that won't churn.
- **Pattern:** Give customers an IDENTITY to become (a movement, not a company). The brand with the stronger identity wins (Apple>Microsoft, Coke>Pepsi, Disney>Universal). Add free swag + a set of agreements (the Manifesto).
- **Worked example:** Renamed the FB group "ClickFunnels" → "Funnel Hackers" (an identity people own). Free signup t-shirt costs ~$100k/month but customers refuse to cancel because they love it.
- **Source:** Secret 26.

### New Opportunity vs Improvement Offer (the opportunity switch)
- **When to use:** Whenever you'd otherwise position as "a better X." 98% hate improvement offers (implicit insult: "you need improving").
- **Pattern:** Never sell an improvement ("be a bit better than now"). Sell a NEW OPPORTUNITY / new vehicle — competition vanishes (blue ocean).
- **Worked example:** Not "a better autoresponder" → "the tool that creates customized FOLLOW-UP FUNNELS." See Section A headline.
- **Source:** Secret 42.

### Stack, Don't Switch (offer sequencing)
- **When to use:** After you've sold the new opportunity — every later offer must STACK onto it, never introduce a second new opportunity.
- **Pattern:** Sell ONE new opportunity; everything after makes IT better/faster/more amazing. Two new opportunities feels cheap ("you said the LAST one was my new opportunity").
- **Worked example:** Expert Secrets book = new opportunity ("you can have an expert business") → ClickFunnels (sell info-products), FunnelScripts (write copy), 2 Comma Club Coaching (run traffic) all STACK on it.
- **Source:** Secret 43.

### The Power of the Bonus — the 4 Functions
- **When to use:** Closing hesitant buyers; structuring any offer ("make sure every offer, no matter what kind, has bonuses").
- **Pattern:** Lead the whole pitch on the ONE thing the product does best; reveal BONUSES only at the END to push them over the cliff. Each bonus makes the core: 1) WORK BETTER (knife sharpener) 2) WORK MORE EFFICIENTLY (knife block) 3) MORE COMPLETE (cutting board) 4) MORE SPECIAL (an extra knife you can't buy anywhere). Bonuses also create scarcity ("first 10 buyers," "expires in 24 hours").
- **Worked example:** Funnel Hacking LIVE — "2 tickets for the price of 1, short time only" + previous year's recordings as a 24-hour bonus → hundreds of sales in hours.
- **Source:** Secret 44.

### Microcontinuity — the 3 Requirements
- **When to use:** Building low-cost recurring revenue.
- **Pattern:** 1) Something NEW + SCARCE each month. 2) LOW barrier to entry. 3) Great onboarding to survive the trial-end.
- **Worked example:** FunnelU funnel (full recipe in Section B).
- **Source:** Secret 45.

### Urgency & Scarcity — the 3 Levers
- **When to use:** Every offer ("two words increase every sale — urgency + scarcity").
- **Pattern:** 1) LIMITED PRICING (price rises after a date). 2) LIMITED QUANTITY (sell out N units — e.g., Inner Circle = 100 spots, opens only when someone drops). 3) LIMITED TIME (offer expires; fast-action bonuses vanish when a live webinar ends). "The best offers use all three at once." For a sale, don't discount — ADD bonuses, then EXPIRE them.
- **Worked example:** Bill Phillips / EAS had unlimited inventory but FAKED scarcity ethically-adjacent: photo of two semi-trucks, "We over-ordered — help us buy our overstocked inventory!" then re-sent the SAME letter with one truck crossed out, "One down, one to go!" — sold MORE from the second letter. (Tickle-Me-Elmo: $20–50 doll → $10,000 on eBay.) **Governance note:** the persona-blueprint forbids FAKE scarcity/countdowns for clients — use real limits only.
- **Source:** Secret 51.

### The Sexiest Offer / FREE Reframe
- **When to use:** Make the SAME products suddenly irresistible by restructuring.
- **Pattern:** Find the part people want MOST → make THAT part FREE (don't change the products, change the structure). "If selling 10 books, make the best 9 FREE when you buy Book 10."
- **Worked example:** "When you buy Funnel Hacks, you'll get ClickFunnels FREE for six months!" (full before/after in Section A).
- **Source:** Secret 52.

### Consumption — the Usage/Retention Lever
- **When to use:** Grow revenue from EXISTING customers (the most forgotten of the 3 growth levers).
- **Pattern:** Write instructions/directives that make customers use MORE per use; for software, reduce churn via onboarding. More consumption = more sales + lower churn.
- **Worked example:** P&G shampoo "WASH, RINSE, REPEAT" → 1x/week became 3x/shower. Coca-Cola's "heavy users" = 5–7 sodas/day. ClickFunnels: free domains, gamification, "working funnel within hours not days."
- **Source:** Secret 53.

### One Gold Nugget (offer highlight)
- **When to use:** Hot market that thinks it knows your topic.
- **Pattern:** Highlight the ONE transformational nugget (don't list everything). You then under-promise/over-deliver; "repeat buyers are almost always driven by expectation."
- **Worked example:** Teleseminar Secrets — "if one thing earns me $1,000…" (Section A).
- **Source:** Secret 54.

### Decoy Pricing (anchor)
- **When to use:** Pushing buyers to your true target price.
- **Pattern:** Create a decoy price that makes the option you WANT them to buy look best (don't assume lowest price wins).
- **Worked example:** $297/mo decoy vs $2,000 lifetime (full script in Section C).
- **Source:** Secret 50.

### The Backdoor Method (sell-the-bonus)
- **When to use:** Awkward/hard-to-sell offers.
- **Pattern:** Sell the ONE wanted component; the hard-to-sell thing comes FREE with it.
- **Worked example:** Black Box → "free when you join the mastermind" (full in Section C).
- **Source:** Secret 77.

### Three Ways to Grow / The 3 Phases
- **When to use:** Deciding which growth lever to pull at your stage.
- **Pattern:** Only 3 ways to grow: 1) MORE customers, 2) SPEND more (ascension), 3) BUY more often (consumption). Phases: Phase 1 (to first $1M) = get as many customers as possible; Phase 2 = ASCENSION (more expensive products + trust/culture/expertise); Phase 3 = CONSUMPTION (use it so much they need more).
- **Worked example:** Hand-lotion print materials showing "apply several times a day" = Phase-3 consumption engineering.
- **Source:** Secrets 53 & 81.

### Hail Marys — the 5 End-of-Period Revenue Sources
- **When to use:** Close to a goal; need a fast spike.
- **Pattern:** Throw Hail Marys from these 5 sources: 1) people who watched but didn't buy. 2) common SUPPORT requests turned into an offer. 3) FUTURE events (sell sponsorships/booths early). 4) UNUSED content bundled into a new product. 5) existing dusty products repackaged.
- **Worked example:** 14,000 non-buyers emailed (1); "pre-pay a year, get 2 months free" pop-up (2); sold 4 booths in a week (3); bundled never-released trainings into "Funnel Immersion" (4) — tripled ClickFunnels revenue needing +$100k/day for a week.
- **Source:** Secret 86.

### Barricade Your Superpower / the 80-20 Flip
- **When to use:** Founder/operator time allocation (governs how a CMO-agent protects the highest-leverage activity).
- **Pattern:** Identify the ONE thing you do best; flip from 80% non-superpower / 20% superpower to the reverse; build barricades (people, hires, SOPs, self-imposed rules) around the superpower.
- **Worked example:** Brunson's superpower = webinar pitching ("more in 90 min than the rest of the month combined") — everything else gets barricaded away from it.
- **Source:** Secret 90.

### Pleasure vs Pain — the 3-Step Procrastination Cure
- **When to use:** Getting yourself (or a client) to do the avoided money task (e.g., turn on the ads).
- **Pattern:** 1) Create a pleasure/reward exciting enough to outweigh the pain. 2) Forbid the reward until the painful task is done. 3) Next time, remember it "wasn't as bad as you made it in your head."
- **Worked example:** Founders avoid turning on FB ads (buy more courses instead) because they fear the ad = the verdict on the whole business; the reward-gate breaks the avoidance.
- **Source:** Secret 99.

## F — Brand Voice & Brand-Building Language Patterns

Reusable voice machinery, each with an example line. This is what lets a copy specialist write ON-brand and RICH instead of thin.

1. **"BOOM" punctuation beat** — drop after a payoff. Ex: "…and the funnel breaks even. BOOM."
2. **"Sexy / sexiest offer" desirability framing** — call a strong offer "sexy." Ex: "Let's make this the sexiest offer they've ever seen."
3. **Analogy-as-teaching** — explain every concept through a concrete picture. Ex: "A value ladder is renovating a house one room at a time."
4. **Self-deprecating vulnerability** — lower status to raise trust. Ex: "I look like I'm 11 years old and I've provided zero value to you at this point."
5. **Secret/curiosity naming** — name assets with "Secrets." Ex: "DotComSecrets, Expert Secrets, Marketing Secrets."
6. **Identity/movement naming** — name the tribe, not the product. Ex: "Funnel Hackers" (never "ClickFunnels users").
7. **Aspirational one-line tagline** — Ex: "You're just one funnel away…"
8. **Math/credit-card proof language** — Ex: "The path is math." / "Let the market vote with their credit cards."
9. **Polarity / line-in-the-sand** — Ex: "If you haven't offended someone by noon, you're not doing it right."
10. **Imperative urgency to ship** — Ex: "Imperfect action builds more millionaires than perfection — ship it today."
11. **Free-reframe offer language** — Ex: "Get [the thing] FREE when you buy [the thing]."
12. **Stack language** — Ex: "Stack, don't switch."
13. **Real-scarcity language** — Ex: "I'm not recording it. It's happening one time only. If you want it, now is the time."
14. **Bonus-reveal language** — Ex: "The first 10 people who buy also get…"
15. **New-opportunity reframe** — Ex: "If your prospect is driving a Volvo, present her with an Audi."
16. **Story-opener intimacy** — Ex: "I don't think we've officially been introduced yet…"
17. **Signature sermon-close** — Ex: "Remember, almost everything in life can be solved with good marketing."
18. **Borrowed-authority quoting** — cite a legend to validate. Ex: "Like Gary Halbert said, 'You're one sales letter away from being financially independent for the rest of your life.'"
19. **Direct second-person command** — Ex: "Find the sexiest part of your offer and make it free."
20. **Words/phrases to AVOID (anti-voice):** "synergy," "leverage best practices," "thought leadership," "let's be safe," "minimal/clean copy," "discount to move volume," "wait until it's perfect," "play it neutral," "trust your gut over the numbers."
- **Source:** Secrets 1, 2, 12, 19, 23, 26, 42, 43, 51, 52, 89, 97 + Section I voice quotes.
