## A — Headline, Hook & Subject-Line Formula Bank

The book's core conviction: a squeeze page must "SQUEEZE out an email address... by using curiosity." You only need two facts about the prospect to write the hook: (a) what they desire most, and (b) what pain they want to avoid. Captured below are every genuine headline/hook/curiosity formula the book provides. Standalone email SUBJECT-LINE formulas are ABSENT IN SOURCE (see Section H) — the book names the emails but gives no subject copy.

### The Core Curiosity Headline (the book's single most reusable formula)
- **When to use:** Page 1 (squeeze/opt-in) of any of the three funnels; any cold-traffic ad or post that must earn an opt-in. This is the headline you reach for first, every time.
- **Pattern:** `How to [WHAT THEY DESIRE MOST] without [THE PAIN THEY WANT TO AVOID].`
- **Worked example:** "How to ace all your exams at school without losing focus or getting distracted during study time."
- **Source:** Secret 9 — Bridge Funnel build (Page 1, "Hook 'Em With Curiosity").

### The Resilience Tagline (kills the hidden objection under the headline)
- **When to use:** Directly under the curiosity headline on the squeeze page, to neutralize the #1 unspoken reason they won't opt in.
- **Pattern:** `This works even if you hate [SOMETHING ELSE THEY DREAD DOING TO GET THE RESULT].`
- **Worked example:** "This works even if you hate dieting." (book's stated structure: "This works even if you hate __________") — paired beneath "How to lose the weight without giving up the foods you love."
- **Source:** Secret 9 — Bridge Funnel build (tagline element).

### The Three-Secrets Registration Bullets (webinar curiosity stack)
- **When to use:** Under the opt-in on a Hotel Meeting / webinar registration page, to manufacture curiosity about what they'll learn and lift opt-in rate.
- **Pattern:** Three curiosity bullets, each shaped `Secret #[n]: How to [desirable outcome] (even though [common obstacle])` — "the THREE SECRETS you're going to unpack in the workshop."
- **Worked example:** "Secret #1: How everyday people are building a full-time income part-time — without a warm-market list. Secret #2: The 1 funnel that recruits while you sleep. Secret #3: Why 98% of distributors quit — and the single step that flips it." (built to the book's "add three main bullets: the THREE SECRETS" instruction).
- **Source:** Secret 11 — Hotel Meeting Funnel (Registration Page).

### The Ticket-Reservation Button (event opt-in CTA microcopy)
- **When to use:** The opt-in button on a Home Party invite page (and any live-event registration), instead of a generic "Submit."
- **Pattern:** `Reserve my ticket to the [EVENT NAME] party.`
- **Worked example:** "Reserve my ticket to the Essential Oils Make-&-Take party."
- **Source:** Secret 10 — Home Party Funnel (Party Invite Page).

### The Confirmation / Viral-Invite Headline (thank-you page)
- **When to use:** The Confirmation Ticket / Countdown page immediately after opt-in, to confirm registration AND trigger a share.
- **Pattern:** `You're registered!` + `Invite a friend [to multiply your odds / so you have someone to do this with].`
- **Worked example:** "You're registered! Want to win the giveaway? Invite a friend — every friend who shows up is another entry for you."
- **Source:** Secrets 10 & 11 — Confirmation / Countdown pages.

### The Curiosity-Squeeze Rule (the mechanism behind every hook above)
- **When to use:** Any time you're tempted to explain on the opt-in page; use this rule to cut copy back to pure curiosity.
- **Pattern:** Promise the RESULT + intrigue, withhold the MECHANISM. "SQUEEZE out an email address... by using curiosity." Two-fact input: `[desire]` + `[pain avoided]` → curiosity headline only; no product name, no ingredients, no how.
- **Worked example:** "Want the body-transformation secret 1.5 million people requested in 6 weeks? Drop your email — I'll send the 12-minute story." (mechanism withheld; result + curiosity only).
- **Source:** Secret 8 — Mechanics (squeeze-page definition).

> True count for Section A: **6 genuine hook/headline formulas** + the curiosity rule. The book is THIN on standalone headline breadth and ABSENT on email subject lines — it defers deeper headline/secrets scripting to *Expert Secrets* (ExpertSecrets.com). Recorded in Section H. NOT padded to 12.

## B — Funnel & Page Recipes (page-by-page)

This is the book's RICHEST section. Brunson's premise: "99% of ALL network marketing companies were built using the SAME THREE FUNNELS (offline)" — replicate each offline ritual as an online funnel. Funnel definition to plant first: "a series of web pages and emails" ordered "Step One > Go here and click this. Step Two > Go here and click this. Step Three > Go here and buy this." Page-type glossary: SQUEEZE PAGE (curiosity opt-in), OTO (one-time offer after purchase), WEBINAR PAGE (live/prerecorded class that builds trust and sells). Below is the FULL recipe set — all three funnels, every page, plus the live-stream operational recipe and the replay loop. None dropped.

### RECIPE 1 — The Bridge Funnel (mimics the offline three-way call)
- **When to use:** Lowest-friction first funnel; fast personal-relationship sell; "act as the bridge between the prospect and the company you represent" so they don't get lost in the corporate site. Build this one FIRST.
- **Pattern (page-by-page):**
  - **Page 1 — Squeeze ("Hook 'Em With Curiosity"), ~3-min build.** Blocks in order: (1) Curiosity headline `How to [desire] without [pain]` — job: stop the scroll, promise the result; (2) Tagline `This works even if you hate [X]` — job: kill the objection; (3) Email opt-in field — job: capture the gatekeeper email for follow-up; (4) Button to Page 2. Wireframe: one screen, one action, no nav, no other links.
  - **Page 2 — The Viral Loop video page.** Blocks: (1) ONE video element (your Epiphany Bridge → Demo → CTA); (2) ONE button. Job: run the full Viral Loop and send to the offer. Nothing else competes with the video.
  - **Company Page — the distributor/corporate order form** (the "anti-sales department" you are bridging past). Job: take the order; on Page 2 "actually show them the order form and how to fill it out so they don't get confused."
- **Worked example:** Page 1 headline "How to get more energy and lose the afternoon crash — without cutting your coffee" / tagline "This works even if you've tried every supplement on the shelf" / [Email] / [Yes — show me the 11-minute story →] → Page 2 video → [Get my kit on the next page →] → company order form.
- **Source:** Secret 9 — "Bridge Funnel."

### RECIPE 2 — The Home Party Funnel (mimics the in-home product party)
- **When to use:** When you want event energy, FOMO ("When people see others buying, it helps them to buy more"), and recurring sales — and you want the event to keep selling on replay. Brunson used one to "generate over 1.5 million leads in a new company in just six weeks."
- **Pattern (page-by-page):**
  - **Page 1 — Party Invite Page.** Blocks: great headline + tagline + picture + description + **TIME** + **DATE** + opt-in fields + button "Reserve my ticket to the ___ party." Bait elements: (a) a countdown **timer** (buzz), (b) a hinted **giveaway** (everyone who attends live is entered). Reuse weekly — just change date/time.
  - **Page 2 — Confirmation Ticket Page.** Blocks: "You're registered" / "Invite a friend" copy + social SHARE buttons + optional prompt to join your Facebook group / Instagram before the party. Triggers the 4-email reminder sequence (Section D).
  - **Page 3 — Party Hangout Page** (3 required sections): (1) headline + date + time; (2) the LIVE STREAM where you host; (3) product images + buttons with YOUR distributor links to buy/join. Optional: live comment/Q&A. Run the FULL Viral Loop LIVE. Missed it? Auto-replay until the next live event.
  - **Company Sales Page** — order form via your distributor link.
- **Worked example:** Invite headline "Come make 3 roll-on blends you'll actually use — live, Thursday 8pm ET" / "🎁 Everyone live is entered to win the starter kit" / [timer] / [Reserve my ticket to the Make-&-Take party] → Confirmation "You're in! Invite a friend = an extra entry" → Hangout page: live stream + "Grab the Starter Kit →" buttons.
- **Live-stream how-to (operational recipe, reproduce exactly):** Use YouTube Live (free) → Events → Enable Live Streaming → create event (time/date/title/Save) → YouTube gives a unique link → paste into the ClickFunnels video element (auto-creates the stream + pre-live countdown). Pick "most interesting, popular, and easy to demo" products. Invite friends/team for banter (YouTube Live = up to 9 on camera, unlimited viewers). Day-of: good lighting + space + products ready; plug computer directly into router/modem; shut down nonessential programs; sign in ~1 hr early; click START (opens broadcast software — does NOT go live yet); use a Lower Third for name/tagline + fun hat/effects; invite up to 9 people via the "person + plus" icon within 15 min to test mics/cameras; then hit START BROADCAST to go live.
- **Replay loop (after it ends):** run weekly; send follow-up email(s) with the replay link (YouTube Live leaves the video up → hangout page auto-becomes a replay page); switch the registration page date/time to the next party → you get "an ongoing stream of people watching the replay, a surge watching live, and new registrants each week."
- **Source:** Secret 10 — "Home Party Funnel."

### RECIPE 3 — The Hotel Meeting Funnel (mimics the hotel opportunity meeting / webinar)
- **When to use:** Brunson's favorite funnel; built to "recruit NEW LEADERS, not just product lovers." Runs LIVE or evergreen/prerecorded ("so it works while you sleep"). Designed to (1) introduce the product, (2) create the money opportunity, (3) show how they grow their own team by bringing people to the meeting.
- **Pattern (page-by-page):**
  - **Page 1 — Registration Page.** Like the Home Party invite — headline, tagline, input fields, time, date — PLUS the REQUIRED EXTRA: "add three main bullets: the THREE SECRETS you're going to unpack in the workshop." Evergreen mode: viewer picks a date OR watches a replay now. Live mode: one fixed future date/time.
  - **Page 2 — Countdown Page.** Same as the Home Party thank-you: "You're registered" / "Invite a friend" + social share buttons + optional Facebook group / Instagram prompt. Triggers the 3-email reminder sequence (Section D).
  - **Page 3 — Webinar Page.** Minimal blocks: workshop title + ONE video element (your recording) + a button to a form/email/contact. PRO MECHANIC: use ClickFunnels' "delayed action" so the button (and any promised free gift) doesn't appear until you announce it in the presentation ("encourage people to watch and not click prematurely"). Optional: a Facebook comments element under the video (check daily). Live alternative: YouTube Live (more structured) OR Zoom/GoToWebinar and "eliminate the webinar page altogether." Video content = the Viral Loop, opportunity-led: Epiphany Bridge → Demo (here you "Demo the product so they can see how great it is to SELL") → CTA to "connect with you and become a part of the team."
  - **Company Page** — the opportunity/enrollment form.
- **Worked example:** Reg headline "The Hidden Funnel Workshop: how ordinary distributors recruit online" / 3 secrets bullets (see Section A) / [pick your time] → Countdown "You're registered — invite a teammate" → Webinar page: title + [delayed] "Join my team →" button revealed at the 38-min mark.
- **Source:** Secret 11 — "Hotel Meeting Funnel."

### Page-Type Mini-Recipes (the reusable building blocks)
- **When to use:** When assembling any of the three funnels; these are the named page types the book defines.
- **Pattern:** **Squeeze page** = headline + tagline + email field + 1 button, "SQUEEZE out an email address... using curiosity," one action only. **Video/Viral-Loop page** = 1 video + 1 button, no distractions. **Hangout/Webinar page** = title + live or recorded video + (optionally delayed) CTA button + optional comments. **Confirmation/Countdown page** = "You're registered" + "invite a friend" + share buttons. **OTO** = "one-time-only offer you show your customer after they've purchased something."
- **Worked example:** A squeeze page is "the gatekeeper to your real offer" — capture the email so they "REMEMBER you, your cool offers," then send to the Viral Loop video page.
- **Source:** Secret 8 — "Mechanics" + funnel/page definitions.

### Duplication Variants (apply to ALL three recipes above)
- **When to use:** Decide BEFORE you ship — the CTA wiring changes depending on who drives traffic, or you'll steal your own team's recruits.
- **Pattern:** **Level 1 (solo):** button → corporate site via YOUR distributor link (show them the order form). **Level 2 (team sends traffic):** do NOT use your distributor link — CTA = "get back in contact with the person who sent them into the funnel" (link the distributor's contact form right under the live stream, one click). **Level 3 (share funnel):** hand out a ClickFunnels share-funnel link; the downline copies the funnel, swaps in their own video, points the button to their own distributor link.
- **Worked example:** Level 2 CTA under the live stream: "Loved this? Message the person who invited you — they'll get you the exact kit at the offer price."
- **Source:** Secrets 9-11 — Duplication variants per funnel.

## C — Script Bank (sales / objection / follow-up / discovery / close)

Every word-for-word "say-this" asset in the book. These are the verbatim or near-verbatim scripts a copy/video specialist drops into the Viral Loop.

### The Epiphany Bridge Script (sell without selling — 10-question sequence)
- **When to use:** The opening of EVERY funnel video and marketing effort; whenever copy is sliding into "technobabble" (logic/science/ingredients — "the #1 killer of sales for network marketers").
- **Pattern:** Answer these 10 in order, then drop the questions and tell only the answers: (1) Backstory — "what gives us a vested interest in your journey?" (2) Desire — "what did you want to accomplish?" (3) External struggle (4) Internal struggle (5) Wall — "what wall/problem did you hit?" (6) Epiphany — "what was the epiphany / new opportunity you discovered?" (7) Plan (8) Conflict (9) Achievement — "the end result you achieved" (10) Transformation.
- **Worked example:** Brunson's own intro IS the script: Backstory (13-yr-old collecting junk-mail business-opp cassette tapes) → Desire (make money sharing a message) → Wall (no credit card, parents wouldn't listen) → 23 years in NM → Wall #2 (homeless man with a Ferrari) → Epiphany (Duplication + funnels) → ClickFunnels.
- **Source:** Secret 4 — "Epiphany Bridge."

### The Billy Mays Demo Script (5 objection-crushers, in order)
- **When to use:** Immediately after the Epiphany Bridge, WHILE physically demoing the product — "to crush any objections the customer may have after hearing your Epiphany Bridge Story." ("If a picture is worth 1,000 words, a live demo is worth 10,000 or more!")
- **Pattern:** (1) **Faster & Easier** — show YOUR product is the faster/easier choice vs alternatives; (2) **Eliminating Alternatives** — make every competing option look unappealing via your unique selling point; (3) **Introduce the Price (with an anchor)** — compare/anchor the price to something expensive; (4) **Urgency & Scarcity** — limited inventory / offer ends at the close of the meeting / "Give people a reason to act NOW"; (5) **Risk Reversal** — explain the guarantee ("try it 30 days, refund if you don't like it").
- **Worked example (price anchor, verbatim):** "So these oils are $25 a bottle, but remember that one visit to the doctor and pharmacist can cost you $500 or more! With that kind of money, you could get 20 bottles and they would last you 5 years... for just the cost of one doctor's visit."
- **Source:** Secret 5 — "The Demo."

### The Risk-Reversal / Guarantee Script
- **When to use:** Demo step 5 and any offer block; whenever a prospect hesitates on price/commitment.
- **Pattern:** `Try it for [30 days]. If you don't [get the result / love it], [get a full refund] — no questions. The risk is on me, not you.` Rationale to keep in mind: "refunds actually HELP people buy, because it takes the risk off of them and puts it on you."
- **Worked example:** "Use it every day for 30 days. If you don't feel the difference, email me and I'll refund every penny — you keep the bottle."
- **Source:** Secret 5 — Risk Reversal.

### The Permission-to-Sell Transition (CTA opener — dissolves the nerves)
- **When to use:** The hand-off from Demo to offer, "where most people get nervous." Ask permission first — "When they say yes (and they always do), all nerves and awkwardness instantly disappear."
- **Pattern:** `So you've heard how this helped me ([Epiphany Bridge]), and I've shown you how it can help you ([demo]), so I've put together a very special offer so you can have the same benefits I've had, and I'd like to go over them for a few minutes. Is that okay?`
- **Worked example (verbatim):** "So you've heard and seen how this product has helped me (Epiphany Bridge), and I've just shown you here how it can help you (the demo), so I've put together a very special offer for you so you can have the same benefits I've had, and I'd like to go over them for a few minutes. Is that okay?"
- **Source:** Secret 6 — "The CTA."

### The Direct-Instruction CTA Rule (say the obvious, repeat it)
- **When to use:** Every CTA; the close of every Viral Loop video.
- **Pattern:** Make the CTA "clear and direct. Tell people what to do. Even things that seem completely obvious... say them anyway." Give the CTA MULTIPLE TIMES, alternating between demo and CTA "so they hear the instructions multiple times and in multiple different ways."
- **Worked example:** "Click the button right below this video. It'll take you to the order form. Fill in your name and address, choose the starter kit, and click submit. That's it — click the button now."
- **Source:** Secret 6 — "The CTA."

### The Duplication-Safe CTA (Level 2 — don't steal your team's recruits)
- **When to use:** When OTHER distributors are sending traffic into a shared funnel; never link to your own signup here.
- **Pattern:** `To get this very special offer, contact the person who referred you to this [webinar/party] today. Tell them you want to get the [OFFER NAME] offer, and they will get you signed up right away.`
- **Worked example (verbatim):** "To get this very special offer, contact the person who referred you to this webinar today. Tell them you want to get the __________ offer, and they will get you signed up right away."
- **Source:** Secret 6 / Secret 7 — CTA + Duplication.

### The Duplication Level-2 Hand-off One-Liner (recruit enablement)
- **When to use:** The exact sentence you give a brand-new recruit so they can use your funnel immediately (repeated identically for all 3 funnels).
- **Pattern:** `Bring people into this funnel. It will convert them for you, and you'll get paid!`
- **Worked example (verbatim):** "Bring people into this funnel. It will convert them for you, and you'll get paid!" (you presell/close for them).
- **Source:** Secret 7 — "Duplication," Level 2.

### The Duplication Level-3 Share-Funnel Script
- **When to use:** When a recruit is ready to run their OWN funnel and tell their own Epiphany Bridge.
- **Pattern:** `Click this link → it copies my whole funnel into your account → go in and replace my videos with yours → point the button to your distributor link.`
- **Worked example:** "When they click on that link, it will copy your funnel into their account! Then they can just go into the funnel and replace your videos with theirs."
- **Source:** Secret 7 — Duplication, Level 3.

### The Three-Way-Call Bridge Script (offline mechanic the Bridge Funnel replicates)
- **When to use:** Understanding what the Bridge Funnel video must DO; also usable as a live three-way-call structure.
- **Pattern:** Upline gets on the call with new rep + prospect → tells the story → asks for the sale → "create a 'bridge' between you and the product/opportunity." Online twin: YOUR video is the bridge.
- **Worked example:** "Hey [prospect], I wanted you to hear this from [upline] who's been where you are — [upline tells the Epiphany Bridge], then asks for the sale."
- **Source:** Secret 2 — "The 3 Lost Funnels."

### The Hotel-Meeting Recruiting Close (bring-a-friend growth loop)
- **When to use:** Closing an opportunity meeting/webinar to drive the next meeting's attendance — the engine behind "40-50 people → 75 → 150 → 400-500."
- **Pattern:** `[Sell product + opportunity] → "Bring [your friends] to the meeting [next week] and I'll close them for you."`
- **Worked example:** "If you know two people who'd love this, bring them to next Thursday's workshop — I'll tell the whole story for you and close them, and they go on your team."
- **Source:** Secret 2 / Secret 11 — Hotel Meeting.

## E — Frameworks, Models & Templates (with all steps)

Every named framework reproduced with its full internal structure, executable without the book.

### Framework 1 — The 4 Phases of the Average Network Marketer (the pain-cascade diagnostic)
- **When to use:** As a lead-in "which phase are you stuck in?" hook for any opportunity audience; the agitation half of any recruiting funnel.
- **Pattern (4 stages → escape):**
  1. **The NFL Club (No Friends Left)** — sell friends & family first; "a huge percentage of people drop out almost immediately"; even supportive families hit "the exhaustion factor"; social media is just a bigger "finite pool."
  2. **The Anti-Sales Department** — you hand a hot buyer to the corporate website, which is "built by lawyers, not marketers, so they do the OPPOSITE of what you want": too many options, dead cart, kills the emotional connection.
  3. **The Clone Wars** — you go online only to discover the company gave 300,000+ distributors "the exact same template and products and instructions"; "98% of businesses fail." FIX CONCEPT = **Pattern Interrupt** ("doing things differently... building and customizing your own funnels").
  4. **The Homeless Man With a Ferrari** — you finally win (Brunson "sold enough product to win a Ferrari... within just 90 days"), then "within a year, my commission checks had gone down to almost zero" — because you skipped **Duplication**.
  5. **Escape line (the turn):** "Escape the Phases With Funnels!" — top earners "use three core funnels."
- **Worked example:** Use as the cold-open of a recruiting webinar: name the four traps as the prospect's lived story, then promise the three-funnel escape.
- **Source:** Secret 1 — "Evolution of the Network Marketing Funnel."

### Framework 2 — The 3 Lost Funnels (offline mechanic → online funnel)
- **When to use:** Choosing which funnel to build; the strategic spine of the whole system.
- **Pattern:** (1) **Bridge Funnel** = the three-way call (your video is the bridge); (2) **Home Party Funnel** = the in-home party (FOMO + replay + "one party turns into four or five other ones"); (3) **Hotel Meeting Funnel** = the hotel opportunity meeting/webinar (recruit NEW LEADERS, live or evergreen). "They simply took what worked so well offline then replicated it online!"
- **Worked example:** Goal = fast personal sell → Bridge; goal = event energy + recurring sales → Home Party; goal = recruit leaders at scale → Hotel Meeting. (Full page builds in Section B.)
- **Source:** Secret 2 — "The 3 Lost Funnels."

### Framework 3 — Your 2 Dream Clients (avatar + funnel-lead rule)
- **When to use:** Before structuring ANY funnel; whenever messaging feels muddy ("a confused buyer doesn't buy").
- **Pattern:** A network marketer has TWO avatars — never both at once: **Avatar A "I love this product" (Suzy + John)** — motivated by RESULTS/transformation, "don't really want to be entrepreneurs"; **Avatar B "I love selling stuff" (Karen + Steve)** — motivated by MONEY, "want to make sure the product sells well." **The funnel-lead rule:** lead with the PRODUCT then introduce the opportunity (if you're like Suzy/John), OR lead with the OPPORTUNITY then introduce the product (if you're like Karen/Steve). Master ONE funnel first, then build the second for the other avatar.
- **Worked example:** A weight-loss product-lover funnel leads with the transformation story, then ascends buyers into "and you can earn by sharing it." A builder funnel leads with "build an income online," then proves it with a product that sells.
- **Source:** Secret 3 — "Your 2 Dream Clients."

### Framework 4 — The Viral Loop (the 4-part sequence that MUST run inside ALL 3 funnels)
- **When to use:** Every offer page and every funnel video. "There is ONE thing you must master... all three of them must have this dialed in, or this system doesn't work."
- **Pattern (4 components in order + the mastery checklist):** (1) **Epiphany Bridge** (sell without selling), (2) **Demo** (Billy Mays — crush objections), (3) **Call to Action** (permission + clear/repeated), (4) **Duplication** (3 levels). Mastery checklist (verbatim): "1. Use Epiphany Bridge Scripts to sell without actually selling. 2. Use the Billy Mays Script during your Demo to crush objections about the price and product. 3. Transition to a Call to Action with a question that gives you permission to sell. 4. Leverage the three levels of Duplication to find those first people on your team to do the job of building out your downline."
- **Worked example:** A 12-minute Home Party live = 5 min Epiphany Bridge story → 4 min Demo (make the blend, anchor the price, guarantee) → permission CTA repeated twice → Duplication ("message the person who invited you").
- **Source:** Secret 7 — "Duplication" (loop summary) + Secrets 4-6.

### Framework 5 — The Epiphany Bridge (10-question story template)
- **When to use:** Building the core selling story (full script in Section C). Listed here as a framework so it's reproducible step-by-step.
- **Pattern:** Backstory → Desire → External struggle → Internal struggle → Wall → Epiphany → Plan → Conflict → Achievement → Transformation. Works for "little or big epiphanies"; deployed "in every single funnel and marketing effort."
- **Worked example:** See Section C, "The Epiphany Bridge Script" (Brunson's cassette-tape origin).
- **Source:** Secret 4.

### Framework 6 — The 3 Levels of Duplication (the step everyone forgets)
- **When to use:** Before shipping ANY funnel — "You get paid by being the best at helping OTHERS to close sales... the most pure form of entrepreneurship I know of." Avoids the Ferrari-no-foundation fate.
- **Pattern:** **Level 1** — sign the person up (run Epiphany Bridge + Demo + CTA). **Level 2** (first level of Duplication) — have your people send others into YOUR funnel ("Bring people into this funnel..."); you presell/close for them. **Level 3** (second level) — share your funnel (ClickFunnels share-funnel link) so they tell their OWN Epiphany Bridge.
- **Worked example:** New recruit this week → hand them the Level-2 one-liner and your funnel link today; when they're ready to lead, send the Level-3 share-funnel link.
- **Source:** Secret 7.

### Framework 7 — The Traffic Playbook ("How To Fill Your Funnel," 10 named methods)
- **When to use:** Once a funnel is live and needs a "steady stream of leads each day." "Traffic is the fuel in your funnel."
- **Pattern (all 10):** (1) Email signature link; (2) Instagram bio link that "gives something away for free"; (3) your own Facebook group ("free community around the topics that most interest your ideal customers"); (4) Facebook ads (caution: "be careful about making business claims on your landing pages"); (5) other people's groups; (6) Pinterest pins → blog posts; (7) SEO on those blog posts; (8) guest posting (funnel link in bio); (9) podcasting (host or guest); (10) YouTube channel (searchable tutorials + end-cards to funnels).
- **Worked example:** A new distributor turns on 2 sources this week: IG bio link → Bridge Funnel opt-in, and one Facebook group post per day pointing to the same opt-in.
- **Source:** Secret 13 — "How To Fill Your Funnel."

### Framework 8 — The 13-Secret / 4-Section Course Architecture (reusable curriculum template)
- **When to use:** Repurposing the book as a course/onboarding curriculum for any "offline-to-online" recruiting system.
- **Pattern:** Intro (Epiphany Bridge) → **Section 1 "The 3 Lost Funnels"** (Secret 1 the 4 Phases / Secret 2 the 3 Lost Funnels / Secret 3 the 2 Dream Clients) → **Section 2 "The Viral Loop"** (Secret 4 Epiphany Bridge / Secret 5 Demo / Secret 6 CTA / Secret 7 Duplication) → **Section 3 "Digging In"** (Secret 8 Mechanics / Secret 9 Bridge build / Secret 10 Home Party build / Secret 11 Hotel Meeting build) → **Section 4 "What To Do Next"** (Secret 12 Get Started / Secret 13 Fill Your Funnel).
- **Worked example:** A 13-module distributor onboarding course mirrors this spine, one Secret per module.
- **Source:** Full book architecture (table of contents).

### Framework 9 — The Page-Type Glossary & Step-Path Funnel Model
- **When to use:** Whenever a builder confuses "website" with "funnel."
- **Pattern:** A funnel = ordered pages + emails: "Step One > Go here and click this. Step Two > Go here and click this. Step Three > Go here and buy this." Page types: SQUEEZE (curiosity opt-in), OTO (post-purchase one-time offer), WEBINAR (live/recorded class that builds trust and sells), HANGOUT (live stream), CONFIRMATION/COUNTDOWN (registered + invite). Knife analogy: funnel page-types are like a chef's/bread/steak knife — "all sharp objects... we label them to describe what they are best used for."
- **Worked example:** Don't send a hot lead to a website with "8,500,000 choices"; send them down a 2-3 page funnel with one action per page.
- **Source:** Secret 8 — "Mechanics."

## F — Brand Voice & Brand-Building Language Patterns

The reusable voice machinery, with example lines. This is what lets a copy specialist write ON-brand and richly. (>= 15 patterns, each with an example.)

1. **Coin sticky, slightly-mocking names for problems AND solutions.** Name the enemy AND the cure. *Example:* "the NFL (No Friends Left) Club," "the Anti-Sales Department," "the Clone Wars," "the Homeless Man With a Ferrari," "Pattern Interrupt," "the Viral Loop," "Epiphany Bridge," "share funnel."
2. **Name the villain explicitly.** *Example:* "These sites are built by lawyers, not marketers, so they do the OPPOSITE of what you want."
3. **Lead with first-person story, then extract the principle.** *Example:* open with the cassette-tape kid / the essential-oils party, then state the lesson.
4. **Vivid extended metaphors to make abstract ideas concrete.** *Example:* the corporate site as "Grandpa... loves to tell you all kinds of stories with completely unimportant details, and has really bad breath"; funnel pages as labeled kitchen knives.
5. **Short punchy sentence fragments for emphasis.** *Example:* "Buzzkill. Lost date. Better luck next time." / "So simple, so powerful." / "It was awesome."
6. **Direct 2nd-person, conversational, anti-jargon.** *Example:* "It's not cake or a kitchen tool." / "sit back, relax, grab some popcorn."
7. **Rhetorical Q&A.** *Example:* "Who has time for that? Not me!" / "Ready? Good. Me too!"
8. **Big-result proof claims woven into the story (never bare brags).** *Example:* "I used a Home Party Funnel to generate over 1.5 million leads in a new company in just six weeks." / "ClickFunnels achieves in 30 minutes what used to take me 3 months, a whole team, and thousands of dollars."
9. **Emotion-over-logic framing as a recurring drumbeat.** *Example:* "People do not buy things based on logic; they buy based on emotion, then try to justify their purchase logically."
10. **Anti-hype humility (we're not reinventing anything).** *Example:* "Funnels are not new, they are not magic, and we're not reinventing the wheel."
11. **The signature sign-off / tagline.** *Example:* "Remember, you're just one funnel away..."
12. **Permission-to-sell language (dissolve the close).** *Example:* "...and I'd like to go over them for a few minutes. Is that okay?"
13. **Price-anchor language (small price vs huge painful alternative).** *Example:* "So [product] is [low price], but remember [expensive alternative] can cost you [big number] or more!"
14. **Risk-reversal / guarantee language (move risk to the seller).** *Example:* "Refunds actually HELP people buy, because it takes the risk off of them and puts it on you."
15. **Urgency & scarcity language tied to the event.** *Example:* "End the offer at the close of the meeting." / "Give people a reason to act NOW rather than later."
16. **Curiosity-squeeze language (withhold the mechanism).** *Example:* "SQUEEZE out an email address... by using curiosity."
17. **Free-CTA stacking / "free + shipping" offer language.** *Example:* relentless free offers — "3 free share funnels," "free copy of Expert Secrets," "free PDF."
18. **Numbered "secrets" framing for curiosity + structure.** *Example:* "13 Secrets," "the THREE SECRETS you're going to unpack."
19. **Repetition of a small set of named concepts until they stick.** *Example:* "the Viral Loop," "Duplication," and "dream client" recur on nearly every page.
20. **Identity/transformation language (who they become), not feature language.** *Example:* "I had become the homeless man, with no money but a really sweet car" → the cautionary identity you don't want.

> Words to AVOID (off-brand for this persona): guru, growth-hack, get-rich-quick, guaranteed income, passive money machine, secret algorithm, viral overnight, disruptor, and any income/medical claim (the book's own legal caution).
