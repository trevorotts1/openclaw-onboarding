## LENS A — COACHING METHODOLOGY (items 1-11)

### 1. Central Thesis
99% of all network marketing companies were built using the SAME THREE FUNNELS offline. When distributors go online they forget these proven funnels and chase fads. The fix is to faithfully replicate the offline selling scenarios online as sales funnels. Closing mantra: "You're just one funnel away."

### 2. The Four Phases (the traps to escape)
New distributors get stuck in a predictable sequence; the goal is to step OUTSIDE the phases onto a funnel foundation, not to grind through them:
- **Phase 1 — The NFL Club (No Friends Left):** sell to friends/family until the finite pool is exhausted; social media is still a finite pond.
- **Phase 2 — The Anti-Sales Department:** sending warm buyers to the corporate website (built by lawyers, not marketers) which repels them. Metaphor: "handing her off to Grandpa."
- **Phase 3 — The Clone Wars:** the company gives 300,000+ reps the identical template; audiences tune out the repeated pattern. Antidote = Pattern Interrupt.
- **Phase 4 — The Homeless Man With a Ferrari:** a big one-time contest win (Brunson won a Ferrari in 90 days) but commissions crater within a year for lack of Duplication/residual.

### 3. The Two Dream Clients
Every network-marketing opportunity has TWO customers: "I love this product" people (Suzy + John — results/transformation driven) and "I love selling stuff" people (Karen + Steve — business builders). Each funnel must speak to only ONE; "a confused buyer doesn't buy." Lead with product then introduce opportunity, OR lead with opportunity then introduce product — and ascend them later.

### 4. The Epiphany Bridge (sell without selling)
People buy on emotion and justify with logic. The #1 sin of selling is "technobabble" (science, ingredients, research). Instead, tell the story of the epiphany that made YOU believe, so the prospect has the same aha moment. (References Kim Klaver's *If My Product's So Great, How Come I Can't Sell It?* and Brunson's own *Expert Secrets*.)

### 5. The Epiphany Bridge Script (10 beats)
Backstory → Desire → External struggle → Internal struggle → Wall → Epiphany → Plan → Conflict → Achievement → Transformation. Answer the questions in sequence; eventually drop the questions and just tell the story.

### 6. The Viral Loop (the ONE thing you must master)
A selling sequence present in all three funnels with four components: Epiphany Bridge → Demo → Call to Action → Duplication. Miss this and funnels never gain momentum.

### 7. The Demo (Billy Mays method)
After the story, demo the product to crush objections. Five elements: Faster & Easier (vs competitors), Eliminate Alternatives, Introduce/Anchor the Price ("$25 a bottle vs a $500 doctor visit"), Urgency & Scarcity, Risk Reversal (a guarantee that moves risk to the seller).

### 8. The Call to Action (CTA)
The nerve-wracking part made easy by asking permission first ("…is that okay?"). Be clear and direct — say even the obvious steps. Give the CTA multiple times, alternating with the demo. For duplication, don't send straight to your distributor signup link; send them back to the referrer.

### 9. Duplication — the Ultimate Secret
You get paid not for closing your own sales but for helping OTHERS close theirs ("the most pure form of entrepreneurship"). Three levels: L1 sign the person up; L2 have your people send others into your funnel (you presell); L3 share-funnel so they tell their own Epiphany Bridge story. This is how to avoid the homeless-man-with-a-Ferrari trap.

### 10. Master One, Then Scale
Build and master ONE funnel and learn to drive traffic to it before building a second. Don't reinvent — replicate what already works offline.

### 11. Emotional vs Logical Selling Belief
Core conviction: "People do not buy things based on logic; they buy based on emotion, then try to justify their purchase logically." Coaching the founder means moving them from logic-led pitching to story-led attraction so they stop burning their warm market and stop freezing at the close.

## LENS B — AGENT GOVERNANCE (items 12-20)

### 12. Definition of a Funnel (vs a website)
A funnel = a series of web pages + emails ordered to create a path to ONE action ("Step 1 click this → Step 2 click this → Step 3 buy this"). A website gives 8,500,000 choices. Governance standard: agents ship ordered paths, never multi-choice websites. Page types: squeeze page (squeeze out an email via curiosity), OTO (one-time offer after purchase), webinar page.

### 13. The Three Lost Funnels — build specs
- **Bridge Funnel** (mimics the three-way call): Page 1 curiosity squeeze (capture email) → Page 2 Viral Loop video (Epiphany Bridge + Demo + CTA) → company order page. Two pages you build + one company page.
- **Home Party Funnel** (mimics the home party; FOMO): Page 1 Party Invite (headline, time/date, timer, giveaway hint) → Page 2 Confirmation Ticket (share buttons) → Page 3 Party Hangout (live stream via YouTube Live, products + distributor links, replay keeps party going). Generated 1.5M leads in 6 weeks.
- **Hotel Meeting Funnel** (mimics the hotel meeting; recruits LEADERS): Page 1 Registration (3-secrets bullets) → Page 2 Countdown → Page 3 Webinar (delayed-reveal button). Live or prerecorded ("works while you sleep").

### 14. Curiosity Squeeze Headline Formula
"How to [what they desire most] without [the pain they want to avoid]" + tagline "This works even if you hate ___." One email field, one button. Governs all opt-in/squeeze page copy.

### 15. Email Reminder Sequence Spec
Party/webinar funnels require a reminder sequence: Confirmation Email → Day-Before Email → One-Hour-Before Email → Party's Live Email. Emails keep people on the path; even with step-by-step instructions, people veer off and need reminders.

### 16. The Three Levels of Duplication (governance encoding)
Funnel builds must encode the duplication level: L1 = direct signup link; L2 = "contact the person who referred you" (never sign up someone else's prospect on your own link); L3 = ClickFunnels share-funnel link that copies the funnel into a recruit's account. The funnel's CTA wiring changes by level to protect downline credit.

### 17. The Anti-Sales-Department Rule
Never strand a warm prospect on the corporate site. The funnel must pre-frame and even show the order form ("show them how to fill it out") before the handoff so they aren't confused by the company's outdated cart. Governance: protect the emotional connection through to checkout.

### 18. One-Dream-Client-Per-Funnel Constraint
A non-negotiable build constraint: never mix the product-lover and builder messages in one funnel ("we can't confuse TWO sales messages… they get diluted"). Each funnel addresses exactly one avatar.

### 19. Kill-Technobabble Standard
Copy and scripts must lead with emotion/story, never with science/ingredients/research. Leading with logic is the #1 sales killer. Governance review rejects logic-led openings.

### 20. Traffic-Fill Requirement
"Traffic is the fuel in your funnel." A funnel without an assigned daily lead source is incomplete. Ten named sources: email signature, Instagram bio link, your own Facebook group, Facebook ads, other people's groups, Pinterest→blog, SEO, guest posting, podcasting, YouTube channel. Funnel builds must specify 2+ sources.
