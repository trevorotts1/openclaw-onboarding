## A — Headline, Hook & Subject-Line Formula Bank

> Edwards' rule: the headline is *"the #1 single most important piece of sales copy ever,"* and he spends up to **50% of total project time** here. Job of a headline: *"get people to stop what they're doing and start reading/watching,"* by connecting emotionally to a **fear** or a **desire**, while repelling the wrong people. Use **odd numbers** (3/5/7/9) — they read as more credible.

### A1. "How To Get [RESULT]"
- **When to use:** Simplest workhorse headline; warm/solution-aware traffic that already wants the result.
- **Pattern:** How To Get [SPECIFIC DESIRED RESULT]
- **Worked example:** "How To Get Rid Of Acne"
- **Source:** Secret #6 (Headline Templates)

### A2. "How To [RESULT] In As Little As [TIMEFRAME]"
- **When to use:** When a believable speed-to-result is your edge. Timeframe must be credible or the BS detector trips.
- **Pattern:** How To [DESIRED RESULT] In As Little As [BELIEVABLE TIMEFRAME]
- **Worked example:** "How To Double Your Pushups In As Little As 10 Days"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A3. "How To [RESULT] In As Little As [TIME] . . . even if [OBJECTION]!"
- **When to use:** Whenever a single dominant objection is killing belief; the "even if" kills it inside the headline (this is the Eliminator from the Profitable Product Promise, see E7).
- **Pattern:** How To [RESULT] In As Little As [TIME] . . . even if [THE THING THEY FEAR DISQUALIFIES THEM]!
- **Worked example:** "How To Pass Your PT Test In As Little As 2 Weeks . . . even if you can't do a single pullup right now!"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A4. "How Every [AVATAR] Can [RESULT]"
- **When to use:** Mass-inclusion promise; signals "this is for people exactly like you."
- **Pattern:** How Every [SPECIFIC AVATAR] Can [DESIRED RESULT + SPEED]
- **Worked example:** "How Every Teenager With Pimples Can Have Clearer Skin Fast!"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A5. "[# odd] Quick And Easy Ways To [RESULT]"
- **When to use:** Articles, blogs, videos, lead magnets — list-style curiosity; great for cold/warm content.
- **Pattern:** [3/5/7/9] Quick And Easy Ways To [DESIRED RESULT]
- **Worked example:** "5 Quick And Easy Ways To Max Out Your Pushups"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A6. "[# odd] Fast Ways To Get [RESULT] And Avoid [PAIN]"
- **When to use:** Pairs the carrot and the stick in one line — desire + the pain they dodge.
- **Pattern:** [# odd] Fast Ways To Get [RESULT] And Avoid [SPECIFIC NAMED PAIN]
- **Worked example:** "3 Fast Ways To Get A Better PT Test Score And Avoid 'Fat Boy' PT Squad"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A7. "[# odd] Quick And Easy Ways To Get [RESULT] . . . Even If [OBJECTION]!"
- **When to use:** List headline that also forgives past failure (lets them off the hook).
- **Pattern:** [# odd] Quick And Easy Ways To Get [RESULT] . . . Even If [PAST FAILURE / DISQUALIFIER]!
- **Worked example:** "5 Quick And Easy Ways To Get Better Test Scores . . . Even If You've Failed Every Time Before!"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A8. "Which Of These [TOPIC] Mistakes Will You Make?"
- **When to use:** Mistake headlines exploit the trained fear of looking stupid. High curiosity + slight threat.
- **Pattern:** Which Of These [TOPIC] Mistakes Will You Make?
- **Worked example:** "Which Of These Copywriting Mistakes Will You Make?"
- **Source:** Secret #6 (Mistakes group)

### A9. "[TOPIC] Mistakes All [AVATAR] Need To Avoid!"
- **When to use:** Targets a specific avatar with a danger-of-error frame.
- **Pattern:** [#] [TOPIC] Mistakes All [SPECIFIC AVATAR] Need To Avoid!
- **Worked example:** "Acne Treatment Mistakes All Pregnant Women Need To Avoid!"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A10. "[# odd] [TOPIC] Mistakes Every [AVATAR] Needs To Avoid!"
- **When to use:** The numbered mistake variant — most clickable of the mistake set.
- **Pattern:** [# odd] [TOPIC] Mistakes Every [AVATAR] Needs To Avoid!
- **Worked example:** "3 PT Testing Mistakes Every Marine Needs To Avoid!"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A11. "WARNING: Here's What Every [AVATAR] Needs To Know About [TOPIC]"
- **When to use:** The jungle-alarm principle — a danger call makes ALL animals pay attention (the all-clear only interests its own species). Do not cry wolf / do not abuse it.
- **Pattern:** WARNING: Here's What Every [AVATAR] Needs To Know About [TOPIC]
- **Worked example:** "WARNING: Here's What Every New Marine Needs To Know About The PT Test"
- **Source:** Secret #6 (Warning group)

### A12. "WARNING: Don't Even Think About [ACTION] Until You Read This"
- **When to use:** Pre-emptive interrupt right before a buying or attempt moment.
- **Pattern:** WARNING: Don't Even Think About Trying To [ACTION THEY'RE ABOUT TO TAKE] Until You Read This
- **Worked example:** "WARNING: Don't Even Think About Trying To Write Your Sales Letter Until You Read This"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A13. "Here's The Perfect Solution If You Want [RESULT] (even if [OBJECTION])"
- **When to use:** Solution-aware promise with a built-in eliminator.
- **Pattern:** Here's The Perfect Solution If You Want [RESULT] (even if [OBJECTION])
- **Worked example:** "Here's The Perfect Solution If You Want To Publish A Book (even if you've never written a word)"
- **Source:** Secret #6 (Other High-Performers)

### A14. My Proven "[NAMED METHOD]" Method To [RESULT]
- **When to use:** When you want to brand a unique mechanism (naming the method = proprietary feel + credibility).
- **Pattern:** My Proven "[NAMED MECHANISM]" Method To [RESULT]
- **Worked example:** "My Proven 'Pullup Push' Method To Double Your Pullups"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A15. Curiosity Fallback Headline (when stuck)
- **When to use:** Default when no other angle is landing; pure curiosity + cost-of-ignorance.
- **Pattern:** The #1 [TOPIC] Mistake [AVATAR] Make That [NEGATIVE CONSEQUENCE]
- **Worked example:** "The #1 Headline Mistake People Make That Bleeds Them Dry"
- **Source:** Secret #6

### A16. Headline-by-Traffic-Temperature (Schwartz segmentation rule)
- **When to use:** ALWAYS — never run one headline across all traffic sources. Clone the landing page 3x, change ONLY the headline/lead per source.
- **Pattern:** HOT (knows you) → start with the PRODUCT/you · WARM (wants a solution, doesn't know you) → start with the DESIRE/solution · COLD (knows the problem, not that a solution exists) → start with the PROBLEM.
- **Worked example:** HOT: "Stew Smith just released 'How To Pass Your Next PT Test In As Little As Two Weeks.'" / WARM: "We help people pass the FBI academy PT test." / COLD: "Failed your last PT test? You're not alone!"
- **Source:** Secret #7 (via Eugene Schwartz awareness levels)

### A17. The Hook (one-sentence story) — Unlikely Character + Timing + Result
- **When to use:** Headline, opening line, ad, social post, meme — anywhere you must create *intense curiosity*. A hook does NOT sell; it only pulls them into the copy. (A hook ≠ a USP: USP = what makes the PRODUCT different; hook = what makes the MESSAGE different.)
- **Pattern:** [UNLIKELY CHARACTER] [does/achieves RESULT] in [TIMING] using [WEIRD/UNEXPECTED MECHANISM]
- **Worked example:** "Former janitor goes from bankrupt to paid-off house in eighteen months with profits from his e-book business."
- **Source:** Secret #29 (Hooks)

### A18. The Hook — Result + Timing − Pain
- **When to use:** When the product's magic is removing the dreaded effort.
- **Pattern:** [RESULT] in [TIMING] without [THE PAINFUL THING THEY EXPECT TO HAVE TO DO]
- **Worked example:** "Create and publish your outrageously profitable book in just three hours without writing a single word."
- **Source:** Secret #29

### A19. Email Subject Line = Short Question
- **When to use:** Email teasers (40–60%+ open on mobile — keep it short). The subject is the email's headline; a question pulls the open.
- **Pattern:** [PROBLEM OR DESIRE AS A SHORT QUESTION]?
- **Worked example:** "Worried about failing your next PT test?" / "Failed your last PT test?" / "PT test coming up?"
- **Source:** Secret #18 (Email Teaser)

### A20. Ad Headline — "[# odd] [TOPIC] secrets every [AVATAR] needs"
- **When to use:** Cold/warm paid traffic; curiosity + exclusivity.
- **Pattern:** [# odd] [TOPIC] secrets every [AVATAR] needs. [CTA].
- **Worked example:** "Want the 3 financial planning secrets every successful entrepreneur needs? Free webinar... Register now."
- **Source:** Secret #28 (Online-Ad Formula)

### A21. Swipe-File Headline Hack (model a proven winner)
- **When to use:** Anytime — model a headline you KNOW converted from an unrelated industry and refit it to your avatar (Edwards swipes video-game magazine covers).
- **Pattern:** Take a proven headline → swap the topic/avatar nouns, keep the proven structure → "[ORIGINAL STRUCTURE] You're Not Supposed To Know"
- **Worked example:** Xbox cover "The Grand Theft Auto 4 Secrets You're Not Supposed To Know" → "The Ebook Marketing Secrets You're Not Supposed To Know" (built a six-figure business).
- **Source:** Secret #30 (Swipe File)

## B — Funnel & Page Recipes (page-by-page)

> Edwards: *don't trust absolutes — TEST.* His default is the VSL ("first option 99.99% of the time"). Each recipe below is the block order, what each block says, and its conversion job. The 13-Part Letter (B1) is the master block sequence; the page recipes (B2–B4) are how that sequence is staged on a page. **HONESTY NOTE:** the book teaches these page types richly. It does NOT give discrete page-by-page recipes for opt-in/squeeze, checkout, upsell/downsell, or thank-you pages — those are marked THIN/derived in the Coverage Map (Section H); assemble them from the 13-Part blocks + the funnel-congruence rule (F-series).

### B1. The 13-Part Sales Letter (master block sequence — long-form OR VSL)
- **When to use:** The structural backbone for any sales page, long-form letter, or VSL script. *"Stack them like Legos / pass the bucket / stepping stones; each part's job is to move them to the next part."* Each part can be a page, a sentence, or a few words. Build top-down.
- **Pattern (block order, every block written — none thin):**
  1. **Headline Package** = pre-headline + headline + sub-headline (+ "From the Desk of [NAME] / Re: [TOPIC]" in a letter; in a VSL = your first spoken sentences). Job: stop them.
  2. **Shocking Statement** — "Did you know [SURPRISING STAT]?" Job: pop them out of hypnosis.
  3. **Define the Problem** — "Here's the problem you face: [PROBLEM]." Job: name it in no uncertain terms.
  4. **Agitate** — "...which means you [PAINFUL CONSEQUENCE]." Job: make it hurt. *"They don't buy if it don't hurt."*
  5. **Present the Solution** — "Luckily for you, there's now a solution. Let me introduce you to [PRODUCT]." Job: relief.
  6. **Bullets to Arouse Curiosity** — 6/8/10 Ultimate Bullets (see E4). Job: build buying pressure.
  7. **Credibility Statement (about you)** — answers "Why you?" (can be one line). Job: earn the right.
  8. **Proof** — "But don't just take my word for it. Take a look at this." Job: answer "Why should I believe you?"
  9. **Sum Up the Offer + Price** — exactly what they get, how, when, how much. Optional "Regularly $[X], right now just $[Y]" (10–30% drop feels real).
  10. **Bonuses** — stack value to ~10X price; "sell dollars for dimes"; turn a competitor's USP into a free bonus. Job: tip the scale.
  11. **Guarantee** — remove all risk. Job: kill the fear of loss.
  12. **Call to Action** — "Click here to buy now!" + a reason to act now (recap for long letters).
  13. **P.S. / P.P.S.** — restate main benefit + reason to act now + buy now.
- **Worked example (headline package + agitate + P.S. line):** Headline package: "Attn: All Funnel Hackers / How To Get ALL Of Your Sales Letters, Scripts, And Webinar Slides Written (In Under 10 Minutes) WITHOUT Hiring An Expensive Copywriter! / (This Works Even If You HATE Writing...)" · Agitate: "...which means you'll never share your message with the world, and when you die, your message dies with you." · P.S.: "P.S. This gem will sell for $49. This introductory price of $29 is a 'buy it now before it's gone' offer, so act fast! P.P.S. Let's be blunt: if you pass on this offer, in a week will you have an e-book written or not?"
- **Source:** Secret #17 (The 13-Part Sales Letter)

### B2. VSL (Video Sales Letter) Page Recipe — the default
- **When to use:** Edwards' first option 99.99% of the time; fastest to test/launch; one-day build.
- **Pattern (top to bottom):** 1) Headline → 2) Video (the 13-part sequence spoken) → 3) BUY button *directly under the video*. [For items above ~$200, continue:] 4) 4–6 curiosity bullets → 5) Guarantee → 6) Summary of what they get → 7) Second BUY button → 8) Closing copy → 9) P.S. that restates the video's main benefits.
- **Worked example:** Headline: "How To Get ALL Of Your Sales Copy Written In Under 10 Minutes..." → [2–10 min sales video using the 13-part script] → green "Yes! Give Me Access Now" button → "Here's what you get the moment you join: [4–6 bullets]" → "30-day no-questions-asked guarantee" → second buy button → "P.S. Remember, everything I showed you in the video is waiting on the other side of this button."
- **Source:** Secret #16 (VSL)

### B3. Long-Form Sales Letter Page Recipe
- **When to use:** Higher price OR a more technical/info-heavy sale that needs full explanation.
- **Pattern:** Headline → sales video → buy button → **then the full long copy beneath** (the long copy may literally be the VSL transcript — some read, some watch, some do both; some read it at work with the sound off). Lay the long copy out as the full 13-Part sequence (B1).
- **Worked example:** Top of page = the VSL recipe (B2). Below the first buy button: the entire transcript rendered as text with sub-headed sections (Problem → Agitate → Solution → Bullets → Proof → Offer → Bonuses → Guarantee → CTA → P.S.), each as a scannable block.
- **Source:** Secret #16 (which flavor to use)

### B4. Short-Form Page Recipe
- **When to use:** Cheap items / physical products that need little explanation. Long copy here = *"overselling"* and makes people suspicious.
- **Pattern:** Headline → tight benefit blurb (1–3 short paragraphs, Feature+Benefit+Meaning, see E4) → a few bullets → price → single BUY button. Skip the heavy proof/agitation stack.
- **Worked example:** "Heavy-Duty Magnetic Drill Plate" → "Keeps every screw stuck to the bit so nothing drops on someone's head while you're ten feet up a ladder." → 3 bullets → "$19.99" → "Add To Cart."
- **Source:** Secret #16

### B5. Online Ad Page-Entry Recipe (3-step ad → the click)
- **When to use:** The ad itself (image, video, or text) whose ONLY job is to get the right people to click into the funnel. *"The only purpose of an online ad is to get the right people to click and the wrong people to keep on scrolling."*
- **Pattern:** 1) **Emotional Attention Grabber** (image in image-ads; first 3 seconds in video — Facebook scores on 3-second views; headline in text ads) → 2) **Curiosity Driver** ("What is it? How do they do that?") → 3) **Clear Call To Action** ("Click here to [ACTION]."). Often open with a question to elicit a silent YES.
- **Worked example:** "Want the 3 financial planning secrets every successful entrepreneur needs? Free webinar helps you achieve total financial peace of mind and get high returns on your money without knowing all the confusing financial jargon. Register now."
- **Source:** Secret #28 (replaces AIDA for online)

## C — Script Bank (sales / objection / follow-up / discovery / close)

### C1. The Stealth Close / "Columbo Close"
- **When to use:** Turn ANY content (article, video, blog, tweet, Kindle book, FB Live) into a salesperson without tripping sales-radar. Do NOT use on an actual sales letter/VSL (there they already know it's a sale — use a direct CTA).
- **Pattern:** Step 1 deliver real VALUE (tip/story/answer). Step 2 pivot with "By the way..." (or "Oh, before I forget," / "One more thing," / "Hey, did you know?") → a benefit → a link/CTA. Format the close EXACTLY like the surrounding content.
- **Worked example:** "By the way, did you know that [X] will help you [BENEFIT]? Yep, it's true. Go here for a quick demo. [LINK]"
- **Source:** Secret #25 (Stealth Close)

### C2. The P.S. Pivot (stealth close on email/article)
- **When to use:** Soft-sell at the end of a value email or post.
- **Pattern:** P.S. We have [SCARCITY] for [OFFER]. If you want to [DISCOVER BENEFIT/RESULT], [CTA] right now. [LINK]
- **Worked example:** "P.S. We have a few seats left for our live Website Video Secrets Workshop in Atlanta. If you want to discover the secrets to making simple little videos that drive traffic, make people click, and generate fat commissions, get more information here right now."
- **Source:** Secret #25

### C3. Email Teaser Script (6-part — the ONLY job is the CLICK)
- **When to use:** 99/100 emails. NEVER sell the offer in the email — the page sells; the email earns the click.
- **Pattern:** 1) Subject (question/short) → 2) Salutation ("Hey [NAME],") → 3) Shocking statement/question ("Did you know [STAT]?") → 4) 2–4 curiosity bullets/sentences → 5) Specific CTA (the link) → 6) Personal close ("I'll see you over there. Thanks, [NAME]").
- **Worked example:** "Subject: Want to be a published author? / Hey Craig, Did you know that 99 out of 100 people who try to write a book fail? Yep. It sucks. The main reasons why: They don't know how to create content that sells. They don't know how to get it formatted. They don't know how to publish it. The cool news is I have a great new video that shows you exactly how to solve all those problems — fast and easy! Click here right now to see it. [LINK] I'll see you over there. Thanks, Jim"
- **Source:** Secret #18

### C4. The "Write to George" Script (beat the blinking cursor)
- **When to use:** When a draft is bloated/stuck. Throw it away and type the message to ONE real friend.
- **Pattern:** "Hey [FRIEND'S NAME], I just found this [COOL THING]... [one honest sentence of why it's cool]... here's the link. [LINK]"
- **Worked example:** "Hey George, I just found this cool piece of software that writes your sales letters for you in about ten minutes. I couldn't believe it. Here's the link. [LINK]"
- **Source:** Secret #13 (emails written to a specific person outperform everything else 99% of the time)

### C5. Shortest-Winner Email (in-house list only)
- **When to use:** Warm/hot list that already trusts you; pure curiosity.
- **Pattern:** Subject: [INTRIGUE] / Salutation / "This was totally unexpected. [SOMEONE] just did [THING] and it's amazing! You've got to check this out. [LINK]" / personal close.
- **Worked example:** "Subject: This blew me away / Hey Craig, This was totally unexpected. Somebody just did a review and it's amazing! You've got to check this out. [LINK] See you over there, Jim"
- **Source:** Secret #18

### C6. Takeaway Close / FOMO (warm & hot traffic)
- **When to use:** To raise perceived exclusivity and trigger fear of missing out near the CTA.
- **Pattern:** "Now, this isn't for everybody. This is only for people who are [QUALIFIER]." / "We're putting a limit of [#] [people/units] on this."
- **Worked example:** "Now, this isn't for everybody. This is only for people who are serious about finally passing their PT test. We're putting a limit of 50 people on this."
- **Source:** Secret on warm/hot traffic ("Everything Else" Q&A)

### C7. Ask-a-Question to Dodge Ad-Platform Censors
- **When to use:** When a direct claim would get an ad rejected; make the claim a question.
- **Pattern:** "Could you [BIG CLAIM AS QUESTION]? Believe it or not, it's true."
- **Worked example:** "Could you write a book in three hours? Believe it or not, it's true."
- **Source:** "Everything Else" Q&A

### C8. Compare-Without-Naming (go negative the RIGHT way)
- **When to use:** To handle the "your competitor says X" objection without attack-ad name-calling.
- **Pattern:** "Now, some of our competitors will tell you [COMPETITOR CLAIM], but that's just not true. Here are the facts you need to know." / "You may have noticed some people charge extra for [THING], but we don't think that's right. Here's why."
- **Worked example:** "Now, some of our competitors will tell you that you need a $5,000 mailing list before you can sell a single book. That's just not true. Here are the facts."
- **Source:** Secret #24 (Should I Join the Dark Side?)

### C9. Going-Negative Entry (enter the conversation in their head)
- **When to use:** Opening line/ad to get in sync by naming their problem/enemy. *"Nobody cares how much you know until they know how much you care."*
- **Pattern:** "Are you having trouble [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] for your [AVATAR'S BUSINESS/LIFE]?" / "Has [ENEMY/FORCE] crushed your [DESIRED OUTCOME]?"
- **Worked example:** "Are you having trouble attracting good clients for your dog-training business?" / "Has malpractice insurance crushed your profitability?"
- **Source:** Secret #24

### C10. Proof Segue Script
- **When to use:** The pivot from your claim to your evidence (block 8 of the 13-part letter).
- **Pattern:** "But don't just take my word for it. Take a look at this. [TESTIMONIAL / DATA / SCREENSHOT / BEFORE-AFTER]"
- **Worked example:** "But don't just take my word for it. Take a look at what happened to Stew after he used this exact method: [results testimonial]."
- **Source:** Secret #17, Secret #14

### C11. Present-the-Solution Script
- **When to use:** The relief beat right after agitation (block 5).
- **Pattern:** "Luckily for you, there's now a solution. Let me introduce you to [PRODUCT NAME]."
- **Worked example:** "Luckily, there's now a solution — The Complete A-to-Z Puppy-Dog Training Guide."
- **Source:** Secret #15, #17

### C12. Guarantee / Risk-Reversal Script
- **When to use:** Block 11 of any sales asset; remove the fear of loss.
- **Pattern:** "Not only do we guarantee this for [TIME], but if it doesn't [DELIVER THE CORE PROMISE], we don't want your money. We'll give it all back. No questions asked. No hard feelings."
- **Worked example:** "Not only do we guarantee this for 30 days, but if it doesn't show you exactly how to pass your next PT test, we don't want your money. We'll give it all back. No questions asked. No hard feelings."
- **Source:** Secret #17

### C13. Magic Desk Discovery Script (internal customer-empathy interview)
- **When to use:** AFTER full avatar research, to "become the customer" and mine copy in their own words. Run with a voice recorder; pre-write your questions and your intention.
- **Pattern (ask AS the prospect, in their voice):** "What terrifies you?" · "If you weren't worried about anyone judging you, how would you describe that fear in words anyone could understand?" · "What is your deepest desire for your life right now?" · "How could I make you want what I'm selling more?" · "What objections do you have, and what could I show/tell you to overcome them?" · "What would stop you from getting the results I'm promising?" · "What's a fair price for what I have to sell?" · "What else would make my offer more appealing?"
- **Worked example:** Intention: "My intention is to get in touch with my prospect's major problems and fears about writing their book." → output answers become headlines, bullets, stories, phone scripts.
- **Source:** Secret #27 (The Magic Desk)

## D — Email & Sequence Bank

> **HONESTY NOTE:** Edwards teaches the *single email teaser* deeply (full 6-part skeleton + multiple complete worked emails, see C3/C5) but does NOT give a named, dated multi-step drip sequence (e.g. a 5-email launch). Per the no-fabrication rule, the multi-step sequence below is **assembled** from the book's own components — the 6-part teaser (Secret #18), the stealth close (Secret #25), the takeaway close, and the funnel-congruence rule (*reuse the same copy/words across ads → emails → page → OTO → downsell*, "Everything Else" Q&A). It is faithful to the method; it is not a verbatim sequence from the book. This is marked THIN in the Coverage Map.

### D1. The Email Teaser (the atomic unit — see C3 for the full skeleton)
- **When to use:** Every promotional email. The job is the click, not the sale.
- **Pattern:** Subject (question) → "Hey [NAME]," → "Did you know [STAT]?" → 2–4 curiosity bullets → CTA link → personal close. Keep it short (40–60%+ read on mobile): *"Friends don't send friends ten-page sales letters in email."*
- **Worked example:** See C3 ("Want to be a published author?") — reproduced in full there.
- **Source:** Secret #18

### D2. Assembled Congruent Launch Sequence (built from book components — flagged THIN)
- **When to use:** A short promo push for one offer, reusing one congruent message across touches.
- **Pattern (each email = one application of the 6-part teaser; subjects escalate; body stays congruent with the landing page):**
  - **Email 1 — Problem/Curiosity (Day 0).** Role: open the loop. Subject pattern: "[Problem as question]?" Body skeleton: salutation → "Did you know [shocking stat]?" → 2 bullets of the cost of the problem → CTA to the value page/VSL → personal close.
    - *Worked example subject:* "Failed your last PT test?" *Body open:* "Hey Craig, Did you know most Marines who fail once fail again within 90 days? Here's the fix → [LINK]"
  - **Email 2 — Solution/Mechanism (Day 1).** Role: introduce the named mechanism. Subject: "The '[Named Method]' that [result]." Body: "Luckily, there's now a solution..." → 3 curiosity bullets → CTA. *(Present-the-Solution script, C11.)*
    - *Worked example subject:* "The 'Pullup Push' method that doubles your score in 2 weeks"
  - **Email 3 — Proof (Day 2).** Role: kill disbelief. Subject: "This blew me away." Body: shortest-winner format (C5) → links to a results testimonial/case study.
    - *Worked example subject:* "This blew me away (real PT test results inside)"
  - **Email 4 — Stealth/Value + Soft CTA (Day 3).** Role: give a real tip, then "By the way..." pivot (C1) to the offer. Subject: a how-to. 
    - *Worked example subject:* "3 fast ways to add 5 pullups this week"
  - **Email 5 — Takeaway/FOMO Close (Day 4).** Role: scarcity + final CTA. Subject: "Closing the doors on [offer]." Body: takeaway close (C6) → recap of what they get → guarantee → CTA.
    - *Worked example subject:* "Last call — only 50 spots, then it's gone"
- **Worked example (one full email — Email 1):** "Subject: Failed your last PT test? / Hey Craig, Did you know most people who fail their PT test once fail it AGAIN within 90 days? It's not because they're lazy — it's because nobody showed them the right way to train. I just put up a short video that fixes the #1 mistake almost everyone makes. Click here to watch it right now. [LINK] I'll see you over there. Thanks, Jim"
- **Source:** Assembled from Secrets #18, #25, #15/#17, and the funnel-congruence rule ("Everything Else" Q&A). Flagged THIN — not a verbatim book sequence.

## E — Frameworks, Models & Templates (with the steps)

### E1. The 10 Reasons People Buy + the 11 Product-Interrogation Questions
- **When to use:** Before writing anything — tie MULTIPLE reasons to your offer ("like tying down a tarp in a windstorm" — one corner blows around; 4–5 corners lock it down).
- **Pattern (the 10 reasons):** 1) Make money 2) Save money 3) Save time 4) Avoid effort 5) Escape mental/physical pain 6) Get more comfort 7) Cleanliness/hygiene → health 8) Gain praise 9) Feel more loved 10) Increase popularity/social status. (Memorize the first five as the load-bearing "pegs.") Then force 10 answers each to the 11 interrogation questions (5 ways it makes money? saves money this week/month/year? saves time + what they do with it? what they don't have to do anymore? physical pain eliminated + what that means? mental pain/worry removed? 3 ways more comfortable? cleanliness easier? more healthy/alive? 3 ways envy of friends / more loved? more popular / higher status?).
- **Worked example (protein shake):** Make money = "Drink this in the morning, get fantastic energy, do better at your job, probably get a raise." Save time = "Nutritious breakfast in 30 seconds — more time with your kids before rushing out the door." Escape pain = "Hate feeling bloated when you eat too much breakfast? Or starving because you skipped it? This solves that."
- **Source:** Secret #3

### E2. F.R.E.D. (the avatar) + PQR2 (the brain code)
- **When to use:** Step one of every project. Don't write to a niche — name one person; "go a few feet wide and a mile deep."
- **Pattern:** FRED = Fears, Results, Expectations, Desires. Drill NICHE → SUB-NICHE → MICRO-NICHE; name + face the avatar (Google-image the name, print the photo, write to that face). PQR2 (3:1 stick-to-carrot) = Problems / Questions / Roadblocks (the cliff he's stuck on) + Results (the cliff across the chasm). Your SALES COPY (not the product) is the bridge. Mine PQR2 from 8 sources: live events, yourself, forums, your help desk/support tickets, popular products (TOC/reviews), answer sites (Quora), surveys, social/hashtags. Write Fred's top 2 (overachievers 5–10) of each — *"the fifth one is probably the magic connection."* Rule: "Nobody buys what they need. People buy what they WANT" — sell the want, include the need.
- **Worked example:** Niche real estate → sub-niche real-estate investor → micro-niche house flipper = "Fred the Flipper." PQR2 → instant assets: headline "At Last! The Real Secret To Finding Great Flips Fast Is Revealed." / subject "Find great deals in half the time." / bullet "Makes it easy to find the deals others missed."
- **Source:** Secret #8

### E3. Emotion via Meaning — Feature / Benefit / Meaning + "why-a-rhea"
- **When to use:** Whenever copy is flat or feature-heavy. *"People buy on emotion and justify on logic."*
- **Pattern:** Feature = what it IS; Benefit = what it DOES; MEANING = what it MEANS to the buyer. Find meaning by interrogating every claim: "Why is that important? Why does that matter? Why is that a big deal?" ("why-a-rhea"), then bolt on "...which means you ___." Tie to core emotions: Love, Hate, Fear of [failure/mistakes/loss], Vanity, Pride, Longing, Greed, Freedom. *"It's not what you think — it's what they FEEL."*
- **Worked example (drill magnetic plate):** "...so there's no chance of sharp screws dropping and hitting somebody on the head while you're ten feet up a ladder — maybe even your kid."
- **Source:** Secret #10

### E4. The Ultimate Bullet Formula
- **When to use:** Building any bullet block — *"the workhorse of any copy."* Write 10–12, keep the best 5–6 ("your fifth bullet is better than your first"). 5 amazing bullets beat 30 good ones.
- **Pattern:** Basic = "It [FEATURE] so you can [BENEFIT]." Ultimate = "It [FEATURE] so you can [BENEFIT] which means [MEANING]." For info products use a page-number/time-stamp bullet: "[CURIOSITY PROMISE]... (Page [#])" or "(at [TIMESTAMP])."
- **Worked example:** "One-piece set so you can keep all your wrenches together in one place which means you'll never be left high and dry with the wrong wrench." · Page-number bullet: "How to quickly avoid the #1 Mistake authors make that causes them to take months or years to write a book... so you can finish in just a few days. (Page 7)" (this style of letter did $1.5M).
- **Source:** Secret #9

### E5. The 3 Sales Formulas That Never Fail
- **When to use:** Any short-form asset (20-page letter, 60-sec VSL, or 10-min talk).
- **Pattern:** **#1 Problem / Agitate / Solve** (best for COLD) = state problem → agitate (make it hurt — "the secret sauce") → introduce product. **#2 "If you want [3X BENEFIT], then do this"** (best for HOT/WARM) = benefit + benefit + benefit → action. **#3 Before / After / Bridge** (all temps; NLP future-pacing) = Before (the problem now) → After ("imagine your life once the negative is gone") → Bridge (your product = the path). You can combine: Before = Problem-Agitate; After = future-pace; Bridge = Solve.
- **Worked example (Before/After/Bridge, marriage):** Before: "You and your spouse pass in the hallway, occasionally make love, but things aren't what they used to be." After: "Imagine feeling the same way you felt when you first met — you can't wait to see them every night." Bridge: "That doesn't have to be a fantasy. Here's how to get there."
- **Source:** Secret #15

### E6. The Online-Ad Formula (3-step replacement for AIDA)
- **When to use:** Every paid ad. See B5 for the page-entry recipe; this is the framework + the question openers + the numbers game.
- **Pattern:** Emotional Attention Grabber → Curiosity Driver → Clear CTA. Open with a YES-question: "Are you tired of [PAIN]?" / "Would you like to [DESIRE]?" / "Have you ever wanted to [DESIRE]?" Numbers game: expect 10–50 tests to find one winner ("Gold Rush" — run tons of dirt to find gold); "Fail fast with the losers; go long with the winners"; always have ads coming/running/going (bucket brigade); never get emotionally attached.
- **Worked example:** "Are you tired of struggling to get traffic to your website? [curiosity] ... Click here now."
- **Source:** Secret #28

### E7. The Profitable Product Promise (4-part: Hurdle / Prize / Timing / Eliminator)
- **When to use:** Assembling headlines, titles, and offer promises — the "Oh Damn—I Got To Have That!" formula. Order is flexible.
- **Pattern:** 1) **Hurdle** = the action they must take (use their exact ACTION verbs — "drive" vs "hit" vs "strike" a golf ball; verbs create mental images). 2) **Prize** = the result they want (their exact RESULT words). 3) **Timing** = answer "When will I get it?" (time for THEM to do it, or time for YOU to teach it). 4) **Eliminator** = let them off the hook ("even if ___" / "without ___" — NEVER tell them it's their fault). Superchargers: money promise, forgive past failure, cost modifier ("for less than the cost of a cup of coffee at Starbucks").
- **Worked example:** "How to write and publish your own outrageously profitable e-book in as little as seven days [hurdle+prize+timing]... even if you can't write, can't type, and failed high school English class [eliminator]." · "How you can use eBay Real Estate to cash in on your first money-making deal within 72 hours, even if you have no money of your own to invest."
- **Source:** Secret #22

### E8. The Hook Formula (one-sentence curiosity story)
- **When to use:** See A17/A18 for capture; framework note: a hook creates intense curiosity, does NOT sell, and is built from hidden angles you take for granted (unlikely character + timing + result, or result + timing − pain).
- **Pattern:** [UNLIKELY CHARACTER] + [TIMING] + [RESULT] + [WEIRD MECHANISM] — used as headline, opening line, first paragraph, story, ad, social post, meme, or infographic.
- **Worked example:** "One-legged golfer outdrives Tiger Woods." (USP behind it: "Our 3-minute video fixes 90% of long-drive problems.")
- **Source:** Secret #29

### E9. The 3 M's of Content + 5 Story Types + 4 Ways to Sell via Content
- **When to use:** Content-based selling (Stealth Close, social, lead magnets). *"Stories make people thirsty; sales copy tells them where to buy a drink."*
- **Pattern:** 5 story types: 1) actual stories, 2) case studies (beginning-middle-end = hero's journey), 3) examples (how it was applied + result), 4) the **3 M's** — MYTH (debunk an untruth), MISCONCEPTION (correct a false belief), MISTAKE (where others go wrong — most powerful; "no adult wants to look stupid"), 5) future-pacing stories. 4 ways to sell via content: (a) give a valuable FREE thing that creates the need for the paid thing ("free boat → sell the trailer + motor"); (b) tell WHAT + WHY, sell HOW/the tool; (c) teach the steps leading up to the purchase; (d) teach the MANUAL hard way, then sell the "easy button."
- **Worked example (future-pacing):** "Imagine having your own book with your name on it as the author... handing it out at a trade show while everyone else hands out business cards..."
- **Source:** Secret #20

### E10. The Proof Ladder (proof without testimonials)
- **When to use:** Any high-stakes claim; satisfies the belief checklist (Does it work? Has it worked for others? Will it work for me?). NEVER manufacture proof; FTC-safe and substantiable ("could you prove it in a court of law?").
- **Pattern (best → fallback):** 1) Results-oriented testimonial (disclaimers in health/finance) → 2) Testimonial about you/your company → 3) Celebrity/authority endorsement → 4) Statistics (Google "[topic] statistics/study") → 5) Quotes that support the buying decision → 6) Pictures/graphics (never flip before/after). Fast way to get testimonials: give the product away free and ask.
- **Worked example:** Statistic proof: "9 of 10 FSBOs fail and list with an agent within 30 days." Endorsement: Jim landed Jay Conrad Levinson — "you only need one or two yeses to change your life."
- **Source:** Secret #14

### E11. "Put Lipstick on the Pig" — Conversion Diagnostic Checklist
- **When to use:** Copy isn't working, or to critique a hired copywriter's draft.
- **Pattern (run each question):** Headline — do you HAVE one, is it about THEM, big bold benefit attracting the RIGHT people? · Offer clarity — do they know what/how/when/how much (hidden price = feels like hiding)? · Reason to buy NOW — bonuses/deadline/limited qty (or future-pace the fear of loss)? · Emotional driver up front (9 of 10 buy from DISSATISFACTION)? · Do your bullets suck (features vs benefit+meaning)? · Price too high vs comparable — or too LOW (= "too cheap, not worth it")? · Right graphics/colors (conservative > "psychedelic acid trip")? · Proof present for every claim (FTC-safe)?
- **Worked example:** A flat page: headline names the company → rewrite to an emotional benefit; price hidden → state it; bullets are bare specs → apply E4; no proof → insert a stat from E10.
- **Source:** Secret #23

### E12. A/B Split-Testing Discipline ("Good Enough Makes You Poor")
- **When to use:** Always-on optimization. *"The enemy of great is good."* Champion = "The Control"; a winning challenger becomes the new control.
- **Pattern:** Test ONE variable at a time. Test for ACTIONS (sales/clicks/subs), not opinions. Automate it. Test headlines, offers, prices, bonuses, subject lines, CTAs, button text, colors.
- **Worked example (the math):** $100 product, 1% conversion, $90 traffic cost/100 visitors = $10 profit. Lift conversion to 1.7% via small tests → $170 revenue, same $90 cost = $70 profit = **+700%**. "A measly .2% headline lift can TRIPLE profitability."
- **Source:** Secret #11

### E13. Polish / QC Gate ("Slippery Slide" Test)
- **When to use:** Before any asset ships. *"Finish your first draft and then we'll talk."*
- **Pattern:** Grammar/spelling/punctuation/format check → render across browsers (Chrome/Firefox) AND devices (PC/Mac/iPhone/iPad/Android/Linux) → check the SECONDARY READING PATH (skimmers read only headline + sub-heads + bold + pictures + price + P.S. — can they get the gist?) → watch the VSL with SOUND OFF (FB autoplays muted) + add subtitles (Rev.com ~$1/min) → second pair of eyes → Slippery Slide test (each section flows into the next with no friction).
- **Worked example:** "if it looks bad it's OUR fault" — a typo-ridden page makes the reader judge the product by the typos; fix, then run the skim path: headline + subheads + bold + P.S. alone must sell the gist.
- **Source:** Secret #31

## F — Brand Voice & Brand-Building Language Patterns

> This section is the voice machinery: ready-to-use lists with example lines so copy reads ON-brand and rich, not concise.

### F1. Verbs in headlines (active > passive)
- **Pattern:** Lead with action VERBS; avoid is/am/was/were/be — verbs make mental pictures.
- **Example:** "Double your pushups," "Find the deals others missed," "Outdrive Tiger Woods."

### F2. The "...which means you ___" meaning bolt-on
- **Pattern:** [BENEFIT] which means you [EMOTIONAL PAYOFF].
- **Example:** "...so you can work them hard which means you'll spend a LOT less money on tools over time."

### F3. You/your dominance (the I/me/my purge)
- **Pattern:** Hunt every "I, me, my, we, ours" and flip to "you, your, yours." Make the prospect the HERO.
- **Example:** "I want to tell you something here." → "Here's something YOU need to know."

### F4. Sentence = paragraph (web rhythm)
- **Pattern:** On the web, "pretty much a sentence is a paragraph"; max 2–4 lines. Short, punchy declaratives.
- **Example:** "Yep. It sucks. Here's the fix."

### F5. Copy Connectors (keep the "one big conversation" flowing)
- **Pattern:** Stitch sections with bridge phrases.
- **Example:** "What do I mean? Let me explain." · "For example..." · "But don't just take my word for it, take a look at this." · "But I'm not the only one..." · "Now, at this point you might wonder how much this will cost?" · "But before you make a decision, let me sweeten the pot even more." · "Now you're 100% covered with our money-back guarantee." · "Oh, one more thing..." · "Now it's time to choose."

### F6. "Did you know?" shocking opener
- **Pattern:** "Did you know [SURPRISING/SCARY STAT]?"
- **Example:** "Did you know most people who try to write a sales letter fail miserably? Some go bankrupt and lose their houses!"

### F7. Future-pacing language ("Imagine...")
- **Pattern:** "Imagine [the happy after-state, present tense, sensory]."
- **Example:** "Imagine feeling the same way you felt when you first met — you can't wait to see them every night."

### F8. Eliminator phrases ("even if" / "without")
- **Pattern:** [PROMISE]... even if [DISQUALIFIER] / without [PAINFUL THING].
- **Example:** "...even if you can't write, can't type, and failed high school English class."

### F9. Cost-modifier reframe
- **Pattern:** "for less than the cost of [TRIVIAL EVERYDAY PURCHASE]."
- **Example:** "for less than the cost of a cup of coffee at Starbucks" / "...a medium everything pizza every month."

### F10. Naming patterns (mechanism, method, avatar)
- **Pattern:** Brand the mechanism: My Proven "[NAME]" Method; name the avatar ("Fred the Flipper"); name the bonus/program.
- **Example:** "the 'Pullup Push' method," "Fred the Flipper," "The 3-Hour Kindle Book Wizard."

### F11. Proof/credibility language
- **Pattern:** "But don't just take my word for it..." / "[CREDENTIAL] invented by a [AUTHORITY]."
- **Example:** "Invented by a Navy SEAL" (sold $100M of Perfect Pushup gear).

### F12. Guarantee language (kill the risk)
- **Pattern:** "We don't want your money [unless it works]. ...No questions asked. No hard feelings."
- **Example:** "We'll give it all back. No questions asked. No hard feelings."

### F13. CTA language (explicit, low-friction, benefit-laden)
- **Pattern:** "[ACTION VERB] here right now to [BENEFIT]."
- **Example:** "Click here right now to see it." / "Register now." / "Get more information here right now."

### F14. Urgency / FOMO language
- **Pattern:** "[SCARCITY/DEADLINE] — buy it now before it's gone."
- **Example:** "This introductory price of $29 is a 'buy it now before it's gone' offer, so act fast!"

### F15. Bonus / value-stack language
- **Pattern:** "sell dollars for dimes" — stack value toward 10X price; turn a competitor's USP into a free bonus.
- **Example:** "Plus, you also get [COMPETITOR'S PAID FEATURE] absolutely free when you join today."

### F16. Take-a-stand / polarizing voice
- **Pattern:** Attack BEHAVIOR / PRACTICES / METHODS — never people by name. Be consistent; change publicly when the world changes ("Hey, I've changed my opinion about this — here's why").
- **Example:** "Love me; hate me. There's no money in the middle."

### F17. WIIFM you-voice (What's In It For Me)
- **Pattern:** Open every section from the prospect's self-interest.
- **Example:** "Here's exactly what this does for YOU..."

### F18. P.S. / P.P.S. pattern
- **Pattern:** P.S. = restate main benefit + reason to act now + buy. P.P.S. = blunt either/or consequence.
- **Example:** "P.P.S. Let's be blunt: if you pass on this offer, in a week will you have an e-book written or not?"

### F19. Words/phrases this persona NEVER uses
- **Pattern:** Avoid jargon, big words, acronyms, "award-winning," brand-first boasts, "good enough," and one-size-fits-all framing. *"A confused mind always answers No."* Test clarity on a non-expert.
- **Example:** Replace "leverage our synergistic platform" with "this does the work for you in ten minutes."

### F20. "Sexify" a boring/commodity offer
- **Pattern:** Find the EMOTION / story / pain-of-absence; future-pace the moment they wish they had it.
- **Example (credit-card paper rolls):** "the time you DON'T want to think about it is when ten people are in line and the purple stripe shows up and you have no replacement roll."
