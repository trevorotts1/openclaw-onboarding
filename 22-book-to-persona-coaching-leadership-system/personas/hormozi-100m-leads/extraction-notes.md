### 2. Core Thesis and Problem Statement

The book's central argument: you already have a great offer (covered in $100M Offers) but strangers can't buy it if they don't know you exist. The gap between a good offer and a growing business is lead generation — specifically, engaged leads: people who have shown interest in what you sell. Engaged leads are not just names on a list; they are people who have opted in, followed you, replied to an email, or otherwise signaled interest.

The book solves: "Who do I sell it to?" by showing exactly how to let the right strangers know about your stuff through four advertising methods (Core Four) and four categories of people who get leads for you (Lead Getters).

### 3. Core Framework: The Advertising Equation

**Two types of audiences:**
- Warm: people who gave you permission to contact them (friends, followers, past customers, contacts)
- Cold: strangers who have not given permission (bought lists, platform-purchased access, cold databases)

**Two ways to communicate:**
- One-to-one (private): calls, texts, emails, DMs
- One-to-many (public): social media posts, ads, podcasts, billboards

**The Core Four (2x2 matrix):**
1. Warm Outreach = 1-to-1 to warm audience
2. Post Content = 1-to-many to warm audience
3. Cold Outreach = 1-to-1 to cold audience
4. Run Paid Ads = 1-to-many to cold audience

These are the ONLY four ways any person can let another person know about anything they sell.

---

### 4. Lead Magnets

A lead magnet is a complete solution to a narrow problem. It's free and valuable enough to get consumed. It creates engaged leads by advertising the lead magnet rather than the core offer directly, dramatically increasing engagement rates.

**Three types of lead magnets:**
1. Reveal the Problem — diagnosis-style ("you have X and here's why")
2. Provide a Solution — give partial or complete value for a narrow problem
3. Give a Sample — a taste of your core product/service

**Four delivery formats:** software/tools, information/resources, services, physical products

**Lead magnet math:** A $25 lead magnet that generates 3x the engaged leads at the same ad spend, converting 10% to buyers = 3x the business for the same budget

### 5. Core Four Deep Dive

**Warm Outreach (10-Step Process):**
Step 1: Build your list (phone, email, social across all platforms)
Step 2: Pick your highest-contact platform
Step 3: Personalize your opening (use something real about them)
Step 4: Reach out to 100 people per day
Step 5: Use ACA framework (Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask)
Step 6: Make an offer referencing the value equation
Step 7: Make it easy — start with free
Step 8: Cycle back to top of list when exhausted
Step 9: Start charging once referrals begin
Step 10: Keep list warm (Dean Jackson "9-word email" method)

Benchmark: 100 warm outreaches → ~20 replies (20%) → ~4 take free offer (20%) → ~1 paying customer

**Post Content (Framework):**
- Create "goodwill" content that solves real problems for free
- The more you give, the more engaged your audience becomes
- Content "stacks" — people who found you via content are warmer than cold leads
- Consistency > quality initially; post daily; improve through iteration
- Test hooks and formats weekly; keep winners; kill losers

**Cold Outreach:**
- Lower response rates than warm — expect 1% vs 20%
- Use automation to increase volume
- Personalize openers even in cold contact
- The same value-equation framework applies
- Great for B2B, reaching specific job titles, targeted industries

**Paid Ads (4-Part Framework):**
1. Make an ad (hook → body → call to action)
2. Understand money mechanics (CAC vs LTGP ratio; target 3:1 minimum)
3. Test and iterate (one change per week per platform)
4. Client Financed Acquisition — use revenue from customers to fund ad spend

---

### 6. More Better New — Scaling Framework

Once a core four method is working:
- **More:** do 10x the volume first (Rule of 100: 100 actions/day for 100 days)
- **Better:** test one thing per week per platform; fix biggest drop-off point (constraint)
- **New:** new ad placements → new platforms → new core four activity

The "Size of the Pie Fallacy": most businesses think their tiny slice of attention IS the whole market. Wrong. There are infinite audiences across platforms, formats, and channels.

### 7. Lead Getters (Section IV)

Four types of people who get leads for you:

**Customer Referrals:**
- Best quality, lowest CAC leads
- Word of mouth grows exponentially (not linearly like paid ads)
- Referral growth equation: referrals in > churn out = compounding growth
- Referral flywheel: get results → ask for referrals → get leads → get results
- Peak product experience = primary driver of organic referrals

**Employees:**
- Sales reps, customer success, outbound teams doing the core four on your behalf
- Hiring content creators, SDRs, and account managers
- Training employees on the same systems you use
- Leverage: one manager + 10 reps = 10x the lead getting

**Agencies:**
- Outsource specific core four activities to specialized agencies
- Paid ads agencies, SEO agencies, PR firms, content agencies
- Evaluate on CAC and LTGP ratios, not vanity metrics
- Fire fast if CAC exceeds acceptable ratio

**Affiliates and Partners:**
- Other businesses with aligned audiences advertise your stuff to their audiences
- Joint ventures, revenue share, co-marketing
- Dream 100: identify the top 100 players in your niche who have your audience
- Contact them with warm outreach; give enormous value first; then propose the deal
- Affiliate math: lower margin per deal but $0 CAC

### 8. The Advertising Roadmap (Open to Goal)

Hormozi's practical decision framework for what to do next:
1. Start with warm outreach (easiest, cheapest, works immediately)
2. If more time than money → post content
3. If more money than time → cold outreach or paid ads
4. Once proven → add lead getters in order: customers → employees → agencies → affiliates

The "Open to Goal" framework: always advertise with a clear end goal (number of leads, CAC target, LTGP:CAC ratio). Never run advertising without a measurable outcome.

---

### 9. Key Formulas and Metrics

- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** total ad spend ÷ number of new customers
- **LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit):** total gross profit from a customer over their lifetime
- **Target LTGP:CAC ratio:** 3:1 minimum; 5:1+ healthy; 10:1+ scaling fuel
- **Client Financed Acquisition (CFA):** when first transaction pays for ad spend; near-infinite scaling
- **Referral growth equation:** referrals in − churn out > 0 = compounding growth
- **Warm outreach benchmark:** 100 outreaches → 20 engagements → 4 free takers → 1 paying customer
- **Rule of 100:** 100 primary advertising actions per day for 100 days minimum
