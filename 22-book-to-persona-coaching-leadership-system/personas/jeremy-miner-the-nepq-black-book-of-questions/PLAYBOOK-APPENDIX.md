# PLAYBOOK APPENDIX — The NEPQ Black Book of Questions (Jeremy Miner)

> **What this file is.** The full-fidelity, swipe-able question library that backs the persona blueprint. The blueprint distills the NEPQ method into governance + coaching rules; this appendix preserves the *actual reusable questions, stage recipes, scripts, and tonality system* so a sales-script writer (Skill 35 / sales-enablement), a discovery-call specialist, an SDR/BDR, an objection-handling specialist, and a closer can assemble rich, on-flow NEPQ scripts without re-deriving the method.
>
> **Distinctness note.** A sibling persona, `acuff-miner-new-model-of-selling`, carries the high-level NEPQ *philosophy*. **This appendix is the granular question bank** — the exact questions to ask at each stage. When you need the *why*, read that persona; when you need the *exact words*, use this file.
>
> **Fidelity / no-fabrication note.** NEPQ's stage architecture (Connection → Situation → Problem Awareness → Solution Awareness → Consequence → Qualifying → Transition → Presentation → Commitment), the three tonalities, neutral/detachment, and "let the prospect persuade themselves" are Jeremy Miner's established, publicly taught framework. The individual questions below are written in the NEPQ *style and structure* as reusable templates — they are model questions a rep customizes to their offer, not claimed verbatim transcriptions of specific book pages. No fabricated statistics, case-study numbers, or invented client results appear anywhere in this file. Where a thing is genuinely not in the source, it is recorded as ABSENT in Section H rather than invented.
>
> **How to use a question.** Customize the `[bracketed]` slots to the prospect's world, keep the marked **tonality**, ask it, then **stay silent** and let them answer. The questions do the selling.

## A — Headline, Hook & Subject-Line Formula Bank
*(For a question-based selling book, the "headlines/hooks" are the verbatim QUESTIONS by stage — the highest-leverage assets in the book. Each entry = a question template + when to use + tonality.)*

The governing law of every question below: **a skilled question lowers the guard and lets the prospect reach the conclusion themselves; tone carries most of the meaning.** Ask, then be quiet.

### A1 — Connection Questions (lower the guard, make it about them)
- **When to use:** The opener and early call; takes focus off the seller, defuses the "I'm being sold" reflex.
- **Tonality:** curious / neutral.
- **Question bank:**
  - "Thanks for taking a few minutes — out of curiosity, what made you take the call today?"
  - "Before I share anything, would it be okay if I asked you a couple of questions so I actually understand your situation first?"
  - "What were you hoping to get out of our conversation today?"
  - "How did you first hear about us / what prompted you to look into this?"
  - "So I don't waste your time — what's going on in [their area] that made you open to looking at this?"
- **Source:** NEPQ Connection stage.

### A2 — Situation Questions (context only — keep these FEW)
- **When to use:** A small set to understand current state; low emotional value, so minimize.
- **Tonality:** neutral / matter-of-fact.
- **Question bank:**
  - "How are you currently handling [the area your offer addresses]?"
  - "How long have you been doing it that way?"
  - "Walk me through what that looks like day to day."
  - "Who else is involved in [this process / this decision]?"
  - "What does your setup look like right now with [tool/process/team]?"
- **Source:** NEPQ Situation stage.

### A3 — Problem Awareness Questions (the emotional core — make them verbalize it)
- **When to use:** The heart of NEPQ; lead the prospect to say the problem out loud, don't name it for them.
- **Tonality:** curious → concerned.
- **Question bank:**
  - "How's that been working out for you so far?"
  - "What do you like most about how you're doing it now? …and is there anything you'd change if you could?"
  - "What made you start looking for a different way of doing things?"
  - "When you say [X] isn't working — what specifically isn't working about it?"
  - "How long has that been a problem for you?"
  - "What have you tried so far to fix it? …and how did that go?"
  - "Why do you feel it hasn't gotten solved up to now?"
- **Source:** NEPQ Problem Awareness stage.

### A4 — Solution Awareness Questions (let them describe the fix they want)
- **When to use:** After the problem is felt; get the prospect to pre-build the case for your offer in their words.
- **Tonality:** curious / collaborative.
- **Question bank:**
  - "If you could wave a wand and fix this, what would the ideal solution look like for you?"
  - "What would need to happen for you to feel like this was finally handled?"
  - "When you picture this working the way you want — what's different about your day / your numbers / your team?"
  - "What's most important to you in whatever solution you go with?"
  - "Have you thought about how you'd want to solve this, or are you still figuring that out?"
- **Source:** NEPQ Solution Awareness stage.

### A5 — Consequence Questions (raise the cost of inaction — then go silent)
- **When to use:** After problem + desired solution are clear; surface what happens if nothing changes.
- **Tonality:** concerned / calm (never alarmist).
- **Question bank:**
  - "If you don't get this solved, where does that leave you in [6 / 12] months?"
  - "What's it costing you to keep doing it the way you're doing it now?"
  - "How does that affect [the bigger goal / the team / you personally]?"
  - "If nothing changes, what's that going to mean for [the outcome they care about]?"
  - "Is that something you're okay living with, or is it something you feel you need to change?"
- **Source:** NEPQ Consequence stage.

### A6 — Qualifying Questions (make them qualify themselves)
- **When to use:** Flip the dynamic; get the prospect to state why solving this matters to them.
- **Tonality:** neutral / curious.
- **Question bank:**
  - "Why is solving this important to you now, specifically?"
  - "On a scale of 1–10, how important is it that you get this fixed? …why that number and not lower?"
  - "What happens if you don't make a decision on this at all?"
  - "Is this a priority for you, or is it more of a 'someday' thing?"
  - "If we could solve this the way you described, is that something you'd want to move forward on?"
- **Source:** NEPQ Qualifying stage.

### A7 — Transition Questions (earn permission to present)
- **When to use:** The bridge from questioning to presenting; invite the present rather than barge into it.
- **Tonality:** neutral / light.
- **Question bank:**
  - "Would it help if I showed you how we've helped others in a similar situation get [the outcome they named]?"
  - "Do you want me to walk you through how this could work for you?"
  - "Based on what you've told me, I think there might be a fit — want me to show you what that looks like?"
  - "Would you be opposed to me sharing a couple of ways we could approach this?"
- **Source:** NEPQ Transition stage.

### A8 — Pre-Frame / Status Questions (hold a neutral, non-needy frame)
- **When to use:** Throughout — to stay detached, keep posture, and let the prospect feel they're driving.
- **Tonality:** neutral / unattached.
- **Question bank:**
  - "I'm not sure if what we do is even a fit for you yet — that's what I'm trying to figure out. Fair?"
  - "Honestly, it might not be a fit — but can I ask you a couple more questions to see?"
  - "I don't want to pitch you something you don't need — mind if we figure out together whether this even makes sense?"
- **Source:** NEPQ neutral-detachment posture.

### A9 — Commitment Questions (let the prospect take the next step)
- **When to use:** The "close" — lead the prospect to commit and define next steps themselves.
- **Tonality:** neutral / calm.
- **Question bank:**
  - "So where would you like to go from here?"
  - "What would you like to do next?"
  - "How do you feel about everything we've talked about?"
  - "Do you feel like this could be the solution you've been looking for? …what makes you say that?"
  - "What questions do you have for me before we figure out next steps?"
  - "Is there any reason we couldn't get you started today?" (only after they've said it's a fit)
- **Source:** NEPQ Commitment stage.

## B — Funnel & Page Recipes (page-by-page)
*(For a conversation book, the "funnel/pages" are the STAGE RECIPES — the block-order job of each stage in the question-flow. B0 is the master flow; B1–B9 are the stages; the high-value variants are alternate call types.)*

The book teaches ONE master conversation and its constituent stages; every sales call is this skeleton. Below is the full recipe set: the master flow, each stage, and the high-value call-type variants.

### B0 — MASTER RECIPE: The NEPQ Question-Flow (the whole conversation)
- **When to use:** Any sales conversation — discovery, demo, or closing call. This is the spine all other recipes hang on.
- **Pattern (stage order + job of each):**
  1. `CONNECTION` — *job: lower the guard, make it about them.*
  2. `SITUATION` — *job: light context (few questions).*
  3. `PROBLEM AWARENESS` — *job: prospect verbalizes + feels the problem.*
  4. `SOLUTION AWARENESS` — *job: prospect describes the ideal fix.*
  5. `CONSEQUENCE` — *job: surface the cost of inaction (urgency).*
  6. `QUALIFYING` — *job: prospect states why it matters to them.*
  7. `TRANSITION` — *job: earn permission to present.*
  8. `PRESENTATION` — *job: tie back to their stated words (short, surgical).*
  9. `COMMITMENT` — *job: prospect-led next step.*
- **Rule:** No Presentation until Problem + Consequence are surfaced. Stay neutral/detached the entire way.
- **Source:** NEPQ stage architecture.

### B1 — CONNECTION stage recipe
- **When to use:** Open of every call.
- **Pattern:** open about THEM, not you → ask why they took the call → get a micro-permission to ask questions → neutral/curious tone. (Questions: **§A1**.)
- **Goal:** the prospect feels this is a conversation, not a pitch; guard drops.
- **Source:** NEPQ Connection.

### B2 — SITUATION stage recipe
- **When to use:** Right after Connection.
- **Pattern:** ask the FEW facts you genuinely need (current setup, how long, who's involved) → don't linger; facts have low emotional value → move to Problem Awareness fast. (Questions: **§A2**.)
- **Goal:** enough context to ask sharp Problem Awareness questions.
- **Source:** NEPQ Situation.

### B3 — PROBLEM AWARENESS stage recipe (the core)
- **When to use:** The emotional heart of the call; spend the most time here.
- **Pattern:** "how's that working?" → "what would you change?" → "what made you look?" → "what specifically isn't working?" → "how long?" → "what have you tried?" → "why hasn't it been solved?" — each answer feeds the next question. Do NOT name the problem for them. (Questions: **§A3**.)
- **Goal:** the prospect says the problem out loud and feels it.
- **Source:** NEPQ Problem Awareness.

### B4 — SOLUTION AWARENESS stage recipe
- **When to use:** After the problem is felt.
- **Pattern:** "if you could fix this, what's ideal?" → "what matters most in a solution?" → "what's different when it works?" — let them describe the after-state in their words. (Questions: **§A4**.)
- **Goal:** the prospect pre-builds your offer's value proposition for you.
- **Source:** NEPQ Solution Awareness.

### B5 — CONSEQUENCE stage recipe
- **When to use:** After problem + desired solution are on the table; the urgency engine.
- **Pattern:** "where does this leave you if it's not solved?" → "what's it costing you?" → "how does it affect [bigger goal]?" → "are you okay living with that?" → then **silence**. Calm, concerned tone — never fear-mongering. (Questions: **§A5**.)
- **Goal:** inaction now has an emotional cost; change feels necessary.
- **Source:** NEPQ Consequence.

### B6 — QUALIFYING stage recipe
- **When to use:** Before transitioning to present.
- **Pattern:** "why is solving this important to you now?" → "1–10 how important? why that number?" → "is this a priority?" → "if we solved it your way, would you want to move forward?" (Questions: **§A6**.)
- **Goal:** the prospect qualifies themselves and signals real intent.
- **Source:** NEPQ Qualifying.

### B7 — TRANSITION stage recipe
- **When to use:** The bridge into the presentation.
- **Pattern:** ask permission to present ("would it help if I showed you…?") → only proceed on a yes. (Questions: **§A7**.)
- **Goal:** the prospect invites the presentation, so they're open to it.
- **Source:** NEPQ Transition.

### B8 — PRESENTATION stage recipe (tie-back, NOT a feature dump)
- **When to use:** Only after permission and after Problem + Consequence are surfaced.
- **Pattern (block order):**
  1. `RESTATE THEIR WORDS` — "You told me [problem], that it's costing you [consequence], and you want [desired outcome]…"
  2. `TIE EACH POINT TO ONE CAPABILITY` — for each stated item, one specific way the offer addresses it (one tie-back per item).
  3. `STOP` — don't list features they didn't ask about.
  4. `CHECK` — "Does that line up with what you were hoping for?"
- **Goal:** a short presentation that connects only to what the prospect already said matters. (Tie-back script: **§C1**.)
- **Source:** NEPQ Presentation.

### B9 — COMMITMENT stage recipe
- **When to use:** The close.
- **Pattern:** "how do you feel about everything?" → "where would you like to go from here?" → "what would you like to do next?" → let the prospect state the next step; handle any objection as a question (§C10–C14). (Questions: **§A9**.)
- **Goal:** the prospect commits themselves; the rep confirms, never pushes.
- **Source:** NEPQ Commitment.

### B10 — VARIANT: Cold-Call / Outbound opener flow
- **When to use:** Interruption outbound (no prior interest).
- **Pattern:** pattern-interrupt + permission → "Hey [name], I know you weren't expecting my call — can I have 30 seconds and then you can tell me to go away?" → one curious Problem Awareness probe → book the real call. Stay neutral; expect and welcome the brush-off.
- **Source:** NEPQ applied to outbound.

### B11 — VARIANT: Inbound / Appointment-Setter flow
- **When to use:** Lead opted in; setter qualifies for the closer.
- **Pattern:** Connection ("what made you fill out the form?") → 2–3 Problem Awareness questions → light Consequence → Qualifying → book the closing call with the problem documented for the closer.
- **Source:** NEPQ applied to setting.

### B12 — VARIANT: Demo / Presentation-heavy call (de-pitch it)
- **When to use:** A process that's become a demo monologue.
- **Pattern:** front-load Connection + Problem Awareness BEFORE opening the demo → demo only the 2–3 features that map to stated problems → ask a tie-back check after each → Commitment. Cut everything they didn't ask about.
- **Source:** NEPQ applied to demos.

## C — Script Bank (sales / objection / follow-up / discovery / close)

NEPQ "scripts" are short say-this / ask-this lines: the tie-back presentation, the silence cue, and — most importantly — the **objection-as-question reframes** (cross-referenced from blueprint Section 12). All captured below.

### C1 — The Tie-Back Presentation script
- **When to use:** The Presentation stage; connect only to what they said.
- **Pattern:** `You mentioned [their problem], that it's been costing you [their consequence], and that ideally you'd want [their desired outcome]. Here's specifically how we help with that…` → one capability per stated item → `Does that line up with what you were hoping for?`
- **Source:** NEPQ Presentation.

### C2 — The Silence / Tongue-Bite cue
- **When to use:** Immediately after any Problem Awareness, Consequence, or Commitment question.
- **Pattern:** `[Ask the question] … [say nothing] … [let them fill the silence].` The pause is part of the script.
- **Source:** NEPQ delivery discipline.

### C3 — The Permission Micro-Ask
- **When to use:** Early, to disarm and earn the right to question.
- **Pattern:** `Would it be okay if I asked you a few questions first, so I actually understand your situation before I say anything?`
- **Source:** NEPQ Connection.

### C4 — The "Compared to what?" clarifier
- **When to use:** Any vague claim ("it's too expensive," "we're fine," "it's not a good time").
- **Pattern:** `Compared to what, specifically?` / `When you say [X], what do you mean by that exactly?`
- **Source:** NEPQ clarifying reflex.

### C5 — The Looping-Back probe
- **When to use:** To deepen any answer without pitching.
- **Pattern:** `You said [their word] — tell me more about that.` / `What do you mean by [their word]?`
- **Source:** NEPQ Problem Awareness deepening.

### C6 — The Setter→Closer handoff script
- **When to use:** Booking a closing call after setting.
- **Pattern:** `Based on what you told me about [their problem] and how it's affecting [their consequence], it sounds like a fit to talk with [closer]. Let's get you a time — and I'll make sure they already know your situation so you don't repeat yourself.`
- **Source:** NEPQ setting application.

### C7 — The No-Show / Re-engage follow-up ask
- **When to use:** A booked call that ghosted (kept question-shaped, never begging).
- **Pattern:** `Hey [name] — we had [time] set to talk through [their stated problem]. Did something change on your end, or is this still something you want to get solved?`
- **Source:** NEPQ neutral follow-up.

### C8 — The Post-Presentation Feel-Check
- **When to use:** Right after the tie-back presentation.
- **Pattern:** `How do you feel about what I just showed you?` (open, neutral — not "are you ready to buy?")
- **Source:** NEPQ Commitment lead-in.

### C9 — The Next-Step Hand-Back
- **When to use:** The close itself.
- **Pattern:** `So where would you like to go from here?` → then be silent and let them choose.
- **Source:** NEPQ Commitment.

### C10 — Objection reframe: "It's too expensive / I can't afford it."
- **When to use:** Price resistance.
- **Pattern (ASK, don't argue):** `I understand — can I ask, is it that the price is more than you expected, or more that you're not yet sure it'll get you [the outcome you wanted]?` → then `What would it be costing you to leave [the problem] unsolved for another year?`
- **Source:** NEPQ objection-as-question.

### C11 — Objection reframe: "I need to think about it."
- **When to use:** The stall.
- **Pattern:** `Totally fair — when you say you want to think about it, what specifically is it you'd want to think through? Is it [A], [B], or something else?`
- **Source:** NEPQ objection-as-question.

### C12 — Objection reframe: "I need to talk to my [partner / team / boss]."
- **When to use:** The deferral.
- **Pattern:** `Makes sense. If they ask you 'why do you want to do this?' — what would you tell them?` (gets the prospect to sell it themselves) → `What do you think they'll be most concerned about, so we can make sure that's covered?`
- **Source:** NEPQ objection-as-question.

### C13 — Objection reframe: "Now's not a good time."
- **When to use:** The timing dodge.
- **Pattern:** `I hear you — is it that the timing's genuinely off, or more that you're not 100% sure this is the right solution yet? Because if it were the right fit, would timing still be the issue?`
- **Source:** NEPQ objection-as-question.

### C14 — Objection reframe: "Just send me some information."
- **When to use:** The brush-off.
- **Pattern:** `Happy to — so I send the right thing and not a generic packet, what specifically would be most useful for you to see about [their problem]?`
- **Source:** NEPQ objection-as-question.

## D — Email & Sequence Bank

NEPQ follow-up is **question-shaped and neutral** — never begging, never "just checking in." The sequence keeps the prospect's stated problem at the center and uses calm questions to re-open the conversation. Captured below as one complete sequence; body copy is patternized from the NEPQ neutral-detachment posture and the objection-reframe style (fidelity MODERATE; see H).

### D — The NEPQ Neutral Follow-Up Sequence (complete, in order)

**Message 1 — Recap + One Question (T+0, after the call, Email)**
- **Role:** Restate their problem in their words, confirm next step, end on a question.
- **Subject pattern:** `[Their stated problem] — quick recap`
- **Body skeleton:** `[Thanks] → [restate their problem + consequence + desired outcome in their words] → [the next step we agreed] → [one open question: "Did I capture that right?"]`
- **Tonality:** neutral / collaborative.

**Message 2 — Value + Soft Probe (T+2 days, Email or SMS)**
- **Role:** Add one relevant resource tied to THEIR problem, then a calm question.
- **Body skeleton:** `[One specific resource/answer for their stated problem] → ["Did this line up with what you were thinking, or did it raise other questions?"]`
- **Tonality:** curious / unattached.

**Message 3 — Neutral Re-engage (T+4–5 days, if no reply, SMS / Email)**
- **Role:** Re-open without pressure; give an easy out (which paradoxically re-engages).
- **Body skeleton:** `[name] — when we talked, [their problem] was something you wanted to get solved. Has that changed, or is it still on your radar?`
- **Tonality:** neutral (the "easy out" reframe, like §C7).

**Message 4 — Consequence Nudge (T+ ~1 week, Email)**
- **Role:** Calmly resurface the cost of inaction as a question.
- **Body skeleton:** `[Short, no-pressure note] → ["If nothing changes with [their problem], where does that leave things in a few months? Worth a quick conversation, or should I close your file for now?"]`
- **Tonality:** concerned / calm.

**Message 5 — The Clean Close-Out (T+ ~2 weeks, Email)**
- **Role:** The respectful takeaway — often the highest-reply message.
- **Body skeleton:** `[name] — I don't want to keep landing in your inbox. Should I assume the timing isn't right and close this out, or is [their problem] still something you want help with?`
- **Tonality:** neutral / detached.

**Broadcasts — Event-driven (layered across the sequence)**
- **Role:** Relevant, non-needy reasons to reconnect (a case relevant to their problem, a deadline, a new option).
- **Pattern:** `[Specific, relevant trigger] → [one calm question].`

## E — Frameworks, Models & Templates (with the steps)

Every named model behind the question bank, reproduced with its internal steps.

### E1 — The Three NEPQ Tonalities
- **Steps:** Deliver every question in one of three tones, chosen on purpose: (1) **Curious/Confused** — soft, low-pressure, "help me understand," used to ask without triggering the guard. (2) **Concerned** — calm care, used on Problem Awareness + Consequence questions so the prospect feels the weight without pressure. (3) **Playful** — light, keeps the call human and unattached. Default posture under all three: **neutral / detached**. The same words push or disarm depending on the tone.
- **Worked example:** "What's it costing you to keep doing it this way?" said *eager* sounds like a pitch; said *concerned + neutral* invites an honest answer.
- **Source:** NEPQ tonality system.

### E2 — Neutral Detachment (release the outcome)
- **Steps:** Let go of attachment to the sale; the moment the prospect feels you NEED it, the guard goes up. Hold a "this might not be a fit, let's find out together" frame (§A8). Be willing to walk; that posture is what lowers resistance.
- **Worked example:** "Honestly, it might not be a fit — but can I ask a couple more questions to see?"
- **Source:** NEPQ posture.

### E3 — The 9-Stage Question-Flow
- **Steps:** Connection → Situation → Problem Awareness → Solution Awareness → Consequence → Qualifying → Transition → Presentation → Commitment (full block recipe of each in **Section B**; verbatim questions in **Section A**).
- **Worked example:** A discovery call run end-to-end through all nine stages, presenting only after Consequence.
- **Source:** NEPQ architecture.

### E4 — Old Model vs New Model of Selling
- **Steps:** OLD = build rapport with small talk → pitch/present early → handle objections by arguing → hard close (built for information scarcity, triggers resistance today). NEW = lower the guard with questions → let the prospect surface the problem/consequence → present by tie-back → let them commit. Match the model to today's informed, skeptical buyer.
- **Worked example:** Replacing "let me tell you about our product" with "what made you look into this?"
- **Source:** NEPQ premise. *(For the full philosophy, route to acuff-miner-new-model-of-selling.)*

### E5 — Let Them Persuade Themselves
- **Steps:** People believe their own conclusions over yours. Engineer questions so the prospect states the problem, the cost, and the desire — then your job is just to confirm and tie back. Stop telling; start asking.
- **Worked example:** Instead of "you're losing money doing it that way," ask "what's it costing you to keep doing it that way?"
- **Source:** NEPQ core principle.

### E6 — The Problem-Awareness Ladder
- **Steps:** Climb from surface to felt problem: (1) how's it working? → (2) what would you change? → (3) what made you look? → (4) what specifically isn't working? → (5) how long? → (6) what have you tried? → (7) why hasn't it been solved? Each rung deepens the emotion.
- **Worked example:** A prospect who started at "we're mostly fine" ends at "honestly it's been costing us deals for a year."
- **Source:** NEPQ Problem Awareness.

### E7 — Objection-as-Question (don't overcome — reframe)
- **Steps:** Never argue an objection. (1) Acknowledge calmly. (2) Ask a clarifying question to find what it really means. (3) Turn it back so the prospect re-evaluates it themselves (full reframes in **§C10–C14**).
- **Worked example:** "Too expensive" → "Is it more than you expected, or are you not yet sure it'll get you the result?"
- **Source:** NEPQ objection handling.

### E8 — The Tie-Back Presentation Model
- **Steps:** Present only what the prospect named: (1) restate their problem/consequence/desired outcome in their words; (2) connect one capability to each; (3) stop; (4) check ("does that line up?"). No feature dump.
- **Worked example:** Three stated problems → three tie-backs → one alignment check (script **§C1**).
- **Source:** NEPQ Presentation.

### E9 — The Commitment-by-Question Close
- **Steps:** Replace pushing with prospect-led questions: "how do you feel?" → "where would you like to go from here?" → "what would you like to do next?" → confirm. The prospect closes themselves.
- **Worked example:** "So where would you like to go from here?" → prospect names the next step.
- **Source:** NEPQ Commitment.

### E10 — Talk-Ratio Discipline
- **Steps:** Target the prospect talking far more than the rep (questions + silence drive this). Track it on call reviews. If the rep is talking more than the prospect, the call has drifted into pitching.
- **Worked example:** A QA flag triggers whenever rep talk-time exceeds prospect talk-time in the discovery portion.
- **Source:** NEPQ practice discipline.

## F — Brand Voice & Brand-Building Language Patterns

Jeremy Miner's reusable NEPQ voice machinery, as ready-to-use lists with example lines. This is what lets a sales-script writer produce ON-method scripts that ask instead of pitch.

### F1 — The neutral, detached opener
- `I'm not even sure if this is a fit for you yet — that's what I'm trying to figure out.`

### F2 — Permission micro-asks
- `Would it be okay if I asked you a couple of questions first?` / `Mind if I ask…?`

### F3 — The curiosity/confused softener
- `Help me understand…` / `Out of curiosity…` / `I'm curious — when you say [X], what do you mean exactly?`

### F4 — The looping echo
- Repeat the prospect's own word back as a question: `You said "[their word]" — tell me more about that.`

### F5 — The calm consequence frame
- `If nothing changes, where does that leave you?` — concerned, never alarmist.

### F6 — The "compared to what?" clarifier
- `Compared to what, specifically?` / `What do you mean by that exactly?`

### F7 — The objection-as-question turn
- `Is it [A], or is it more [B]?` — always two calm options, never an argument.

### F8 — The self-qualifying prompt
- `On a scale of 1–10, how important is solving this? …why that number and not lower?`

### F9 — The hand-back close
- `So where would you like to go from here?` / `What would you like to do next?`

### F10 — The tie-back stem
- `You told me [their words]… here's specifically how that gets handled…`

### F11 — Power-word / phrase bank (use)
- skilled questions · tonality · neutral · detached · curious · out of curiosity · specifically · help me understand · what made you · how's that working · what's it costing you · where would you like to go from here.

### F12 — Words / phrases to AVOID
- "overcome the objection," "always be closing," "lead with value," "let me tell you about us," "amazing offer," "limited time act now," "crush it," "no-brainer," "trust me," "to be honest with you" (as a filler), and any line that signals you NEED the sale.

### F13 — The silence directive
- After a key question: `…` — say nothing. The pause is a language pattern.

### F14 — The "it might not be a fit" posture line
- `Honestly, this might not be for you — but can I ask a couple more questions to see?`

### F15 — The status / non-needy frame
- `I don't want to pitch you something you don't need — can we figure out together whether it even makes sense?`

### F16 — Concerned-tone consequence stems
- `Is that something you're okay living with, or something you feel you need to change?`

### F17 — The next-step feel-check
- `How do you feel about everything we talked about?` (open, not "ready to buy?")

### F18 — The clean takeaway (follow-up)
- `Should I assume the timing isn't right and close this out, or is this still something you want to solve?`

### F19 — The pre-frame for questions
- `So I don't waste your time, can I ask you a few things first?`

### F20 — Tonality tags (mark these on every written question)
- `[curious]` · `[concerned]` · `[playful]` · default `[neutral]`. Every deliverable question carries one.
