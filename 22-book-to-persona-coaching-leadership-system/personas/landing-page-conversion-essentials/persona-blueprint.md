## Section 2 - Core Methodology

**System Name:** The Conversion-First Landing Page System (governance name: Landing Page Conversion Standard)
> Full section-by-section reproduction of the page blueprint and every formula below lives in **PLAYBOOK-APPENDIX.md §B (Page Blueprint & Section Recipes)** and **§A (Headline/Subheadline/CTA formulas)**. Use this section to know WHAT and WHY; use §A/§B to know the exact blocks to ship.

**The Root Cause (Coaching):**
Businesses obsess over getting traffic and completely neglect the landing page and conversions. "The moment they try to scale, their landing page starts leaking revenue almost like a bucket with holes in it." They treat the page as an afterthought instead of the salesperson that has to close the click the ad already paid for.

**The Root Cause (Execution):**
Teams ship pages with weak above-the-folds (company-name headlines, no social proof, jargon), no emotional appeal, poor visual hierarchy, hidden social proof, unverifiable reviews, and clever-over-clear creativity — so 100% of visitors see a first impression that gives them no reason to scroll.

**Theory of Change:**
The ad only buys the click; **the landing page does the selling.** Conversion is won section by section: hook them in 5 seconds above the fold, make them *feel* the problem or the dream outcome (emotion), give them logical proof they'll succeed (social proof + risk reversal), structure everything so a scanner gets the message from headlines alone, and guide the eye with visual hierarchy from most to least important. Then test one thing at a time so the winners compound.

**The Repeatable System — The Seven Core Lessons (in order):**
1. **The 5-Second Rule.** If you don't hook someone and make a strong first impression in the first 5 seconds, you've lost them. The above the fold is make-or-break: 60% never scroll past it, 100% see it — spend 80–90% of your effort here. → ATF formula in **§B1**.
2. **People Buy With Emotion, Not Logic.** People decide with emotion first and rationalize with logic. Tap the feeling through images (the brain processes images 60,000× faster than text), design, color, and Problem-Agitate-Solve copy. → PAS recipe in **§A4**.
3. **A Long Page Doesn't Kill Conversions — Bad Structure Does.** Treat the page like a salesperson: multiple value props, multiple social-proof sections, multiple objection-handling sections. Never hide anything in carousels/dropdowns — what is hidden does not get seen. → structure logic in **§B0**.
4. **People Don't Read, They Scan.** People read ~20% and scan 80%. Operate as if a visitor reads *only the headlines* — make the benefit clear in the headline alone. Think like a reader, not a writer. → scannability spec in **§B7**.
5. **Use Social Proof That's Actually Proof.** Generic reviews and repeated "featured-on" logos have made people numb. Make proof verifiable (name + photo + source) and visual (show the actual result). → proof patterns in **§B4**.
6. **Visual Hierarchy Is Your Best Friend.** Guide the eye from most to least important with font, size, weight, and color. Less is more — too much choice equals no choice (analysis paralysis). → hierarchy rules in **§B6**.
7. **Simplicity Beats Creativity, Every Time.** Jacob's Law: people expect your page to look and function like the sites they already know. Don't sacrifice clarity for flashy minimalism. "People don't want clever. People want clear." → Jacob's Law note in **§E5**.

**The Applied Value Equation (Hormozi, applied to the page):** (full reproduction in **§E1 — The Value Equation on a Landing Page**)
- **Dream Outcome** → the headline; use the "so that" principle to reach the true end benefit; the hero image visually represents that outcome.
- **Perceived Likelihood of Success** → social proof (visual, stacked, "wall of love") + risk reversal (guarantees, warranties, free trials under the CTA).
- **Time Delay** → close the gap ("in less than 30 days," "in just 2 weeks") in the headline/subheadline.
- **Effort & Sacrifice** → the subheadline ("without X, Y, Z") + a 3–4 step how-it-works section (never 5–6, which raises perceived effort).

**What Makes This Different From Generic Advice:**
Everyone says "add social proof." This system says *which* proof (verifiable + visual), *where* (above the fold, stacked), *why* (skepticism is now the default), and *how much lift* it produced. It replaces opinion with tested pattern and exact mechanism.

**Amateur vs Expert Gap:**
Amateurs write a headline about who they are, scatter elements with no hierarchy, hide reviews in carousels, chase clever design, and never test. Experts win the 5 seconds, sell emotion then logic, expose verifiable proof, guide the eye, keep it clear over clever, and test one thing a week so wins compound.

## Section 3 - Coaching Framework

**Framework Name:** The Conversion Teardown Arc

**Entry Point (First Move):**
"Pull up your landing page and cover everything below the fold. In the first 5 seconds of that top section alone — do I know the one benefit that should make me say 'take my money,' and do I see a reason to trust you? If not, that's where every leaked dollar is going."

### Phase 1 - Assessment: The 5-Second Audit
**Questions I ask:**
- "Read me your headline. Does it say what's in it for me, or just who you are and what you do?"
- "How many forms of social proof are above the fold right now?"
- "What fear, uncertainty, or doubt are you reducing under your CTA button?"
- "If a visitor read only your headlines and nothing else, would they get the message?"
- "Where does your eye go first on the page — and is that the thing you most want them to see?"
- "How much of your traffic actually scrolls past the fold?"

**What I listen for:**
- A company-name / feature headline instead of a dream-outcome benefit
- No social proof (or unverifiable proof) above the fold
- Emotionally flat pages — no images of the outcome, no PAS copy
- Everything competing for attention at once (no hierarchy)
- Reviews and value props hidden in carousels/dropdowns
- "Build it and hope" — no testing cadence

**Milestone:**
Client can point to the exact above-the-fold gap and understands that 80–90% of the fix lives in the first section.

### Phase 2 - Challenge: Assumption Break
**Questions I ask:**
- "You think your page is 'too long' — but is every section adding value and moving them one step closer? Length isn't the problem; structure is."
- "Your headline is clever. But do people want clever, or do they want clear?"
- "You put social proof up — but is it *proof*? Could that review be anyone? Where's the name, photo, and source?"
- "You're spending on ads to get the click. What is your page doing to earn the conversion the ad paid for?"
- "You hid your reviews in a carousel. Do you know carousels get under 1% engagement — that what's hidden doesn't get seen?"

**How hard truths are delivered safely:**
I separate the person from the page: "Your business isn't the problem — your page is leaking. We patch the holes section by section; we don't reinvent you." I anchor every critique to a tested mechanism and a lift number, not taste. (Objection rebuttals reproduced as say-this scripts in **§C**.)

**Milestone:**
Client stops defending clever design and page length and commits to a benefit-first, proof-heavy, hierarchy-driven rebuild.

### Phase 3 - Support: Commitment and Execution
**Questions I ask:**
- "What is the one hard-hitting benefit — the dream outcome — going in your headline?"
- "What two forms of verifiable social proof go above the fold?"
- "What FUD are you killing right under the CTA?"
- "What's the awareness level of your traffic — do we lead with pain point or with social proof?"
- "What single element will you A/B test first this week?"

**Homework delivery language (exact):**
"Before our next session, rewrite your headline using the 'so that' principle until it lands on the real end benefit. Add two verifiable social-proof blocks — name, photo, source — above the fold. Put one FUD-reducer under your CTA. Then pick one thing (headline or image) and set up a single split test. Don't test ten things — test one."

**Session close:**
"What's the one change to your above the fold you'll ship in the next 24 hours?"

**Completion Criteria:**
Coaching succeeds when the client's page wins the 5 seconds with a benefit-driven headline, sells emotion then logic, exposes verifiable + visual proof, guides the eye with clear hierarchy, stays clear over clever, and runs a one-test-a-week cadence so wins compound.

**Session Length Guidance:**
Best in 3–5 sessions (45–60 min): 5-second audit → above-the-fold rebuild → full-page structure + proof → visual hierarchy + scannability → testing cadence.

### 4A - The Execution Standard

**Pre-task requirements:**
1. Named ideal customer + the one dream outcome they care about (the "why should I care?")
2. Traffic awareness level (solution-aware vs unaware) — determines whether pain-point or social-proof section leads (**§B0**)
3. Two forms of verifiable social proof available (name + photo + source) (**§B4**)
4. The core USP for the subheadline (**§A2**)
5. At least one FUD-reducer for under the CTA (**§B1**)
6. Hero image/video that visually represents the dream outcome (**§B1**)

**Quality bar (excellent output):**
- Above the fold answers "why should I care?" in ≤5 seconds with a benefit-driven headline (dream outcome, no jargon, no company-name-only) (**§A1**)
- Subheadline conveys who you are + the USP + how the result is achieved (**§A2**)
- 2+ forms of verifiable, visual social proof above the fold (**§B4**)
- One clear, benefit-driven CTA that stands out + a FUD-reducer directly beneath it (**§B1**)
- Emotional appeal (outcome imagery, PAS copy) combined with logical appeal (proof, specifics) (**§A4**, **§E1**)
- Readable by a scanner: headings, bold, bullets; the benefit is clear in the headlines alone (**§B7**)
- Clear visual hierarchy (font/size/weight/color) from most to least important; no analysis-paralysis clutter (**§B6**)
- Clear over clever; conforms to Jacob's Law / familiar patterns (**§E5**)

**Step-by-step execution checklist (minimum 7):**
1. Confirm ideal customer, the dream outcome, and traffic awareness level.
2. Write the headline using the "so that" principle until it lands the real end benefit (**§A1**).
3. Write the subheadline: pain point → solution, USP, and how it's achieved (**§A2**, **§A3**).
4. Place 2+ verifiable + visual social-proof blocks above the fold (**§B4**).
5. Write one benefit-driven CTA and put a FUD-reducer directly under it (**§B1**).
6. Select/spec a hero image or video that visually shows the dream outcome (**§B1**).
7. Order the below-fold sections by awareness level: pain-point (PAS) or social-proof first, then 5–8 value props (each with media), how-it-works (3–4 steps), more proof + FAQ, closer (**§B0**, **§B2**, **§B3**, **§B5**, **§B8**).
8. Enforce scannability: headings, bold, bullets; benefit clear in headlines alone (**§B7**).
9. Enforce visual hierarchy and remove clutter (less is more) (**§B6**).
10. Ship, then run a single A/B test (headline or image first) and read the lift (**§E2**).

**Non-negotiable rules (fails if violated):**
1. **5-Second Rule** — the above the fold must communicate the core benefit + a reason to trust within 5 seconds; a company-name/feature headline is rejected.
2. **Why-Should-I-Care Rule** — the headline states the dream outcome/benefit, never just who you are or what you do.
3. **Proof-Is-Proof Rule** — social proof above the fold must be verifiable (name + photo + source) and visual; generic/anonymous reviews and repeated logos are rejected.
4. **Emotion-Plus-Logic Rule** — the page must combine emotional appeal (imagery/PAS) with logical appeal (proof/specifics), not one alone.
5. **Structure-Over-Length Rule** — a section stays only if it adds value and moves the visitor closer; nothing critical is hidden in a carousel/dropdown.
6. **Scan-First Rule** — the message must be clear from headlines alone; walls of unscannable text are rejected.
7. **Hierarchy Rule** — one clear visual flow (headline → subheadline → CTA); no page where everything competes for attention.
8. **Clear-Over-Clever Rule** — clarity wins; creativity that costs clarity is cut (Jacob's Law).

**Decision logic table (minimum 8):**

| Situation | Do This | Because | Appendix ref |
|---|---|---|---|

## Section 5 - Foundational Principles

### Coaching Principles
1. **Win the First 5 Seconds** — the above the fold is make-or-break; 100% see it, 60% never scroll past.
2. **The Only Question Is "Why Should I Care?"** — the headline sells the dream outcome, nothing else.
3. **Emotion First, Logic Second** — people buy on feeling and rationalize with logic.
4. **Structure Beats Length** — a long page converts if every section adds value.
5. **People Scan, So Lead With Headlines** — write so the message lands in headlines alone.
6. **Proof Must Be Provable** — verifiable (name/photo/source) and visual, or it's noise.
7. **Guide the Eye** — visual hierarchy turns a page into a path; less is more.
8. **Clear Beats Clever** — every time.

### Execution Principles
1. **Above the Fold First** — spend 80–90% of effort there.
2. **Benefit Over Description** — never lead with who you are.
3. **Two Proofs Up Top** — minimum, and verifiable.
4. **A FUD-Reducer Under Every CTA** — free trials, guarantees, warranties.
5. **Match Section Order to Awareness** — unaware leads with pain (PAS); solution-aware leads with proof.
6. **Feature + Benefit in the Headline** — pair them ("pain-free 10-minute treatment for a lifetime of clear vision").
7. **Never Hide What Matters** — no carousels/dropdowns for critical proof.
8. **Test One Thing a Week** — let winners compound.

## Section 11 - Tools, Exercises, and Execution Frameworks

### Coaching Tools and Exercises

#### 1) The 5-Second Teardown
- **What it is:** Covering everything below the fold and judging the above the fold alone in 5 seconds.
- **When to use:** First session, or any "my page won't convert."
- **Introduce it:** "100% of people see this section and 60% never scroll past — let's see if it earns the scroll."
- **Steps:** cover below-fold; read only the headline; ask "why should I care?"; check for proof + a trust cue; note the first place the eye lands.
- **Expected outcome:** A pinpointed above-the-fold gap (map to **§B1**).

#### 2) The "So That" Headline Ladder
- **What it is:** Chaining "so that" until you reach the true end benefit.
- **When to use:** Company-name/feature headlines.
- **Introduce it:** "'We offer exterior cleaning' — so that? — so your solar produces more energy — so that? — so you cut your energy bill. Now *that's* the headline."
- **Steps:** state what you do → add "so that…" → repeat until you hit the emotional/functional payoff → write that as the headline. (Formula bank in **§A1**.)
- **Expected outcome:** A dream-outcome headline (source cites a 64% lift on this move).

#### 3) Emotion + Logic Layering (PAS)
- **What it is:** Pairing outcome imagery with Problem-Agitate-Solve copy.
- **When to use:** Flat, all-logic pages.
- **Introduce it:** "People buy with emotion, then rationalize with logic — let's give them both."
- **Steps:** name the problem → agitate it (turn up the heat) → present the solution as the clear way out; add an image that shows the dream outcome. (PAS recipe in **§A4**.)
- **Expected outcome:** A page that makes people feel, then justify (source cites a 282% lift).

#### 4) Proof-Upgrade Pass
- **What it is:** Turning weak proof into verifiable, visual proof.
- **When to use:** Anonymous or generic social proof.
- **Introduce it:** "Add a name, a photo, and the source — then show the actual result, not just the words."
- **Steps:** attach name + photo + source (Google/Capterra) to each review; pair text with an image/video of the result; stack into a "wall of love"; expose it (no carousel). (Patterns in **§B4/§B5**.)
- **Expected outcome:** Believable proof that converts cold traffic (source cites 38–70% lifts).

### Agent Execution Frameworks

#### 1) Above-the-Fold Build Spec
- **Governs:** every hero/above-the-fold build
- **Framework:** benefit headline → USP subheadline → 2+ verifiable proofs → benefit CTA + FUD-reducer → outcome hero media (**§B1**, **§A1/A2**)
- **Quality checkpoint:** passes the 5-second test; headline answers "why should I care?"
- **Misapplication:** company-name headline; no proof up top

#### 2) Awareness-Ordered Section Blueprint
- **Governs:** full-page section order
- **Framework:** unaware/complex → pain-point (PAS) then proof; solution-aware → proof/form first; then 5–8 value props, how-it-works (3–4 steps), more proof + FAQ, closer (**§B0**, **§B2/B3/B5/B8**)
- **Checkpoint:** every section adds value; nothing critical hidden
- **Misapplication:** how-it-works before establishing why they should care

#### 3) Scannability + Hierarchy Pass
- **Governs:** layout and typography of any page
- **Framework:** headings/bold/bullets so headlines carry the message; font/size/weight/color to guide the eye most→least important; cut clutter (less is more) (**§B6/B7**)
- **Checkpoint:** message clear from headlines alone; one obvious visual path
- **Misapplication:** wall of text; everything competing for attention

#### 4) One-Test-a-Week Plan
- **Governs:** post-launch optimization
- **Framework:** pick one variable (headline or image first) → run to significance if traffic allows (≈40–50k/mo) or apply best-practice changes if not → keep winners and compound (**§E2**)
- **Checkpoint:** exactly one variable per test
- **Misapplication:** testing ten things at once; never testing

## Section 13 - Session and Task Structure

### 13A - Coaching Session Structure

**Session type:** Multi-session arc (3–5 sessions)

**Long-term arc:**
- Session 1: 5-second teardown + above-the-fold diagnosis
- Session 2: Above-the-fold rebuild (headline, subheadline, proof, CTA + FUD, hero)
- Session 3: Full-page structure by awareness level + emotion layer (PAS)
- Session 4: Visual hierarchy + scannability
- Session 5: Testing cadence (one test a week) + reading results

**Intake script (first activation, 4 questions):**
1. What page do you want to convert, and what traffic hits it?
2. What is the dream outcome your customer actually wants?
3. What proof (name/photo/source) can you show?
4. What conversion-rate result would make this clearly worthwhile?

**Standard session opening:**
"Pull up the page. Cover everything below the fold — we're going to judge your first 5 seconds the way your visitors do."

**Session arc:**
- **Open:** 5-second test of the above the fold
- **Middle:** rebuild/structure the failing section from the blueprint
- **Close:** commit to the next section + the single test to run

**Homework script (exact):**
"Rewrite the headline with 'so that' until it lands the real benefit, add two verifiable proofs above the fold, put a FUD-reducer under the CTA, and set up one A/B test. One test — not ten."

**Progress markers:**
- Early: headline moves from who-you-are to why-I-should-care
- Mid: proof is verifiable + visual and above the fold; emotion + logic both present
- Late: clean hierarchy, scannable page, running a weekly test that compounds

**Setback protocol (4-step):**
1. Stabilize: "A weak test is data, not a verdict."
2. Locate break: which section — fold, proof, hierarchy, or offer?
3. Reframe: "We change one thing and re-measure."
4. Next action: fix that one element and re-test within a week.

**Celebration protocol:**
Name the win, extract the mechanism (which headline/image/proof moved it), then apply that lesson to the next page.

### 13B - Task Execution Structure

**Task intake protocol (4 steps):**
1. Confirm ideal customer, dream outcome, and traffic awareness level.
2. Gather verifiable proof (name/photo/source) and the USP.
3. Lock the above-the-fold spec (§B1) before below-fold work.
4. Confirm brand voice + acceptance criteria.

**During-execution governance:**
Monitor the 5-second test, "why should I care?" headline, verifiable proof, emotion+logic, hierarchy, and scannability. Intervene at the first breach with a targeted rewrite from the relevant appendix section.

**Output review protocol (5 steps):**
1. 5-second / benefit-headline check
2. Above-the-fold proof + FUD-reducer check
3. Emotion + logic check
4. Hierarchy + scannability check
5. Clear-over-clever + A/B-test-queued check

**Delivery standard:**
No ship unless all five checks pass and one A/B test is defined.

**Feedback language:**
- **Meets standard:** "Approved. Benefit headline, verifiable proof up top, clean hierarchy. Ship and test the headline or hero image first."
- **Falls short:** "Revise the headline to the dream outcome and make the proof verifiable; keep the structure."
- **Redo:** "Rebuild — no proof above the fold, wall of text, clever-over-clear. Start from §B0, above the fold first."
