# ANALYSIS NOTES - The Brand Mapping Strategy (Karen Tiber Leland)
**Phase 2 Analysis — 12 analytical dimensions**

---

## 1. Amateur-to-Expert Gap (6 dimensions)
| Dimension | Amateur | Expert |
|---|---|---|
| Sequence | Builds the website first, brands later | Brands first, then builds (Brand Before You Build) |
| Definition | Look, logo, line (the three Ls) | All 7 core elements, fully mapped |
| Copy | Describes activities ("we do X") | Speaks Be/Do/Have; named Signature Services |
| Design | "I like this color" / "left it to the web guy" | Brand tone + audience psychology drive design |
| Channels | Each one its own look/voice | All consistent, anchored to the website hub |
| Reach + measure | Chases one big hit; judges by next-week conversion | Becomes ubiquitous; measures Customers/Credibility/Cash over a marathon |

## 2. Failure Taxonomy (root patterns)
Build-Before-Brand; Three-Ls Trap (visual-only "rebrand"); Brand Confusion (vague benefit-speak); Do-Only Copy (no Have/Be); Design-by-Whim; Channel Drift; Oprah Thinking (one big hit); Promise-Beyond-Capacity; Blow-Dryer Staleness; Vicious Cycle of Change (in org brand transformation).

## 3. Execution Standard (the measurable bar)
A brand artifact is "excellent" when: a cold reader (journalist, 59-second prospect) finds it clear/credible/professional; it expresses Be/Do/Have; it is specific (named offers); voice/color/design match the defined tone; it is consistent with the website hub; copy is clear/concise/accurate; it is fresh for today (no Blow Dryer Effect); and it is built for compounding Customers/Credibility/Cash.

## 4. Decision Rules (8+)
1) No brand defined -> halt the build. 2) "Rebrand" = visuals only -> reframe to full brand map. 3) Vague copy -> rewrite to a specific Signature Service. 4) Do-only copy -> add Have + Be. 5) Color/design by taste -> derive from tone + audience psychology. 6) Channel drift -> re-align to the website hub. 7) "One big hit" request -> add ubiquity plan; treat hit as bonus credibility. 8) Promise > capacity -> right-size or build capacity. 9) Asset feels dated -> refresh (Blow Dryer). 10) Founder content vs company brand -> build a complementary parallel brand.

## 5. Department / Role Application Map
- **Marketing:** brand platform, content pillars, taglines, campaigns -> Brand Strategist, Content Strategist.
- **Creative/Design:** visual identity, headshots, color/design briefs -> Designer (tone-drives-design).
- **Web Development:** website + funnel/landing/opt-in COPY -> Website/Funnel Copywriter (Skill 6 build seam).
- **Social Media:** bios, profiles, branded backgrounds, content (Skill 35) -> Social Media Manager.
- **CRM/Convert-and-Flow:** email/nurture voice consistency -> Email Copywriter.
- **Leadership:** founder/CEO personal + parallel brand, thought-leadership content -> CEO/Executive Brand Specialist.

## 6. 15-Keyword Routing Intelligence
brand, branding, brand strategy, personal brand, rebrand, brand mapping, anchor statement, elevator pitch, unique branding proposition, brand voice, tone of voice, thought leadership, CEO brand / executive brand, brand consistency, signature story / story branding (+ adjacent: brand audit, content pillars, brand-centric culture).

## 7. Two-Sided Split (coaching vs governance)
- **Coaching (founder-facing):** the Brand Mapping Process as a guided arc — define who you are, find your UBP, your energy, your story; decide when to rebrand; choose your thought-leadership flavor. Powered by extraction items 1-11.
- **Governance (agent-facing):** the Brand-Centric Execution Standard — Brand Before You Build, Be/Do/Have copy, tone-drives-design, channel consistency to the hub, measure Customers/Credibility/Cash, the Five Expressions of a brand-centric company. Powered by items 12-20.

## 8. Differentiation vs Adjacent Books
- vs StoryBrand (Miller): StoryBrand = the messaging framework (hero/guide). Brand Mapping = the full brand definition (7 lenses) + platform + CEO/company brand. Pairs well: map first, then clarify the message.
- vs Copywriting Secrets / Copywriters Handbook (Edwards/Bly): those execute conversion copy; Brand Mapping sets the voice/tone/positioning the copy must carry.
- vs 1-Page Marketing Plan (Dib) / Brunson funnels: those govern offer + funnel mechanics; Brand Mapping governs the brand the funnel wears. Hand-off, not overlap.
- Unique angle: the only persona in the marketing pool centered on personal + CEO + company brand congruence and "Brand Before You Build" sequencing.

## 9. Evidence / Credibility Base
Real client cases (Michelle Seiler-Tucker, LaGrande Family Foods, Care 2 Communities / Elizabeth Sheehan, Curls Bartling, Steven the manager, Martha the CIO); cited research (Weber Shandwick/KRC CEO reputation, Burson-Marsteller, BRANDfog social-CEO, Kissmetrics color, Pew, Nielsen Norman 59-second visit); quoted authorities (Tom Peters "brand called You," Jeff Bezos, Christopher Booker). Author credibility: LinkedIn, Twitter, American Express, Roche clients; Fast Company / Fortune coverage.

## 10. Risk / Misapplication Watch
- Don't let "personal brand" tip into self-aggrandizement (Myth 3/4) — keep it service-oriented and authentic.
- Don't apply to acute PR/legal crises (out of lane -> escalate).
- Don't confuse a tone/voice task with offer economics or paid-media (hand off).
- Don't let the persona author offer/funnel business rules — it grounds BRAND, not pricing/funnel mechanics.

## 11. Skill Alignment
- **Skill 35 (social) = PRIMARY:** brand voice, bios, branded backgrounds, content pillars, social consistency.
- **Skill 6 (GHL funnels/pages):** receives APPROVED, on-brand website/opt-in/funnel COPY via the conversion-copywriter seam; Brand Mapping sets the voice, then Skill 6 builds the page.
- **Website + funnel COPY depth (Trevor's "too concise / not robust for brand-building" concern):** Brand Mapping is the direct fix — it forces Be/Do/Have depth, Signature Story, emotional capital, and brand-consistent voice into copy that would otherwise be thin.
- **Skill 38 (conversation playbook):** brand tone informs conversational/chatbot voice.

## 12. Interaction-Mode Signals (for persona-selector detect_interaction_mode)
- **Coaching signals:** "help me define my brand," "who am I as a brand," "should I rebrand," "I want to be a thought leader," founder/owner guidance, "I can't explain what I do."
- **Leadership/governance signals:** "write our website/funnel/social copy," "make our brand consistent," "audit our brand," "govern how the team writes on-brand," dept/role execution.
- **Hybrid:** "build our brand and the assets to express it" (define + execute) -> secondary persona = a copywriting persona (Bly/Edwards/Wiebe) for conversion depth.
