# EXTRACTION NOTES - The Brand Mapping Strategy (Karen Tiber Leland)
**Phase 1 Extraction — 20 structured items across two lenses**
Coaching methodology lens = items 1-11. Agent-governance lens = items 12-20.
All items grounded in the actual book text (Entrepreneur Press, 2016).

---

## COACHING LENS (items 1-11)

### 1. Core thesis
Brand by design, not by default: "If you don't define your brand, someone else will do it for you." A brand (personal, team, or business) is "a multilayered, congruent narrative told across multiple channels — both online and off," not a single statement, logo, or quip.

### 2. The Brand Mapping Process© — 7 Core Elements (central methodology)
1) **Anchor Statement** (elevator pitch / "what do you do?"), built on **Be/Do/Have**; 2) **Unique Branding Proposition (UBP)** ("Why you?"); 3) **Brand Tone & Temperament** (pick ~10 traits from Analytical/Achiever/Agreeable/Animated groups); 4) **Brand Energy** (1-2 of 12 archetypes — "what's the weather you bring?"); 5) **Signature Story** ("how did you get here?"); 6) **Signature Services** (named, specific offers/IP); 7) **Brand Enhancers & Reducers** (brand SWOT). Metaphor: seven optometrist lenses / a New York skyline puzzle assembled piece by piece.

### 3. The Be / Do / Have model
The Anchor Statement must carry three layers: what you DO (activities), what clients HAVE (results/outcomes), and the way of BEING (qualities/why) that makes the difference. Leland's key insight: "the most powerful part of our brand is the 'being' part, yet ironically, that's the aspect we spend the least time articulating."

### 4. Brand Clarity vs Brand Rambling vs Brand Confusion
The brain is "hardwired to find patterns." Brand Rambling (talks too long — lost at "Drosophila") and Brand Confusion (vague — "I help women find their passion and be happier") both make listeners tune out. Goal: a clear Anchor Statement that fits a pattern fast.

### 5. The 12 Brand Energy archetypes
Advocate, Maker, Connector, Motivator, Synthesizer, Fixer, Implementer, Visionary, Interpreter, Storyteller, Facilitator, Mentor. We usually develop 1-2 fully. "Know your brand energy limitations" — staff around your gaps instead of being a yes-man to yourself.

### 6. Signature Story craft + 5 story types
Qualities: Authenticity (no exaggeration/lies), Serendipity (fate/coincidence), Specifics ("show, don't tell"). Five archetypes (after Christopher Booker): Rags to Riches (Cinderella), Beat the Bad (Star Wars), There and Back Again (Wizard of Oz), Eyes on the Prize (Lord of the Rings), Awakening (A Christmas Carol).

### 7. The Three Expressions of a personal brand (Visual / Intellectual / Emotional capital)
A photograph (and a brand) expresses three things: **Visual** (dress, color/design, headshot, branded backgrounds, visual CV), **Intellectual** (brand sound bites, branded bio in 4 versions, social profiles + keywords, content creation: blog/podcast/video/book), **Emotional** (the feeling you want others to experience — joy, security, hope, excitement, glamour, effectiveness, authenticity, toughness).

### 8. The 7 Myths of personal branding
1) It's new (false — Napoleon, Churchill, Chaplin); 2) Your reputation IS your brand (online brand differs); 3) It's all about you (good leaders benefit their people); 4) A personal brand is always good (phone-throwing bank president = strong but destructive brand); 5) Your image is your brand (brand is "authentically discovered, not strategically conceived"); 6) Nice but unnecessary (you already have one — Bezos: "what people say when you're not in the room"); 7) At odds with your business brand (parallel brand doubles exposure — Hsieh/Zappos, Jobs/Apple).

### 9. When is it time to rebrand? (7 triggers)
Message moved on; audience moved on; pre-emptively apologizing for collateral; new niche/audience/opportunity; audience not responding; reputation damaged beyond repair; personal transformation (divorce/illness/death — an "eye of the needle" experience). "Your personal brand is an organic process — not a fixed entity."

### 10. Coaching stance: "It's impossible to see the inside of your own eyelids"
Even brand experts can't brand themselves; founders are too close to their own brand to see it. Coaching/outside perspective is essential — Leland hired a colleague to rebrand herself.

### 11. CEO thought-leadership flavors (Celebrity / Cerebral / Consequential)
Thought leadership "happens because the world says so," not by self-declaration ("first you have to have some thoughts"). Three flavors: **Celebrity** (known for personality — Branson, Robbins, Oprah), **Cerebral** (known for ideas — Gates, Zuckerberg, Lagarde), **Consequential** (known for results — Jobs, Sandberg, Merkel).

## GOVERNANCE LENS (items 12-20)

### 12. "Brand Before You Build" — the foundational execution rule
Building a website before defining the brand "is always a mistake. Your brand is what drives the website design — not the other way around." Order matters: articulate brand → modern marketing website → brand consistency on social. Skipping foundation = the #1 complaint ("spending on PR/marketing, not getting results").

### 13. The Marketing Mastery Pyramid (3 phases, sequential)
Phase 1 **Platform Development** (Brand Design & Strategy); Phase 2 **Brand & Buzz Building** (Expansion & Acceleration — choose 3-5 tactics from PR/content/social/speaking/networking/awards); Phase 3 **Thought/Industry Leadership** (Brand Authority — add something new, be a trusted source, generate high-quality content consistently). Don't rush past Phase 1.

### 14. Beyond the three Ls + website execution standard
"The three Ls" = look, logo, line — only the tip of the iceberg. Website non-negotiables: color/design by brand+psychology (not taste), speedy navigation, contact+social above the fold, active calls to action ("ethical bribe"), SEO/keywords, responsive design, professional writing (clear/concise/accurate). The "disappointed journalist" and "clueless dentist" prove a weak site destroys credibility ("looks like a bit of a hack").

### 15. The 8-row brand decision logic (for agents)
Defined-vs-default branding governs execution: halt build if brand undefined; reframe visual-only "rebrands"; rewrite vague copy to specific Signature Services; add Have+Be to do-only copy; choose color by tone/audience not taste; re-align channel drift to the website hub; replace one-big-hit with ubiquity; right-size promises to capacity; refresh against the Blow Dryer Effect. (See blueprint Section 4A table — 10 rows.)

### 16. Consistency across channels — the website as hub
"Your branded website should serve as the hub of your social media world, and all other sites should take their cue from it. The same logo, colors, design elements, keywords, messages, and brand voice should transfer over." Social inconsistency (different look/tone/conflicting messages) is a brand defect.

### 17. Measure by Customers, Credibility, Cash — over a marathon
Brand work is judged not by short-term conversion ("that's the wrong question") but by compounding change over time in three areas: **Customers** (leads/pipeline/new business), **Credibility** (social proof, authority, thought-leadership, media), **Cash** (measurable financial result). "Branding is a marathon, not a sprint."

### 18. The Five Expressions of a Brand-Centric Company (org governance)
1) **Customer Experience** (3 dimensions: Interpersonal, Product, Process); 2) **Management Commitment** ("how they actually live it" not what they say); 3) **Employee Engagement** (everyone is a brand ambassador, including back office); 4) **Processes, Procedures, Systems & Standards** (the fulcrum — revise what doesn't line up with the brand promise); 5) **Organizational Infrastructure** (roles, reward, recognition, job descriptions). Capacity must match the brand promise (Colleen Rudio: build capacity to your brand non-negotiables, not the reverse).

### 19. Commitment-Based Change vs the Vicious Cycle of Change Management
Brand-centric transformation fails via the Vicious Cycle: plans/promises made → something comes up → people don't communicate → lack of ownership → anger/frustration → re-plan with lower momentum. The fix: Commitment-Based Change (and a Kaizen / continuous-improvement, one-fix-at-a-time approach). The "How Brand-Centric Is Your Business?" 10-question quiz (At the gate / Taking off / Flying) audits alignment.

### 20. The Parallel Brand + C-Suite/CEO Brand Matrix
48% (up to 45-48%) of a company's reputation attaches to its CEO. A **parallel brand** must complement (not conflict with) the business brand, be authentic/sustainable, be obvious internally and externally, and enhance both. **C-Suite/CEO Brand Matrix = 4 domains:** 1) Reputation Management (Claim Your Name — URL + vanity social handles; stay on top of search; be a social CEO; leverage LinkedIn); 2) Establish Thought Leadership; 3) Engage in Content Marketing; 4) Strengthen Personal & Executive Presence (+ network in "meet space" — 55% body language, 38% tone, 7% words). Beware the **Blow Dryer Effect**: yesterday's special becomes today's expected — keep refreshing.
