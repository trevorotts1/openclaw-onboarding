## LENS A - COACHING METHODOLOGY (items 1-11)

### 1. The Central Promise: Turn Expertise Into a Profitable, Premium Coaching Business
The whole book is "written as a playbook" and "practical." Premise: "Your experience and the knowledge you've accumulated are more valuable than you think. They can be turned into a coaching business, but only if you follow the steps." Target outcome: "a strong, six-figure coaching business in under a year and then double the size of that business within the following eighteen months." The eight steps work for "a business coach, a life coach, a financial advisor, a gardening consultant, a parenting coach, a real estate agent, or in any other field."

### 2. The Eight Steps (the master sequence)
(1) Create a Menu of Products You Can Sell. (2) Create and Manage a Potential Client List (CRM). (3) Get Your Website (Sales Pitch) Right. (4) Learn to Write Great Emails That Close the Deal. (5) Map Out Your Client Journey via a Marketing and Product Ladder. (6) Establish Realistic Goals and Accomplish Them. (7) Build or Join a Coaching Community. (8) Master the Soft Skills of Coaching. Critical ordering insight: build PRODUCTS FIRST, not the client list — "once you have created a menu of products, a list of potential clients will naturally fall into place."

### 3. The Menu of Products (Step One) — sell specifics, not "a patch of fog"
"The fastest way to bring in cash is to have a menu of products you're able to sell that offer clear value and are sold at a stated price." Vague retainer coaching is the enemy: "Offering 'business advice' on a retainer is akin to a restaurant that advertises 'food items.'" "Nobody wants to pull out their debit card and buy a patch of fog." Three product tiers: (a) **Paid lead-generating products** — named, fixed-price, under 90 min, $0-$495, solve one pressing problem (e.g. "The One-Page Hiring Plan," "The Marketing and Messaging Review," "The Tax Exposure Checklist"). (b) **Flagship product** — the "flagship small group" / six-month small group with a specific deliverable on a precise timeline (e.g. "increase revenue by 25 percent over six months"), $5,000/seat. (c) **Advanced products** — One-on-One Coaching Retainer ($12k), Guiding Principles Workshop ($5-10k), Marketing/Sales Workshops ($5-10k), Elite Mastermind.

### 4. Pricing: the 10 Percent Rule + value-perception
"The amount of money you can charge... depends on one thing: how big of a return your client will receive." The 10 Percent Rule: "the price I charge for my coaching will be under 10 percent of the return I conservatively expect my client to get." Miller offers a full refund if the client doesn't get a 10x return — but only takes qualified clients who can realistically get the return. "As a small business coach, you sell money." Disclose prices on the website ("starting at $5k"). "Human beings associate value with price" — the doubled-speaking-honorarium story: raising price RAISED demand. "Never devalue yourself or your products."

### 5. The Potential Client List + personal CRM (Step Two)
"You tell people you are a coach. Over and over." Career pivots "take about three years to metabolize one's identity." Build a database in a low-cost CRM (the book partners with Keap). Math: "if only 10 percent of the people on your list make a purchase... you only need to collect two hundred names." Segment the list by the client's problem; "give away the why but sell the how." Community-building tools: launch email ("announcement + problems you solve + testimony + CTA + referral request"), monthly informal breakfast, keynotes, webinars, free assessment, referrals.

### 6. The Six Problems Small Business Owners Have (the diagnostic spine)
The book's recurring six-problem taxonomy that drives products, segments, qualifying questions, and emails: **Leadership** ("making it up as we go"), **Marketing & Messaging** ("not sure how to talk about what we offer"), **Sales** ("don't know how to close"), **Products/Product Optimization** ("shrinking margins"), **Operations/Management** ("frustrating and unproductive"), **Cash Flow** ("always running out of cash"). Each problem maps to a product, a segment, a qualifying question, and a nurture sequence.

### 7. The Website as a Sales Pitch (Step Three) — the 7-section wireframe
"The job of your website is to deliver a sales pitch." Wireframe text first ("a text-only rough draft... the text is the hardest and most important part"). Seven nonnegotiable sections in order: **(1) Header** (what you offer / how it makes their life better / what to do next — CTA "Schedule a discovery call"); **(2) The Stakes** ("the problem is the hook"); **(3) The Menu of Services**; **(4) Proof of Value** (statistics + testimonials — "you want your future clients to 'see themselves'"); **(5) The Plan** (a 3-step plan — "a bridge" — never more than three steps); **(6) The Explanatory Paragraph** (long-form sales pitch using The Customer Is the Hero framework); **(7) Your Lead Generator** (+ exit-intent pop-up).

### 8. The Three Relationship Phases: Curiosity → Enlightenment → Commitment
"Coaching relationships are just like any other relationship in that they move through three phases. The first phase is curiosity, the second is enlightenment, and the third is commitment." "You cannot skip a phase... If you jump to a request for a commitment too soon, your potential client will walk away as fast as somebody would walk away if you asked them to marry you on the first date." Each website section / email = another "date." Curiosity = survival sound-bites; Enlightenment = "Will this work for me?"; Commitment = invest money/time.

### 9. Email Nurture + The Customer Is the Hero Sales Framework (Step Four)
"What is the formula for earning trust? It's this: value over time." Clients "hire a coach because they entered a window in which their problems... triggered them" — so stay in touch 6-12-52 weeks to overlap that window. **The Customer Is the Hero Sales Framework (6 steps):** (1) Start with the problem; (2) Position your product as the solution; (3) Build a bridge (the 3-step plan); (4) Paint the negative stakes; (5) Paint the positive stakes; (6) Call the customer to action. Built on the "story loop" (open a loop, the audience pays attention until you close it). Sequence: ~7 sales emails, then ongoing nurture emails. Five nurture types: Problem/Solution, "Here's How They Did It" (case study), Step-by-Step, Introduction to an Expert, Sales. "If you don't keep a hook in the water, you will never catch a fish" (the Montana fly-fishing principle).

### 10. The Marketing & Product Ladder + the 100/10/1 hierarchy (Step Five)
Map the client journey on one ladder. **Curiosity tools** (one-liner, business card, website, lead generators, breakfasts, webinars, presentations); **Enlightenment tools** (website, lead generators, keynotes, assessments, one-off coaching, one-day workshops); **Commitment products** (entry community group, elite masterminds, one-on-one, private workshops, fractional COO/CRO/CMO). The hierarchy: "about 10 percent of your paying clients will pay you ten times more" — 1,000 clients at $100 → ~100 at $1,000 → ~10 at $10,000. "You are the largest billboard for your business." Need a polished **one-liner** (elevator pitch).

### 11. The Soft Skills of Coaching (Step Eight) — the Guide and the 50/40/10 split
"When people sign up for your coaching, they aren't trusting your playbooks or frameworks; they're trusting you." Five characteristics of great coaches: **(1) They play the Guide** — empathetic ("Your pain is my pain") + competent (an "empathetic authority figure"); **(2) They don't rush the process** (patience + persistence); **(3) They lead clients on a journey of self-discovery** — "we don't teach as much as we help people realize certain truths for themselves" via questions; **(4) They create a safe, trusting, non-judgmental environment** — the work split is "Listening/understanding/verifying: 50 percent. Affirming the client is not alone: 40 percent. Offering advice/frameworks: 10 percent"; **(5) They affirm the transformation of their clients.** Plus a personal "Top Ten Rules of Coaching."

## LENS B - AGENT-GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORKS (items 12-20)

### 12. Governance Object: persona governs how AI agents build a coaching/expertise business's funnel and copy
The deployable governance surface is the funnel value chain: product menu → CRM/list → website (sales pitch) → lead generators → email sequences → marketing/product ladder → goals. Every artifact an agent produces in Sales/Marketing/Web-Development/Copy departments for a coach/consultant/expert client must conform to Miller's named frameworks. The governance standard is "specific named product at a stated price," "7-section website wireframe," "Customer Is the Hero 6-step email," and "Curiosity→Enlightenment→Commitment ladder."

### 13. Execution Standard: never ship "a patch of fog"
Hard execution rule set drawn from the text: (a) Every offer must be a NAMED product with a STATED price and a clear deliverable — never "coaching" or "business advice" generically. (b) Every page/email must LEAD WITH THE CLIENT'S PROBLEM ("the problem is the hook"). (c) Give away the WHY, sell the HOW. (d) Every section/email is "one more date" — never request commitment before curiosity and enlightenment are earned. (e) Every sales asset ends with ONE clear call to action. (f) Wireframe (words) before design. (g) Disclose price to qualify and to signal value.

### 14. Decision Logic: problem → product → segment → sequence
The book gives an explicit routing table that becomes an agent decision-logic table. Each of the six problems maps to: a qualifying question, a product, a CRM segment tag, and a nurture sequence. E.g. Operations problem → "Does every member of your team have a clear job description?" → fractional COO product → segment "management" → 5-6 management-tip emails + sales email. Marketing problem → "Is your marketing working or are you struggling to get the word out?" → Marketing Strategy Workshop → segment "marketing" → message-clarity emails.

### 15. Quality Bar: the website/email passes only if it has all required parts
Definition-of-done is structural and checkable. Website DoD: all 7 sections present and IN ORDER (Header w/ 3 elements + CTA, Stakes, Menu of Services, Proof of Value, 3-step Plan, Explanatory Paragraph, Lead Generator + exit-intent). Email DoD: all 6 Customer-Is-the-Hero steps present (Problem, Solution, Bridge/Plan, Negative Stakes, Positive Stakes, CTA). Product DoD: name + fixed price + specific deliverable + the problem it solves + the return it produces.

### 16. Failure Patterns (what the persona will NOT allow)
Named anti-patterns from the text: "patch of fog" vague offer; retainer-only / "food items" menu; no price disclosed (or discounting out of fear, which "reduces the perceived value"); skipping a relationship phase (asking for commitment on "the first date"); giving away the HOW for free; a plan with more than three steps ("might intimidate"); a page/email with no stakes ("if there are no stakes, there is no story") or no CTA; a website that exists but never gets built out of "pride and fear"; going silent on leads ("if you go silent on them, they will forget your offer").

### 17. Amateur-to-Expert Gap (governance dimension)
The book repeatedly contrasts amateur vs. expert coaches: amateurs offer vague retainers / experts sell named premium products; amateurs hide behind word-of-mouth / experts build a website + CRM + lead generators; amateurs give marching orders / experts ask questions and let clients realize principles; amateurs discount from fear / experts price at <10% of return and disclose it; amateurs make the story about themselves / experts make the customer the hero and play the guide; amateurs talk features / experts lead with the client's problem.

### 18. Department / Role Application Map
Maps cleanly onto BlackCEO's workforce: **Marketing** (website wireframe, lead generators, brand/one-liner, nurture content), **Sales** (qualifying questions, product menu, offer construction, closing emails, discovery calls), **Web Development / Funnel** (the 7-section landing page + opt-in/lead-gen pages — Skill 6), **CRM / Convert-and-Flow** (list segmentation, automated nurture sequences, the 12-email campaign — Skill 44), **Copywriting** (page copy, email copy, explanatory paragraphs), **Operations/Coaching delivery** (small-group curriculum, the 5-meeting management system referenced in nurture content), **Finance** (the cash-flow / 5-checking-accounts content the coach teaches).

### 19. Scaling-to-Seven-Figures Governance (org design)
The book's scaling playbook is a hiring plan with 5+ job descriptions and decision gates: Solo Coach → +Virtual Assistant → +Second Coach → +Event Coordinator → +Content Manager → +Operations Director → +Revenue Director. The three founder personalities: **Artist** (product/brand), **Operator** (people/process), **Entrepreneur** (revenue/growth). Rule: "only make the next hire when the previous hire has settled in and is producing profitable results." Then "build and refine and then refine again" via a weekly review-question ritual.

### 20. Routing Intelligence (~15 keywords)
High-signal task keywords that should route a coach/consultant/expert client's task to this persona on the leadership side: "coaching business," "coaching products / menu of services," "lead generator / lead magnet," "discovery call," "nurture sequence / email campaign," "value ladder / product ladder," "small group / mastermind," "flagship offer," "landing page / sales page," "opt-in page," "course/offer pricing," "client pipeline / CRM," "funnel for a coach," "webinar / keynote to sell," "productize my expertise." On the coaching side, route on: "I want to start coaching," "I undercharge," "how do I price my offer," "build my coaching business," "I'm afraid to sell myself."
