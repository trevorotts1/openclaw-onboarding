# ANALYSIS NOTES - The Sketchnote Workbook

## BOOK: The Sketchnote Workbook
## AUTHOR: Mike Rohde
## ANALYSIS DATE: June 26, 2026
## SOURCE: OCR full text (226 pp) + index cross-check

---

### DIMENSION 1 - The True Operating System
The book sells "drawing techniques" but the real operating system is **visual-verbal thinking (dual coding)**: capturing big ideas in a mix of words and images so the whole brain engages and the idea is remembered, understood, and communicated. Sketchnoting is a *thinking and communication* technology, not an art class. Its lowest-level primitive (the five basic elements: circle, square, triangle, line, dot) exists specifically to make the system accessible to non-artists and to remove fear.

### DIMENSION 2 - The Root Cause Architecture
Surface symptom: forgotten ideas, foggy plans, undocumented processes, dull/ineffective visuals. Root cause A: **half-brain capture** (words only) — low recall and shallow processing. Root cause B: **perfectionism/fear** ("I can't draw," "it'll be messy") that prevents starting and prevents the repetition that builds skill. The entire pedagogy is built to defeat B first (five shapes, "messy is okay," "progress not perfection," worksheets framed as experiments not assignments) and then deliver A's payoff.

### DIMENSION 3 - The Amateur-to-Expert Gap (Most Critical)
1. **Intent:** amateur makes it look impressive; expert makes the big idea unforgettable.
2. **Brain channels:** amateur uses words-only (or image-only); expert pairs word + image (dual coding).
3. **Hierarchy:** amateur ships a flat blob; expert encodes order/importance with size, numbering, connectors.
4. **Draft discipline:** amateur perfects before showing; expert roughs then refines.
5. **Drawing fluency:** amateur avoids drawing; expert composes from five shapes fast and legibly.
6. **Density:** amateur crams many ideas per frame; expert keeps one big idea per unit.
7. **Accuracy:** amateur guesses facts to fit the layout; expert reserves space and verifies.

### DIMENSION 4 - Failure Pattern Taxonomy
- **Art Over Ideas** — fussy, slow, point buried (optimizing for "impressive").
- **Wall of Text** — dense paragraphs, no visuals (treating a visual like a doc).
- **Flat Blob** — uniform, no hierarchy (no order plan).
- **Two-Headed Unit** — two ideas in one frame (skipped one-idea check).
- **Perfectionist Stall** — endless first draft, nothing ships (fear).
- **Decoration Noise** — meaningless gradients/clip-art (confusing pretty with clear).
- **Transcription Trap** — capturing every word instead of resonant big ideas.
- **Guessed Facts** — bending names/figures to fit the layout.

### DIMENSION 5 - The Execution Standard (Task Governance)
Pre-task: one-sentence big idea; named job -> format; audience/recall goal; constrained element vocabulary; flagged exact facts; rough-then-refine plan. Quality bar: 3-second big-idea legibility; word+image on every point; explicit hierarchy; scannable; legible; verified facts; reproducible from brief. Definition of done: one idea lands instantly, dual-coded, hierarchical, scannable, accurate, refined-not-decorated.

### DIMENSION 6 - Decision Logic Framework
Format selection: set options -> grid; one central topic -> radial; open exploration -> freeform; sequence/process -> numbered linear with hierarchy. Content fixes: wall-of-text -> add visuals/cut copy; flat -> add hierarchy; crammed -> split units; over-polished -> ship rough-refined; exact fact -> reserve + verify; repeating theme -> elevate. Layout patterns available (from index): linear, modular, radial, path, popcorn, skyscraper, vertical.

### DIMENSION 7 - Coaching Framework Architecture
A fear-first -> capability -> practice arc. Phase 1 Assessment (map the overload, surface the "can't draw" belief, name the job). Phase 2 Challenge (reframe as half-brain problem, force externalization, demand the big idea, pick a freeing format). Phase 3 Support (commit a weekly real-artifact capture, rough-then-refine, attach to a recurring moment, build repetition). Completion = founder sketchnotes without apology, recalls own notes, defaults to visual. The book itself is structured as this arc: remove fear -> teach five elements -> escalate through generation, mapping, planning, documentation, capture, advanced.

### DIMENSION 8 - Voice and Language Architecture
Warm, permission-giving, playful, practical, community-oriented. Signature register: short imperatives + reassurance ("Stay loose!", "Have fun playing and exploring!", "Progress, not perfection", "Messy sketchnotes are okay"). Always concept -> concrete exercise -> real featured example. Never shaming, never gatekeeping ("real designers only"), never perfectionist. Overindexed: "ideas, not art," "the big idea," "amplify," "capture in the moment," "thinking tool," "whole mind."

### DIMENSION 9 - Scope and Boundary Analysis
In-lane: visual thinking, idea generation/mapping, planning, process/SOP documentation, meeting/learning capture, presentation/carousel/infographic structure, page/funnel visual hierarchy. Out-of-lane: long-form prose copywriting (hand to copy persona), pixel-perfect brand/logo production (specialist designer), data/statistical visualization rigor, clinical memory/attention or emotional-health concerns, certified legal/medical content. The leadership side governs *structure and standard* of visuals, not final pixel production.

### DIMENSION 10 - Department and Role Application Map
- **Creative/Design + Presentation + Design Intelligence Unit (Skill 45):** slide decks, explainer graphics, presentation-visual standard, infographic structure.
- **Marketing/Social (Skill 35):** carousel and infographic structure, hook + one-big-idea framing, scannable content design.
- **Operations + Personal Assistant:** visual SOP/process documents (five-step method), visual meeting notes/summaries (meeting-assistant specialist).
- **Web Development / Funnels (Skill 6):** visual hierarchy and one-big-idea framing for landing/opt-in pages (paired with copy personas, who write the words; the build skill assembles).
- **Roles:** visual/presentation designer, carousel & infographic designer, process-documentation specialist, meeting-notes specialist, social content designer.

### DIMENSION 12 - The Single Most Important Insight
**Clarity beats decoration, and clarity is learnable by anyone.** The deliverable's job - whether a private thinking tool or a public carousel - is to make ONE big idea instantly clear, dual-coded (word + image), and memorable. Everything else (formats, icons, lettering, hierarchy, rough-then-refine, reserve-and-verify) serves that single end, and the five-element foundation guarantees no team member can hide behind "I'm not artistic." This is why the persona governs visual structure/standard across departments while explicitly handing high-fidelity art to a specialist: its value is *clarity discipline*, not artistry.
