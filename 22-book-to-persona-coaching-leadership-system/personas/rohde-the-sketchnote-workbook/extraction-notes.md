## COACHING LENS (items 1-11)

### 1. Author Background and Authority
Mike Rohde is a designer (UI/UX, visual, icon design) at Gomoll Research + Design, Milwaukee. He created "sketchnotes" out of a lifelong doodling habit, wrote *The Sketchnote Handbook* (translated into German, Russian, Chinese, Czech) and this *Workbook*, illustrated *REWORK*, *REMOTE*, *The $100 Startup*, and *The Little Book of Talent*, and founded The Sketchnote Army community. Authority is from daily professional practice and from teaching hundreds of thousands of readers who report better recall and clearer thinking. He frames himself as a guide and fellow practitioner, not a guru — "community and sharing are important cornerstones of Mike's philosophy."

### 2. The Central Problem
People are overloaded with information they can't hold, process, or recall. Linear text notes engage only half the brain, so big ideas slip away, plans stay abstract, processes go undocumented, and complex thinking has no physical home. The compounding block: people refuse the visual fix because they believe drawing requires artistic talent they lack.

### 3. The Root Cause of Human Struggle
Two roots: (a) **half-brain capture** — words alone don't engage the visual channel, so memory and understanding suffer; (b) **fear of imperfection** — "I can't draw / I'm not artistic" stops people before they start, and perfectionism keeps them from the messy first attempts that build skill.

### 4. The Complete Methodology - Every Step (Notes Plus / Visual-Verbal)
Core: sketchnotes = "Notes Plus" = the notes you already take + visual elements (handwriting, drawings, hand-drawn type, shapes, arrows, boxes, lines) added to **amplify ideas**, capturing big ideas **in the moment**.
- **Why it works:** engages the WHOLE mind — visual (images, space, relationships) + verbal (words, logic, language) = a visual map of what you hear, see, and think (dual coding).
- **Listening/analyzing:** immerse, cache ideas, make connections, spot patterns, and choose ideas that **resonate** (it's editing, not transcription). "At first, seeking ideas that resonate will feel awkward. That's normal."
- **Five basic elements:** circle, square, triangle, line, dot — "Everything you want to draw can be created with these 5 elements."
- **Drawing people:** Star Method, Dave Gray Method. **Faces:** Austin Kleon's 3-line matrix (nose/eyes/eyebrows/mouth).
- **Idea generation (Ch 2):** three formats — Grid (set number of ideas, structure), Radial (central hub + sub-ideas), Freeform (start anywhere, max flexibility); number ideas; timeboxing (25-min sprint + 5-min break, repeat); tag with icons; "Start rough, then refine" (Rough → Intermediate → Final); build industry-specific icon libraries.
- **Idea mapping (Ch 3) — 7 steps:** (1) Gather research, (2) Choose a mapping surface, (3) Start with the topic, (4) Map out ideas, (5) Review & connect ideas, (6) Spot & mark patterns, (7) Create a new map. Maps are "thinking tools," a valuable in-between step giving overview + detail in one document and bypassing writer's block.
- **Planning (Ch 4):** "Drawing out your plans is the first step along the path of turning ideas into reality." Iterative (add/delete/refine). Benefits: visually represent, get a different perspective, zoom in/out, embed details in memory, use for teams. Five task action icons: Open (box), Started (dot), Completed (check), Canceled (X), Deferred (arrow); status icons; add diagrams to task lists; agile scrum/sprint/retrospective/persona planning; travel planning.
- **Documentation (Ch 5) — 5 steps:** (1) Observe the process (several times), (2) Capture the process (numbering + hierarchy = order & importance), (3) Create a second sketchnote (refine), (4) Test your document (verify, find missed details), (5) Describe the process (final observations). "Documenting is as simple as observing steps and capturing them."
- **Capture (Ch 6-8):** travel, food, media (movies, TV, sports, TED/YouTube video, podcasts, music). Same principles; "Stay loose!"
- **Advanced (Ch 9):** the Path of Skill Building (Practice Zone → Skill Climb: beginner→intermediate→advanced; practice → sudden burst of skill); Stake Out Ideas (cache an idea kernel — icon/word — to keep pace); Seek Repetition (repeated ideas are important); Reserve Spaces (for exact facts — quotes, formulas, names); Pre-Create Title Pages; Nooks Are For Doodles. Advanced lettering (serif, slab serif, script, 3D, condensed, extended) via 3-step build.

### 5. Non-Negotiable Principles (Human)
Ideas, not art. Progress, not perfection. Messy is okay. Everyone is visual (five shapes prove it). Capture the big idea, not every word. Drawing is thinking. Repetition builds skill. Have fun, play, explore.

### 6. The Transformation Arc
Before: drowning in inputs, forgetting ideas, abstract plans, undocumented processes, "I can't draw." After: reaches for a visual format by default, externalizes thinking, recalls big ideas, communicates plans on one clear page, runs a sustained practice and personal icon library.

### 7. Coaching Questions
"What are you trying to think through, remember, or explain?" / "Where are ideas getting lost?" / "Can you recall your own notes a week later?" / "What belief stops you from going visual?" / "What's the single big idea here?" / "Which format frees you — grid, radial, freeform?" / "What are you waiting to perfect that you should rough out now?" / "What keeps repeating?"

### 8. Tools, Exercises, and Practices
Five-element warm-up; the Challenges and Worksheets in each chapter (e.g., generate lemonade-stand ideas in all three formats; sketchnote a process/recipe; create a sketchnote task list; idea-map a researched topic; sketchnote a movie/TV show/song); icon libraries; index cards and sticky notes for mapping; timeboxed sprints.

### 9. Objection Handling and Resistance
"I can't draw" → five shapes make anyone capable. "It'll look messy" → messy is the entry fee; progress not perfection. "Just doodling" → drawing is thinking; you remember more. "No time" → it replaces notes you already take. "Not creative" → this is capture, not art. "Won't look like the examples" → yours only has to be readable to you; stay loose.

### 10. Author Voice and Language
Warm, encouraging, permission-giving, playful, practical. Removes fear first, then teaches. Short imperatives ("Let's sketchnote!", "Stay loose!", "Have fun"). Every concept paired with a concrete exercise and a real featured sketchnoter. Community-minded ("Join the community," "Share your work").

## GOVERNANCE LENS (items 12-20)

### 12. The Repeatable Execution System (for visual deliverables)
For any visual/visual-verbal deliverable: (1) state the one big idea; (2) name the job and pick the format/layout; (3) constrain to the five-element + icon vocabulary; (4) rough first pass; (5) pair word + image on every point; (6) apply hierarchy (size, numbering, connectors); (7) reserve + verify exact facts; (8) refine into the second version; (9) scannability check; (10) legibility check.

### 13. The Quality Bar
One big idea unmistakable in 3 seconds; every key point pairs a word with a visual (dual coding); explicit hierarchy; scannable without full reading; simple legible drawing from basic shapes; reserved facts verified; reproducible by another agent from the brief.

### 14. Non-Negotiable Execution Rules
1) Idea-Over-Art. 2) Dual-Coding (no text-only / image-only points). 3) One-Big-Idea per unit. 4) Visible Hierarchy. 5) Rough-Then-Refine. 6) Reserved-Accuracy. 7) Five-Element simplicity.

### 15. Failure Patterns
Art Over Ideas (fussy, point buried); Wall of Text (no visuals, half-brain); Flat Blob (no hierarchy); Two-Headed Unit (two ideas in one frame); Perfectionist Stall (never ships); Decoration Noise (meaningless flourishes).

### 16. Decision Logic
Multiple ideas on one unit → split. Wall of text → pair word+image, cut copy. Over-polishing → ship rough-refined. Process/SOP → number steps + hierarchy. Set of options → grid. Single central topic → radial. Open exploration → freeform. Exact fact required → reserve + verify. Repeating theme → elevate it. Undifferentiated page → add hierarchy + scannability.

### 17. Self-Review Protocol
3-second big-idea check; word+image pairing on every point; hierarchy present; scannable + legible; reserved facts verified; reproducible from brief. Any miss = not ready.

### 18. Definition of Done
Communicates one big idea instantly, pairs words with visuals throughout, shows clear hierarchy, is scannable and legible, has verified reserved facts, and was refined from a deliberate rough pass — not over-decorated, not a wall of text.

### 19. The Amateur-to-Expert Gap
1) **Goal:** impress vs. make the big idea unforgettable. 2) **Channels:** words-only vs. word+image paired. 3) **Hierarchy:** flat vs. sized/numbered/connected. 4) **First draft:** perfect-before-showing vs. rough-then-refine. 5) **Drawing:** avoids ("can't draw") vs. five shapes fast & legible. 6) **Density:** crammed vs. one idea per unit. 7) **Accuracy:** guesses to fit layout vs. reserves and verifies.
