## A — Headline, Hook & Subject-Line Formula Bank

The governing law of every formula below (Ch.3, "3 Cardinal Rules"): **vague claims kill opt-ins; specific + curious claims win.** A winning hook creates a *problem* and a *curiosity gap* in the same breath, so the reader thinks *"that doesn't make sense — I have to know more!"*

### A1 — The Curiosity-Gap "Implausible Detail" headline
- **When to use:** Opt-in / squeeze-page headline and the lead-magnet title itself; the single highest-leverage hook in the book.
- **Pattern:** `How I [achieved specific desirable result + exact number] while [doing the implausible / forbidden thing the audience assumes is impossible]`
- **Worked example:** "How I lost 32 pounds while eating hot dogs, hamburgers, and ice cream." / "Learn the truths that helped me lose 65 pounds without giving up pizza and ice cream." (Beyond Diet)
- **Source:** Ch.3 / Swipe-LP

### A2 — The "(Number) + (Desirable Thing)" list-promise
- **When to use:** Lead-magnet titles, blog/opt-in headlines, ad headlines — the most repeated formula across the 114 examples.
- **Pattern:** `[Specific odd number] [proven/sensational/winning] [things] that [deliver the result] [for your market]`
- **Worked example:** "72 proven headline formulas" (DigitalMarketer); "44 Sensational Salads" (Beyond Diet); "7 Monstrous Ways to Improve Your AdWords Performance" (KlientBoost); "9 Ways You Can Make a Very Good Living as a Writer!" (Barefoot Writer).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A3 — The "Curiosity Object" container (Black Book / Bible / Playbook / Cookbook / Toolbox)
- **When to use:** Naming a lead magnet so the *container* itself is a curiosity hook; pairs with a padlocked/secret-object image.
- **Pattern:** `The [Adjective] [Black Book | Bible | Playbook | Cookbook | Toolbox | Blueprint] of [Secret/Desire] for [Market]`
- **Worked example:** "The Marketing Secrets Blackbook" (padlocked-book image; opted in at 55.63%); "The Little Black Book of Billionaire Secrets" (Forbes); "SEO Keyword Bible for Real Estate Investors" (Carrot); "The Funnel Hacker's Cookbook."
- **Source:** Ch.2 / Swipe-LP

### A4 — The "FREE [thing]" low-friction hook
- **When to use:** Top-of-funnel opt-in where friction must be near zero; the word FREE does the heavy lifting.
- **Pattern:** `Free [tangible asset: download/picks/inspiration/evaluation] [+ optional dollar value] for [market]`
- **Worked example:** "Get Your Free Facebook Ads Evaluation ($500 value)" (StubGroup); "Free Audiobook Download of High Performance Habits!" (Brendon Burchard); "Free Facebook / Twitter / Instagram Ads Inspiration for You!" (AdEspresso).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A5 — The Specific-Number proof/income hook
- **When to use:** Make-money / finance / results offers where a precise number out-pulls a round one.
- **Pattern:** `[Verb: Discover/Get/Make] [exact, oddly specific number or %] [result] [in oddly specific timeframe]`
- **Worked example:** "30 Days to $10K" (Maximum Leverage); "FREE! The 'Forgotten' Trading System That Is Right 89.1% of the Time" (Motley Fool); "How to Get up to 37 Checks per Month" (Real Freedom).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A6 — The WARNING / pattern-interrupt hook
- **When to use:** A jaded, skeptical, or over-marketed-to audience; the warning frame flips skepticism into intrigue.
- **Pattern:** `WARNING: The [strategy/secret] you're about to discover could [produce a result so big] — [use at your own risk / not for everyone]`
- **Worked example:** "WARNING: The Strategy You're About to Discover Could Produce Life-Changing Results. Use at Your Own Risk…" (Frank Kern, warning-label art).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A7 — The "Dark Side / Loophole" hook
- **When to use:** Positioning a magnet as forbidden insider knowledge a system doesn't want you to have.
- **Pattern:** `The '[Dark Side | Forgotten | Underground]' of [familiar thing] — the [loophole] that [produced this exact, verifiable outcome]`
- **Worked example:** "The 'Dark Side' of DotComSecrets" (crossbones art) — the "search engine loophole that put $7,231.63 in our pockets in under 20 hours."
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A8 — The "How To Get Anything You Want" intrigue opener
- **When to use:** Broad-appeal personal-brand opt-ins where the promise is large but softened by a wink.
- **Pattern:** `How To [Get/Do the maximal thing] ([self-aware qualifier in parentheses])`
- **Worked example:** "How To Get Anything You Want (Well, Just About Anything)" (Marie Forleo); "Want Random People on the Internet to Buy Stuff from You?" (Bryan Harris / VideoFruit).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A9 — The Social-Proof "Join N+" sub-headline
- **When to use:** The line directly under the hook; converts curiosity into a herd-safe decision.
- **Pattern:** `Join [big, specific member count]+ [identity label] who [get this recurring benefit]`
- **Worked example:** "Join 100,000+ members who receive expert previews and predictions every day" (Betting Gods); "Join our community of 250,000+ aspiring writers" (Barefoot Writer).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A10 — The Authority-by-Association sub-headline
- **When to use:** B2B / pro audiences where *who else reads this* is the proof.
- **Pattern:** `Join [count]+ [people] (including folks from [Brand-name brands: Google, Apple, Harvard])`
- **Worked example:** "Join 4,600+ people (including folks from Google, Apple, and Harvard)" (Jimmy Daly, Swipe File Newsletter).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A11 — The Secret-of-the-Elite hook
- **When to use:** Premium / aspirational magnets; borrows status from a rare, named source.
- **Pattern:** `This '[sacred/secret] [tool/system]' was taught to me by [a rare, high-status source]. This one [thing] has been responsible for [Xing my result].`
- **Worked example:** "This 'sacred' Sunday Planning System was taught to me by a 94-year-old multi-billionaire. This one tool has been responsible for 5Xing my impact, influence, and income." (Brendon Burchard).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A12 — The "Copy and Paste these [N] proven…" done-for-you hook
- **When to use:** When the magnet's value is *zero effort* — templates, swipes, scripts the prospect can deploy instantly.
- **Pattern:** `Copy and paste these [N] proven [assets] to [get result] [on-demand / without skill]`
- **Worked example:** "Copy and paste these 8 proven Facebook ad campaigns to create low-cost, high-converting ads on-demand" (DigitalMarketer, FB Ad Template Library).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A13 — The "even if…" objection-crush hook
- **When to use:** Audiences whose #1 buying objection is *"this won't work for someone like me."*
- **Pattern:** `[Get the result] — even if [you have the disqualifying trait the reader fears]`
- **Worked example:** "Create and scale profitable digital ad campaigns — even if you've never wrote an ad before!" (Jason Hornung); "…even if you have zero expertise."
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A14 — The Time-Box promise hook
- **When to use:** Make the transformation feel near and finite; pairs well with A1 and A5.
- **Pattern:** `[Build/Get the desirable result] in [short, specific timeframe] or less`
- **Worked example:** "Architect a 5-figure recurring income business in 30 days or less" (Luke Jaten); "Get your first 1,000 fans on Facebook & Instagram for free in 7 days" (Brian Moran).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A15 — The Contrarian Command hook
- **When to use:** Pattern-interrupt for a tired market; tells them to STOP doing the obvious thing.
- **Pattern:** `Stop [the conventional action]. Stop [the second conventional action]. Start [the contrarian new action]!`
- **Worked example:** "Stop advertising. Stop marketing. Start putting on a show!" (Darren Hardy, "You Need a Show!!!").
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A16 — The Local-Service CTA-as-hook
- **When to use:** Local/service/franchise opt-ins where the call to action IS the headline.
- **Pattern:** `[Imperative service verb] [Now/Today] for [Immediate benefit]` + a secondary online option
- **Worked example:** "Call Now for Immediate Service" + "Schedule Service Online" (Roto-Rooter); "Start a Tool Franchise Today" (Matco Tools).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A17 — The Subscription / blog opt-in hook (no product, pure list-build)
- **When to use:** Content businesses building a list with the content itself as the magnet.
- **Pattern:** `Get [our best content / the top N] free via [channel]`
- **Worked example:** "Get Our Content Free via Email" (Copyblogger); "The Moz Top 10"; "Get Access to 85% of My Hottest Business Hacks" (Noah Kagan / OkDork).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A18 — The Passive / Effortless-result hook
- **When to use:** Income/lifestyle offers selling freedom from effort (use carefully + truthfully).
- **Pattern:** `Earn [exact amount] while [you sleep / do nothing], [without the dreaded task]`
- **Worked example:** "Earn upwards of $4,954.55 while you sleep at night, and recruit without ever having to pick up the phone!" (Daegan Smith).
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

### A19 — The Big-Number Transformation story-hook
- **When to use:** Authority/finance magnets where one staggering before→after carries the offer.
- **Pattern:** `How to turn [small start amount] into [enormous outcome] in [timeframe]`
- **Worked example:** "How to turn $20k into $26 million in 12 years or $1.2 billion in 30 years" (Forbes); "$100 million in 23 months" (Vince James).
- **Source:** Ch.2 / Swipe-LP

### A20 — The "Same Product, Many Titles" multi-hook technique
- **When to use:** When one magnet must attract several segments — re-title/re-image the *identical* content.
- **Pattern:** `[Identical asset] → Title A "[Curiosity-Object name]" for Segment 1 → Title B "[Action/Speed name]" for Segment 2`
- **Worked example:** "Marketing Secrets Blackbook" ≈ "Marketing Secrets Binge Guide" — same content, different title/image to attract different people.
- **Source:** Ch.2

## B — Funnel & Page Recipes (page-by-page)

The book teaches ONE master funnel and its five constituent pages; every one of the 114 examples is this skeleton. Below is the full recipe set: the master flow, each of the five pages, and the high-value page *variants* the swipe sections add.

### B0 — MASTER RECIPE: The 5-Part Lead Funnel (the whole iceberg)
- **When to use:** Any list-building / lead-generation build. This is the spine all other recipes hang on.
- **Pattern (block order + job of each):**
  1. `LEAD MAGNET` — *job: Attract leads / build value.* Free asset traded for an email.
  2. `LANDING PAGE` — *job: Exchange value / build the email list.* Captures name + email.
  3. `THANK YOU PAGE` — *job: Fulfill the promise / build trust.* Delivers magnet + first push.
  4. `SALES PAGE` — *job: Offer a product / recoup ad cost / build the customer list.* Self-liquidating offer.
  5. `FOLLOW-UP FUNNEL` — *job: Communicate with the list / build the relationship.* Multi-channel message sequence.
- **Worked example:** Eben Pagan / Double Your Dating — lead magnet "The Secrets Most Men Will Never Know About Women" (centerpiece: the Kiss Test) → squeeze page → thank-you delivering the test → ebook upsell → home-study course → live event. Built a $20M/yr business off what "looked like just a landing page."
- **Source:** Ch.1, Ch.2, Ch.3

### B1 — LEAD MAGNET recipe
- **When to use:** Step 1 of any funnel; the offer that powers the opt-in.
- **Pattern (required ingredients):** `[A PROBLEM] + [A CURIOSITY HOOK] in one asset` → focus on a **pain point** (move people *away from pain*) → keep it simple (can be one page) → make MANY fast, not one perfect → same content can be re-titled for different segments. Prefer info products (fast + confer authority).
- **Worked example:** Eben Pagan's "Kiss Test: how to tell if she's ready to be kissed" — "literally two paragraphs, five sentences," yet the engine of a $20M business. Brendon Burchard's magnet is "literally one piece of paper" (the 1-Page Productivity Planner).
- **Source:** Ch.2

### B2 — LANDING PAGE (squeeze page) recipe
- **When to use:** Step 2; the page that captures the email. Target conversion **20–30%** (Brunson's Blackbook page hit **55.63%**, 148,000 leads in ~7–8 months).
- **Pattern (block-by-block, minimalist — obey the 3 Cardinal Rules: Keep It Simple, Build Curiosity, Tell Them What To Do):**
  1. `HEADLINE` — presents the **problem** (problem + promise). *Job: stop the scroll, name the pain.*
  2. `CURIOSITY SUB-HEAD / HOOK BULLETS` — stack curiosity, not features. *Job: open the gap.*
  3. `LEAD-MAGNET IMAGE` — picture of the asset. *Job: make the free thing tangible.*
  4. `OPT-IN BOX` — name + email only. *Job: the exchange.*
  5. `EXPLICIT INSTRUCTION CTA` — "tell them what to do." *Job: remove all ambiguity.*
  6. *(Nothing else — no extra images/videos/links; complexity kills conversion.)*
- **Worked example:** Brian Moran's "The Going Pro Manifesto" — a clean page exchanging a free book for an email. The instruction line: "To get [your lead magnet], simply put in your email address and I'll send it right away."
- **Source:** Ch.3

### B3 — THANK YOU PAGE recipe
- **When to use:** Step 3; immediately after opt-in. Two jobs only — never bait-and-switch.
- **Pattern (block order):**
  1. `DELIVERY BLOCK` — the download link right on the page. *Job: over-deliver → build trust.*
  2. `FIRST-OFFER / NEXT-STEP BLOCK` — push to your first product **or** (if no product) "subscribe to my podcast / YouTube / blog." *Job: recoup / start ascension.*
  3. `MESSENGER OPT-IN ASK` — subscribe to Facebook Messenger (layered list). *Job: 3–5× open rates.*
  4. `DESKTOP PUSH-NOTIFICATION ASK` — request "Allow." *Job: a third list (~20% allow).*
- **Worked example:** DigitalMarketer's 3-step thank-you page: (1) Download my lead magnet, (2) Redeem more free training, (3) Buy DigitalMarketer Lab. Brian Moran's: download the manifesto + "Next Step" button → "I'm going to show you how to get your first 1,000 fans on Facebook & Instagram for free in 7 days."
- **Source:** Ch.3

### B4 — SALES PAGE (Self-Liquidating Offer) recipe
- **When to use:** Step 4; the optional-but-recommended page that recoups ad cost and converts a lead to a buyer. Removable when you have no product (replace with an affiliate offer).
- **Pattern (required elements):**
  1. `CURIOSITY HOOK HEADLINE` — *job: re-open desire.*
  2. `DESCRIPTIVE TEXT` — *job: describe the offer/benefit.*
  3. `IMAGE` — *job: make the product real.*
  4. `ORDER FORM` — *job: the transaction.*
  5. `UPSELL PATH` — initial product → home-study course → live event. *Job: raise average order value.*
  - Price it as a **self-liquidating offer** so the sale repays ad cost → "free leads forever."
- **Worked example:** Eben upsold ebook → home-study course → live event. SLO logic: spend $100 → 100 leads; 10% buy a $10 product = $100 back = self-liquidated → 100 free leads.
- **Source:** Ch.1, Ch.3

### B5 — FOLLOW-UP FUNNEL recipe
- **When to use:** Step 5; the automated message sequence that builds the relationship. (Full email/cadence detail in **Section D**.)
- **Pattern:** Build it like a funnel but with **messages** instead of pages: `Message 1` (instant), `+2 days` text, `+2 days` email… add messages as long as you can create content; layer time-specific **broadcasts** (webinar invites, sales, open houses). Channels: Email · Facebook Messenger · SMS · desktop push · funnel messages · retargeting.
- **Worked example:** Message 1 = instant thank-you email (download link + "subscribe to my podcast") → +2 days a text → +2 days another email → ongoing broadcasts.
- **Source:** Ch.3

### B6 — VARIANT: Free + Shipping "book" funnel page
- **When to use:** When the magnet is a physical book and you want a buyer (not just a lead) at step 2.
- **Pattern:** `[Book cover image]` + `[Problem headline]` + `[Free — just cover shipping]` + `[name/email/address/card form]` → thank-you with order-bump/upsell.
- **Worked example:** Brian Moran's "Going Pro Manifesto" free-book exchange model.
- **Source:** Swipe-F

### B7 — VARIANT: Webinar-redirect Thank-You Page
- **When to use:** When the next step is a webinar/VSL instead of a product page.
- **Pattern:** Thank-you page `auto-redirects` into `[webinar/VSL]` with a promise headline; offer registration before they leave.
- **Worked example:** "Watch the webinar: The 6-Step Formula for Making $10k/mo Online"; "List Building Bootcamp $99."
- **Source:** Swipe-F

### B8 — VARIANT: Book-a-Call / Request-Proposal Thank-You Page
- **When to use:** Higher-ticket / agency / service offers where the next step is a conversation.
- **Pattern:** Thank-you page → `[qualifier form: name/email/phone/website]` → "Get Proposal" / "wait for a call from a specialist."
- **Worked example:** KlientBoost "Get Proposal"; StubGroup "wait for a call from a Facebook specialist."
- **Source:** Swipe-F

### B9 — VARIANT: Blog / Newsletter Subscription opt-in page
- **When to use:** Content businesses with no product yet — content IS the magnet.
- **Pattern:** `[Brand] [Blog/Newsletter] Subscription Center` + `[frequency promise]` + `[email field]` + `[Join N+ social proof]`.
- **Worked example:** "HubSpot Blog Subscription Center"; "The Moz Top 10"; "The Swipe File Newsletter."
- **Source:** Swipe-LP

> **Section B: full recipe set captured** — master funnel + all 5 pages + 4 high-value page variants. Source richness: **RICH**.

## C — Script Bank (sales / objection / follow-up / discovery / close)

*Lead Funnels* is a funnel/copy book, not a sales-call book, so its "scripts" are short say-this lines for opt-ins, thank-you bridges, list-building outreach, plus the founder-coaching objection rebuttals. All captured below.

### C7 — The Guest-Blog author-bio CTA

- **Pattern:** `[Author bio sentence], grab [my free lead magnet] at [yoursite.com/leadmagnet].`

### C8 — The Facebook-Messenger opt-in ask (thank-you page)

- **Pattern:** `Want me to send [the magnet / updates] straight to Messenger too? Tap here.` (34% of email opt-ins also subscribe; Messenger opens 3–5× email.)

## D — Email & Sequence Bank

The book teaches the follow-up funnel as a **multi-channel message sequence** with a defined cadence; it gives the sequence *architecture and the first messages* rather than long verbatim email copy. Captured faithfully below as one complete sequence; body copy is patternized from the book's stated structure and worked examples (fidelity = MODERATE; see H).

### D — The Lead-Funnel Follow-Up Sequence (complete, in order)

**Message 1 — Instant Delivery + First Ascension (T+0, Email)**
- **Role:** Deliver the magnet, build trust by over-delivering, start the relationship/ascension.
- **Subject pattern:** `Your [lead magnet] is inside ✅` / `Here's your [magnet] — open this`
- **Body skeleton:** `[Warm thanks] → [Download link, repeated] → [1-sentence "best way to use it"] → [First ascension: "while you're here, subscribe to my podcast/next free training" OR the SLO] → [sign-off in brand voice].`
- **Worked example:** Mirrors DigitalMarketer's 3-step thank-you logic delivered by email: "Download my lead magnet → redeem more free training → here's the next step." Brian Moran's next-step promise: "I'm going to show you how to get your first 1,000 fans on Facebook & Instagram for free in 7 days."
- **Timing:** Immediately on opt-in.

**Message 2 — Value Nudge (T+2 days, SMS / Text)**
- **Role:** Re-engage on a higher-open channel; nudge consumption of the magnet.
- **Subject/opener pattern:** `[First name] — did you get a chance to [use the one key thing] from the [magnet]? Reply if you want the shortcut.`
- **Body skeleton:** `[1 punchy benefit reminder] + [single link/CTA]` (SMS = one idea, one link).
- **Worked example:** "+2 days = a text" reminding them of the magnet's core win.
- **Timing:** 2 days after Message 1.

**Message 3 — Story + Offer (T+4 days, Email)**
- **Role:** Build relationship with a quick story, then bridge to the SLO (recoup ad cost).
- **Subject pattern:** `[Curiosity/story hook from Section A]` (e.g., "How I [implausible result]…")
- **Body skeleton:** `[Short relatable story / "document the journey"] → [the lesson] → ["if you liked the magnet, you'll love this"] → [SLO CTA].`
- **Worked example:** Uses the C2 bridge "If you liked this lead magnet, you'll love this product!" into the $7–$49 SLO.
- **Timing:** 2 days after Message 2.

**Messages 4…N — Ongoing Value + Layered Channels (cadence: as long as you can create content)**
- **Role:** Keep delivering value; layer Facebook Messenger, desktop push, and retargeting on top so each lead's value multiplies.
- **Pattern:** `[New value content] + [soft ascension to next Value-Ladder rung]`, repeated; each new channel = a new list on the same lead.
- **Worked example:** "Add messages as long as you can create content"; Messenger opens 3–5× email; ~20% allow desktop push.
- **Timing:** Recurring.

**Broadcasts — Time-Specific (sent across the whole sequence)**
- **Role:** Event-driven promotion layered over the evergreen sequence.
- **Pattern:** `[Webinar invite | sale | open house | launch]` to the segment.
- **Worked example:** Webinar invites, sales, and open-house broadcasts sent to the list.
- **Timing:** As events occur.

## E — Frameworks, Models & Templates (with the steps)

Every named model in the book, reproduced with its internal steps + a worked example.

### E1 — The 5-Part Lead Funnel
- **Steps:** Lead Magnet → Landing Page → Thank You Page → Sales Page → Follow-Up Funnel (each page = one job; see **Section B** for the full block-level recipe of each).
- **Worked example:** Eben Pagan / Double Your Dating, end to end.
- **Source:** Ch.1–Ch.3

### E2 — Marketing Math (Revenue-Per-Name)
- **Steps:** Value each name at **≥ $1/month** done poorly; **$3–$10+** with relationship; **$10–$50+** local. Project: `list size × monthly value/name = monthly revenue`. 1,000 names ≈ $1k/mo; 10,000 ≈ $10k/mo; 100,000 ≈ $100k/mo. Frame leads as an *investment*, not a cost.
- **Worked example:** Brunson's actual average ≈ just under $10/mo/name; "$100k spent on 100k leads → $100k/mo vs a $100k house → $500/mo."
- **Source:** Ch.1

### E3 — Self-Liquidating Offer (SLO) / Break-Even
- **Steps:** (1) Know your cost per lead ($1–$3 in his market). (2) Put a low-ticket product on the thank-you/sales page. (3) Aim to recoup ad spend on day one; if not, the follow-up funnel recovers it by day 2–3. Goal: **"free leads forever."**
- **Worked example:** Spend $100 → 100 leads; 10% buy a $10 product = $100 back = self-liquidated. R.O.I. by avg value: $3/lead ÷ $1/mo/name = break even month 3; at $3/mo/name = month 1.
- **Source:** Ch.1, Ch.3

### E4 — The Value Ladder / Ascension
- **Steps:** free lead magnet → low-ticket SLO → mid offers → high-ticket (e.g., Inner Circle $50k/yr). Many enter free; fewer ascend to higher-end.
- **Worked example:** Eben: ebook → home-study course → live event.
- **Source:** Ch.1

### E5 — Pain vs Pleasure (move AWAY from pain)
- **Steps:** Recognize most humans decide by moving *away from pain*, not toward pleasure. Build lead magnets around getting people OUT of a specific pain.
- **Worked example:** Via Myron Golden — "the majority move away from pain. Entrepreneurs mistakenly assume everyone moves toward pleasure."
- **Source:** Ch.2

### E6 — The RFMS Lead-Value Score
- **Steps:** Score each contact on **R**ecency (how recently engaged/bought), **F**requency (how often), **M**onetary (how much), **S**ocial (did they give a social-login email — worth "80× more" than a throwaway). 4 bands each; higher total = more valuable lead.
- **Worked example:** A social-login email auto-pings Facebook to match a profile and show follower counts → prioritize high-RFMS leads.
- **Source:** Ch.1/Ch.3

### E7 — The 3 Cardinal Rules of Landing Pages
- **Steps:** (1) **Keep It Simple** — headline + image + opt-in only; complexity kills conversion. (2) **Build Curiosity** — make it feel new/novel and able to end their pain. (3) **Tell Them What To Do** — explicit instruction (see C1). Applies to landing AND thank-you pages.
- **Worked example:** The Blackbook page obeys all three and converts 55.63%.
- **Source:** Ch.3

### E8 — The Curiosity-Hook Formula + Titling System
- **Steps:** Replace vague claims with specific + curious ones that open a gap. Use "how to ___ without ___"; tie to a fear of missing out; then re-title the same asset for multiple segments.
- **Worked example:** NOT "I'll show you how to lose weight" → INSTEAD "how I lost 32 pounds while eating hot dogs, hamburgers, and ice cream." Reference hooks: the Kiss Test; Burchard's 1-page CEO productivity paper; "99 marketing secrets."
- **Source:** Ch.2, Ch.3

### E9 — The 9 Fast Ways to Create an Info Product / Lead Magnet
- **Steps (grouped Text / Audio / Video):** **TEXT** — (1) Crowd-sourced books (experts write chapters; you write the title), (2) Example/compilation books (case studies, swipes, screenshots), (3) Public-domain books (pre-1923 works, rebrand from gutenberg.org). **AUDIO** (raises perceived value) — (4) Audio books of public-domain works, (5) Interview recordings of experts, (6) Hard-to-find audio compilations (curate one best link). **VIDEO** (highest perceived value) — (7) Screen captures (Camtasia/ScreenFlow), (8) iPhone how-to's, (9) Workshops (give first hour free). Master move: pull the best piece as the free magnet, sell the whole product on the thank-you page.
- **Worked example:** Think and Grow Rich = $9.97 as a book vs $97 as a CD — "word for word the same… the only difference was how the product was delivered." First Funnel Hacks training (screen captures) sold $10M.
- **Source:** Ch.2

### E10 — The Follow-Up Funnel (multi-channel framework)
- **Steps:** Build messages (not pages): Email core → Facebook Messenger → desktop push → SMS → retargeting; layer lists so each channel multiplies a lead's value; add time-specific broadcasts. (Full cadence in **Section D**.)
- **Worked example:** Message 1 instant email → +2d text → +2d email → broadcasts.
- **Source:** Ch.3

### E11 — The Publishing Engine ("3 types in 1 fell swoop")
- **Steps:** Publish daily; pick ONE format by preference — write→blog (medium.com), talk→podcast, video→vlog. Then record video once and: (1) strip audio → podcast; (2) transcribe at temi.com ($0.10/min) → blog post; (3) keep the video. "Document the journey" (be 1–2 steps ahead); ship at 80%; test hooks like a comedian, then promote winners as paid lead magnets.
- **Worked example:** "Marketing in Your Car" → "Marketing Secrets," recorded on an iPhone while driving; the podcast "got good ~episode 45" (Steve Larsen).
- **Source:** Ch.3

### E12 — Two Free List-Building Methods
- **Steps:** (1) **Get interviewed** on podcasts holding your dream customers; pitch "The 7 Biggest Mistakes…"; send listeners to your lead-magnet URL (see C6). (2) **Guest blog** on big industry blogs; put the lead-magnet URL in your author bio (see C7) — builds JV relationships too.
- **Worked example:** Send podcast listeners to marketingsecrets.com/blackbook.
- **Source:** Ch.3

### E13 — "Give Yourself a Raise" Daily Question
- **Steps:** Ask daily: "How do I give myself a raise every single day?" Answer is always: build the list + build the relationship.
- **Worked example:** Tied to Marketing Math (E2): each new name and each strengthened relationship is a literal raise.
- **Source:** Ch.1

## F — Brand Voice & Brand-Building Language Patterns

Russell Brunson's reusable voice machinery, as ready-to-use lists with example lines. This is what lets a copy specialist write ON-brand and richly instead of thinly.

### F1 — "Boom!" — the punctuation of completion
- `Just outline, record, and **boom!** You've got a lead magnet in less than 15 minutes.`

### F2 — Reader-check rhetorical tags
- `Does that make sense?` / `Repeat after me:` — used to land a point and pull the reader along.

### F3 — Self-deprecating origin story
- Open with your own failure: "I bought pop-up ads… then pop-up blockers wiped my business out overnight." Vulnerability earns trust ("they care that you're vulnerable").

### F4 — The Iceberg metaphor (whole-funnel framing)
- `I could only see the tip of the iceberg when I saw his webpage… beyond his landing page was the mass of his business.`

### F5 — Mantra repetition for emphasis
- `The money is in the list!` (repeated as a drumbeat throughout).

### F6 — Rhetorical-question objection voicing
- Voice the reader's inner objection, then answer it: `Do you need leads or customers? …then yes, you need a funnel.`

### F7 — Oddly-specific number proof
- Use exact figures, never round: `put $7,231.63 in our pockets in under 20 hours`; `right 89.1% of the time`; `$4,954.55 while you sleep`.

### F8 — "even if…" objection-crush tail
- `[Get the result] — even if you've never written an ad before.`

### F9 — Contrarian command opener
- `Stop advertising. Stop marketing. Start putting on a show!`

### F10 — The curiosity trigger phrase
- `That doesn't make sense. I have to know more!` — the literal feeling every hook must create.

### F11 — Permission-to-ship language
- `Done is better than perfect.` / `Get your stuff to 80% done, and then go!`

### F12 — Power-word bank (use)
- Secrets · Free · Proven · Mysterious · Weird · Loophole · Hack · Sacred · Forgotten · Black Book · Bible · Underground · Exposed · Copy-and-paste · On-demand.
- Example: `The 'Forgotten' Trading System` · `the weird strategy a complete newbie stumbled on`.

### F13 — Words/phrases to AVOID
- "Newsletter," "sign up for updates," vague "valuable," "learn more," generic "subscribe." (A magnet described only as "valuable" or "updates" gets a dead opt-in rate.)

### F14 — Naming patterns (offers/magnets/mechanisms)
- `[Topic] Secrets [Blackbook | Binge Guide]` · `[Topic] Bible` · `[Topic] Cookbook/Playbook/Toolbox` · `The [N]-Day [Result]`.
- Example: "Marketing Secrets Blackbook," "Funnel Hacker's Cookbook," "30 Days to $10K."

### F15 — Social-proof / credibility language
- `Join [big number]+ [identity]` · `including folks from [Google, Apple, Harvard]` · `[N]+ members who [benefit] every day`.

### F16 — Story/analogy templates
- The iceberg (funnel depth); packaging Think & Grow Rich at $9.97 vs $97 (delivery = value); `Not putting in the work yet expecting the reward is like watching a race from the finish line and then holding out your hand for a medal.`

### F17 — Offer / CTA language
- `simply put in your email and I'll send it right away` · `Put in your email and I'll give you the first hour of my $2,000 workshop.`

### F18 — Urgency / FOMO language
- Cut the free stream to drive action: streaming the keynote free, then `cutting the stream sold 1,000+ tickets for next year before the event ended.`

### F19 — Risk-reversal / low-commitment language
- `Free` · `just cover shipping` · `$1 30-day trial` · `7-day free trial (then $27/mo)` · `14-day free trial`.

### F20 — "Give before you get" exchange framing
- `Everything that happens inside of a lead funnel is an exchange… we're bartering.` Value always precedes the ask.

### F21 — The "documenting the journey" authority frame
- `You don't have to meet your end goal to help someone; you just have to be one or two steps in front of them.` / "People are silently begging to be led" (Jay Abraham).

> **Section F count: 21 brand-building language patterns, each with an example line** (floor was 15). Source richness: **RICH**.
