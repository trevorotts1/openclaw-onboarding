# Extraction Notes — Lead Funnels (Russell Brunson)

**Phase 1 — Dual-Lens Extraction.** 20 structured items: coaching methodology (items 1–11) + agent-governance frameworks (items 12–20). Every item is grounded in the actual text of the book (Chapters 1–3 strategy + the 114-example swipe file). Quotes are verbatim from the source.

---

## LENS A — COACHING METHODOLOGY (items 1–11)

### 1. Central Belief — "The money is in the list"
> "The only actual asset inside of your business is your customer list."
The list is the *only salable asset* (eBay bought Skype for the 60M users; Facebook bought Instagram for the 30M users, not the code). Coaching frame for a founder: stop valuing the product or your own genius; the durable asset is the relationship-bearing list. "Build my email list and build my relationship with them… that's what it comes down to."

### 2. The transformation Brunson coaches — leads → relationship → ascension
The book promises a specific before/after: by the end, the reader can "attract leads, build trust, grow a list of leads and customers, build relationships with your list, convert leads into customers, recoup your ad costs, offer your customers additional opportunities… and ascend customers up your value ladder." Two-step process, stated explicitly: **"build a list, then build the relationship."**

### 3. Core mental model — the 5-part Lead Funnel
Lead Magnet → Landing Page → Thank You Page → Sales Page → Follow-Up Funnel. Each part has ONE job: Lead Magnet *attracts to build value*; Landing Page *exchanges value to build the email list*; Thank You Page *fulfills the promise to build trust*; Sales Page *offers a product to build the customer list and recoup ad cost*; Follow-Up Funnel *communicates to build relationship*. "Everything that happens inside of a lead funnel is an exchange."

### 4. Pain over pleasure (Myron Golden / NLP)
> "The majority of humans on this planet… make their decisions moving away from pain. However, entrepreneurs usually make their decisions moving toward pleasure."
The #1 coaching correction: founders build lead magnets toward pleasure ("six-pack abs," "get rich") when their audience moves *away from pain*. Reframe lead magnets around escaping pain. The repeatable template: **"The (#) Biggest Mistakes (Your Market) Make"** (Myron's "12 Biggest Mistakes Network Marketers Make").

### 5. Curiosity & the Hook
Every winning asset carries a "hook" — a curiosity gap. Bad: "I'll show you how to lose weight" (vague, said before). Good: "how I lost 32 pounds while eating hot dogs, hamburgers, and ice cream" ("That doesn't make sense. I have to know more!"). The Marketing Secrets Blackbook's padlocked-book image "just oozes curiosity." Curiosity is what converts the opt-in.

### 6. Marketing Math as a coaching benchmark
> "Each name on your email list ≈ at least $1/month."
Do it poorly = $1/name/month; do it well (build relationship) = $3, $5, $10, even $50/name/month. List size correlates directly with monthly revenue (Brunson's own numbers "synced" from 217 names→~$300, to 1.5M names). The coaching device: **"give yourself a raise"** by adding leads — 100,000 leads ≈ $100,000/month vs. a $100k house at $500/month.

### 7. Done is better than perfect / speed of creation
> "You need to get your stuff to 80% done, and then go! Repeat after me: Done is better than perfect!"
Build a *whole bunch* of lead magnets fast — "you have no idea what people are going to respond to." Spend "no more than five to ten minutes creating a landing page." Stop polishing one perfect thing.

### 8. Publish daily — document the journey
Publish every single day (blog / podcast / vlog — pick one; "do one, publish three"). **"Document the journey"** — you only need to be "one or two steps in front of them." Jay Abraham: "people are silently begging to be led." Steve Larsen's podcast "fueled his entire business." First 45 episodes "weren't very good" — publishing *is* the skill-building.

### 9. Test your material / find your hooks
Like a comedian refining jokes in dive bars: publish, watch which stories and headlines resonate, "let your audience tell you what they need and want." The hooks that win in content become the hooks in your ads. "rewards only come to those who put in the work."

### 10. Funnel hacking — model, don't clone
> "That's what funnel hackers do: we model."
Brunson's whole method began by *funnel hacking* Double Your Dating — going through a competitor's funnel as a customer while watching every step as a marketer ("see the rest of the iceberg below the ocean"). Coach the founder to study proven funnels in any industry and model the structure, then plug their own market in.

### 11. Serve first, sell second (relationship before ROI)
The Marketing Secrets Blackbook funnel sells nothing on the back end — it just builds relationship via the podcast. "If you focus on building a relationship, the money will follow." Inner Circle members ($50k/yr) "got into my Inner Circle through that podcast… six months later they give me a check." Patience with ROI is a relationship stance, not a tactic.

## LENS B — AGENT-GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORKS (items 12–20)

### 12. The non-negotiable 5-part structure (execution standard)
Any lead-gen deliverable MUST contain the five parts in order, each doing its one job. Skipping the Thank-You-Page fulfillment ("bait-and-switch where you say 'Buy this product and then I'll give you what you wanted'") is explicitly "the worst thing you can do." Governance rule: deliver the promised value *before* any ask.

### 13. Hook / Story / Offer as the universal QC lens
All 114 swipe examples are decomposed into **HOOK → STORY → OFFER → THANK YOU PAGE**. This is the deployable scoring rubric for any lead-funnel asset: every asset must have a curiosity HOOK, a STORY that frames the problem/curiosity, a clear OFFER (the exchange), and a fulfillment step. An agent producing funnel copy without all three fails QC.

### 14. The 3 Cardinal Rules of a Landing Page (decision logic)
1) **Keep it simple** (headline + image of the lead magnet + opt-in box; "no fancy design"). 2) **Build curiosity** (the hook). 3) **Tell them what to do** ("To get [lead magnet], simply put in your email address and I'll send it right away"). A landing page that won't convert violates one or more. Benchmark: a good landing page converts 20–30%; the exemplar hit 55.63%.

### 15. The 9 fast ways to create an info product (production playbook)
TEXT: (1) crowd-sourced books (Chicken Soup model; Brunson's *30 Days* = 30 authors), (2) example/swipe books (108 split tests; 74 Funnel Swipe File), (3) public-domain books (Gutenberg/eBay pre-1923; *Think and Grow Rich* rebrand). AUDIO: (4) audiobooks of public-domain works, (5) interview recordings (Vince James 6-hr interview → first Two Comma Club funnel), (6) hard-to-find audio/podcast compilations. VIDEO: (7) screen captures (Camtasia/ScreenFlow — "$10M by just recording my screen"), (8) iPhone how-tos, (9) workshops (give away hour one, sell the rest; FOMO drives ticket sales). Pull the best piece out for the lead magnet; sell the whole thing on the sales page.

### 16. Self-Liquidating Offer (SLO) + break-even logic (financial decision table)
Put an SLO on the sales page so "the product you sell on your thank you page should immediately recoup your ad costs" → free leads forever. Worked example: $100 ad spend → 100 leads → 10% buy a $10 product → $100 recouped → break-even. Goal = **break even on day one**; acceptable = day 3 / day 5 (via follow-up funnel); some businesses run a 6-month break-even ($70–80M/yr consultant). Lead cost benchmark: $1–$3/lead; "I buy up to 1,700 leads per day."

### 17. Follow-Up Funnel = multi-channel automated sequence (orchestration rule)
A follow-up funnel is "Message 1, Message 2, Message 3…" — NOT just an email campaign. Channels: email, Facebook Messenger, desktop push, text/SMS, retargeting. Verified rates: 34% of email opt-ins also join Messenger; Messenger has 3–5× higher open rate; 20% of thank-you-page visitors allow desktop push. Stack lists: each added channel multiplies the lead's value. First message fires immediately with the download link.

### 18. RFMS Score (lead-quality scoring system)
Each contact gets a **Recency, Frequency, Monetary value, Social** action score (4 bands each). Recency = how recently they engaged; Frequency = how often they buy; Monetary = total spend; Social = whether they gave a real social-login email ("worth 80 times more than… a throwaway email"). High RFMS across a list = "you're doing a good job serving your audience." This is the leading indicator an agent should optimize toward.

### 19. The Value Ladder / ascension (routing logic)
The lead funnel is the *entry point* of a larger value ladder: free lead magnet → first offer → upsells → high-ticket → coaching/Inner Circle. Eben: ebook → home-study course → live event. The lead funnel's job is to get the lead and recoup cost; ascension happens downstream. An agent must know a lead funnel is "a lead funnel, not a book funnel, webinar funnel, high ticket funnel" — match funnel type to the goal.

### 20. "What this persona would never allow" (guardrails)
Never bait-and-switch (always fulfill the promise on the thank you page first). Never build a complex landing page (complexity kills conversion). Never aim a lead magnet purely at pleasure when the market moves away from pain. Never wait for perfect / wait until you've "hit your goal" to publish. Never build a list without then building the relationship ("a list like that doesn't make you much money"). Never confuse the product with the asset — the list is the asset. Never run a single "perfect" lead magnet — create many, test, keep the winners.

## Anchor examples / proof points (for the analyst & synthesizer)
- **Double Your Dating / Eben Pagan (David DeAngelo)** — "The Kiss Test," $20M/yr — the first lead funnel Brunson ever funnel-hacked; the canonical 5-part template.
- **Marketing Secrets Blackbook** — 55.63% opt-in, 148,000 leads in ~7–8 months; sells nothing on the back end (relationship play via podcast).
- **Brendon Burchard 1-Page Productivity Planner** — "It doesn't have to be hard"; one sheet of paper as a lead magnet.
- **Myron Golden** — "12 Biggest Mistakes Network Marketers Make"; pain-point lead magnet → webinar.
- **Brian Moran — The Going Pro Manifesto** — free-plus-shipping book as lead magnet; clean simple landing page exemplar.
- **Industry breadth (proves universality):** SaaS (HubSpot, Salesforce, MOZ, Unbounce, AdEspresso, KISSmetrics), info-marketers (DigitalMarketer, Marie Forleo, Frank Kern, Neil Patel), fitness (ATHLEAN-X, Beyond Diet, LadyBoss), finance (Motley Fool, Fisher Investments), local/trades (Matco Tools, Carrot for real estate agents), travel (Nomadic Matt), agencies (Jason Swenk). 114 examples = explicit proof "lead funnels work in any industry."
