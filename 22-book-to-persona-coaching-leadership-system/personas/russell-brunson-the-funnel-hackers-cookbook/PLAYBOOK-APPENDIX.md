## A — Headline, Hook & Subject-Line Formula Bank

### A1. Curiosity-Based Headline (the master squeeze/opt-in lever)
- **When to use:** Top of any squeeze page, opt-in, bridge, webinar-registration, or cold-traffic page whose ONLY job is the click/opt-in.
- **Pattern:** `[Result/secret claim] + [intrigue device: "weird trick" / "#1 secret" / "what nobody tells you"] — WITHOUT revealing the mechanism.` Promise the destination; hide the vehicle so the only way to satisfy the curiosity gap is to opt in.
- **Worked example:** "How I Lost 30 Pounds In Two Weeks Using This 1 Weird Trick" (the book's bridge-funnel squeeze; the mechanism — ketosis — is hidden until the next page).
- **Source:** Ch.2 Squeeze Page ("The #1 secret I found to get the highest conversion rate on a squeeze page is to use a curiosity-based headline."); Ch.3 Bridge Funnel.

### A2. The 6-Angle Curiosity Bullet Formula ("Brunson Bullet" logic)
- **When to use:** Any bullet list that must create *desire to know more* — under VSLs, on sales letters, lead-magnet pages, webinar registration.
- **Pattern:** Write each bullet from one of six angles: `[1] something they just have to know the answer to · [2] something bizarre about how you found the solution · [3] something they're secretly insecure about/afraid of · [4] how to avoid something painful · [5] something counterintuitive about how it works · [6] any detail that makes them say "tell me more."`
- **Worked example (verbatim template):** "• Something they just have to know the answer to… • Or something bizarre [detail] about how you found your solution… • Maybe something they're secretly insecure about or afraid of… • Or how to avoid something really painful… • Or something counterintuitive about how your solution works… • Basically, any detail that gets people to say 'tell me more'."
- **Source:** Ch.2 universal template copy (the curiosity-bullet block).

### A3. Attention-Getting Headline (generic top-of-page slot)
- **When to use:** The H1 on virtually every page; the default headline element.
- **Pattern:** `[A curiosity- OR benefit-driven hook]` — one line, top of page, biggest text on the screen.
- **Worked example:** template placeholder "Here's Your Attention Getting Headline" (replace with the curiosity/benefit hook).
- **Source:** Ch.2 universal headline element.

### A4. Subheadline (credibility + curiosity + instruction)
- **When to use:** Directly under the H1 to add credibility/curiosity OR to instruct the visitor ("opt in below," "watch this video," "click here").
- **Pattern:** `[Credibility or curiosity amplifier] + [optional micro-instruction telling them exactly what to do next].`
- **Worked example:** template placeholder "This is your sub-headline to increase credibility or curiosity."
- **Source:** Ch.2 subheadline element.

### A5. The "Mystery Story" Sales-Copy Voice Formula (baked into every paragraph)
- **When to use:** Any body-copy / paragraph element on any sales, VSL, or sales-letter page.
- **Pattern:** `Short sentences + short paragraphs. Use *bold*, underline and italics to emphasize the key points. Tell it like a mystery story — keep them captivated. Cut all filler; get to the point.`
- **Worked example (verbatim template):** "Here's where you write your compelling message, keep your sentences and paragraphs short. Use *bold*, underline and italics to emphasize important points. Don't be boring! Pretend you're telling a mystery story. Keep your audience captivated. Eliminate any and all filler that serve to unnecessarily complicate and hinder the reader's experience (i.e. get to the point)."
- **Source:** Ch.2 universal paragraph/sales-copy template.

### A6. FAQ Question Formula
- **When to use:** FAQ sections on order forms, sales pages, and webinar pages to crush late objections.
- **Pattern:** `Q. [a question you are sick of answering over and over] → A. [the reassuring answer that removes the objection].` Stack several.
- **Worked example (verbatim template):** "Q. Here's where you post a question that you're sick of answering over and over…"
- **Source:** Ch.1 FAQ element / Ch.2 order & sales pages.

### A7. The Brunson Box (a.k.a. "Video Spoiler Box") — the keep-watching hook
- **When to use:** Beneath any VSL to raise watch-time before the Add-To-Cart appears.
- **Pattern:** Place `4 × [Image Feature]` boxes under the video, each = `[thumbnail image] + [intrigue headline pulled from the video] + [1–2 lines of curiosity blurb]`. Together they spoil just enough to make people watch longer.
- **Worked example (verbatim template):** Box = image + "Featured image headline" + "here is some info about the picture on the left…" × 4, placed under the VSL above the Add-To-Cart button. ("Underneath the video we have what we refer to as the 'Brunson Box'… we pull out four elements from the video that cause intrigue and curiosity. This gets people to watch the video longer.")
- **Source:** Ch.2 Video Sales Letter page; PART 2 template patterns.

### A8. The "Something Sexier Than 'Submit'" CTA Button Formula
- **When to use:** Every button element. Never label a button "Submit."
- **Pattern:** `[Action verb] + [the thing/benefit they get]` — first-person where possible; never the dead word "Submit."
- **Worked example (verbatim swipe set):** "GET FREE BOOK NOW!" · "YES! I Want This FREE OFFER Now!" · "Ship Me My Product" · "SECURE YOUR SPOT NOW" · "Register for Webinar Now" · "Go To Step #2 Now" · "Reconfirm Your Seat Here…" · and the explicit instruction label "Something Sexier Than 'Submit Order'."
- **Source:** Ch.2 button element + PART 2 CTA swipe library.

### A9. The OTO Yes/No Takeaway-CTA Stack
- **When to use:** Upsell (OTO) and downsell pages, and order-bump decisions.
- **Pattern:** A big affirmative button `[YES! Add [product] to my order — just $X]` placed ABOVE a small, low-contrast takeaway link `[No thanks, I don't need this now]` written in the customer's voice so declining feels like a small loss.
- **Worked example (verbatim template):** "YES! Add To My Order Now… — Add to Order - Just $27" above "No Thanks, I Don't Need This Now."
- **Source:** Ch.2 OTO/Upsell page template copy.

### A10. 2-Step Order-Form Section Headers (micro-commitment framing)
- **When to use:** The header copy on a 2-step order form / tripwire checkout.
- **Pattern:** `Step 1 = [low-friction info ask, framed as shipping]: "Where Should We Ship It?" → Step 2 = [the payment ask]: "Complete Your Order."` Step 1 captures contact even if Step 2 is abandoned.
- **Worked example (verbatim template):** "1 SHIPPING — Where Should We Ship It? → 2 Your Info — Complete Your Order." (Variant for OTO pages: "Step 1: Create Account → Step 2: Customize Order → Step 3: Order Complete.")
- **Source:** Ch.2 2-Step Order Form / checkout template copy.

### A11. Risk-Reversal & Trust Microcopy (under-button line)
- **When to use:** The small line directly beneath any order or opt-in button.
- **Pattern:** `[Privacy / security / guarantee reassurance in plain, human language].`
- **Worked example (verbatim template):** "We will never spam, rent, or sell your information…" and the half-joking legal line "Text that goes down here that nobody actually reads but you need it so you don't get sued :)".
- **Source:** Ch.2 order-form & opt-in template copy.

### A12. Million Dollar CONTENT Headline (give-before-you-take / value-first pages)
- **When to use:** Reverse-squeeze, article/presell, broadcast, member's-access, storefront, hero, indoctrination — any page that LEADS with teaching value before asking.
- **Pattern:** `[Promise of the specific teaching/insight they get for free, right now]` — the headline sells the *content*, not a purchase.
- **Worked example:** the reverse-squeeze "give before you take" frame — e.g., "Watch This 4-Minute Training And You'll Know Exactly How To [result] — Then Opt In Below If You Want The Full System." (`formula NAMED in the book — "Million Dollar Content Headline Script" — but the fill-in template is generated by Funnel Scripts; the printed asset is the doctrine + placeholder, not the formula internals.`)
- **Source:** Ch.2 (named on Article, Reverse-Squeeze, Broadcast, Member's-Access, Storefront, Hero, Indoctrination pages); PART 5 Funnel Scripts Index.

### A13. Webinar Registration Curiosity Headline
- **When to use:** Webinar / web-class / autowebinar registration pages (use an image, not a video — "video very rarely won on a registration page").
- **Pattern:** `[Curiosity headline that makes them EXCITED to register] + [subheadline naming the #1 thing they'll learn].`
- **Worked example:** "Register Free: The [counterintuitive method] That Lets [audience] [achieve result] Without [the thing they dread] — Live On [date]." (Perfect Webinar Wizard + Webinar Opt-in scripts named in book.)
- **Source:** Ch.2 Webinar Registration page; PART 5 Funnel Scripts Index.

### A14. Opportunity-Switch Headline / Title (Hero / new-vehicle pages)
- **When to use:** Hero pages and anywhere you reposition the prospect onto a NEW opportunity/vehicle rather than improving the old one.
- **Pattern:** `[Name the NEW opportunity] + [the better future it unlocks] — switch them off the old vehicle.` (`NAMED script — "Opportunity Switch Headline/Title Script"; internal formula deferred to Funnel Scripts / Expert Secrets; book provides the placement + intent, not the fill-in.`)
- **Worked example:** Hero-page frame pairing the Origin Story + Epiphany Bridge to introduce the new opportunity, then "join my list / follow me."
- **Source:** Ch.2 Hero Page; PART 5 Funnel Scripts Index.

## B — Funnel & Page Recipes (the FULL recipe set, none dropped)

> This section reproduces the book's two big recipe layers: **B0** the universal page-build primitive, **B1** all **37 page recipes** (Chapter 2, 10 categories), and **B2** all **22 funnel recipes** (Chapter 3, 4 families). For each: ingredients (in build order) = Pattern; job/strategy + template copy = Worked example.

### B0. The Universal Page-Build Primitive (use on every page)
- **When to use:** Building ANY ClickFunnels-style page from scratch or from a template.
- **Pattern:** `Sections (green) > Rows & Columns (blue) > Elements (orange).` Build order: (1) lay out Sections, (2) drop in Rows/Columns, (3) place Elements into the containers. With templates, the Sections+Rows ship pre-built — you only swap the Elements.
- **Worked example:** "Build funnels like playing with Legos… stick to the templates we've given you. The majority of all the funnels we launch are based on these exact templates. We rarely deviate." Simplicity rule: "in most situations, simplicity will out convert more complex page designs."
- **Source:** PART 0 / Ch.2 Advanced Page Design.

### B1 — THE 37 PAGE RECIPES (Chapter 2)

#### PRESELL PAGES
##### B1.1 Presale Page
- **When to use:** Warming cold/ad traffic before a sales page (some ad networks ban sending straight to a sales page).
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Video → Paragraph → Subheadline → Bullet List → Button` (6 elements).
- **Worked example:** Educate/pre-frame, then a single button to the funnel. Scripts: Killer Headlines, Who-What-Why-How, Brunson Bullet, FBM Bullets, Call to Action (+ Epiphany Bridge/Origin Story if story-bridging).
- **Source:** Ch.2 Presell Pages.

##### B1.2 Article Page
- **When to use:** "Advertorial"/educate-first presell that looks like content.
- **Pattern:** `Image(logo) → Navigation → Headline → Paragraph → Subheadline → Bullet List → Subheadline → Paragraph → Image Feature → Button` (~8).
- **Worked example:** "The key to a good presell article is to include a button at the end that links to your funnel." Scripts: Million Dollar Content Headline, Call to Action, Seinfeld Email Topic Idea.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Presell Pages.

##### B1.3 Survey Page
- **When to use:** Lift conversion on whatever page comes next via micro-commitment or segmentation.
- **Pattern:** `Image(logo) → Survey → Button` (3 core).
- **Worked example:** "A basic quiz can skyrocket your conversions… even 'where did you hear about us?' works… You don't even have to collect the answers." Segment (man/woman → different next video). Script: Easy Survey.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Presell Pages.

##### B1.4 Click Pop
- **When to use:** Button-click or exit-intent popup to grow the list from blogs/other pages.
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Input → Button → Subheadline` (in a lightbox).
- **Worked example:** "We have seen a huge increase in conversion rates by using popups as opposed to just embedding forms." Scripts: Short Headline, Million Dollar Sales Headlines.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Presell Pages.

#### OPTIN PAGES
##### B1.5 Squeeze Page
- **When to use:** The single highest-leverage list-builder; one job — capture the email.
- **Pattern:** `Headline (curiosity) → Input → Button.` Few/no distractions — often no logo, footer, or nav.
- **Worked example:** "The only goal of a squeeze page is to get somebody to give you their email address… use a curiosity-based headline." Scripts: Killer Headlines, Call to Action.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Optin Pages.

##### B1.6 Reverse Squeeze Page
- **When to use:** Pre-frame/indoctrinate with teaching FIRST, then ask for the opt-in ("give before you take").
- **Pattern:** `Image(logo) → Video (train) → Input → Button.`
- **Worked example:** Lower conversion, higher-quality pre-framed leads. Scripts: Million Dollar Content Headline, Short Headline.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Optin Pages.

##### B1.7 Lead Magnet Page
- **When to use:** Straight bribe — give a report/ebook/video for the email.
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Image or Video → Subheadline → Input → Button.`
- **Worked example:** "A straight up bribe. 'You give me your email address, I will give you this thing.' You'll know you created the wrong lead magnet if your conversion rate is low." Scripts: Free Report, Million Dollar Sales Headlines, Killer Headlines, (video) Who-What-Why-How.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Optin Pages.

##### B1.8 Coupon Page
- **When to use:** Lead magnet whose bribe is a discount; reactivate warm buyers with urgency.
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Subheadline → Image → Countdown Timer → Input → Button.`
- **Worked example:** Discount + scarcity clock. Scripts: Million Dollar Sales Headlines, Killer Headlines.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Optin Pages.

#### THANK YOU PAGES
##### B1.9 Thank You Page
- **When to use:** After any opt-in or purchase — thank, relationship-build, or sell the next thing.
- **Pattern:** `Image(logo) → Headline → Video → Subheadline.`
- **Worked example:** "A great place to build a relationship or sell them the next product." Scripts: VSL Wizard, OTO "Need Help/Coaching/Done-For-You," OTO "Next Thing"/Next Step.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Thank You Pages.

##### B1.10 Offer Wall
- **When to use:** After the initial purchase — push buyers into the front-ends of your OTHER funnels (incl. affiliate offers).
- **Pattern:** `Image(logo) → Headline → Subheadline → Order Summary → Images → Buttons.`
- **Worked example:** "Customers will continue to purchase until you stop making them offers." Scripts: Short Headline, Killer Headlines, Million Dollar Sales Headlines.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Thank You Pages.

##### B1.11 Bridge Page
- **When to use:** Affiliates/network marketers — tell YOUR story to pre-sell before sending to a page you don't control.
- **Pattern:** `Image(logo) → Headline → Video → Headline → Paragraph → Button.`
- **Worked example:** "If a funnel isn't converting, inserting a bridge page will often increase your conversions on the sales page." Scripts: Short Sales Letter, Call to Action, Killer Headlines, Million Dollar Sales Headlines, VSL Wizard.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Thank You Pages.

##### B1.12 Share Page
- **When to use:** Turn every buyer into free traffic — ask them to share what they just did.
- **Pattern:** `Image(logo) → Headline → Video → Social Share.`
- **Worked example:** "Like a thank-you page but asks the customer to share… = viral marketing." Script: Short Headline.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Thank You Pages.

#### SALES PAGES
##### B1.13 Video Sales Letter (VSL)
- **When to use:** Let a video make the sale; highest-converting sales layout.
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Subheadline → Video → Headline → Image Features (the Brunson Box) → Button.`
- **Worked example:** "This layout has been what we've found typically converts the highest for video sales letters." Add-To-Cart → order form. Scripts: Killer Headlines, VSL Wizard, PPT VSL, Who-What-Why-How, Brunson Bullet, FBM Bullets, Call to Action.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Sales Pages.

##### B1.14 Sales Letter
- **When to use:** Long-form written pitch — converts well precisely because few people still use it.
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Subheadlines → Paragraphs → Image(s) → Button → Image(logo).`
- **Worked example:** "Most people in our community use the Star-Story-Solution script (from DotCom Secrets)." Scripts: Killer Headlines, Long-Form Sales Letter, Short Sales Letter, Call to Action.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Sales Pages.

##### B1.15 Product Launch Page
- **When to use:** Jeff-Walker-style launch — a series of teaching videos unlocked over time, the last one sells.
- **Pattern:** `Image(logo) → Headline → Video → Images → Facebook Comments` (usually build 4 of these).
- **Worked example:** Each video teaches + presells the next; seeded FB-comment social proof. Scripts: Million Dollar Content Headline, Killer Headlines, Million Dollar Sales Headlines, Short Headline, Call to Action.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Sales Pages.

#### ORDER FORMS
##### B1.16 2-Step Order Form
- **When to use:** Favorite for low-ticket / free+shipping; can double as the sales page.
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Subheadline → Video → Headline → Paragraph → 2-Step Order (Step1 contact/address → Step2 card).` Add an order bump.
- **Worked example:** Incomplete Step 2 still captures the contact → abandon-cart follow-up. Scripts: Killer Headlines, Short Headline, Who-What-Why-How, VSL Wizard, PPT VSL, Call to Action.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Order Forms.

##### B1.17 Traditional Order Form
- **When to use:** Higher-priced products; everything on one page with proof + guarantees.
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Subheadline → Progress Bar → Inputs → Shipping Address → Order Select → Credit Card → Order Bump → Button → Image Features.`

- **Source:** Ch.2 Order Forms.

##### B1.18 VSL + Order
- **When to use:** VSL with the order form revealed below the video on the SAME page.
- **Pattern:** `Headline → Subheadline → Video → Button → Inputs → Shipping Address → Order Select → Credit Card → Order Bump → Button.`
- **Worked example:** "Having the order form open on the same page dramatically increases conversion rates." Scripts: VSL Wizard, PPT VSL, Who-What-Why-How, Order Bump, Killer Headlines, Short Headline, Call to Action.
- **Source:** Ch.2 Order Forms.

##### B1.19 SL + Order (Sales Letter + Order)

##### B1.20 Product Launch + Order

- **Source:** Ch.2 OTO Pages.

### F8. Take-Away / Exclusivity Selling
- **Pattern:** `Make them qualify/apply; frame the offer as scarce and not-for-everyone.`
- **Worked example:** Application Funnel — "make people APPLY to join the team (exclusivity)."

### F9. Urgency & Scarcity Language
- **Pattern:** `Real deadlines: countdown clocks, expiring replay pages, closing carts.`
- **Worked example:** "We see the highest conversion rates when we pull down these pages after 48 to 72 hours"; "SECURE YOUR SPOT NOW."

### F10. Risk-Reversal / Trust Voice
- **Pattern:** `Remove the risk in plain human language — guarantee, "pay only if you liked it," privacy reassurance.`
- **Worked example:** "If you didn't like the training, just let me know and we won't bill you at all"; "We will never spam, rent, or sell your information…"

### F11. The Any-Niche Stress-Test ("Nude Squirrel Art")
- **Pattern:** `Demonstrate every recipe on a deliberately absurd/any product so the PATTERN is obvious independent of niche.`
- **Worked example:** The "selling nude squirrel art" worked example (the ClickFunnels.com video inside-joke) applied to each page recipe.

### F12. Offer / Funnel Naming Patterns
- **Pattern:** `Name funnels and offers by RESULT or mechanism so the name sells: [Squeeze / Bridge / Tripwire / Invisible / Hero / Offer Wall / Value Ladder].`
- **Worked example:** "Free + Shipping," "2-Step Tripwire," "Offer Wall," "Funnel Stacking."

### F13. The "Everyone Needs This" Reframe (objection-melting voice)
- **Pattern:** `Reframe "will this work for me?" → "every living business needs leads/customers, so it works for you."`
- **Worked example:** "The only businesses I know that aren't trying to generate leads or create customers are businesses that aren't dying, but are already dead. So unless your company is dead, you need a funnel."

### F14. Reference-First, Plain-Spoken Instructional Voice
- **Pattern:** `Talk like a desk reference, not a theorist — "open to the exact page you're working on," hand them the recipe.`
- **Worked example:** "We're going to give you 22 funnel recipes that we use every single day."

### F15. Proof & Social-Proof Language Patterns
- **Pattern:** `Stack proof in Image Features / Brunson Boxes / seeded Facebook Comments / affiliate stat tiles.`
- **Worked example:** Seeded launch comment — "Create a post that will motivate your customers to act…"; affiliate stat tiles "2,000 visitors / 568 buyers / $5,589 commissions."

### F16. Guarantee / Security / Checkout-Trust Language
- **Pattern:** `On checkout, surround the button with guarantee, security badge, and privacy/legal microcopy.`
- **Worked example:** The under-button legal line + privacy line + FAQ guarantee block (see A11/A6).

### F17. Offer-Stack / OTO Value Language
- **Pattern:** `Always add value to what they just bought; frame the upsell as the "next logical thing."`
- **Worked example:** OTO doctrine — "Always offer something that adds value to what they just bought"; Ecommerce upsell = "the next logical thing."

> **Section F count:** 17 brand-building language patterns, each with an example line. Source richness: RICH.
