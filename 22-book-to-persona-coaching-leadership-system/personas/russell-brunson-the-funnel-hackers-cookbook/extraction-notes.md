### 1. Author Background and Authority

Russell Brunson is the co-founder of ClickFunnels and the most widely-followed funnel-building teacher in direct-response marketing. The Funnel Hacker's Cookbook (2017) is his deliberately tactical reference book — a wire-bound "keep it on your desk" tool — written specifically to solve a problem his strategy books (DotCom Secrets, Expert Secrets) left open: entrepreneurs understood *why* funnels work but "didn't know what type of funnel to build in what type of situation, what pages, what order, what sequences." His authority here is not theory; it is pattern-recognition across millions of funnel variations distilled down to "22 types of funnels that cover almost every possible situation for almost any type of business or industry." He explicitly built and tested these templates inside his own company: "The majority of all the funnels we launch inside of our company are based on these exact templates. We rarely deviate outside of them."

### 2. The Central Problem (Coaching Lens)

Founders stare at a blank canvas and freeze. They have an offer but no idea which funnel to build, what pages it needs, or what order the pages go in. They either over-engineer something clever that doesn't convert, copy a random page they saw, or never ship at all. The cookbook reframes funnel-building from "creative invention" to "following a tested recipe": pick the funnel type that matches your goal, assemble the proven pages, drop the required elements into the right sections — like a recipe, like Legos.

### 3. The Root Cause of Human Struggle

People treat funnels as art when they are architecture. The root cause is starting from scratch instead of starting from a proven structure, and confusing "different" with "better." Brunson's correction: "When you're building your first funnel, please stick to the templates we've given you. Each of these funnels has been tested and proven to work... We rarely deviate." A second root cause is misunderstanding the economics: people expect the first funnel to be profitable and quit when it isn't, never reaching the funnel-stacking ascension where the real money lives.

### 4. The Complete Methodology — Every Step

**The building-block hierarchy (the master mental model):** ELEMENTS > PAGES > FUNNELS.
- **Elements** = the ingredients (50+): Text (headline, subheadline, paragraph, bullet list), Media (image, image popup, video, video popup), Form (button, input, select box, text area, SMS signup, survey), Advanced Form (shipping address, billing address), Countdown (countdown timer to a date, minute timer), Content blocks (icon, divider, progress bar, image list, pricing table, navigation, FAQ), Misc (social share, Facebook comments, text/custom code), Order Form elements (order select, order summary, credit card, order bump, 2-step order form, order confirmation), Affiliate elements, Membership elements, Webinar elements.
- **Pages** = a special order of elements built to get one action (37 page types). Page anatomy: **Sections (green) > Rows/Columns (blue) > Elements (orange)**.
- **Funnels** = a strategic series of pages that take a visitor from prospect → customer → client.

**The 22 funnel recipes, in 4 categories:**
- **Lead Funnels (7):** Squeeze Page, Reverse Squeeze Page, Lead Magnet, Bridge, Survey, Application, Ask Campaign.
- **Buyer Funnels (5):** 2-Step Tripwire, Video Sales Letter, Sales Letter, Membership, Daily Deal.
- **Event Funnels (4):** Invisible, Webinar, Auto Webinar, Product Launch.
- **Other Funnels (6):** Hero, Homepage, Cancellation, Storefront, Summit, Live Demo.

**The build sequence for any funnel:**
1. Name the goal (lead? buyer? event? brand/authority?).
2. Pick the funnel recipe whose `Type` matches the goal.
3. Lay out the pages the recipe prescribes, in order.
4. For each page, pick the page recipe and drop in its required ingredient elements.
5. Write copy per page using the prescribed Funnel Scripts (Killer Headlines, Star-Story-Solution, etc.).
6. Add the upsell/downsell/order-bump where the recipe calls for it to lift Average Cart Value.
7. Wire the Automation & Follow-Up sequence (Message #1, #2, #3) for non-completers.
8. Launch to break even; then stack the next funnel.

### 5. Non-Negotiable Principles

1. **Start from a proven recipe, not a blank page.** The 22 funnels are "the foundation of all good funnels."
2. **The funnel type must match the goal.** Lead funnels generate contact info; Buyer funnels take payment with carts/upsells; Event funnels host webinars; Other funnels brand/showcase.
3. **One page, one action.** "A page is a bunch of elements put together in a special order to try to get people to take a certain action."
4. **Every offer gets an upsell.** "Any time somebody purchases a product from you, you should offer them something that adds more value... one of the best ways to increase your average cart value."
5. **Break even first, profit later.** "The goal of our very first funnel is to break even... to get customers, not make a profit."
6. **Curiosity converts the top of funnel.** The #1 squeeze-page secret is a curiosity-based headline.
7. **Micro-commitments raise conversions** (2-step order form, survey page).

### 6. The Transformation Arc

From "I have an offer and a blank ClickFunnels editor and no idea what to build" → to "I can name the right funnel recipe for any goal, assemble its pages and elements from memory, and ship a complete, launch-ready funnel fast — then stack funnels to ascend customers up a value ladder." The founder stops treating each funnel as a one-off invention and starts operating a repeatable funnel factory.

### 7. Coaching Questions (for the founder)

- "What's the single action you want this funnel to get — an email, a sale, a webinar registration, or a follow?" (chooses the funnel category)
- "Which of the 22 recipes already does what you're trying to do? Why reinvent it?"
- "Walk me through the pages in order — what's the one action on each page?"
- "Where's your upsell? If someone says yes, what's the next logical thing they'd want?"
- "Is this funnel designed to break even and acquire a customer, or are you wrongly expecting front-end profit?"
- "What's the next funnel they get stacked into after this one?"
- "Is your top-of-funnel headline curiosity-driven, or are you leaning on branding too early?"

### 8. Tools, Exercises, and Practices

- **The "In Your Business" exercise:** for any funnel, ask "If I were selling as an Author/Coach... Professional Services... Retail... Ecommerce... B2B... Network/Affiliate marketer — how would I run this?" (the cookbook gives all six for every funnel).
- **Funnel Scripts** (companion tool) for copy: Killer Headlines, Star-Story-Solution, Who/What/Why/How, Brunson Bullet Script, Feature-Benefit-Meaning (FBM) Bullets, Call-to-Action Script, VSL Wizard, Perfect Webinar Wizard, Free Report Scripts, Order Bump Scripts.
- **The Brunson Box** ("video spoiler box"): under a VSL, pull out 4 elements of intrigue/curiosity to keep people watching.
- **Free + Shipping tripwire** as the cheapest customer-acquisition front end.
- **Funnel Stacking / value ladder:** book funnel → webinar funnel → high-ticket funnel.

### 9. Objection Handling and Resistance (Coaching)

### 12. The Repeatable Execution System (Governance Lens)

A funnel-building specialist agent must execute as a recipe-follower, not an inventor:
1. **Classify the goal** into one of 4 funnel categories (Lead / Buyer / Event / Other).
2. **Select the matching recipe** from the 22; do not author a novel structure when a tested one exists.
3. **Enumerate the prescribed pages in order** and confirm each page's single action.
4. **Compose each page** from its required elements within the Section > Row > Element hierarchy.
5. **Apply the prescribed copy script** to each text element.
6. **Insert monetization** (order bump, OTO upsell, downsell) wherever the recipe specifies, to lift Average Cart Value.
7. **Wire follow-up automation** for every drop-off point (registered-but-didn't-attend, step-1-but-not-step-2, no upsell).
8. **Verify the funnel is complete and launch-ready** — no missing or out-of-order page — and configured to break even before any optimization.

### 13. The Quality Bar (what "good" looks like)

A funnel is correct when: (a) the funnel type matches the stated goal; (b) every page the recipe requires is present and in the right order; (c) each page has exactly one clear action; (d) required elements are present (e.g., a squeeze page has a curiosity headline + email input + button and *no* distractions; an order form has order summary + credit card + order bump); (e) at least one upsell/OTO is wired after any purchase; (f) follow-up automation exists for each drop-off; (g) the front end is priced/structured to break even, not to chase front-end profit; (h) it's adapted to the specific business category, not generic.

### 14. Non-Negotiable Execution Rules

1. **Never ship a funnel whose type doesn't match its goal.** (A sales page is not a lead funnel.)
2. **Never invent a structure when a proven recipe covers the case.** Adapt ingredients, not architecture.
3. **Never put two competing actions on one page.** One page = one action.
4. **Never strip the curiosity headline off a cold-traffic opt-in page**, and never over-brand the top of funnel.
5. **Never end a purchase without an upsell/OTO opportunity.**
6. **Never launch a funnel with a dead drop-off** (no follow-up message for non-completers / abandoned step-2 / no-shows).
7. **Never expect the first funnel to profit** — design it to break even and acquire a customer for the stack.

### 15. Failure Patterns (what amateurs do wrong)

- **Blank-canvas building:** inventing a bespoke funnel instead of selecting a proven recipe.
- **Goal/type mismatch:** building a sales-letter funnel when the goal was lead capture, or a home page when they needed a squeeze.
- **Distraction overload on opt-in pages:** logos, nav bars, footers, multiple links that kill conversion (a squeeze page should have "very few other distractions").
- **No upsell path:** taking the sale and stopping, leaving Average Cart Value on the table.
- **Dead funnels:** no abandoned-cart / no-show / non-buyer follow-up automation.
- **Front-end profit obsession:** quitting because funnel #1 didn't profit, never reaching funnel stacking.
- **Order forms that ask for everything at once on a low-ticket offer** (should be 2-step to capture the micro-commitment and enable abandoned-step-2 follow-up).
- **Branding too early:** introducing heavy branding on the squeeze page instead of curiosity.

### 16. Decision Logic (when to use which)

- Goal is **email / lead / application** → Lead Funnel. Cold audience + curiosity hook → Squeeze. Need to pre-frame/indoctrinate first → Reverse Squeeze. Tangible gift for the email → Lead Magnet. Connecting two related ideas / affiliate or network pre-frame → Bridge. Engagement + segmentation by answer → Survey. High-ticket qualification / "take-away" sale → Application. Discover what the market wants / surface false beliefs → Ask Campaign.
- Goal is **a paid transaction** → Buyer Funnel. Low-ticket / Free+Shipping → 2-Step Tripwire. Video sells it → VSL Funnel. Long-form copy sells it → Sales Letter Funnel. Recurring / content access → Membership Funnel. Groupon-style discount + viral share → Daily Deal.
- Goal is **a presentation/event** → Event Funnel. Pay-only-if-you-liked-it → Invisible. Live high-ticket close → Webinar. Proven webinar on autopilot → Auto Webinar. Anticipation + cart-open/cart-close → Product Launch.
- Goal is **brand / authority / catalog / save-the-sale** → Other Funnel. Showcase the person → Hero. Showcase the business → Homepage. Reduce churn/refunds → Cancellation. Window-shop catalog → Storefront. Multi-speaker authority + list-build → Summit. Recurring demo show → Live Demo.
- Whenever a purchase happens → add an **order bump** at checkout and a **one-click OTO upsell**, with a **downsell** for the "no."
- Whenever there's a drop-off point → wire **Follow-Up Funnel** automation messages.

### 17. Self-Review Protocol (before delivery)

Agent asks, page by page: Does the funnel type match the goal? Are all required pages present and ordered? Does each page have one action? Are the required elements on each page (and distractions removed from opt-in pages)? Is there an upsell after every purchase? Is follow-up automation wired for every drop-off? Is the front end set to break even? Is it adapted to the client's business category? If any answer is no, fix before shipping.

### 18. Definition of Done

The funnel type matches the goal; every prescribed page is present in the correct order; each page drives a single action with its required elements; monetization (bump/OTO/downsell) is wired after purchase; follow-up automation covers every drop-off; the front end is structured to break even and feed the next funnel in the stack; and the recipe has been adapted to the client's specific offer and business category — complete enough to launch with no missing step.

### 19. The Amateur-to-Expert Gap

1. **Blank canvas vs. proven recipe:** Amateurs invent; experts select and adapt one of the 22 tested funnels.
2. **Pretty vs. converting:** Amateurs optimize for looks; experts optimize for the one action per page.
3. **Front-end profit vs. break-even acquisition:** Amateurs chase profit on funnel #1; experts break even to buy a customer and stack.
4. **Single sale vs. cart maximization:** Amateurs stop at the sale; experts always add bump + OTO + downsell.
5. **One funnel vs. funnel stacking:** Amateurs build one funnel; experts ascend customers across book → webinar → high-ticket.
6. **Distraction vs. focus:** Amateurs clutter opt-in pages; experts strip them to curiosity + input + button.
7. **Dead funnel vs. follow-up:** Amateurs let drop-offs vanish; experts wire Message #1/#2/#3 automation.
8. **Generic vs. business-specific:** Amateurs copy a template literally; experts run the "In Your Business" adaptation for their category.
