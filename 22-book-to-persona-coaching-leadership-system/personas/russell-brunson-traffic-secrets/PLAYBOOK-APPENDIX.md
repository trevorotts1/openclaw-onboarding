## A — Headline, Hook & Subject-Line Formula Bank

The governing law of every formula below (S3): an ad or post lives or dies on the **Hook** — the 1–3 second pattern-interrupt that stops the scroll. A hook must create enough curiosity or relevance that the dream customer thinks *"wait — I need to know more"* and stops. Vague, generic, or "boosted" content has no hook and gets no traffic. Use these as scroll-stopping ad/post hooks, thumbnail/headline hooks, and Dream-100 outreach openers.

### A1 — The Pattern-Interrupt scroll-stopper
- **When to use:** The opening frame of any paid or organic feed creative; the single highest-leverage hook in the book.
- **Pattern:** `[Visually or verbally jarring image/claim that breaks the expected feed] → [relevance to the dream customer's pain]`
- **Worked example:** An unexpected image + a line like "I almost lost everything because of ONE thing nobody warned me about…" — the interrupt earns the stop, the relevance earns the next second.
- **Source:** S3

### A2 — The Curiosity-Gap "you won't believe what" hook
- **When to use:** Opening a story-driven ad/post where the click is the goal.
- **Pattern:** `[Specific surprising outcome] + [withheld mechanism]` → reader must keep going to close the gap.
- **Worked example:** "How I filled my funnel with my dream customers — without spending a dollar on ads (at first)."
- **Source:** S3

### A3 — The "Where are they hiding?" relevance hook (audience call-out)
- **When to use:** Calling out the exact dream customer so the right person self-selects and the wrong person scrolls on.
- **Pattern:** `Attention [exact identity/role]: [their specific pain or desire]…`
- **Worked example:** "Attention course creators who are tired of posting into the void…"
- **Source:** S1/S3

### A4 — The Result-in-Advance / give-value-first hook
- **When to use:** Earning your way in — lead with a free, usable win before any ask (Frank Kern's "results in advance"; the law of reciprocity).
- **Pattern:** `Here's [a complete, usable thing] — free, no opt-in needed: [deliver value] → [soft next step]`
- **Worked example:** A full mini-tutorial or template given away in the post itself, which earns the relationship and the reciprocity.
- **Source:** S3/S4

### A5 — The Specific-Number proof hook
- **When to use:** When a precise, oddly specific number out-pulls a round claim.
- **Pattern:** `[Verb] [exact, oddly specific number/%] [result] [in a specific timeframe]`
- **Worked example:** "The 3 places my dream customers were already hiding — and how 1 of them sent me 2,300 leads."
- **Source:** S3

### A6 — The WARNING / pattern-interrupt frame
- **When to use:** A jaded, over-marketed-to audience; the warning frame flips skepticism into intrigue.
- **Pattern:** `WARNING: if you're [doing the common thing], you're [paying for traffic you'll lose] — here's why.`
- **Worked example:** "WARNING: you don't own your followers. One algorithm change and they're gone."
- **Source:** S3/OWN

### A7 — The "I got burned" origin hook
- **When to use:** Opening with your own failure to build trust through vulnerability (the PPC-wipeout parable).
- **Pattern:** `I built my whole business on [one rented source] — then [it changed overnight] and I lost [it]. Here's what I do now.`
- **Worked example:** The author's own search-ads wipeout, reframed as the lesson that you must own your traffic.
- **Source:** OWN

### A8 — The Contrarian Command hook
- **When to use:** Pattern-interrupt for a tired market; tell them to STOP the obvious move.
- **Pattern:** `Stop [the conventional traffic action]. Start [the contrarian one].`
- **Worked example:** "Stop trying to go viral. Start building traffic you actually own."
- **Source:** S3

### A9 — The "even if…" objection-crush hook
- **When to use:** Audiences whose #1 fear is "this won't work for me."
- **Pattern:** `[Get the traffic result] — even if [you have no audience / no budget / no list yet]`
- **Worked example:** "Fill your funnel with dream customers — even if nobody knows who you are yet."
- **Source:** S3

### A10 — The Dream-100 outreach DM opener (value-first, not pitch-first)
- **When to use:** First touch with a Dream 100 influencer/host — earning your way in.
- **Pattern:** `[Genuine specific compliment on their work] + [one piece of value with no ask]`
- **Worked example:** "Your episode on X changed how I run my outreach — I made you a quick [resource] based on it, here it is, no strings."
- **Source:** S4

### A11 — The Podcast-Guest pitch hook
- **When to use:** Pitching yourself onto shows that already hold your dream customers.
- **Pattern:** `I'd love to teach your audience "[The N Biggest Mistakes [their audience] make with [topic]]."`
- **Worked example:** "I'd love to come on and teach your listeners 'The 3 Biggest Traffic Mistakes Coaches Make.'"
- **Source:** S4

### A12 — The Integration / JV proposal hook (reciprocity offer)
- **When to use:** Proposing a value-exchange deal to a Dream 100 list-holder.
- **Pattern:** `I'll [drive value to your audience/list] in exchange for [a mention/integration to mine] — you win first.`
- **Worked example:** "I'll create a free training your list will love and promote you to mine — you get the goodwill, I get the intro."
- **Source:** S4

### A13 — The "Conversation Domination" omnipresence hook
- **When to use:** Retargeting / being everywhere so the dream customer keeps seeing you across the platform.
- **Pattern:** `[Same core message] re-cut for feed + stories + video + DM` so the customer "sees you everywhere."
- **Worked example:** One hook deployed as a reel, a story, a carousel, and a retargeting ad so you own the conversation.
- **Source:** PUB

### A14 — The Time-Box promise hook
- **When to use:** Make the traffic result feel near and finite.
- **Pattern:** `[Build the audience/traffic result] in [short, specific timeframe]`
- **Worked example:** "How I found my first 100 dream customers in 7 days using a list I already had access to."
- **Source:** S3

### A15 — The Polarity / take-a-stand hook (Attractive Character)
- **When to use:** Build followership by being polarizing on purpose — repel the wrong people, magnetize the right ones.
- **Pattern:** `[Stake a clear, divisive position your dream customer agrees with] — [why the other side is wrong].`
- **Worked example:** "Chasing virality is a trap. Here's why owned traffic beats it every single time."
- **Source:** S3

### A16 — The "Secret Telling" curiosity hook
- **When to use:** Promising hidden/forbidden knowledge the dream customer doesn't have.
- **Pattern:** `The [secret/underground/loophole] way [your dream customers] get [result] that nobody talks about.`
- **Worked example:** "The 'underground' way to get on any influencer's radar without paying a cent."
- **Source:** S3

### A17 — The Before/After transformation hook
- **When to use:** One staggering before→after carries the whole creative.
- **Pattern:** `[Where I was] → [where I am] → [the one traffic change that did it]`
- **Worked example:** "From posting to crickets → to a funnel full of buyers — one Dream 100 move did it."
- **Source:** S3

### A18 — The Social-Proof "join the others" sub-hook
- **When to use:** The line under the hook; converts curiosity into a herd-safe action.
- **Pattern:** `Join [the specific group of dream customers] who already [get the benefit].`
- **Worked example:** "Join the founders who stopped renting traffic and started owning it."
- **Source:** S3

### A19 — The Thumbnail/Headline curiosity hook (video platforms)
- **When to use:** YouTube/Reels thumbnails and titles where the hook is visual + 3–5 words.
- **Pattern:** `[Bold face/visual] + [3–5 word curiosity gap]`
- **Worked example:** "They Hid My Dream Customers" / "I Lost ALL My Traffic."
- **Source:** PUB

### A20 — The Affiliate/Linchpin recruit hook (growth hacking)
- **When to use:** Recruiting partners/affiliates to send you their traffic (integration/viral loop).
- **Pattern:** `Promote [my thing] to your audience and earn [reward]; I make it copy-paste easy.`
- **Worked example:** "Send your audience my free training and earn [X] on every sale — swipe copy included."
- **Source:** S4/PUB

## B — Funnel & Page Recipes (page-by-page)

*Traffic Secrets* teaches ONE master traffic system and its component "recipes." Below is the full set: the master flow, the Dream 100 build, both doors, the platform publishing/ads recipes, the convert-to-owned funnel hub, and the follow-up. (Because this is a traffic book, "recipes" are traffic/distribution flows, not landing-page block orders — for landing-page block recipes route to russell-brunson-lead-funnels §B.)

### B0 — MASTER RECIPE: The Dream 100 Traffic System (the whole flow)
- **When to use:** Any traffic/audience-building build. This is the spine all other recipes hang on.
- **Pattern (step order + job of each):**
  1. `DREAM CUSTOMER` — *job: define WHO.* One specific person, their pain + language.
  2. `DREAM 100` — *job: define WHERE.* The ~100 places that person already congregates.
  3. `WORK YOUR WAY IN` — *job: earn access (free).* Value, reciprocity, integration, getting featured.
  4. `BUY YOUR WAY IN` — *job: buy access (paid).* Ads to their audiences, sponsorships, solo ads.
  5. `HOOK-STORY-OFFER` — *job: stop + sell.* The creative that earns the click.
  6. `FILL YOUR FUNNEL` — *job: convert to OWNED.* Route the click into a capturing funnel (email).
  7. `FOLLOW-UP FUNNEL` — *job: nurture forever.* Soap Opera → Seinfeld sequence (Section D).
  8. `PUBLISHING PLAN` — *job: dominate one platform, then go omnipresent.*
- **Worked example:** Pick a niche → name the dream customer → list 100 podcasts/channels/groups/influencers → provide value to 50 (earn) and run audience ads to the other 50 (buy) → each touch is Hook-Story-Offer → all traffic hits an opt-in funnel → captured leads enter a follow-up sequence → master one platform, then syndicate.
- **Source:** S1–S5, PUB, OWN

### B1 — DREAM 100 build recipe
- **When to use:** Step 2 of any system; the targeting map.
- **Pattern (required ingredients):** `[Named dream customer] → list ~100 across 6 buckets: Influencers/Personalities · Podcasts · YouTube channels · Blogs/sites · Facebook & other groups · Forums/communities` → tag each EARN or BUY → prioritize by audience fit + accessibility → track outreach status in a living sheet.
- **Worked example:** For a fitness-coach offer: list the top 100 fitness podcasts, IG accounts, YouTube channels, subreddits, and FB groups your exact dream customer already follows; mark which you can guest on (earn) vs. advertise to (buy).
- **Source:** S2 (concept credited to Chet Holmes, *The Ultimate Sales Machine*)

### B2 — WORK YOUR WAY IN recipe (earn the door)
- **When to use:** Step 3; the free door — builds relationships and reciprocity.
- **Pattern (block order):**
  1. `FOLLOW + ENGAGE` — follow every Dream 100 target; genuinely engage. *Job: get on the radar.*
  2. `PROVIDE VALUE FIRST` — give a result-in-advance with no ask (reciprocity). *Job: earn goodwill.*
  3. `BUILD THE RELATIONSHIP` — become useful and known over time. *Job: trust.*
  4. `MAKE THE INTEGRATION ASK` — guest spot, JV, collab, mention, takeover. *Job: borrow their audience.*
  5. `CONVERT THEIR AUDIENCE` — send that earned attention to your funnel (B6). *Job: own it.*
- **Worked example:** Reciprocity → get interviewed on the Dream 100 podcast → point listeners to your funnel URL; or guest-post and put the funnel link in your bio.
- **Source:** S4

### B3 — BUY YOUR WAY IN recipe (buy the door)
- **When to use:** Step 3; the paid door — instant, controllable, scalable.
- **Pattern (block order):**
  1. `IDENTIFY THE AUDIENCE` — target the Dream 100's followers/interests/lookalikes/email lists. *Job: aim precisely.*
  2. `CREATIVE = HOOK-STORY-OFFER` — build the ad per §E3/§A. *Job: stop + sell.*
  3. `ROUTE TO FUNNEL` — send to an opt-in, never a homepage (B6). *Job: capture.*
  4. `RETARGET` — re-show across the platform (conversation domination). *Job: omnipresence.*
  5. `MEASURE BY OWNED-LIST GROWTH` — judge by opt-ins/CPL, then nurture (D). *Job: convert to owned.*
- **Worked example:** Run ads to the audience of a Dream 100 influencer; the ad is a Hook-Story-Offer to a free lead magnet; retarget non-opt-ins; nurture opt-ins via the follow-up funnel.
- **Source:** S4

### B4 — HOOK-STORY-OFFER creative recipe (per asset)
- **When to use:** Step 5; every ad/post/video.
- **Pattern (block order):**
  1. `HOOK` — pattern-interrupt that stops the scroll (§A). *Job: earn the 3 seconds.*
  2. `STORY` — build desire + crush objections via the Attractive Character (§E4). *Job: create want.*
  3. `OFFER` — the CTA into the funnel. *Job: drive the click.*
- **Worked example:** Hook ("I lost all my traffic overnight") → Story (the wipeout + the lesson) → Offer ("grab my free Dream 100 cheat sheet").
- **Source:** S3

### B5 — ATTRACTIVE CHARACTER setup recipe
- **When to use:** Underlies all creative + outreach voice; defines WHO is speaking.
- **Pattern (ingredients):** pick `IDENTITY` (Leader · Adventurer/Crusader · Reporter/Evangelist · Reluctant Hero) + add `ELEMENTS` (Backstory · Parables · Character flaws · Polarity) + choose `STORYLINES` (Loss & Redemption · Us vs. Them · Before & After · Amazing Discovery · Secret Telling · Third-Person Testimonial).
- **Worked example:** A "Reluctant Hero" who got burned by rented traffic (backstory + flaw), now crusades for owned traffic (polarity + us-vs-them), telling Loss-&-Redemption stories.
- **Source:** S3

### B6 — FILL YOUR FUNNEL / convert-to-OWNED recipe (the funnel hub)
- **When to use:** Step 6; where rented traffic becomes owned.
- **Pattern (block order):**
  1. `DESTINATION = FUNNEL, NOT HOMEPAGE` — every source points to an opt-in. *Job: capture.*
  2. `LEAD MAGNET + OPT-IN` — trade value for the email (hand off detail to lead-funnel persona §B). *Job: own it.*
  3. `LAYER SECONDARY LISTS` — Messenger/SMS/push as additional owned channels. *Job: multiply ownership.*
  4. `ENTER FOLLOW-UP` — drop new owned leads into the sequence (Section D). *Job: nurture.*
- **Worked example:** A Dream 100 podcast guest spot → listeners go to yoursite.com/freegift → opt-in → Soap Opera Sequence.
- **Source:** S5/OWN

### B7 — PLATFORM PUBLISHING recipe (organic, per platform)
- **When to use:** Building owned/earned reach on your dominant platform (Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, Google/SEO, etc.).
- **Pattern (block order):**
  1. `LEARN THE PLATFORM'S CULTURE` — how its native content + algorithm reward behavior. *Job: fit in.*
  2. `PICK YOUR FORMAT` — writer→blog/SEO, talker→podcast, video→YouTube/Reels. *Job: play to strength.*
  3. `PUBLISH DAILY / DOCUMENT THE JOURNEY` — be 1–2 steps ahead of your audience. *Job: build the stadium.*
  4. `HOOK EVERY POST` — §A discipline on every piece. *Job: stop the scroll.*
  5. `ROUTE TO FUNNEL` — bio link / pinned post / CTA into B6. *Job: convert to owned.*
- **Worked example:** Choose YouTube; learn its culture; publish daily how-to videos with curiosity thumbnails (§A19); link the funnel in every description.
- **Source:** PUB

### B8 — PLATFORM ADS recipe (paid, per platform)
- **When to use:** The paid mirror of B7 on the same platform.
- **Pattern (block order):** `TARGET THE DREAM 100's AUDIENCE` → `HOOK-STORY-OFFER creative native to the platform` → `ROUTE TO FUNNEL` → `RETARGET (conversation domination)` → `SCALE WINNERS, KILL LOSERS`.
- **Worked example:** Same YouTube channel, now running in-stream/discovery ads to the dream customer with a Hook-Story-Offer to a lead magnet.
- **Source:** PUB/S4

### B9 — OMNIPRESENCE / "Be Everywhere" syndication recipe
- **When to use:** After you've dominated ONE platform — expand without going thin.
- **Pattern (block order):** `MASTER ONE platform first` → `REPURPOSE the core content into every other platform's native format` → `the funnel hub stays constant` → `retarget across platforms` so the dream customer "sees you everywhere."
- **Worked example:** Record one video → strip audio to a podcast → transcribe to a blog post (SEO) → cut clips for Reels/Shorts → all link back to the same funnel.
- **Source:** PUB

## D — Email & Sequence Bank

The book's follow-up motion converts owned leads into buyers via two sequences Brunson teaches across his work and references here: the **Soap Opera Sequence** (a 5-email bonding arc for new leads) and the **Seinfeld** daily emails (ongoing value + offer). The Dream-100 nurture is the third sequence. Captured faithfully as architecture + per-message roles; long verbatim drip copy is not published in the source, so bodies are patternized (fidelity = MODERATE; see H).

### D1 — The Soap Opera Sequence (new-lead bonding, 5 emails, in order)

**Email 1 — Set the Stage (T+0)**
- **Role:** Welcome, deliver what was promised, and tease the drama to come so they open the next ones.
- **Subject pattern:** `Welcome — your [free thing] is inside (and a quick story…)`
- **Body skeleton:** `[Warm welcome + deliver the asset] → [open a loop: "tomorrow I'll tell you how I almost quit"] → [Attractive-Character sign-off].`
- **Timing:** Immediately on opt-in.

**Email 2 — High Drama / Backstory (T+1 day)**
- **Role:** Tell the Loss-&-Redemption backstory; relate to their pain.
- **Subject pattern:** `The day I lost everything…`
- **Body skeleton:** `[Hit the lowest point / the wall] → [relate it to their struggle] → [tease the turning point tomorrow].`
- **Timing:** Day after Email 1.

**Email 3 — Epiphany / The Wall (T+2 days)**
- **Role:** The discovery that changed everything (the new opportunity / mechanism).
- **Subject pattern:** `Then I discovered the one thing that changed it all`
- **Body skeleton:** `[The epiphany] → [what it unlocked] → [bridge toward the offer].`
- **Timing:** +1 day.

**Email 4 — Hidden Benefits (T+3 days)**
- **Role:** Surface benefits they hadn't considered; handle objections.
- **Subject pattern:** `What nobody tells you about [the result]`
- **Body skeleton:** `[Unexpected benefit/objection-crush] → [proof/parable] → [soft CTA to the offer].`
- **Timing:** +1 day.

**Email 5 — Urgency & CTA (T+4 days)**
- **Role:** Clear call to action with a real reason to act now.
- **Subject pattern:** `Last call: [reason for urgency]`
- **Body skeleton:** `[Recap the transformation] → [the offer] → [genuine urgency/deadline] → [direct CTA].`
- **Timing:** +1 day.

### D2 — The Seinfeld Daily Sequence (ongoing, evergreen)
- **Role:** Daily/near-daily "about nothing" entertainment + value emails that each pivot to a soft offer; keeps the owned list warm and monetizes forever.
- **Pattern per email:** `[Hook subject from §A] → [short entertaining story or observation] → [tie to a lesson] → [soft CTA / link to a funnel].`
- **Worked example:** Story-driven emails (episodic, character-rich) each ending with a link; mix value, polarity, and offers.
- **Timing:** Ongoing after the Soap Opera Sequence completes.

### D3 — The Dream-100 Nurture Sequence (relationship, not list)
- **Role:** The slow-burn touch cadence for Dream 100 *targets* (not customers) while working your way in.
- **Pattern:** `[Value-first DM/email] → wait → [reciprocity follow-up] → wait → [collab/integration ask]` (mirror C1→C2→C4); track each target's status in the B1 sheet.
- **Timing:** Weeks-long; relationship-paced, never pitch-paced.

## E — Frameworks, Models & Templates (with the steps)

Every named model in the book, reproduced with its internal steps + a worked example.

### E1 — The Three Types of Traffic (the ownership thesis)
- **Steps:** Classify every source as **CONTROL** (paid ads/solo ads — you aim it but rent it), **EARN** (organic/PR/SEO/virality/mentions — free but unpredictable, also rented), or **OWN** (your email/SMS list + your own audiences — the only kind a platform can't revoke). Goal: funnel ALL controlled + earned traffic into OWNED.
- **Worked example:** The PPC-wipeout parable: a business built on one rented source vanished when the rules changed; rebuilt on an owned list it controls.
- **Source:** OWN

### E2 — The Dream 100
- **Steps:** Name the dream customer → list ~100 people/places they already congregate around (influencers, podcasts, channels, blogs, groups, forums) → tag each EARN vs BUY → prioritize → work the list. Credited to Chet Holmes, *The Ultimate Sales Machine*.
- **Worked example:** A niche's top 100 congregations become the entire targeting strategy.
- **Source:** S2

### E3 — Hook, Story, Offer
- **Steps:** **Hook** — pattern-interrupt that stops the scroll (§A). **Story** — build desire and crush objections through the Attractive Character. **Offer** — the CTA that drives to the funnel. Every ad, post, email, and video uses all three.
- **Worked example:** Hook (wipeout) → Story (lesson) → Offer (free cheat sheet to a funnel).
- **Source:** S3

### E4 — The Attractive Character
- **Steps:** Choose an **identity** (Leader / Adventurer or Crusader / Reporter or Evangelist / Reluctant Hero) → add **elements** (Backstory, Parables, Character flaws, Polarity) → tell across **storylines** (Loss & Redemption, Us vs. Them, Before & After, Amazing Discovery, Secret Telling, Third-Person Testimonial). People follow a character, not a logo.
- **Worked example:** Reluctant-Hero who got burned → crusades for owned traffic with polarity + loss-and-redemption stories.
- **Source:** S3

### E5 — Work Your Way In + Buy Your Way In (the two doors)
- **Steps:** For each Dream 100 target, run BOTH: (1) **Work your way in** — follow, engage, provide value first (reciprocity), build the relationship, then ask for an integration/feature. (2) **Buy your way in** — target their audience with paid ads, sponsorships, solo ads, shoutouts. Earning is durable; buying is instant and scalable; together they compound.
- **Worked example:** Guest on the podcast (earn) AND run ads to the same show's audience (buy).
- **Source:** S4

### E6 — Fill Your Funnel
- **Steps:** Take the attention earned/bought from the Dream 100 and route it into a funnel that captures an email — never a homepage. Layer secondary owned channels (Messenger/SMS/push). The opt-in is the moment rented becomes owned.
- **Worked example:** Every Dream 100 touch ends at yoursite.com/gift, not the homepage.
- **Source:** S5

### E7 — Who → Where → Bait (the three questions)
- **Steps:** (1) **WHO** is your dream customer (one person)? (2) **WHERE** are they hiding (the Dream 100)? (3) What **HOOK/BAIT** pulls them out (the Hook)? Answer in order; you can't do 2 without 1, or 3 without 2.
- **Worked example:** Coach → fitness founders → "the void-posting trap" hook.
- **Source:** S1–S3

### E8 — The Law of Reciprocity (results in advance)
- **Steps:** Give value BEFORE any ask; the dream customer and the Dream 100 feel a pull to reciprocate. Lead with a complete, usable result-in-advance (Frank Kern), not a pitch.
- **Worked example:** Give a free resource to a Dream 100 host before ever asking for a guest spot.
- **Source:** S4

### E9 — The Publishing Plan (dominate one → omnipresence)
- **Steps:** Pick ONE platform that fits your strength (write→blog/SEO, talk→podcast, video→YouTube) → learn its culture → publish daily / "document the journey" (be 1–2 steps ahead) → master it and build your stadium → THEN repurpose into every other platform (be everywhere). Ship at ~80%; treat hooks like a comedian tests jokes — promote the winners.
- **Worked example:** Record once → podcast + blog (transcribe) + Reels/Shorts clips → all link the same funnel.
- **Source:** PUB

### E10 — Conversation Domination (omnipresence + retargeting)
- **Steps:** Be everywhere your dream customer looks on a platform — feed, stories, video, ads, retargeting — so they "see you everywhere" and you own the conversation. Re-cut one core message into every native format.
- **Worked example:** One hook deployed as a reel, story, carousel, and retargeting ad.
- **Source:** PUB

### E11 — Growth Hacking / Integration Marketing
- **Steps:** Find viral loops and integration points — partner where one company's traffic feeds yours (the "linchpin"); recruit affiliates; engineer share/referral mechanics; insert your offer into existing distribution.
- **Worked example:** An affiliate/linchpin deal where a Dream 100 partner's audience flows into your funnel (model of integration marketing).
- **Source:** S4/PUB

### E12 — The Funnel Hub (your owned platform)
- **Steps:** Build a central hub you own (site/funnel ecosystem) that all earned + controlled traffic points to and that captures every visitor into an owned list; rented channels orbit the hub, they don't replace it.
- **Worked example:** Every podcast, post, and ad routes to your hub's opt-in, not a third-party profile.
- **Source:** S5/OWN

### E13 — The Stadium of Dream Customers (the mental model)
- **Steps:** Picture your dream customers as a full stadium that already exists; someone is already filling it. Your job is to identify the stadium (Dream 100) and walk in through both doors — not to build a crowd from scratch.
- **Worked example:** "They're already gathered — go to where they are."
- **Source:** S1/S2

## F — Brand Voice & Brand-Building Language Patterns

Russell Brunson's reusable voice machinery, as ready-to-use lists with example lines. This is what lets a copy/traffic specialist write ON-brand and richly instead of thinly.

### F1 — "Boom!" — the punctuation of completion
- `Find the stadium, walk in both doors, and **boom!** — your funnel's full of dream customers.`

### F2 — Reader-check rhetorical tags
- `Does that make sense?` / `Repeat after me:` — used to land a point and pull the reader along.

### F3 — Self-deprecating origin story (the wipeout)
- Open with your own failure: "I built my whole business on one rented traffic source — then it changed overnight and I lost it all." Vulnerability earns trust.

### F4 — The Stadium metaphor (audience-already-exists framing)
- `Somewhere there's a stadium full of your dream customers — and someone is already filling it. Let's go get them.`

### F5 — The "three types of traffic" drumbeat
- `Traffic you control, traffic you earn, traffic you own — and the goal is always to convert it into traffic you own.`

### F6 — Rhetorical-question objection voicing
- Voice the reader's inner objection, then answer it: `Where are they hiding? They're already gathered — you just have to find the room.`

### F7 — The "two doors" framing
- `There are only two ways in: you work your way in, or you buy your way in. Do both.`

### F8 — "even if…" objection-crush tail
- `Fill your funnel with dream customers — even if no one knows who you are yet.`

### F9 — Contrarian command opener
- `Stop chasing virality. Start building traffic you own.`

### F10 — The curiosity trigger phrase
- `Wait — I need to know more.` — the literal feeling every Hook must create.

### F11 — Permission-to-ship language
- `Done is better than perfect.` / `Get it to 80%, then publish — document the journey.`

### F12 — Power-word bank (use)
- Dream customer · Dream 100 · Stadium · Hook · Story · Offer · Own it · Hiding · Congregating · Reciprocity · Underground · Loophole · Omnipresent · Linchpin · Results in advance.
- Example: `the 'underground' way to get on any influencer's radar`.

### F13 — Words/phrases to AVOID
- "Boost the post," "go viral and hope," "target everyone," "the followers are enough," "we'll build a list later," vague "engagement." (Faceless, hookless, broadly-targeted language gets no traffic.)

### F14 — Naming patterns (frameworks/mechanisms)
- `The [Number] [Secret/Type]` · `The Dream 100` · `Work Your Way In / Buy Your Way In` · `Hook, Story, Offer` · `The [Platform] [Publishing/Ads] Framework`.

### F15 — Social-proof / credibility language

### F17 — Offer / CTA language
- `Grab my free [lead magnet] — drop your email and I'll send it right now.` · `Listeners can get [free thing] at [yoursite.com/gift].`

### F18 — Urgency / FOMO language
- `Every click you don't capture is traffic you paid for and gave away.` · `One algorithm change and rented traffic is gone — own it now.`

### F19 — Reciprocity / give-first language
- `Give value first; the door opens on its own.` · `Lead with results in advance, not a pitch.`

### F20 — "Be everywhere" / omnipresence framing
- `Dominate one platform, then go omnipresent — be everywhere your dream customer looks.`

### F21 — The "document the journey" authority frame
- `You don't have to be the expert — just be one or two steps ahead, and document the journey.`
