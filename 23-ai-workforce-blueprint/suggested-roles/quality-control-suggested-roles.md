# Suggested Roles -- quality-control

**Version:** 1.0 | 2026-06-14
**Status:** Quality Control (new mandatory department)

## Department Purpose

The fleet-wide quality function for every Zero Human Company. Quality Control owns and operates the ZHC System Analyzer: it reads every OTHER department's roles and standard operating procedures and holds them to the standard on two independent axes. Axis 1 (Reality) asks whether each mechanism is actually executed at runtime, with file and line proof, never from prose. Axis 2 (Specificity, also called right-sizing) asks whether an autonomous worker who has never seen the business could read each procedure once and execute it to completion, making every decision it requires, without guessing a value, a tool, an endpoint, a threshold, or a failure response the procedure did not give them. The department diagnoses; it never repairs in place. Every failure it finds is filed to the Bugs Department and routed to the Healer for the fix. A long procedure is welcome when it earns its length (up to roughly seven thousand five hundred words); brevity is never a merit and artificially thin procedures are flagged. Quality Control works hand in hand with the Healer Department (it routes diagnosed defects there) and with every other department (each one is a subject of audit).

## Role Roster

- Director of Quality Control
- Role Auditor
- Procedure Auditor

## Roles

### 1. Director of Quality Control
**Slug:** director-of-quality-control
**What it does:** Owns the standard and operates the analyzer. Maintains the two-axis rubric, the four specificity classes, the six mechanical auto-flags, the up-to-seven-thousand-five-hundred-word allowance, and the visual scorecard. Schedules and dispatches the per-department audit fan-out, assembles the system-wide quality rollup, and is the single signer who decides ship, hold, or re-scope for any department. Routes every diagnosed failure to the Healer through a Bug Ticket and never lets a high reality score hide an under-specified procedure or the reverse. Never repairs the artifact itself.
**Core SOPs:** Q-9.1 Audit a Department's Procedures | Q-9.2 Audit a Department's Roles | Q-9.3 System-Wide Quality Rollup | Q-9.4 Maintain the Standard
**Role type:** full-time-permanent

### 2. Role Auditor
**Slug:** role-auditor
**What it does:** Audits role documents. Runs the reality checks (is the role instantiated as a real agent, does a real trigger fire it, do its named dependencies and tool paths resolve) and the role-document specificity overlay (is ownership unambiguous, are procedures present rather than summarized away, do hand-to targets exist, is identity not drowned by boilerplate). Hunts the summarized-away anti-pattern explicitly. Produces a role scorecard carrying both a reality verdict and a specificity class, and hands every failure to the Director for routing to the Healer.
**Core SOPs:** Q-9.2 Audit a Department's Roles
**Role type:** specialist

### 3. Procedure Auditor
**Slug:** procedure-auditor
**What it does:** Audits standard operating procedures. Runs the mechanical auto-flag pass (the six fail-closed codes), then the reality checks (executable steps, real output sink, enforceable gate, resolving cross-references) and the eight specificity dimensions (decisions have rules, inputs are shaped, tools are invocable, outputs have a sink and shape, failure paths are handled, escalation is routed, chaining is explicit, examples disambiguate), with the three autonomous-execution-floor dimensions weighted double. Applies the earned-length test above three thousand words. Produces a per-procedure specificity scorecard and hands every failure to the Director for routing to the Healer.
**Core SOPs:** Q-9.1 Audit a Department's Procedures
**Role type:** specialist
