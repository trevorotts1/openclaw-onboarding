## 1. What This Department Does (in plain language)

The Healer Department is the company's doctor. When something in the AI workforce
breaks, misfires, or keeps repeating the same mistake, this is the department that
diagnoses the root cause, applies the fix, and then makes the company smarter so
the same defect cannot happen again.

It handles: bug tickets routed from the Bugs Department, SOP surgery (updating or
writing the procedures that caused a failure), JSON and settings repair, model
currency checks (making sure no outdated AI model is in production), embedding and
retrieval index refreshes, and the weekly company healing digest that tells you
what broke and what was fixed.

The Healer also has one embedded specialist inside every other department. Those
embedded Healers handle that department's day-to-day incidents. The Chief Healer
holds oversight over all of them and owns any failure that is systemic (the same
problem showing up in two or more departments).

In one sentence: **The Healer Department fixes what breaks and rebuilds the
company so the same thing cannot break twice.**

You do not need to know which specialist does what. You just tell the department
what you want in plain English, and the Chief Healer figures out who handles it
and routes it.

## 2. When to Use It

Reach for this department when you want any of the following:

- An agent or department keeps making the same mistake and you want it fixed at
  the root -- not just for this one task but permanently.
- A SOP, setting, or configuration file needs to be repaired or rewritten.
- You suspect an outdated AI model is being used somewhere in your workforce.
- A workflow crashed and the sub-agent cannot recover on its own.
- You want a full incident report: what failed, why, and what was done to prevent
  recurrence.
- The Bugs Department has flagged a ticket and the fix requires changes to
  procedures or core files rather than just rerunning the task.
- You want the monthly model census: a plain-language audit of every AI model
  used across all departments.

If you are not sure whether a request belongs here or in the Bugs Department, ask
anyway. The Bugs Department handles the ticket; the Healer Department does the
surgery. The Healer will sort it out.

## 3. How to Ask It for Work

You have three ways to put this department to work. All of them are fine.

1. **Just say it in plain English.** Message your agent like you would a teammate:
   "Something keeps going wrong with X -- I need it fixed properly." That is
   enough to start.
2. **Name the department if you want to be specific.** "Have the Healer
   Department look at why X keeps failing." This routes it straight to the Chief
   Healer.
3. **Name a specialist if you know exactly who you want.** See the specialist
   list in Section 4 and ask for them by role: "Get the Deep Research Specialist
   in Healing to research the correct fix for this model error."

A good request includes, where it applies: **what** broke, **when** it started,
**how often** it happens, and any **error messages or symptoms** you saw. You do
not have to provide all of that. If something important is missing, the department first offers you a quick or an
in-depth path, then asks one focused question at a time (never a wall of
questions) and waits for each answer. It gathers what you tell it into a single
brief before the work starts, rather than guessing.

**Chief Healer**

Example request: "Before we apply that settings change, have the Devil's
Advocate tell us what could go wrong."

## 5. What to Expect Back

When you ask this department for something, here is the normal flow:

1. **Acknowledgment.** The Chief Healer confirms the request landed and, if
   anything important is unclear, asks one focused question at a time (never a wall of questions).
2. **Diagnosis.** The root cause is identified on evidence -- not assumption.
   The Deep Research Specialist is called in when the fix is not obvious.
3. **The fix.** Applied at the right authority tier: mechanical fixes go in
   immediately; SOP patches are versioned, logged, and reported; anything
   constitutional is proposed to you first and waits for your approval.
4. **Delivery.** You get: a plain-language incident report (what broke, root
   cause, what was done, what changed), any updated SOP or setting files,
   and the regression check added to prevent recurrence. For systemic issues
   across multiple departments, you also get the weekly healing digest.

## 6. How It Hands Off (to you and to other departments)

- **To you:** the healing report arrives with a plain-language summary. Anything
  that is a Tier 3 change (model manifest, pricing, brand, architecture) is
  proposed to you in writing and waits for your approval before it is applied.
- **To other departments:** when a fix requires a SOP change in another
  department, the Chief Healer authors the patch and directs that department's
  embedded Healer to apply it locally.
- **To the Bugs Department:** once healing is complete, the original bug ticket
  is closed with the resolution documented.
- **Escalation:** if a fix requires a credential, payment, or a decision only
  you can make, you are notified directly rather than stalling silently.
