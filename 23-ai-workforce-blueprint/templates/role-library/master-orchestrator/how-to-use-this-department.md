## 1. What This Department Does (in plain language)

The Master Orchestrator is the CEO-level agent at the top of your AI workforce.
Every message you send lands here first. The Orchestrator reads it, figures out
which department or specialist owns it, routes it there with clear instructions,
tracks that it gets done, and brings it back to you when it is finished.

It is the air traffic controller, not the pilot. It does not write your emails,
design your graphics, close your sales, or produce any deliverable. Its job is to
make sure every request reaches the right specialist and that the whole company
runs as a coordinated system.

The Orchestrator also answers "how do I use" questions about any department or
specialist from their plain-language guides, monitors department KPI dashboards,
surfaces blockers that need your attention, and protects your time from
operational noise.

In one sentence: **The Master Orchestrator receives every request, routes it to
the right department, and makes sure the whole AI company runs together.**

## 2. When to Use It

Reach for the Master Orchestrator when you want any of the following:

- You have a task or request but are not sure which department owns it -- just
  send it here and it will be routed.
- You want a status update on work in progress across your AI company.
- You want to know which department or specialist handles a specific kind of work.
- You have a question that spans multiple departments.
- Something is stuck or blocked and you want someone to sort it out.
- You want a company-level performance report (KPI dashboard, weekly digest).
- You want to escalate something that cannot be resolved at the department level.

The Orchestrator is also the right place for: "how do I use the [department]
department?" or "what does the [specialist] do?" It will read the relevant
plain-language guide and answer you.

## 3. How to Ask It for Work

You have two main ways:

1. **Just say what you want in plain English.** Message your agent the way you
   would message a smart EA: "I need a proposal for a new webinar." That is
   enough. The Orchestrator reads it, figures out which department owns it
   (Presentations? Marketing? Both?), and routes it.
2. **Tell it who you want if you know.** "Have the Marketing department draft a
   launch email." The Orchestrator will route it directly to Marketing.

If you want the Orchestrator to do something directly (not route it), you need
to say so explicitly in that message: "Handle this yourself -- do not route it."
Otherwise it routes.

For "how do I use" questions, just ask: "How do I use the Sales department?" or
"What does the Appointment Setter do?" The Orchestrator will look it up and answer
in plain language.

## 5. What to Expect Back

When you send a request to the Master Orchestrator, here is the normal flow:

1. **Acknowledgment.** The Orchestrator confirms the request landed and names the
   department it is being routed to. If anything important is unclear, it asks
   you one or two quick questions first.
2. **Routing.** The task is matched to the correct department and specialist and
   posted to the Command Center task board.
3. **Tracking.** The Orchestrator monitors that the work is progressing. If
   something stalls, it surfaces the blockage to you rather than going silent.
4. **Delivery.** The finished result comes back from the department that did the
   work. The Orchestrator notifies you and confirms it is complete.

For informational questions ("how do I use X?"), the response is immediate: the
Orchestrator reads the department's plain-language guide and answers you in plain
language right in the conversation.

## 6. How It Hands Off (to you and to other departments)

- **To you:** the Orchestrator escalates only what genuinely needs a human
  decision -- a credential it cannot access, a payment, a strategic call, or an
  approval before something goes live. Everything else is handled without
  interrupting you.
- **To departments:** every task arrives at the receiving department with a
  routing brief: what the task is, what department owns it, and what the
  Orchestrator expects back. The department does not have to guess.
- **Escalation:** if the Command Center is unreachable, the Orchestrator
  escalates via Telegram rather than attempting to execute the task directly.
