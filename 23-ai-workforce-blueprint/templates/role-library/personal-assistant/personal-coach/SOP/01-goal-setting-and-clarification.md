## Define

Goal Setting and Clarification helps the owner turn vague aspirations into clear, committed goals with a defined finish line. The coach's job is not to set goals for the owner but to draw them out, sharpen them, and make them measurable.

**Purpose of this procedure**
- Translate the owner's wants into specific, time-bound goals the owner truly owns.
- Ensure each goal has a clear definition of success so progress can be judged honestly.
- Surface and resolve goals that quietly conflict with one another.

**When to run**
- At the start of a coaching relationship, at the start of each planning period, and whenever the owner expresses a new aspiration.

**Frequency**
- At each planning cycle (e.g., quarterly) plus on-demand when a new goal surfaces.

**Inputs**
- The owner's stated aspirations, frustrations, and 'I wish I...' statements.
- Prior goals and their outcomes.
- The owner's current commitments and capacity.

**Outputs**
- A short list of clarified goals, each with a definition of success and a target date.
- A note of any conflicts between goals and how the owner chose to resolve them.

**Hand-to (downstream)**
- The Accountability and Progress Check procedure (PA-35-02) which tracks each goal, and the owner for final commitment.

**Definition of done**
- The owner has a small set of clear, owned, measurable goals — and has said out loud that they are committed to them.

## Measure

Goal clarity is measured by whether the owner can state, unprompted, what they are working toward and how they will know they have arrived.

**Signals this procedure tracks**
- The owner can restate each goal and its success measure without prompting.
- Goals are specific enough to act on this week.
- The number of active goals is small enough to actually pursue.

**Key metrics**
- **Clarity check** — owner restates each goal + success measure correctly; target 100%.
- **Active goal count** — number of goals in flight; keep small (typically 1-3) so focus is possible.
- **Commitment confirmation** — owner explicitly commits to each goal; required before tracking begins.

**Where the measurement is recorded**
- Clarified goals and the owner's commitment are recorded in the coaching MEMORY.md and reviewed each cycle.

## Analyze

Vague goals fail silently because there is no way to tell whether they are progressing. Analyze each stated aspiration for specificity, measurability, and genuine ownership.

**Decision rules**
- A goal is ready only when it has a concrete definition of success and a target date.
- If the owner has more than a few active goals, force-rank them — focus beats breadth.
- If two goals conflict (time, money, energy), surface it and make the owner choose, do not paper over it.

**Common failure modes and how to read them**
- **Vague aspiration:** 'Get healthier' with no measure. Convert to something observable and dated.
- **Borrowed goals:** Goals the owner thinks they 'should' want but does not own. Test for genuine motivation.
- **Goal overload:** Ten goals at once guarantees none get done. Narrow ruthlessly.

## Improve

The clarification process improves by asking sharper questions and by checking, cycle over cycle, which kinds of goals the owner actually follows through on.

**Step-by-step procedure**
1. Invite the owner to name what they want — listen for the aspiration behind the words.
2. For each aspiration, ask what success would concretely look like and by when.
3. Rewrite it as a clear goal: a specific outcome, a measure of success, and a target date.
4. Test ownership: ask why it matters to them; if the motivation is borrowed, set it aside.
5. If there are too many goals, force-rank and select the few that matter most this cycle.
6. Surface any conflict between goals and let the owner decide the trade-off explicitly.
7. Ask the owner to commit out loud to the final short list before tracking begins.

**Escalation path**
- If the owner cannot articulate what success looks like after genuine effort, slow down — the goal is not ready, and forcing it produces a goal that will not be pursued.

**Optimization loop**
- Keep a record of which goal types the owner completes vs. abandons; use it to set realistic goals.
- Refine the clarifying questions based on what unlocks specificity for this particular owner.

## Control

Control keeps this procedure stable and trustworthy over time. The guardrails below are non-negotiable; the review cadence catches drift; and the audit trail makes every run verifiable after the fact.

**Guardrails (never violate)**
- Never set a goal for the owner; draw it out and let them own it.
- Never start tracking a goal the owner has not explicitly committed to.
- Never let the active goal list grow beyond what can actually be pursued.

**Review cadence**
- Each planning cycle: re-clarify goals; confirm commitment; prune what is stale.

**Failure mode to actively prevent**
- Accepting a vague aspiration as a goal — with no success measure, progress can never be honestly judged.

**Audit trail**
- Clarified goals, success measures, and commitment are recorded each cycle.
- Conflicts and the owner's chosen trade-offs are logged.
