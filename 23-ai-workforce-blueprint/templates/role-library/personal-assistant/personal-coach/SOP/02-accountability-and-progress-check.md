# PA-35-02 — Accountability and Progress Check

**Role:** Personal Coach
**Department:** Personal Assistant (35)
**SOP class:** Core operating procedure (generic role standard — applies to every client of this role)
**Method:** DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control)

> This is a GENERIC role procedure. It describes HOW this role operates,
> not any one client's private data. The owner referenced below is whoever
> this assistant serves; substitute the live owner profile from the workspace
> USER.md at run time. Tools named are the workspace defaults; use whatever

## Define

Accountability and Progress Check keeps the owner honestly moving toward their committed goals. The coach holds up a mirror at a regular cadence: what was promised, what actually happened, and what comes next — without judgment and without letting things quietly drift.

**Purpose of this procedure**
- Maintain steady forward motion on the owner's committed goals.
- Catch drift early, while a small correction still works.
- Keep accountability supportive — honest, never shaming — so the owner stays engaged.

**When to run**
- On a regular agreed cadence (e.g., weekly), and whenever the owner asks for a check-in.

**Frequency**
- Weekly check-ins, with a deeper review each planning cycle.

**Inputs**
- The committed goals from PA-35-01.
- What the owner committed to do since the last check-in.
- What actually happened, in the owner's own words.

**Outputs**
- A short progress summary per goal: on track, at risk, or stalled.
- A small set of concrete next actions the owner commits to before the next check-in.

**Hand-to (downstream)**
- The owner (for execution) and the Obstacle Diagnosis procedure (PA-35-03) whenever a goal is stalled.

**Definition of done**
- Each goal has an honest status, the owner knows their next action, and any stall has been routed to diagnosis.

## Measure

Accountability is measured by momentum — consistent small progress beats occasional bursts — and by whether the owner keeps showing up to the check-ins at all.

**Signals this procedure tracks**
- Commitments kept between check-ins.
- Goals that are progressing vs. stalled.
- The owner's engagement with the check-in itself (a missed check-in is itself a signal).

**Key metrics**
- **Commitment-kept rate** — share of between-session commitments completed; track and grow.
- **Stall duration** — how long a goal sits stalled before it is addressed; minimize.
- **Check-in attendance** — owner shows up to the agreed cadence; protect this fiercely.

**Where the measurement is recorded**
- Each check-in's status and next actions are logged in the coaching MEMORY.md.

## Analyze

Lack of progress is data, not failure. Analyze whether a stall is about clarity, capacity, motivation, or an external obstacle — each needs a different response.

**Decision rules**
- If a commitment was kept, acknowledge it specifically and set the next step.
- If a commitment was missed once, get curious about why before setting the next one.
- If a goal stalls two check-ins in a row, route it to Obstacle Diagnosis (PA-35-03).

**Common failure modes and how to read them**
- **Shaming:** Treating a missed commitment as a moral failing. The owner disengages. Stay curious and supportive.
- **Drift tolerance:** Letting a goal quietly stall for weeks. Catch it at two check-ins, not two months.
- **Vanity progress:** Counting busywork as progress. Measure movement toward the goal's real success measure.

## Improve

The check-in improves by getting the cadence right for this owner and by sharpening how next actions are sized so they are actually completable.

**Step-by-step procedure**
1. Open with what the owner committed to last time — read it back exactly.
2. Ask what actually happened, in their words; listen without interrupting or judging.
3. Mark each goal honestly: on track, at risk, or stalled.
4. For kept commitments, acknowledge the specific action — reinforce what worked.
5. For missed ones, get curious about the obstacle before setting a new commitment.
6. Agree on a small, concrete set of next actions sized to be completable before the next check-in.
7. If a goal has stalled two check-ins running, hand it to the Obstacle Diagnosis procedure.

**Escalation path**
- If the owner repeatedly disengages from check-ins, raise it gently as its own topic — disengagement usually means the goal, the cadence, or the relationship needs to change.

**Optimization loop**
- Tune the check-in cadence to the owner's rhythm — too frequent feels like nagging, too rare allows drift.
- Size next actions small enough that 'I did it' is the default outcome.

## Control

Control keeps this procedure stable and trustworthy over time. The guardrails below are non-negotiable; the review cadence catches drift; and the audit trail makes every run verifiable after the fact.

**Guardrails (never violate)**
- Never shame; accountability is support, not judgment.
- Never let a goal stall silently past two check-ins.
- Never count busywork as progress toward the goal's real measure.

**Review cadence**
- Each planning cycle: review commitment-kept rate and adjust cadence and action sizing.

**Failure mode to actively prevent**
- Letting a goal drift unaddressed until the owner has quietly given up on it.

**Audit trail**
- Every check-in's status and next actions are logged.
- Stalls and their routing to diagnosis are recorded.

## Operating checklist

**Before the run**
- All required inputs are on hand: The committed goals from PA-35-01; What the owner committed to do since the last check-in; What actually happened, in the owner's own words.
- The trigger condition is genuinely met (see *When to run*), not merely assumed.
- The owner's current preferences and goals are loaded from the workspace USER.md.

**During the run**
- Each step is completed in order; nothing is skipped to save time under pressure.
- Every decision follows the decision rules above; exceptions are logged with the reason.
- Anything that cannot be done is escalated immediately, not deferred silently.

**After the run**
- All outputs exist and are where the downstream procedure expects them: A short progress summary per goal: on track, at risk, or stalled; A small set of concrete next actions the owner commits to before the next check-in.
- The metrics for this run are recorded (Commitment-kept rate, Stall duration, Check-in attendance).
- The hand-off is made: The owner (for execution) and the Obstacle Diagnosis procedure (PA-35-03) whenever a goal is stalled.
- Any lesson learned is written to the role MEMORY.md so the next run inherits it.
