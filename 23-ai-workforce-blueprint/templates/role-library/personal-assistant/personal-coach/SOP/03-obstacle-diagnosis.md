# PA-35-03 — Obstacle Diagnosis and Reframe

## Define

Obstacle Diagnosis and Reframe is run when a goal is stuck. The coach helps the owner see clearly what is actually blocking them — which is rarely the thing they first name — and reframes it into something they can act on.

**Purpose of this procedure**
- Find the true obstacle behind a stalled goal, not the surface excuse.
- Reframe the obstacle from an immovable wall into a solvable problem.
- Restore the owner's sense of agency so they can move again.

**When to run**
- Whenever a goal has stalled (typically flagged by two consecutive missed check-ins) or the owner reports feeling stuck.

**Frequency**
- On-demand, triggered by a stall.

**Inputs**
- The stalled goal and its check-in history.
- The owner's own account of why they are stuck.
- Any prior obstacles and how they were resolved.

**Outputs**
- A clear statement of the real obstacle (often different from the first one named).
- A reframe that turns the obstacle into a next action the owner can take.

**Hand-to (downstream)**
- The Accountability and Progress Check procedure (PA-35-02) to resume tracking with the reframed next action.

**Definition of done**
- The owner can name the real obstacle, sees it as solvable, and has a concrete next action.

## Measure

Diagnosis is measured by whether the goal moves again afterward — a reframe that does not unstick the owner has not found the real obstacle.

**Signals this procedure tracks**
- The goal resumes progress after the session.
- The owner's language shifts from 'I can't' to 'I will try'.
- The same obstacle does not recur unchanged.

**Key metrics**
- **Unstick rate** — stalled goals that resume progress after diagnosis; target high.
- **Recurrence rate** — the same obstacle returning unchanged; minimize — recurrence means the real cause was missed.
- **Agency shift** — owner moves from blocked language to action language; observe and note.

**Where the measurement is recorded**
- Each diagnosis — obstacle named, reframe, and whether the goal resumed — is logged in MEMORY.md.

## Analyze

The first obstacle the owner names is usually a symptom (no time, too hard). The real obstacle is often beneath it (a fear, a missing skill, a goal that is not truly theirs). Analyze for the layer beneath.

**Decision rules**
- Ask 'what makes that hard?' repeatedly until the answer stops being about logistics and starts being about the real block.
- If the obstacle is a missing skill or resource, the reframe is a plan to get it.
- If the obstacle is a fear, name it plainly and reframe the next action to be small enough to be safe.
- If the real obstacle is that the goal is not the owner's, return it to Goal Setting (PA-35-01) — do not force a goal the owner does not want.

**Common failure modes and how to read them**
- **Symptom-fixing:** Solving 'no time' when the real block is fear. The goal stalls again. Dig to the real obstacle.
- **Advice-dumping:** Telling the owner what to do instead of helping them see the block. Ownership is lost.
- **Toxic positivity:** Reframing away a real, hard obstacle. Name hard things honestly, then make them workable.

## Improve

Diagnosis improves by getting better at distinguishing symptom from cause and by reframing in language that restores agency rather than dispensing instructions.

**Step-by-step procedure**
1. Restate the stalled goal and acknowledge, without judgment, that it is stuck.
2. Ask the owner what they believe is blocking them; listen fully.
3. Probe beneath the first answer: 'and what makes that hard?' until the real obstacle surfaces.
4. Name the real obstacle plainly so the owner can see it clearly.
5. Reframe it: if it is a skill gap, a plan to close it; if a fear, a small safe next step; if a wrong goal, return it to goal setting.
6. Agree on one concrete next action that moves past the reframed obstacle.
7. Hand the goal back to the progress-check cadence with the new next action.

**Escalation path**
- If the obstacle is beyond coaching scope (e.g., a health or mental-health issue), name it with care and encourage the owner to seek the right specialist — do not coach outside scope.

**Optimization loop**
- Keep a record of recurring obstacle patterns for this owner; spotting the pattern speeds diagnosis.
- Refine the probing questions that most reliably surface the real block for this owner.

## Control

Control keeps this procedure stable and trustworthy over time. The guardrails below are non-negotiable; the review cadence catches drift; and the audit trail makes every run verifiable after the fact.

**Guardrails (never violate)**
- Never solve the surface symptom and call the goal unstuck.
- Never dispense advice in place of helping the owner see their own block.
- Never coach outside scope; route real health or crisis issues to the right specialist.

**Review cadence**
- Each planning cycle: review unstick and recurrence rates; refine diagnosis approach.

**Failure mode to actively prevent**
- Fixing the symptom the owner first names while the real obstacle stays untouched — so the goal stalls again.

**Audit trail**
- Each diagnosis logs the obstacle, reframe, and outcome.
- Recurring obstacles are flagged for a deeper look.
