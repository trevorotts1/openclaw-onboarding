# PA-35-04 — Reflection and Periodic Review

**Role:** Personal Coach
**Department:** Personal Assistant (35)
**SOP class:** Core operating procedure (generic role standard — applies to every client of this role)
**Method:** DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control)

## Define

Reflection and Periodic Review closes each cycle by helping the owner look back honestly: what worked, what did not, what was learned, and what to carry forward. It converts experience into insight so the owner gets better, not just busier.

**Purpose of this procedure**
- Turn a completed period into durable lessons rather than a blur of activity.
- Celebrate genuine progress so motivation is renewed.
- Decide deliberately what to continue, change, or stop next cycle.

**When to run**
- At the end of each planning cycle, and after any major goal completes or is abandoned.

**Frequency**
- Each planning cycle (e.g., quarterly), plus milestone-triggered reviews.

**Inputs**
- The cycle's goals and their outcomes.
- The check-in and obstacle records from the cycle.
- The owner's own sense of how the period went.

**Outputs**
- A short written reflection: wins, misses, lessons, and what to change.
- A 'continue / change / stop' decision feeding into the next Goal Setting cycle.

**Hand-to (downstream)**
- The Goal Setting and Clarification procedure (PA-35-01) to start the next cycle informed by what was learned.

**Definition of done**
- The owner has named their wins and lessons and made deliberate continue/change/stop choices for next cycle.

## Measure

Reflection is measured by whether lessons actually change behavior next cycle — a reflection that produces no change was a journaling exercise, not a review.

**Signals this procedure tracks**
- Lessons from one cycle showing up as changes in the next.
- The owner recognizing patterns across cycles.
- Motivation renewed rather than depleted at cycle's end.

**Key metrics**
- **Lesson-to-action rate** — reflection lessons that became next-cycle changes; target high.
- **Pattern recognition** — owner names recurring patterns across cycles; observe and encourage.
- **Motivation check** — owner ends the review energized, not drained; gauge and protect.

**Where the measurement is recorded**
- Each cycle's reflection and its continue/change/stop decisions are recorded in MEMORY.md.

## Analyze

Honest review requires balancing celebration with candor. Analyze the cycle for both what genuinely worked (to repeat) and what genuinely did not (to change), resisting the pull to only do one.

**Decision rules**
- Name at least one genuine win per cycle — progress unacknowledged is motivation lost.
- For each miss, separate 'bad luck' from 'bad system' — only the second one should change behavior.
- Every lesson must become a concrete continue/change/stop decision or it is not really a lesson.

**Common failure modes and how to read them**
- **Skip-the-review:** Rushing into the next cycle without looking back. The same mistakes repeat.
- **All-critique:** Only cataloguing failures. Motivation collapses and the owner avoids the next review.
- **Insight-without-action:** Naming lessons that never change anything. Tie every lesson to a decision.

## Improve

The review improves by making the reflection short enough to actually happen and by reliably converting lessons into next-cycle changes.

**Step-by-step procedure**
1. Lay out the cycle's goals next to their actual outcomes.
2. Ask the owner what they are proud of; name at least one genuine win specifically.
3. Ask what did not go as hoped; for each, separate bad luck from a system that needs changing.
4. Draw out the lessons — what does this period teach about how the owner works best?
5. Convert each lesson into a continue / change / stop decision for next cycle.
6. Write the short reflection and the decisions where the next cycle's planning will see them.
7. Hand the decisions to the next Goal Setting cycle so the next goals are set wiser.

**Escalation path**
- If the review reveals the owner is consistently setting goals that do not fit their real life, escalate that as a topic in its own right before the next cycle begins.

**Optimization loop**
- Keep reflections short and structured so the review reliably happens rather than getting skipped.
- Track which continue/change/stop decisions actually stuck, and refine how decisions are framed.

## Control

Control keeps this procedure stable and trustworthy over time. The guardrails below are non-negotiable; the review cadence catches drift; and the audit trail makes every run verifiable after the fact.

**Guardrails (never violate)**
- Never skip the review in the rush to the next cycle.
- Never let the review become all-critique; celebrate real wins.
- Never let a lesson end without a concrete decision attached.

**Review cadence**
- Each planning cycle (the review IS the cadence); annually, review the reviews for long-range patterns.

**Failure mode to actively prevent**
- Ending a cycle without reflecting — so hard-won lessons evaporate and the same mistakes repeat next cycle.

**Audit trail**
- Each cycle's reflection and decisions are recorded.
- Lesson-to-action follow-through is checked the next cycle.

## Operating checklist

**Before the run**
- All required inputs are on hand: The cycle's goals and their outcomes; The check-in and obstacle records from the cycle; The owner's own sense of how the period went.
- The trigger condition is genuinely met (see *When to run*), not merely assumed.
- The owner's current preferences and goals are loaded from the workspace USER.md.

**During the run**
- Each step is completed in order; nothing is skipped to save time under pressure.
- Every decision follows the decision rules above; exceptions are logged with the reason.
- Anything that cannot be done is escalated immediately, not deferred silently.

**After the run**
- All outputs exist and are where the downstream procedure expects them: A short written reflection: wins, misses, lessons, and what to change; A 'continue / change / stop' decision feeding into the next Goal Setting cycle.
- The metrics for this run are recorded (Lesson-to-action rate, Pattern recognition, Motivation check).
- The hand-off is made: The Goal Setting and Clarification procedure (PA-35-01) to start the next cycle informed by what was learned.
- Any lesson learned is written to the role MEMORY.md so the next run inherits it.
