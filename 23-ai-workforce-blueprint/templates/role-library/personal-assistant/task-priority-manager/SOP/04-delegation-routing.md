## Define

Delegation and Routing moves work off the owner's plate to wherever it can be done best — other people, other departments, or automation — with enough context that it actually gets done and does not boomerang back as a crisis.

**Purpose of this procedure**
- Protect the owner's time by routing every task that does not require their unique judgment or authority.
- Hand off work with complete context so it is done right the first time.
- Track delegated work so nothing falls through the cracks after it leaves the owner.

**When to run**
- Any time a task is identified as delegatable — it does not require the owner's unique judgment, relationship, or authority.

**Frequency**
- Daily, as delegatable tasks surface during capture and triage.

**Inputs**
- Tasks flagged as delegation candidates during Task Capture (PA-04-01).
- The map of who or what can do each kind of work (people, departments, tools).
- The context each handoff needs: outcome, deadline, success criteria, constraints.

**Outputs**
- A delegated task with a named owner, deadline, success criteria, and a follow-up checkpoint.
- A tracking record so the delegation is monitored until it is confirmed done.

**Hand-to (downstream)**
- The chosen executor (person, department, or automation) and the Deadline Tracking procedure (PA-04-03) to watch the handoff to completion.

**Definition of done**
- Every delegatable task has left the owner with full context, a clear owner, and a follow-up checkpoint scheduled.

## Measure

Delegation is measured by how much it actually frees the owner and by how rarely delegated work fails or returns.

**Signals this procedure tracks**
- Tasks successfully completed by the delegate without bouncing back.
- Delegations that returned to the owner as crises (a context or tracking failure).
- Owner time reclaimed by routing work away.

**Key metrics**
- **First-pass success** — delegated tasks done correctly without rework; target above 85%.
- **Boomerang rate** — delegations that came back to the owner as problems; target under 10%.
- **Owner time reclaimed** — estimated hours per week routed off the owner's plate; track and grow.

**Where the measurement is recorded**
- The assistant logs each boomerang with its cause in MEMORY.md and reviews the pattern weekly.

## Analyze

Delegations fail for two reasons: the wrong executor was chosen, or the right executor was given incomplete context. Analyze every boomerang against both.

**Decision rules**
- Delegate only what does not require the owner's unique judgment, relationship, or authority.
- Match the task to the executor best placed to do it well, not merely the one most available.
- Every handoff must include outcome, deadline, success criteria, and constraints — no bare 'please handle this'.

**Common failure modes and how to read them**
- **Context-free handoff:** 'Please handle X' with no deadline or success criteria. Tasks fall through and return as crises.
- **Over-delegation:** Routing work that genuinely needs the owner. Keep judgment-and-authority tasks with the owner.
- **No follow-up:** Delegating and forgetting. Every handoff needs a checkpoint until it is confirmed done.

## Improve

Routing improves as the map of who-does-what-well gets sharper and as handoff context gets tighter.

**Step-by-step procedure**
1. Confirm the task is genuinely delegatable — it does not require the owner's unique judgment, relationship, or authority.
2. Choose the executor best placed to do it well; record why.
3. Write the handoff with the full package: the outcome wanted, the deadline, the success criteria, and any constraints.
4. Send the handoff and confirm the executor has accepted it and understands the success criteria.
5. Create a tracking record and set a follow-up checkpoint before the deadline.
6. At the checkpoint, verify progress; if it is off track, intervene early rather than at the deadline.
7. On completion, confirm the outcome meets the success criteria and close the tracking record.

**Escalation path**
- If a delegation is failing and the deadline is at risk, escalate to the owner with options before it becomes a crisis — do not let it silently slip back onto their plate.

**Optimization loop**
- Keep a living map of executors and what each does best.
- Standardize the handoff package so no context is ever omitted.

## Control

Control keeps this procedure stable and trustworthy over time. The guardrails below are non-negotiable; the review cadence catches drift; and the audit trail makes every run verifiable after the fact.

**Guardrails (never violate)**
- Never delegate without a deadline and success criteria.
- Never delegate work that needs the owner's authority or judgment.
- Never delegate without a follow-up checkpoint.

**Review cadence**
- Weekly: review boomerang rate and first-pass success; refine the executor map and handoff template.

**Failure mode to actively prevent**
- Delegating without context — a bare 'please handle X' that comes back to the owner as a crisis.

**Audit trail**
- Each delegation record carries executor, context package, and checkpoint outcome.
- Boomerangs are logged with causes and a corrective change.
