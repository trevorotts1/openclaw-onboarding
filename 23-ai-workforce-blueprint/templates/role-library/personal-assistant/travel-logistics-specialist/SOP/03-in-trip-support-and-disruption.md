# PA-38-03 — In-Trip Support and Disruption Handling

## Define

In-Trip Support and Disruption Handling watches the trip while it is happening and steps in when something breaks — a delay, a cancellation, a missed connection — to re-route the owner with the least disruption.

**Purpose of this procedure**
- Keep the owner moving when travel goes wrong, with options ready before they even ask.
- Minimize the impact of disruptions on the owner's destination commitments.
- Be the calm, prepared point of contact during travel stress.

**When to run**
- Continuously from departure to return, with active intervention the moment a disruption is detected.

**Frequency**
- Per trip, continuous monitoring; on-demand intervention.

**Inputs**
- The confirmed itinerary and travel pack from PA-38-02.
- Live status of each leg (delays, gate changes, cancellations).
- The owner's destination commitments and their flexibility.

**Outputs**
- Proactive alerts to the owner about disruptions, with options already worked out.
- Re-booked or re-routed travel that protects the owner's most important commitments.

**Hand-to (downstream)**
- The owner (decision and execution) and the Post-Trip Review procedure (PA-38-04) which captures what happened.

**Definition of done**
- Every disruption was caught early, the owner was given worked-out options, and the most important commitments were protected.

## Measure

In-trip support is measured by response speed and by how well the owner's priorities survive a disruption — not by whether disruptions happen at all.

**Signals this procedure tracks**
- Disruptions caught proactively vs. discovered by the owner first.
- Time from disruption to a worked-out option presented.
- Destination commitments protected through a disruption.

**Key metrics**
- **Proactive-catch rate** — disruptions surfaced by the specialist before the owner notices; target high.
- **Time-to-option** — minutes from disruption to options ready; minimize.
- **Commitment-protection rate** — key destination commitments kept despite disruption; maximize.

**Where the measurement is recorded**
- Each disruption and how it was handled is logged in real time and reviewed post-trip in MEMORY.md.

## Analyze

When a leg breaks, the question is not 'what is the rule?' but 'what does the owner most need to protect?' Analyze every disruption against the owner's priorities, not just the airline's rebooking defaults.

**Decision rules**
- Detect disruptions proactively from live status; never wait for the owner to discover them.
- When re-routing, protect the owner's most important destination commitment first.
- Present worked-out options with a recommendation, not a raw problem — the owner is stressed and short on time.

**Common failure modes and how to read them**
- **Reactive-only:** Waiting for the owner to report a problem. By then options have narrowed. Monitor proactively.
- **Default rebooking:** Accepting the carrier's automatic rebooking without checking it against the owner's priorities.
- **Problem-dumping:** Telling the owner what broke with no options. Always arrive with a recommended path.

## Improve

Support improves by detecting disruptions faster and by getting better at reading which commitments the owner will sacrifice and which are untouchable.

**Step-by-step procedure**
1. Monitor live status of every leg from departure onward.
2. The instant a disruption appears, assess its impact on the rest of the itinerary and the destination commitments.
3. Work out re-routing options that protect the owner's most important commitment first.
4. Alert the owner proactively with the situation, the worked-out options, and a clear recommendation.
5. On the owner's decision, execute the re-booking and update the travel pack.
6. Confirm every downstream leg still connects after the change; re-buffer if needed.
7. Log the disruption and the response for the post-trip review.

**Escalation path**
- If a disruption threatens an untouchable commitment and no travel option protects it, escalate immediately so the owner can act on the commitment side (reschedule, send a delegate, join remotely) in parallel.

**Optimization loop**
- Build awareness of which routes and times disrupt most, and pre-plan fallbacks.
- Keep the owner's commitment-priority order current so re-routing decisions are fast.

## Control

Control keeps this procedure stable and trustworthy over time. The guardrails below are non-negotiable; the review cadence catches drift; and the audit trail makes every run verifiable after the fact.

**Guardrails (never violate)**
- Never wait for the owner to discover a disruption.
- Never accept a default rebooking without checking it against the owner's priorities.
- Never present a problem without options and a recommendation.

**Review cadence**
- After each trip: review every disruption and the response; refine fallback plans.

**Failure mode to actively prevent**
- Finding out about a disruption only when the owner is already stranded and the good options are gone.

**Audit trail**
- Each disruption and its handling are logged in real time.
- Post-trip, responses are reviewed for what to pre-plan next time.
