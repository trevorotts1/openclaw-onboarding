## 1. THE RULE

> "First identify the creation mode, then run the process for that mode, then run the checklists in Part 10 before returning any presentation."

**Step Zero of every Presentation Department job is to identify the creation mode.** There is one framework and three modes. The priority shift, the seven P's, the anatomy, the build sequence, and the checklists are **required in all three**. The mode decides only **where you start** and **how you gather your raw inputs** — never whether the requirements apply.

The mode must be **recorded** (not assumed) at intake. An unrecorded mode is an auto-fail (`AF-MODE-UNSET`), because a deck built without knowing its mode silently skips the gathering discipline the mode requires.

Also identify the **outcome** alongside the mode:
- A **selling presentation** — the action is a purchase (product, service, membership, coaching, appointment).
- A **training presentation** — the action is the audience prioritizing, retaining, and applying the knowledge and the change.

Both succeed or fail on the **same mechanism** (the priority shift) and are built the same way at the core. The differences are marked throughout the framework with lines that begin "In a sale" and "In a training."

## 2. THE THREE MODES

### 2.1 Mode One — FROM SCRATCH (the brainstorm build) · `from_scratch`
**Definition.** There is no existing presentation. The department brainstorms with the presenter to discover what the presentation should be, then builds it end to end.

- The four diagnostic P's are **not a post-mortem — they are a design spec used up front.** Confirm the Product, decide the Price and its structure, design the Position from the first moment, and prepare the Person to deliver with conviction.
- There is no failure to diagnose, only a presentation to engineer. You **install the priority shift from move one** rather than retrofitting it later.
- **What to gather** (the brainstorm surfaces the inputs the Part 9 build sequence needs):
  - The one destination (the single action the presentation must lead to).
  - The one big promise (the transformation, stated plainly).
  - The audience's priority stack (what they already spend money, time, or attention on).
  - The higher priority to connect to (legacy, family, freedom, purpose, identity).
  - The proof and the one demonstration that makes the promise believable.
  - The one wow factor (the most vivid moment).
  - The true value to anchor, and for a sale, the price tiers for the value drop.
- **Owned by:** brainstorming-buddy-presentations (gathering) → Attention-Content-Strategist (spec).

### 2.2 Mode Two — CONTENT-TO-PRESENTATION, PERSONAL (one-to-one) · `content_personal`
**Definition.** The presenter shares existing content (a previous deck, a video, a transcript) to be turned into a presentation delivered to **one specific client, one to one.**

- You **extract the substance** from the source and reshape it. You do **not** merely tidy the source.
- Because it is aimed at one known person, you tailor the priority shift to **that individual's** exact priority stack, situation, identity, and likely objections. This is the most personalized application of the entire framework.
- Run the diagnostic on the **source content first**, because raw content almost never contains an engineered priority shift, a single clear promise, or built-in urgency. Those are usually exactly what you add.
- **What to gather:** everything from the source that carries real substance or proof; everything learnable about the one client (stated priorities, situation, what they have tried, specific objections, the outcome they most want); the single action this one client should take by the end.

### 2.3 Mode Three — CONTENT-TO-PRESENTATION, GENERAL AUDIENCE (one-to-many) · `content_general`
**Definition.** The presenter shares the same kind of existing content, orchestrated to be presented to a **general audience, one to many,** for mass appeal.

- Same source content, **different orchestration.** You build for the room, not for one person.
- Because you cannot tailor to one person's stack, you find the **common priority stack** across the audience: the shared higher priorities, the universal version of the promise, the objections most of them hold.
- The crowd mechanics shine here: scarcity and urgency (limited seats, limited time, a right-now window), social proof, and unity. These move many people at once.
- The teaching-as-showcase discipline matters even more — a mixed audience will happily follow a presenter into over-teaching and lose the priority shift along the way.
- **What to gather:** the source substance/proof; the shared audience profile (the priority most hold in common, the promise that lands across the room, the objection most share); the single action the room should take, plus the scarcity and time mechanics that move the action-takers.

## 3. TELLING MODE TWO FROM MODE THREE

The dividing line is **not the content — it is the delivery.** The same shared material can become either one.
- Presented to one specific client → **personal version (Mode Two).**
- Orchestrated for a general audience for mass appeal → **general audience version (Mode Three).**

The coaching session this framework is drawn from is a Mode-Two→Mode-Three case: the presenter had existing pitch content delivered one-to-one in individual meetings (Mode Two), and the guidance was to move to group masterclass / pitch sessions (Mode Three) for leverage, because one-to-one delivery was draining. **The content stayed the same. Only the mode changed.**

## 4. LEVER-EMPHASIS BY MODE (the full framework applies; the lean differs)

| Mode | Hardest lever | The risk |
|---|---|---|
| **From Scratch** | Discovery; lock ONE destination and ONE promise before building | Wandering into many ideas — "first things first, second things never" (SOP-PHILOSOPHY-00) |
| **Personal (1:1)** | Deep personalization, identity, answering that one person's specific objections | You can ask them directly what their true priority is |
| **General (1:many)** | The common priority, scarcity, urgency, social proof, unity | Discover the shared priority through patterns and surveys, not by asking one person |

---

## 5. WHAT STAYS CONSTANT ACROSS ALL THREE MODES

Regardless of mode: the **true goal is a priority shift**; the **seven P's apply**; the **anatomy applies** (one promise, one wow factor, one demonstration, leading to one action); the **build sequence** (SOP-PRIORITY-02) applies; and the **checklists** (SOP-INTEGRATION-00, the 14-item gate) must pass before it ships. Mode determines the starting point and the gathering method, never whether these requirements apply.

## 6. RUNNING IT BY MODE + EDGE CASES

**Step Zero — identify the mode** (Part 7.1). Then:
- **From-Scratch path** (Part 7.2): use the four P's as a design spec → design Position from the first moment → put the three advanced P's in deliberately → run the build sequence (SOP-PRIORITY-02) → verify (SOP-INTEGRATION-00).
- **Content-to-Presentation path** (Part 7.3): extract substance → run the primary diagnostic by elimination on the source → run the advanced diagnostic inside Position → rebuild for the mode → run the build sequence → verify.

**Edge cases this SOP owns:**
- **Mixed general audience (167b)** — some ready to buy, some not: build to the **common priority**, let scarcity and the time-bound trigger sort the action-takers, and provide a **standing offer** for the not-yet-ready so no one is lost and the room is not held hostage to the slowest decider.
- **Hybrid teach-and-sell (167c)** — there is still **one** priority shift. Sequence it: deliver the showcase teaching as proof, then shift the priority toward the action. **Never run two competing priority shifts.** (Doctrine root: SOP-NORTHSTAR-00.)
- **Training presentations** (Part 7.4): same process — eliminate the primary four, localize to positioning of the material, check the advanced three, remediate the same way. A training that informs without changing what the audience prioritizes has **failed its true goal even if every fact was correct.**
