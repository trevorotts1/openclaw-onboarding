**Cluster:** Pitch-Craft Rules (the offer choreography)

**Status:** Standalone pitch-craft SOP. This document states the slow-drop process as a single checkable pass/fail procedure. ---

## 1. PURPOSE

A slow drop is a reward for staying, earned across minutes of narrative, not a cliff dropped at the end. The forensic reference deck dropped the price cold and fast ($5,000 to $2,500 to $1,200 with the beats 2 and 3 slides apart, no build-up, no added value), which the reviewer scored 2 of 10. The gold-standard reference deck spreads the same kind of ladder across the back two-thirds with gaps of 11, 16, 14, and 8 slides, a dedicated build-up slide before every drop, and a new earned reason and added value at each beat. This SOP makes the slow drop a mechanical procedure with a hard minimum spacing, so the offer can never be crammed again.

The doctrine, verbatim from the coaching transcript (transcript-spec.md Section 4): start with TRUE VALUE but gradual, never the "$25,000 for $2" cliche; the anchor is a value-plant, not a drop; every drop is EARNED and ADDS value ("every time we do a price drop, you still gotta add more value", said twice); the lower the price the greater the value; the final real price is revealed LATE with a hard scarcity window; people buy promises, not products. The audience must feel "the longer I stay, the better it gets" so they reach the final price ravenous.

---

## 2. THE HARD RULE (the slow-drop procedure)

1. **Plant the anchor early as a value-plant, NOT a drop.** Near the one-third mark of the deck (target 25 to 45 percent depth; the gold-standard deck s24 = 32 percent), state what the system is actually worth (the anchor number) and tell the audience to remember it. No purchase price is revealed at the anchor. The anchor carries the explicit memory hook ("Remember this number. Keep watching." or equivalent).

2. **Spread the beats; never cram them.** Anchor to Drop 1, Drop 1 to Drop 2, Drop 2 to Drop 3, Drop 3 to Final, every adjacent pair is spread across the back two-thirds, never stacked. Two enforcement layers apply and the stricter one governs:
   - LIVE GATE (already wired, AF-C7 sub-condition (a)): no 2 drops fall within 2 slides of each other, and the rungs land at roughly 47 / 68 / 87 percent depth on a full-length deck (the Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.1 percentage placement). This is the floor a deck cannot pass below.
   - DOCTRINAL TARGET (the gold-standard, recommended): aim for at least 8 slides between every adjacent beat, computed against the FULL deck slide count, never against the offer window only. (gold-standard gaps: 11, 16, 14, 8. Minimum observed = 8.) On a 35-to-45-slide deck the percentage placement and the 8-slide target broadly agree; where the deck is short enough that the percentages would crowd two beats inside 8 slides, prefer lengthening the offer window so the doctrinal target is met, rather than relying only on the 2-slide minimum. The 8-slide target is the spacing the reviewer praised in the gold-standard deck; the 2-slide minimum is the hard auto-fail floor that catches the forensic-deck cliff.

3. **A dedicated emotional BUILDUP slide immediately precedes every DROP.** No price ever drops cold. The build-up future-paces ("imagine this", "this is the part that changes everything") so the audience leans in, THEN the number falls. (gold-standard build-ups: s34, s50, s64, s72.)

4. **Every drop carries a NEW earned reason.** Each drop names WHY the price just fell, and the reason is different each time, tied to the audience's behavior or belief. (gold-standard: Drop 1 "because you showed up live today"; Drop 2 "because you believed"; Drop 3 "because you stayed".) A drop with no earned reason, or a repeated reason, fails.

5. **Every drop ADDS value; no drop strips value.** The drop slide or its immediate successor names additional value added to the table at that beat (a new deliverable, a new bonus, a guarantee). The value keeps stacking while the price keeps falling, so the perceived value-to-price gap widens at every rung. (The gold-standard reference deck stacks the whole Day-1/Day-2/Day-3 offer and bonuses between Drop 1 and Drop 2.)

6. **Render the drop VISUALLY and CUMULATIVELY.** Each drop slide shows every prior (dead) price struck through and the new (live) price glowing, so the audience literally watches the ladder die. (The price-typography system that renders this lives in the design-system cluster; this SOP requires that the drop be shown cumulatively, not that a single new number simply appears.)

7. **The FINAL real price is revealed LATE and sits below the entire ladder.** The final price is strictly less than the lowest drop, revealed near the end with a hard scarcity window (the "next 15 minutes" Hormozi-style drive) and a last-call beat. The value gap (total value vs the final price) is stated before the final reveal.

8. **Never two price slides back to back.** A price beat is always separated from the next by narrative, build-up, added value, and proof. Two adjacent slides both carrying a price is the immediate-drop bug and fails.

## 3. THE ENFORCEMENT CHECK (pass/fail)

This SOP is the authored doctrine behind the LIVE qc-specialist-presentations.md gate. The QC Specialist already enforces it; this section maps each rule above to the existing live check so the SOP and the gate stay in lockstep.

| Rule | Live enforcement (already wired) | PASS | FAIL |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 anchor early, value-plant | AF-C7 sub-condition (a) SPREAD: anchor is a value plant not a drop; copy QC c17 ANCHOR memory hook present | anchor at 25-45% depth, carries the memory hook, no purchase price | anchor is itself a drop, or stated past the back third |
| 2 spread the beats | AF-C7 sub-condition (a) SPREAD: no 2 drops within 2 slides, not all in the final 10%; rungs at ~47/68/87% (SOP 9.1) | beats spread, no pair within 2 slides; doctrinal target >= 8 slides apart met | any 2 drops within 2 slides, or all beats in the final 10% |
| 3 BUILDUP before every drop | AF-C7 sub-condition (b) EARNED + BUILT-UP: every drop has a preceding buildup; c17 "BUILDUP slide immediately precedes every DROP" | a BUILDUP slide immediately precedes every DROP | any DROP with no BUILDUP before it |
| 4 new earned reason | AF-C7 sub-condition (b): every drop has a reason | each drop names a distinct earned reason | a drop with no reason, or a duplicated reason |
| 5 every drop adds value | AF-C7 sub-condition (c) ADDS value: each drop or its successor names new $ value; zero stripping | the drop or its successor names added value | a drop that strips value or names none |
| 7 final below the ladder, late | AF-C7 sub-condition (d): FINAL below the entire ladder; value gap stated before reveal | FINAL < lowest drop, revealed late, value gap stated | FINAL not below the ladder, or revealed early with no value gap |
| 8 never two price slides adjacent | AF-C7 sub-condition (a) SPREAD | no two adjacent slides both carry a price | two consecutive price slides |

A deck that trips any AF-C7 sub-condition fails the DECK at Phase 1Q, before scoring, and again at Phase 6 against the assembled slide order. The auto-fail records which sub-condition triggered.

## 4. PASS vs FAIL EXAMPLES (drawn from the forensic reference deck and the gold-standard reference deck)

**FAIL (the forensic reference deck's ladder):** anchor s32, Drop 1 s34 (gap 2), Drop 2 s37 (gap 3), final s43 (gap 6), no build-ups, no added value between drops, no cumulative strike. Trips AF-C7 (a) spread (gaps < 8), (b) no build-ups, (c) no added value. The reviewer scored it 2 of 10.

**PASS (the gold-standard reference deck, the numbers to re-sequence):** anchor $5,000+ at s24 ("remember this number", value-plant), Drop 1 $2,500 at s35 ("because you showed up live", gap 11, build-up s34, adds the offer reveal), Drop 2 $1,000 at s51 ("because you believed", gap 16, build-up s50, adds the example program blueprint and automation), Drop 3 $500 at s65 ("because you stayed", gap 14, build-up s64, adds the guarantee), Final $47/$97 at s73 (gap 8, build-up s72, all four struck cumulatively, 15-minute window). Passes every AF-C7 sub-condition.

**PASS (the example brand re-sequenced, prices from the client interview):** anchor near the one-third mark stated as a priceless value-plant (the example brand sells a non-monetary outcome, so the value frame is priceless, not a dollar ROI), then at least 8 slides of promises and proof and added value before Drop 1, a build-up before each subsequent drop, a new earned reason at each, the final entry price revealed late with a 15-minute window. The client's actual numbers come from the client interview and are never fabricated; the doctrine is re-sequenced onto the client's real ladder when the interview provides it.

## 5. ESCALATION / REPAIR PATH

1. The Director owns arc_allocation.json and runs a density pre-check at arc time: compute the planned ladder positions, the gaps, the anchor depth, and confirm every AF-C7 sub-condition is clear in the PLAN before releasing the arc to the Slide Copywriter and Offer Price Strategist.
2. The live floor is AF-C7's "no 2 drops within 2 slides" plus the SOP 9.1 percentage placement (~47/68/87%); the doctrinal target is at least 8 slides between every adjacent beat (the gold-standard). Both are computed against the FULL deck count. Where a short deck would crowd two beats inside the 8-slide target, the Director lengthens the offer window or the deck so the target is met; the 2-slide minimum is never the goal, only the hard auto-fail floor.
3. On any AF-C7 trigger at Phase 1Q, the repair is an arc change (move the anchor earlier, add build-ups, add value, spread the beats), routed to the Director, then to the Slide Copywriter and Offer Price Strategist.
4. Coordinate with SOP-PITCH-02 (value-stack and promises must precede the anchor and Drop 1) and SOP-PITCH-03 (the re-pitch block follows the final price). The mandatory one-big-idea splits (a value trio = 4 slides, four pains = 4 slides) consume slots; the Director reserves them BEFORE checking the gaps so a split does not later collide with a beat.
5. Loop up to 3 times (shared Phase 1Q budget). On the 4th failure escalate to the Director and ROLE-16 Healer per QC SOP 9.4. A persistently un-spaceable deck means the deck length itself is too short; the Director increases the total slide count.
