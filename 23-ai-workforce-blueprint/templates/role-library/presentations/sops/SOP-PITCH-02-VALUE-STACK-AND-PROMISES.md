## 1. PURPOSE

People buy promises, not products. "People don't really buy products, they really buy promises; if the promise is strong enough, the product will sell itself" (the transcript reference, Section 4). The forensic reference deck had no itemized value stack and stated no promises before the price: the offer was a bare number with nothing summed behind it. The gold-standard reference deck plants the promises early, stacks every deliverable with a real-world dollar value, sums the stack to a total that exceeds the anchor BEFORE the cheapest price appears, and proves the anchor was not hype. This SOP makes the value stack and the promises a required, ordered, checkable beat set.

A drop without added value is wrong (the transcript reference, Section 5: "every time we do a price drop, you still gotta add more value", said twice). So value and promises are not a single late slide; they are planted before the first number and re-stacked at every drop. And the re-stack ESCALATES: with every price drop the value INCREASES with a bigger and better promise, so the audience sees the price falling while the value rises. That falling-price / rising-value inverse, shown on screen as a climbing running value total beside the struck price and escalating at every rung, is what makes the audience ravenous by the final price.

## 2. THE HARD RULE (the value-stack and promises procedure)

1. **Promises are planted BEFORE the first number (before the anchor). This is the FIRST LAW of PRICING INTELLIGENCE (SOP-ENGINE-00 Engine 6): PROMISE PRECEDES PRICE.** A dedicated promises beat exists before the anchor slide: the things the program will DO for the buyer, stated as outcomes, not features. Price-first decreases perceived value; the promise must be on screen before the number so the price arrives as a relief, not a shock. The framing law: "it's not the product they buy, it's the promise of what it does." (The example brand's promises planted before the anchor, transcript-grounded: the example brand sells a non-monetary outcome; the promise inventory is drawn from the client interview.) The promise inventory is recorded so it can be restated at each drop and in the re-pitch. Pricing Intelligence then runs the slow-drop, anchor, and value-stack mechanics (rules 2-8 below + SOP-PITCH-01); a number shown before any promise is planted fails the density gate (AF-DEN-5, no promises beat before the anchor).

2. **Every deliverable is itemized with a real-world value.** Each offer component is named and carries a concrete dollar value (or, for a non-monetary offer, a priceless frame, see rule 6). (Gold-standard: Day 1 component $997, Day 2 component $1,497, Day 3 component $997, VIP component $497, Action-Taker component $297, Strategy Session $997.)

3. **An itemized value-stack slide sums to a TOTAL.** A dedicated stack slide lists every component with its value in one clean view and sums them to an explicit total. (Gold-standard s57 "Everything You Get" sums to "$5,282", capped with "And I still haven't told you what you actually pay.")

4. **The stack total is proven to EXCEED the anchor BEFORE the cheapest prices appear.** A tally slide states the total and ties it back to the anchor the audience was told to remember ("I told you to remember that number. Here it is."), proving the anchor was not hype. (Gold-standard s58 "Add It All Up, Over $5,000"; the tally equals the anchor per FIX-5a.) This lands before Drop 1, so the value is undeniable when the price collapses.

5. **Value is re-stacked, and ESCALATED, at every drop.** Each drop names additional value added at that beat (a new deliverable, a bonus, a guarantee); a promise is restated between drops (FIX-5b PROMISE SLIDE BETWEEN DROPS). The perceived value-to-price gap widens at every rung. The add is not a token: it must be BIGGER and BETTER than the prior rung (a substantive, distinct deliverable that is larger than the last), so a cumulative RUNNING VALUE TOTAL climbs by a non-trivial amount at every drop. The promises get bigger and better as the price drops. This running value total is the rising line the audience watches against the falling price; it is rendered on the drop slide beside the struck price by the design-system price-typography SOP. The escalation rule and the running value total are owned by the Offer Price Strategist (SOP 9.2 steps 5a and 5b) and recorded in offer_stack.json; a drop that adds only a trivial or restated item, leaving the running total flat, fails.

6. **A non-monetary offer uses the PRICELESS frame, never a fabricated dollar ROI.** If the offer does not produce money (the example brand sells a non-monetary outcome), the value frame is priceless (the American Express "seeing your kid smile, priceless" device), not an invented "$96,000 of value." Dollar values are never fabricated for non-monetary outcomes; the itemized stack still lists the components, and the value gap is framed as priceless rather than as a fake dollar total.

7. **A VALUE-GAP slide precedes the FINAL price.** Before the final reveal, a slide states the total value versus what the buyer actually pays (the gap), so the final price lands as an obvious bargain (FIX-5 VALUE-GAP slide before FINAL).

8. **Nothing is fabricated.** Components, values, and promises come from the client's interview. Until the interview supplies them, use clean placeholders ([CLIENT TO SUPPLY] at copy stage), never invented numbers, deliverables, or promises.

## 3. THE ENFORCEMENT CHECK (pass/fail)

This SOP is the authored doctrine behind the LIVE Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.2 and the QC named-component gates. The mapping:

| Rule | Live enforcement (already wired) | PASS | FAIL |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 promises before the anchor | Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.2 `promise_inventory`; QC named-component presence (Section 4.4) | a promises beat exists before the anchor; inventory recorded | no promises stated before the first number |
| 2 itemized with values | Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.2 Offer Stack construction | every component named with its value (or priceless frame) | a bare offer with no itemized components |
| 3 stack slide sums to a total | Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.2 TOTAL STACK VALUE | a stack slide lists components and sums to a total | no itemized stack slide, or no summed total |
| 4 total exceeds anchor before cheapest price | Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.2 TALLY slide == anchor (FIX-5a) | a tally slide proves the total ties to/exceeds the anchor before Drop 1 | tally missing, or after the cheapest price |
| 5 re-stack and ESCALATE at every drop | AF-C7 sub-condition (c) ADDS value, incl. its ESCALATION and RISING-CURVE clauses; Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.2 steps 5a/5b; FIX-5b promise slide between drops | each drop adds a distinct substantive value bigger than the last; the running value total climbs non-trivially and is rendered against the struck price; a promise is restated between drops | a drop that strips value, restates no promise, or adds only a trivial/restated item so the running value total stays flat |
| 7 value-gap before FINAL | Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.2 VALUE-GAP slide before FINAL (FIX-5) | a value-gap slide precedes the final price | final price with no value-gap framing |
| 8 nothing fabricated | placeholder discipline (c19 line, never fabricated) | values/promises from interview or clean placeholders | invented numbers, deliverables, or promises |

A deck missing a required named component (stack, tally, promises, value-gap) fails the relevant QC presence gate at Phase 1Q and the deck-level confirmation at Phase 6.

## 4. PASS vs FAIL EXAMPLES (drawn from the forensic reference deck and the gold-standard reference deck)

**FAIL (the forensic reference deck):** no itemized value stack (one slide only re-asserts the anchor number without summing components), no promises stated before the anchor, no value-gap slide before the final price. Fails the stack, promises, and value-gap presence gates.

**PASS (the gold-standard reference deck):** promises planted early (the example signature promise repeated); each Day delivered with a dollar value; s57 "Everything You Get" sums to "$5,282" with "And I still haven't told you what you actually pay."; s58 "Add It All Up, Over $5,000" ties the tally to the anchor before the cheapest drops; the re-pitch (s74) restates the total value "$5,276+" versus "$47 / $97." Passes every gate.

**PASS (the example brand re-sequenced, priceless frame):** the client's promises planted before the anchor (from the client interview); the components itemized (the framework, the strategy session, the program curriculum, the challenge, ongoing sessions) from the client interview; because the outcome is non-monetary, the value frame is priceless (the American Express device), not a fabricated dollar total; a value-gap beat precedes the final entry price. The client's actual components and any prices come from the client interview, never fabricated.

---

## 5. ESCALATION / REPAIR PATH

1. On a missing-component QC fail (no stack, no tally, no promises, no value-gap), the repair is an arc and copy change routed to the Director, then to the Offer Price Strategist (for the stack/tally/value-gap numbers) and the Slide Copywriter (for the promise copy).
2. Coordinate with SOP-PITCH-01: the promises beat precedes the anchor, the stack and tally precede Drop 1, and the value-gap precedes the FINAL. The Director reserves these slots at arc time so they do not collide with the price-beat spacing.
3. For a non-monetary offer, confirm the priceless frame is used and that no dollar value was fabricated; if the interview has not supplied components, hold clean placeholders and do not invent.
4. Loop up to 3 times. On the 4th failure escalate to the Director and ROLE-16 Healer per QC SOP 9.4.
