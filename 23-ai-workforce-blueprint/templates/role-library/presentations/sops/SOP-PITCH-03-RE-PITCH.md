# SOP-PITCH-03: RE-PITCH (THE MANDATORY POST-PRICE RECAP)

**Cluster:** Pitch-Craft Rules (the offer choreography)
**Master authority:** universal-sops/CLIENT-WEBINAR-DECK-SOP.md (Section 4.2 the proven flow, Section 5.5 the price sequence)
**Owning role at write time:** Offer Price Strategist (the re-pitch choreography), Slide Copywriter (the recap copy)
**Enforced at the gate by:** QC Specialist - Presentations (copy QC criterion c23 re-pitch presence gate, c24 close-density gate)
**Gold-standard numbers:** the gold-standard 75-slide reference deck (the gold-standard principles reference, Section 5): re-pitch s74 "Here's Everything You're Getting" ("TOTAL VALUE $5,276+" vs "$47 / $97", "This is not a deal. This is a decision."), send-off s75.
**Status:** Standalone pitch-craft SOP. This document states the re-pitch doctrine as a single checkable pass/fail procedure. It points at the already-live enforcement (Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.9 Re-Pitch Choreography and QC copy criteria c23/c24, FIX-7 / FIX-8) and adds no contradictory code.

---

## 1. PURPOSE

After the final price is revealed, the deck does NOT just close. The forensic reference deck revealed the price and ended on a moment slide and a thank-you, leaving the offer un-recapped. The gold-standard reference deck recaps the entire offer after the final price: the full stack re-listed, the total value versus the price restated, the promises restated, the guarantee restated, the urgency reset, then a warm send-off. The re-pitch is the genuinely-missing structural beat the forensic named. This SOP makes a 4-to-7-slide re-pitch block mandatory after the final price and before the close.

---

## 2. THE HARD RULE (the re-pitch procedure)

1. **A 4-to-7-slide re-pitch block follows the FINAL price reveal**, before the hook-reprise close and the send-off. A deck whose price is revealed and then simply ends (price reveal goes straight to the close with no post-price recap) is INCOMPLETE and fails.

2. **The re-pitch contains, at minimum:**
   - A full "here is everything you get" recap (each component, its dollar value or priceless frame, checkmarks) in one clean view.
   - The restated TOTAL VALUE versus the FINAL price (the value gap).
   - The restated promise inventory (the outcomes, from SOP-PITCH-02).
   - The guarantee restated.
   - Objection kills (the top objections answered).
   - A reset urgency / scarcity beat (the "next 15 minutes" window restated; this price does not exist after this).
   - The final CTA.

3. **The re-pitch restates, it does not introduce.** The components, values, and promises in the re-pitch are the same ones already stacked before the price (SOP-PITCH-02). The re-pitch is a recap; it does not invent a new deliverable, a new number, or a new promise.

4. **The urgency is reset inside the final beat.** The scarcity window is restated ("next 15 minutes", "this price does not exist after this webinar, on Google, or on the sales page; it exists right here, right now").

5. **The close is dense enough.** Between the Wall of Wins and the final CTA the close carries the objection kills, the guarantee, the value recap, the re-pitch, the scarcity, the last-call plus URL, and the welcome: never fewer than about 8 slides on a 45-plus-slide deck. A too-thin close (the Wall of Wins crammed against the CTA) fails (cross-reference SOP-PITCH-04 spacing).

6. **The send-off is warm and last.** After the re-pitch, a short celebratory send-off closes the deck ("You made it. Welcome to the family. See you on Day 1.").

---

## 3. THE ENFORCEMENT CHECK (pass/fail)

This SOP is the authored doctrine behind the LIVE QC criterion c23 (re-pitch presence) and c24 (close density), and the Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.9 (Re-Pitch Choreography). The mapping:

| Rule | Live enforcement (already wired) | PASS | FAIL |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 a 4-7 slide re-pitch after FINAL | QC copy criterion c23 (FIX-7): a 4-7 slide recap block exists AFTER the FINAL price; absent = fail | a 4-7 slide re-pitch block follows the final price before the close | price revealed then deck simply ends (no re-pitch) |
| 2 minimum contents | QC c23 (recap table + value gap + promises + guarantee + objection kills + reset urgency + CTA); Offer Price Strategist SOP 9.9 | all minimum contents present in the block | re-pitch present but missing a required element |
| 3 restates, does not introduce | SOP-PITCH-02 cross-check; placeholder discipline | recap uses already-stacked components/values/promises | a new deliverable/number/promise invented in the recap |
| 5 close density | QC copy criterion c24 (FIX-8): post-Wall close >= ~8 slides on a 45+ deck; Wall not within 2 slides of CTA | close is at least ~8 slides; Wall spaced from the CTA | too-thin close, or Wall within 2 slides of the CTA |

A deck missing the re-pitch fails c23 at Phase 1Q (absent = fail) and the deck-level re-pitch confirmation at Phase 6. A too-thin close fails c24.

---

## 4. PASS vs FAIL EXAMPLES (drawn from the forensic reference deck and the gold-standard reference deck)

**FAIL (the forensic reference deck):** the final price was revealed and the deck went straight to a moment slide and a thank-you, with no recap of the stack, no restated value gap, no restated promises, no objection kills, no reset urgency. Fails c23 (re-pitch absent).

**PASS (the gold-standard reference deck):** after the final $47/$97 reveal at s73, s74 "Here's Everything You're Getting" re-lists the entire 8-item stack as a grid, each with its value, "TOTAL VALUE $5,276+" versus "$47 / $97", capped with "This is not a deal. This is a decision."; the urgency is reset inside the final beat ("next 15 minutes"; "this price does not exist after this webinar"); s75 is the warm send-off. Passes c23 and c24.

**PASS (the example brand re-sequenced):** after the client's final entry price, a 4-to-5-slide re-pitch recaps the client's components (from the client interview), restates the value gap (priceless frame), restates the client's promises (from the client interview), restates the guarantee, answers the top objections, resets the 15-minute window, then a warm send-off. Nothing new is introduced in the recap; everything restates the earlier stack.

---

## 5. ESCALATION / REPAIR PATH

1. On a c23 fail (re-pitch absent or incomplete), the repair is an arc and copy change routed to the Director, then to the Offer Price Strategist (SOP 9.9 choreography) and the Slide Copywriter (recap copy). The Director reserves the 4-to-7 re-pitch slots at arc time.
2. On a c24 fail (too-thin close, or Wall within 2 slides of the CTA), the Director adds close slides until the post-Wall close is at least about 8 slides on a 45-plus-slide deck and the Wall sits about 5 slides before the offer (cross-reference SOP-PITCH-04).
3. Confirm the re-pitch restates only already-stacked content; if a new number or promise appears, route it back to the Slide Copywriter to remove (the re-pitch recaps, it does not introduce).
4. Loop up to 3 times. On the 4th failure escalate to the Director and ROLE-16 Healer per QC SOP 9.4.
