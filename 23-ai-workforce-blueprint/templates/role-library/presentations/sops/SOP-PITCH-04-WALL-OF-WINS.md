---

## 1. PURPOSE

The Wall of Wins is an homage to REAL people who got results, a crowd of past clients who "just won", placed near the close as a proof avalanche right before the decision. The forensic reference deck misread it: slide 29 ("Do Not Take Our Word For It. Watch What Changes.") made it a future-pace about the prospect's OWN outcome, which is exactly wrong. The reviewer, verbatim (the transcript reference, Section 3): "her wall of results, a wall of wins; as time goes on you're gonna have more and more people who just won; this is not one testimonial, this is a crowd; she sees herself in those faces." This SOP makes the Wall of Wins a real-client homage, correctly framed and correctly spaced, and bans the future-pace anti-pattern.

---

## 2. THE HARD RULE (the Wall-of-Wins procedure)

1. **The Wall of Wins is REAL named client results, never a future-pace.** It concentrates multiple named, located, real client wins in one view: a crowd of past clients who got results, not the product, not the prospect's own future. A wall that future-paces the buyer's OWN outcome ("Watch What Changes" about the buyer's child, business, or subject) is NOT a Wall of Wins and fails.

2. **A passing wall shows, at minimum:**
   - At least 4 real named clients, each with a city or location and a specific result number (a stat, an enrollment count, a revenue figure, a timeframe).
   - An aggregate stat band (the totals: clients served, outcomes delivered, average timeframe).
   - A "these are your peers" line (the audience sees themselves in the faces: "These aren't case studies. These are your future colleagues." or equivalent).

3. **Real wins only; never fabricated.** The names, locations, and result numbers come from the client's interview. Until the interview supplies them, use clean placeholders ([CLIENT WIN - owner to confirm]) and never invent a name, an initial, or a number. Placeholder discipline holds until real data arrives.

4. **It is its own proof beat.** The Wall of Wins is distinct from the single proof-within-two-slides testimonials and from any white-paper/research proof; it is a deliberate concentration of social proof, a growing board that expands as more clients win over time.

5. **It sits about 5 slides before the final offer, with a build-up run between.** Place the Wall of Wins roughly 5 slides before the final offer (4 to 6 acceptable), with the final emotional close, last-call urgency, and a build-up run between it and the price, never jammed within 2 slides of the CTA. (Gold-standard s68 to s73 = 5 slides, with the choice/this-is-your-moment/doors-closing/build-up run between.)

6. **The grid is the device, not a single testimonial.** It is a multi-portrait grid (the gold-standard reference deck used a 3x2 of six owner portraits with name+city chips and result chips), an honest results-disclaimer included, so the proof reads as a crowd, not one cherry-picked story.

## 3. THE ENFORCEMENT CHECK (pass/fail)

This SOP is the authored doctrine behind the LIVE QC criterion c19 (Wall-of-Wins framing) and c24 (spacing). The mapping:

| Rule | Live enforcement (already wired) | PASS | FAIL |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 real, not future-pace | QC copy criterion c19 (FIX-6 anti-pattern): future-pacing the buyer's OWN outcome ("Watch What Changes") FAILS | real named client results, a crowd of past clients | a future-pace about the buyer's own child/business/subject |
| 2 minimum content | QC c19: >= 4 real named clients, each city/location + result number, plus aggregate band, plus "these are your peers" line | >= 4 named clients with city + result, aggregate band, peers line | fewer than 4 named clients, or missing the aggregate band or peers line |
| 3 real wins only | QC c19 (real wins from interview only; placeholders until real data; never fabricated) | interview-sourced wins, or clean [CLIENT WIN - owner to confirm] placeholders | invented names, initials, or numbers |
| 4 own proof beat, present | QC c19 (a deck with no wall-of-wins element fails); named component (master rule 20) | a dedicated Wall of Wins exists near the close | no Wall of Wins element |
| 5 ~5 slides before the offer | QC copy criterion c24 (FIX-8): Wall not within 2 slides of the CTA; close >= ~8 slides on a 45+ deck | Wall ~5 slides before the offer with a build-up run between | Wall within 2 slides of the CTA, or jammed against the offer |

A future-paced wall, a fabricated wall, a missing wall, or a too-tightly-spaced wall fails the relevant gate at Phase 1Q and the deck-level confirmation at Phase 6.

## 4. PASS vs FAIL EXAMPLES (drawn from the forensic reference deck and the gold-standard reference deck)

**FAIL (the forensic reference deck):** slide 29 "Do Not Take Our Word For It. Watch What Changes." future-paced the prospect's OWN outcome instead of presenting real client results, and it sat about 3 slides before the offer. Fails c19 (future-pace anti-pattern) and c24 (spacing, within the jammed zone).

**PASS (the gold-standard reference deck):** s68 "A Wall Of Wins" is a 3x2 grid of six real owner portraits, each with a name+city chip and a result chip, plus three aggregate stat chips, tagged "These aren't case studies. These are your future colleagues.", with an honest results-disclaimer. It sits 5 slides before the final offer (s73) with the choice/moment/last-call/build-up run between. Passes c19 and c24.

**PASS (the example brand re-sequenced):** the client's Wall of Wins is real client wins with real results (name + city + outcome) from the client interview, a multi-portrait grid with an aggregate band and a "these are your peers" line, placed about 5 slides before the offer with a build-up run between. Until the client interview supplies the real wins, the tiles hold clean [CLIENT WIN - owner to confirm] placeholders and are never fabricated; the slide is not rendered with a bracket token on its face (cross-reference the audience-facing placeholder ban).

---

## 5. ESCALATION / REPAIR PATH

1. On a c19 framing fail (future-pace, fabricated, or missing wall), the repair routes to the Slide Copywriter (real-client framing, interview-sourced wins, peers line) and, if a slide slot must be added, to the Director for the arc.
2. On a c24 spacing fail (Wall within 2 slides of the CTA), the Director adds close slides so the Wall sits about 5 slides before the offer with a build-up run between (cross-reference SOP-PITCH-03 close density).
3. If the interview has not supplied real wins, hold clean [CLIENT WIN - owner to confirm] placeholders; never invent. The placeholder must be resolved or the slide pulled before render (a bracket token must never reach a rendered image; see the audience-facing placeholder ban).
4. Loop up to 3 times. On the 4th failure escalate to the Director and ROLE-16 Healer per QC SOP 9.4.
