## 1. THE MODEL -- SEVEN P'S, ONE VEHICLE (P53-P54)

There are seven P's, organized as one memorable vehicle that carries the audience to the destination:

- **PRIORITY is the destination.** Everything exists to move the audience to re-rank your thing to number one. (The North Star, SOP-NORTHSTAR-00.)
- **POSITION is the vehicle.** Positioning is the mechanism that actually drives them there. The master lever.
- **PROCLAMATION and PROMISE are the fuel.** The bold declaration of truth and the bold declaration of outcome power the vehicle. (Proclamation: SOP-PROCLAMATION-01. Promise: the one repeatable outcome, §5 below.)
- **PERSON is the driver.** The presenter, correctly positioned, steers the whole thing.
- **PRODUCT and PRICE are the cargo.** What you deliver and what it costs. Must be sound and correctly valued -- but rarely the real reason a deck fails.

> The model in one line: **move them to the destination (Priority) by driving the vehicle (Position), powered by the fuel (Proclamation and Promise), steered by the driver (Person), carrying sound cargo (Product and Price).**

## 2. THE TWO LEVELS OF DIAGNOSIS (P55-P60)

This is the crucial structure for using the framework correctly. It applies whether you are diagnosing a submitted deck (repair) or pressure-testing one you are building from scratch (design spec).

**PRIMARY DIAGNOSTIC -- the four P's: Product, Price, Position, Person.** Run first to localize the gross problem. In repair, run by **elimination**. In a from-scratch build, run as a **design spec**, confirming each is sound before building.

> "Out of all the people that you met with and they haven't purchased, which of the four P's do you feel is most likely your problem?" -- Trevor Otts

> "I think pricing is part of the position." -- Trevor Otts (why price usually collapses into positioning)

**ADVANCED DIAGNOSTIC -- the three power P's: Priority, Proclamation, Promise.** Precision tools. Reach for them once the primary diagnostic localizes the problem -- which it **almost always localizes to Position**. The advanced P's tell you exactly what is broken inside the positioning:
- is the priority shift missing entirely? (**Priority** -> run the eight moves, SOP-PRIORITY-02)
- are the truths hedged and generic? (**Proclamation** -> SOP-PROCLAMATION-01)
- is there no single clear outcome? (**Promise** -> §5)

The four P's find the **broad area**. The three power P's find the **precise mechanism**.

## 3. THE PRIMARY FOUR P'S -- DIAGNOSE / SPEC / REMEDIATE

### 3.1 PRODUCT (cargo)
**Diagnose:** has this ever worked or sold for anyone before? If yes, it is not the product. **Spec (from scratch):** confirm the value is real before designing anything around it. **Remediate:** if genuinely unproven, pivot to the proven offer; if sound but sprawling, simplify scope to one clear outcome (a scope problem is a positioning problem in disguise); if proven, stop adjusting it and move to Position. **Failure mode:** rebuilding a proven product when the real gap was positioning or person.

### 3.2 PRICE (cargo)
> "Price only matters in the absence of value." -- Trevor Otts

When value is clearly seen, price recedes; when value is absent, price is the only thing left to weigh. Almost every price objection is a **value** problem, which is a **positioning and vision** problem. **Diagnose:** would lowering it actually change the ranking? A price low enough to afford trivially cannot be the obstacle. *"The loudest people in the room are always the brokest people in the room"* -- survey the quiet non-buyers, don't react to the loud minority. **Remediate:** anchor value high and use the value drop; never cut price to fix positioning. **Full price-is-a-value doctrine lives in SOP-OBJECTION-01; the value drop in SOP-PITCH-01/02.**

### 3.3 POSITION (vehicle) -- the master lever, the most underdeveloped P
**Definition:** how the offer is framed so the audience prioritizes it. Positioning is the ONE P that shifts priorities -- product, price, and person do not do that job. It is the second half of the presenter's job (*sell the vision and position the product*) -- placing the product as the prioritized **vehicle to the vision the audience now wants**, not describing features. (Vision-selling and the lightning-rod doctrine: SOP-VISION-01.) **Diagnose:** positioning is the culprit when interested people stay, engage, ask questions, even ask for payment plans -- and then do nothing. That is the signature of a positioning failure and the most common failure of all. **Remediate:** the eight-move build sequence in **SOP-PRIORITY-02** (the core remediation of the whole framework). **Failure mode:** speaking clearly to features while never speaking to priority, so the audience understands the thing perfectly and still ranks it last.

### 3.4 PERSON (driver) -- diagnose yourself
**Definition:** the presenter and how they self-position. Two faces: a diagnostic (am I the reason?) and a construction element (deliberate self-positioning that earns standing). The clearest tell: *"I always know when it's me. When I try to teach to the sale."* **Full Person diagnostic + first-option self-positioning lives in SOP-PERSON-00.** This SOP only flags Person as one of the four primary buckets.

## 4. THE THREE POWER P'S -- THE ADVANCED DIAGNOSTIC

### 4.1 PRIORITY (the destination, the master P)
**Diagnose:** did the deck engineer a priority shift at all, or merely inform and offer? Did it surface the current priorities, expose the cost of leaving the thing low, and explicitly move it to the top? If those moves are absent, the shift never happened -- the root cause beneath most positioning failures. **This is the gate `AF-NO-SHIFT`.** **Remediate:** the full eight-move build sequence (SOP-PRIORITY-02).

### 4.2 PROCLAMATION (the fuel)
A bold, clear, declarative statement of truth -- not a hedge, suggestion, or soft opinion. **Diagnose:** is the language hedged and generic ("maybe," "kind of," "you might want to consider") or stated plainly and specifically? Tentative language transfers doubt and leaves the priority feeling optional. **Full doctrine + the `AF-PROCLAMATION-HEDGE` gate live in SOP-PROCLAMATION-01.**

### 4.3 PROMISE (the fuel) -- governed here
**Definition:** the **one** big, clear outcome the deck promises -- the transformation. *"What's your big wow factor? What's your big promise? And what's your demonstration?"* **Diagnose:** can the audience repeat back ONE clear promise? If the deck promises many small things or no single bold outcome, there is nothing for the priority shift to attach to -- a diffuse promise is no promise. **Remediate:** choose one promise and make it the spine; make it specific, bold, and believable through the demonstration and proof (SOP-PITCH-02 carries the promise/value-stack machinery); cut or subordinate secondary claims -- keep the main thing the main thing (SOP-SLIDE-01). Example: *"This is how you run a company as a solo operator and build a seven-figure legacy from your own story."* One promise, stated plainly.

## 5. RUNNING THE DIAGNOSTIC -- THE STRATEGIST'S PROCEDURE (P0B-PRIORITY)

At the P0B-PRIORITY seat (order 0.2), the Attention Content Strategist records the diagnosis into `working/copy/priority_shift_spec.json`:

1. **Identify the creation mode first** (from-scratch / content-personal / content-general -- SOP-MODE-00). The mode decides whether you DESIGN the P's in or DIAGNOSE why they failed.
2. **Run the primary four P's.** Repair: eliminate Product (ever sold?), Price (would a lower number change the ranking?), Person (sold with conviction, offered cleanly, did not teach to the sale?). Whatever remains -- usually **Position** -- is the localized problem. From scratch: confirm each as a design spec.
3. **Run the advanced three P's inside the localized problem.** Priority engineered at all? Truths bold and specific (Proclamation)? One repeatable Promise?
4. **Hand the spec to SOP-PRIORITY-02** to build/repair the eight moves that install the shift.

## 6. APPENDIX A -- THE COMPLETE P GLOSSARY (sharpened, P168)

- **Priority** -- the destination; the re-ranking of your thing to the top of the audience's stack. The master P.
- **Position** -- the vehicle; how the offer is framed so it gets prioritized. The master lever; usual culprit.
- **Proclamation** -- bold, specific, declarative truth. Where there is a proclamation of truth, there is a demonstration of power.
- **Promise** -- the one bold, repeatable outcome; the transformation. The fuel, with Proclamation.
- **Person** -- the driver; the presenter and their self-positioning.
- **Product** -- the cargo; the actual thing offered or taught.
- **Price** -- the cargo; the cost and structure. Matters only in the absence of value.
