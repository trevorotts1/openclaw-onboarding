## 1. What This Department Does (in plain language)

The fleet-wide quality function for every Zero Human Company. Quality Control owns and operates the ZHC System Analyzer: it reads every OTHER department's roles and standard operating procedures and holds them to the standard on two independent axes. Axis 1 (Reality) asks whether each mechanism is actually executed at runtime, with file and line proof, never from prose. Axis 2 (Specificity, also called right-sizing) asks whether an autonomous worker who has never seen the business could read each procedure once and execute it to completion, making every decision it requires, without guessing a value, a tool, an endpoint, a threshold, or a failure response the procedure did not give them. The department diagnoses; it never repairs in place. Every failure it finds is filed to the Bugs Department and routed to the Healer for the fix. A long procedure is welcome when it earns its length (up to roughly seven thousand five hundred words); brevity is never a merit and artificially thin procedures are flagged. Quality Control works hand in hand with the Healer Department (it routes diagnosed defects there) and with every other department (each one is a subject of audit).

In one sentence: **Owns and operates the system analyzer: holds every other department's roles and procedures to the standard on two axes (is it real, is it specific enough to follow) and routes the failures it finds to the Healer**

You do not need to know which specialist does what. You just tell the department
what you want in plain English, and the department head (Director of Quality Control)
figures out who handles it and routes it for you.

## 3. How to Ask It for Work

You have three ways to put this department to work. All of them are fine.

1. **Just say it in plain English.** Message your agent like you would a
   teammate: "I need help with something from the quality control team." That is enough to start.
2. **Name the department if you want to be specific.** "Have the
   Quality Control department handle something from the quality control team." This routes
   it straight to Director of Quality Control.
3. **Name a specialist if you know exactly who you want.** See the specialist
   list in Section 4 and ask for them by role: "Get the Procedure Auditor
   to take on a quality control task for you."

A good request includes, where it applies: **what** you want, **who or what it
is for**, **when you need it**, and any **must-haves or limits**. You do not have
to provide all of that. If something important is missing, the department will
first ask whether you want a quick interview (a few key questions) or a more
in-depth one, then ask one question at a time, never a wall of questions.

## 5. What to Expect Back

When you ask this department for something, here is the normal flow:

1. **Acknowledgment.** Director of Quality Control confirms the request landed and, if
   anything important is unclear, first asks whether you want a quick interview (a
   few key questions) or a more in-depth one, then asks one question at a time,
   never a wall of questions.
2. **Routing.** The work is matched to the right specialist and the relevant
   procedure (its SOP). Nobody guesses; if there is no procedure for your
   request, one is written before the work starts.
3. **The work itself.** The specialist does the job and it is checked by the
   department's quality-control review before it reaches you.
4. **Delivery.** You get the finished result: the finished quality control work you asked for.
   Anything that needs your sign-off before it goes live is flagged for your
   approval first.

Typical turnaround depends on the size of the request. Quick items come back the
same working session; larger projects come back with a clear estimate up front.

## 6. How It Hands Off (to you and to other departments)

- **To you:** finished deliverables arrive in your workspace and you are notified.
  Anything marked owner-approval-required waits for your yes before it ships.
- **To other departments:** when your request needs another team, this department
  coordinates the handoff for you through the company's routing map
  (`universal-sops/00-ROUTING.md`). You do not have to manage the handoff.
  
- **Escalation:** if something is blocked, needs a decision only you can make, or
  needs a credential or payment, it is escalated to you directly rather than
  stalling silently.
