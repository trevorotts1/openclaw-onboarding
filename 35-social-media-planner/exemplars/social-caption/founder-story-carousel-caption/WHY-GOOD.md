# WHY-GOOD.md — social-caption / founder-story-carousel-caption

This is the worked example playbook.md Section 18/19 names as the one
intentional gap ("no full worked example… to prevent the document from
becoming bloated"). It exists as a CALIBRATION reference, not as a
replacement for the zone system, pitch examples, or comment examples
already documented there.

**Register.** First-person, specific-numbers-over-adjectives ("four
clients," "eighteen months," not "struggled a lot"). Social captions live or
die on the first line surviving the platform's "...more" truncation — the
hook here is a complete, load-bearing sentence on its own, not a teaser
fragment that only makes sense once the reader taps to expand.

**Structure — the zone system, applied.** Hook names a real stake (almost
shut down) without over-explaining it. Body follows a
turn-the-cliché-on-its-head shape: state the conventional advice, reject
it, name the ACTUAL problem underneath, resolve it with something concrete
(one honest conversation, not a rebrand). This is the mechanism that makes
a founder-story post read as earned rather than performed — the turn has
to be specific enough that it could not be copy-pasted onto a different
founder's story unchanged.

**The carousel bridge.** "Swipe through for the three questions" does real
work: it is the caption's job to make the SWIPE feel necessary, not
optional — a caption that fully resolves the story in text gives the reader
no reason to open the carousel at all.

**The close.** The soft CTA asks a specific, answerable question tied
directly to the story just told ("which question hit?") rather than a
generic "thoughts? 👇" — a specific question gets specific comments, which
is what the comment-reply loop (Skill 35's engagement mechanism,
`comment_reader.py`) needs to have something real to work with.

**What a copy-through of this exemplar would look like (avoid this):**
reusing "Harbor & Pine," "year-two spreadsheet moment," "four clients," or
the exact turn structure on a real client's post. The MECHANISM to copy is:
a complete load-bearing hook line, state-the-cliché-then-reject-it body
shape, a swipe-bridge that makes the carousel necessary, and a specific
answerable closing question — built fresh from THIS client's own story and
numbers, never from this illustrative text.
