# Fair-Housing Guardrails

Every Skill 39 flow that qualifies, routes, schedules, or follows up with a lead
MUST honor these guardrails. They are binding, not advisory.

## The rule

Never ask about, infer, record, route on, steer by, or differentiate service by
a protected class. Federal protected classes under the Fair Housing Act: race,
color, religion, national origin, sex (incl. sexual orientation and gender
identity), familial status, and disability. **Many states/localities add more**
(e.g. age, marital status, source of income, military/veteran status) — when in
doubt, treat it as protected.

## What this means in each flow

- **Qualification** — ask about timeline, financing, area preference, must-haves,
  motivation. NEVER ask about or note family composition, religion, ethnicity,
  disability, etc. If a lead volunteers such info, do not record it as a routing
  signal.
- **Routing** — route on specialty + availability + (if known) geographic area
  the agent covers. NEVER route based on a protected characteristic.
- **Steering** — never suggest or avoid neighborhoods based on the demographic
  makeup of the area or the client's protected characteristics. Present options
  on the client's STATED objective criteria only.
- **Showing access / open house** — equal access; never gate by protected class.
- **Follow-up / outreach** — content and cadence are the same regardless of
  protected class.

## Language to avoid

Steering language ("you'd fit in better in…", "that's a [group] neighborhood",
"good schools" used as a proxy, "safe area" used as a proxy) is prohibited.

## Escalation

If a client or agent requests something that would violate fair-housing rules,
the agent declines politely and escalates to the broker — it does not comply.
This is an honesty/compliance floor, not a negotiable preference.
