# Email Teaser Click-Driver — Short Click-to-Page Broadcast Email

**id:** `email-teaser-click-driver` · **category:** engagement-broadcast
**aliases:** email teaser · click-driver email · short broadcast email · affiliate email · JV email · curiosity click email
**source:** Copywriting Secrets **Secret #18 — How To Write Killer Email Teasers—FAST** · Secret #6 (subject = headline) · Secret #9 (curiosity bullets) · Secret #13 (write to one person)

> **DISTINCT from the Seinfeld story email.** This template is the SHORT click-driver — its ONLY job is to get a click to a page (sales letter, video, blog post, webinar, review). The page does the selling. Book: *"Most people make the mistake of selling the offer in the email teaser. Don't! Your sales letter or video sales letter will sell them."*

---

## TRIGGER
Any broadcast send where the goal is a **CLICK to a specific page** — offer drive, content push, webinar registration, affiliate or JV send. GHL: Email Campaign (Broadcast) to `seinfeld-broadcast` Smart List, or a workflow `Send-Email` step pointing to the destination page.

## CHANNELS
Email (primary) · SMS teaser (same short formula applies).

## SEQUENCE — the 6-part email teaser anatomy
| # | Beat | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Subject line** | Short, concise — often a question. Book examples: *"Worried about failing your next PT test?"* · *"This blew me away"* · *"Want to be a published author?"* — about THEM, not you. Prequalifies the right opener. |
| 2 | **Salutation** | First-name merge: *"Hey {{contact.first_name}},"* — Book: *"Make people feel like they're part of the group and being acknowledged."* If no merge: *"Hey everyone,"* or *"Hi Fellow [tribe identity]."* Never skip it. |
| 3 | **Shocking statement / pattern interrupt** | One punchy line OR open question to pop the reader out of autopilot. Examples: *"Got something that's going to blow your mind."* / *"Did you know that 99 out of 100 people who try to write a book fail?"* / *"This was totally unexpected."* |
| 4 | **2–4 curiosity bullets** | SHORT bullets that build curiosity about what is on the page — NOT selling the offer. Book: *"pumping up the benefits of what they will see, not of what you're selling."* Apply Ultimate Bullet Formula lightly (`it ___ so you can ___ which means ___`) but keep each bullet short. |
| 5 | **CTA + link** | One directional instruction + the link. Book examples: *"Click here right now to see it."* / *"You've got to check this out."* One link. Zero competing links. |
| 6 | **Personal close** | *"I'll see you over there,"* + name. Book: *"'I'll see you over there' signals they're going somewhere they'd want to be."* One line. |

## WORKED EXAMPLES (verbatim from Copywriting Secrets Secret #18)
**Short (most successful):**
> Subject: This blew me away
> Hey [first name], This was totally unexpected. Somebody just did a review and it's amazing! You've got to check this out. [LINK] See you over there, [Name]

**Bullet format:**
> Subject: Want to be a published author?
> Hey [first name], Did you know that 99 out of 100 people who try to write a book fail? Yep. It sucks. The main reasons: they don't know how to create content that sells · they don't know how to get it formatted · they don't know how to publish it.
> The cool news is I have a great new video that shows you exactly how to solve all those problems—fast and easy! Click here right now to see it. [LINK] I'll see you over there. [Name]

## FORMAT RULES
- **Keep it SHORT.** Book: *"If you must err, go with shorter."* 40–60% of readers are on mobile.
- **Write to ONE person.** Book: *"Though you may send that email to a million people, they will read it one person at a time."*
- **Friends don't send friends ten-page sales letters in email!** (verbatim book rule)
- **Email teaser ≠ sales letter.** The email prepares them; the PAGE converts.

## WHEN TO USE THIS vs. THE SEINFELD STORY EMAIL
| Seinfeld story email | Email teaser |
|---|---|
| 90% entertainment story → pivot → offer in the email | No story. Short. Only job = click to page |
| 200–500+ words | 40–100 words |
| Bonds daily; sells via story | Sends traffic; page sells |
| Best for: daily relationship/engagement | Best for: promo drives, content pushes, JV/affiliate sends |

## COPY PERSONA + SCRIPT
Jim Edwards (Copywriting Secrets) — short, punchy, friend-to-friend, curiosity-first. The email writes like a quick note to a buddy, not a marketing campaign.

## GHL BUILD (Skill 44)
- Save as **"Email Teaser Shell"** in Marketing ▸ Emails ▸ Templates (separate from "Seinfeld Broadcast Shell").
- Design: plain text feel, first-name merge, no heavy image header, **one** CTA text link or button, no P.S. (length discipline).
- For JV/affiliate use: export the finished teaser as a `.txt` file for the partner — short enough they'll actually send it unchanged.
- Primary tracking metric: **click-through rate**, not open rate.

## FLEXIBILITY
- **Mode 1 (User has specific copy):** Template is optional reference only for structure. Never impose.
- **Mode 2 (User is unsure):** Suggest the teaser for any traffic-driving send and explain SHORT is the feature.
- **Mode 3 (Just do it):** Fill in subject + salutation + 1-sentence interrupt + 2-3 bullets + CTA link + personal close. Done.

## KPIs
Click-through rate (primary) · post-click page conversion rate · unsubscribe rate (mismatch signal) · for JV sends: click-per-list-member.
