# Three M's Value-Broadcast — Myths, Misconceptions, Mistakes + Future Pacing Story Engine

**id:** `three-ms-content-broadcast` · **category:** engagement-broadcast
**aliases:** Three M's · Myths Misconceptions Mistakes · content broadcast framework · "make them thirsty" · value content emails · broadcast topic generator
**source:** Copywriting Secrets **Secret #20 — Make 'Em More Thirsty** (4+1 story types; Three M's of Content; Future Pacing)

> **The content-creation engine for daily broadcasts.** Jim Edwards' answer to *"how do I give value that creates buying desire WITHOUT giving away the solution?"* — Book: *"Stories make people thirsty, but sales copy tells them where to buy a drink."* The Three M's produce perceived-value content that increases purchase urgency without giving away the product.

---

## TRIGGER
Content-brainstorming layer over the daily Seinfeld broadcast. Answers *"what do I write today?"* when no strong personal story is available. No new GHL workflow — applied when composing any broadcast Email Campaign.

## CHANNELS
Email broadcast · Blog (repurpose verbatim) · Social media (repurpose the hook) · Short-form video script.

## THE CORE INSIGHT (book, Secret #20)
> *"You've most likely heard the saying 'stories sell.' However, I've found stories make people 'thirsty', but sales copy tells them where to buy a drink."*
>
> *"You can create stories around myths, misconceptions, and mistakes WITHOUT providing any solution that is part of your product. However, when you create stories with the Three M's of Content, people will feel like you have been spilling the beans all day long."*

This solves the content creator's dilemma: too much free value → no reason to buy. Too little → boring. Three M's give perceived generosity while deepening desire for the purchase.

---

## THE 4+1 STORY TYPES
Pick ONE per broadcast email:

| # | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Actual story** | A real event from your life, business, or about someone else that illustrates the lesson. |
| 2 | **Case study** | Customer result story: *"I was here → had this problem → did this → here are my results."* 3-act hero's journey. |
| 3 | **Example** | Show HOW something works, HOW it was applied, WHAT results followed. |
| 4 | **Three M's** | Myth · Misconception · Mistake — see the rotation below. One M per email. |
| 5 | **Future Pacing** | *(Bonus)* Paint the vivid story of their future self AFTER implementing. |

---

## THE THREE M'S ROTATION — one per email
| M | Purpose | Subject angle | Body framework |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MYTH** | Bust a false belief keeping them stuck. Book: *"Myths are basically untruths people believe... whole books are written around myths."* | *"The myth about ___"* / *"Everyone says X — here's why that's wrong"* / *"The biggest lie in ___"* | Name the myth → show why false (story/data) → plant new belief the offer satisfies → Stealth/Columbo pivot → CTA. |
| **MISCONCEPTION** | Correct an incorrect idea; replace false view with true one. Book: *"Misconceptions involve incorrect ideas about something. You can clear up misconceptions — including false beliefs."* | *"Why most people get ___ wrong"* / *"What ___ actually means"* / *"The truth about ___"* | Identify misconception → what is actually true → consequence of continuing to misunderstand → connect to offer → CTA. |
| **MISTAKE** | Point out what people are doing wrong. Book: *"Mistakes are the most powerful of the three because nobody wants to make a mistake. No adult wants to look stupid."* | *"The #1 mistake ___ makes with ___"* / *"Warning: are you doing this?"* / *"Stop doing ___ if you want ___"* | Name the mistake → agitate the consequence → hint at the correct approach (enough to create desire, not enough to solve it) → CTA to the full solution. |

### BONUS — Future Pacing (5th story type, Secret #20)
> *"I want you to imagine having your own unique book with your name on it as the author. You hold that book up…"*

Subject angles: *"Imagine if…"* / *"What your life looks like 90 days from now"* / *"Picture this…"*
Body: Open with *"I want you to imagine…"* → vivid sensory detail of their life AFTER implementing → specific wins, emotions, status → anchor: *"The only difference between that future and your right now is [X]"* → CTA.

Book cross-reference from DCS Secret #8: *"EVERY EMAIL and each story must be tied back into some type of offer."*

## FLEXIBILITY
- **Mode 1 (User has content/angle):** Framework is reference only. Never impose the Three M's if the user has their own direction.
- **Mode 2 (Unsure what to write):** Suggest the **Mistake** angle first (book: "most powerful of the three"). Present options; let user pick.
- **Mode 3 (Just do it):** Pick the M, mine a topic from customer questions, write with the Stealth/Columbo tie-to-offer, send to `seinfeld-broadcast` Smart List.
