# Attractive Character Intro Sequence (Epiphany Bridge Bonding)

- **id:** `attractive-character-intro-sequence`
- **Category:** welcome-indoctrination
- **Aliases:** AC Intro Sequence · Hero Story Sequence · Founder Story Sequence · Epiphany Bridge Email Sequence · Brand Bonding Sequence · Origin Story Welcome
- **Source:** DotCom Secrets — **Secret #6: The Attractive Character** (4 Elements, 4 Identities, 6 Storylines). Supporting: Expert Secrets (Epiphany Bridge Script, 5 phases / 14 questions); DotCom Secrets Secret #7 (Soap Opera Sequence open-loop delivery mechanic); Copywriting Secrets Secret #18 (Email Teasers).

> "Most people either don't bother to create this character, or they don't do it correctly. Once you intentionally create your AC, your business will change forever." — DotCom Secrets

## What it is

A 5–6 email arc that deliberately introduces the Attractive Character to a brand-new subscriber by **rolling out all four AC Elements** (Backstory, Parables, Character Flaws, Polarity), the chosen AC **Identity** (Leader / Adventurer / Reporter-Evangelist / Reluctant Hero), and the key **Storylines** (Loss & Redemption, Us vs Them, Before & After, Amazing Discovery, Secret Telling, Third-Person Testimonial) across an Epiphany Bridge structure.

**Distinct from the Soap Opera Sequence:** the SOS can close a single product offer in five emails. This sequence is for **personal brands and expert funnels** where bonding with a PERSON is the entire game. Each email's narrative phase maps to one phase of the Expert Secrets Epiphany Bridge Script (five phases / fourteen questions).

## TRIGGER

- **What starts it:** Opt-in on a personal-brand / expert funnel (hero funnel, story opt-in, expert lead magnet).
- **Fires:** Once per contact. **Guard:** enter only if tag `ac-intro-started` is NOT present.

## CHANNELS

Email (primary). Optional: short video per email (Epiphany Bridge video — podcast clip, Facebook Live excerpt, or Video Sales Letter). If video is included, the email's only job is the click to the video; the video does the bonding.

## CADENCE

1 email/day for 5–6 days (auto-responder), mirroring the Soap Opera Sequence daily open-loop delivery cadence.

## THE SEQUENCE (5–6 Emails)

| # | Beat (Epiphany Bridge Phase) | Delay | Job | Subject angle |
|---|------------------------------|-------|-----|---------------|
| 1 | **Backstory & Desire** (Phase 1) | immediate / Day 1 | Return to where the reader is now; share the external + internal desire; name the old vehicles you tried | `Before any of this worked, I was [relatable low point]` |
| 2 | **The Wall / Conflict + Character Flaw** (Phase 2) | +1 day | Name the villain; hit the wall where the old way collapsed; get vulnerable about the internal struggle | `The day it all fell apart` |
| 3 | **The Guide & The Epiphany / New Opportunity** (Phase 3) | +1 day | Who handed you the aha; the epiphany; the new opportunity vs the old way | `The 'aha' that changed everything` |
| 4 | **The Framework** (Phase 4) | +1 day | Name the proprietary framework; teach the WHAT (strategy), not the HOW (tactics); use a parable | `The [framework name] that did it` |
| 5 | **Achievement & Transformation + Polarity + Social Proof** (Phase 5) | +1 day | External result + internal transformation; Before/After + Third-Person Testimonials; Polarity line in the sand; strong CTA | `From [before] to [after] (and how you can too)` |
| 6 | **Identity & Invitation** (optional capstone) | +1 day | Declare the AC Identity explicitly; restate the worldview; final invitation to the tribe / value ladder | `Why I do this (and the invitation)` |

### Per-email body frameworks

**E1 — Backstory & Desire (Phase 1):**
- Backstory that starts roughly where the reader is right now
- External desire (the result you wanted) + internal desire (who you wanted to become)
- Old vehicles you tried that failed (the wall begins to form)
- Open loop to Email 2 (tease: "then something happened")

**E2 — Wall / Conflict + Character Flaw (Phase 2):**
- Name the villain / the moment of frustration
- The wall: the old opportunity stopped working
- **Character Flaw element:** get vulnerable about the internal struggle — fear, doubt, shame ("Every believable, three-dimensional Attractive Character has flaws" — DCS #6). Vulnerability builds rapport faster than anything else because the audience shares the same struggle.
- Open loop: "then something changed — I'll tell you tomorrow"

**E3 — The Guide & Epiphany / New Opportunity (Phase 3):**
- Who handed you the epiphany (the guide)
- The epiphany itself (the realization — state it plainly)
- The new opportunity you created from it (vs the old way) — use **Amazing Discovery** or **Secret Telling** storyline
- Soft CTA / open loop to the framework email

**E4 — The Framework (Phase 4):**
- Name the framework (proprietary; give it a name)
- Teach the strategy at a high level (the WHAT, not the tactics — Expert Secrets principle: sell the "what," course teaches the "how")
- **Parable** that illustrates the core principle ("When you stop teaching facts and start teaching through parables, your messages will stay with an audience longer" — DCS #6)
- CTA toward the offer that delivers the framework

**E5 — Achievement & Transformation + Polarity + Social Proof (Phase 5):**
- Achievement: the external end result accomplished (numbers / transformation)
- Internal transformation: how you changed (fear → courage)
- **Before & After** + **Third-Person Testimonial** storylines for social proof
- **Polarity:** draw the us-vs-them line in the sand (divide the audience: diehard fans vs neutral vs those who disagree; "fair weather fans" become raving fans — DCS #6)
- Clear CTA to join / buy / ascend the value ladder

**E6 — Identity & Invitation (optional):**
- Declare the AC's Identity archetype and the moral duty / mission (especially for the Reluctant Hero: "He knows the information he has is so important that he must overcome his shyness and share it" — DCS #6)
- Restate the worldview line in the sand
- Final invitation to the value ladder / community

## THE FOUR AC ELEMENTS (DotCom Secrets — Secret #6)

Each element maps to a specific email beat in this sequence:

| Element | Definition | Where it appears |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Backstory** | Where you came from; takes the reader back to a similar place they're in now. Without a backstory, the AC is just "a dude eating subs" (the Jared/Subway principle). | E1 (primary), E5 (before/after) |
| **Parables** | Little stories, easy to remember, that illustrate a relevant point. "When you stop teaching facts and start teaching through parables, your messages stay with an audience longer." | E4 (primary), any email |
| **Character Flaws** | Every believable character has flaws. Vulnerability = relatability = trust. Don't put on a perfect facade; it alienates. | E2 (primary) |
| **Polarity** | Draw the line in the sand. Share opinions on hard matters and stick to them. Being neutral is boring; being polarizing creates raving fans AND haters — both are better than being unknown. | E5 (primary), E6 |

## THE FOUR AC IDENTITIES (choose one)

| Identity | Who uses it | How it shapes communication |
|----------|------------|----------------------------|
| **Leader** | Has already achieved the desired result; shares a similar backstory to the audience. | Leads the audience along a known path. Stories of where you've been and where you're going. |
| **Adventurer / Crusader** | Curious, doesn't always have all the answers; sets out on a journey to discover the truth and brings back treasures. | Less "I'll lead you" and more "I discovered this and brought it back for you." |
| **Reporter / Evangelist** | Hasn't blazed the trail yet but has a desire to; interviews dozens or hundreds of people and shares what was learned. | Great for starting a business in a niche you don't know well. Credibility comes from access to high-status people. |
| **Reluctant Hero** | Humble hero who doesn't want the spotlight but has a moral duty to share important information. | Compelled by mission, not fame. Comfortable framing: "I'm sharing this because you NEED to know it." |

## THE SIX AC STORYLINES (deploy across the sequence)

| Storyline | Template | Best email | DCS quote |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Loss & Redemption | "I had everything. Then ___ happened. It turned out to be a blessing because I learned ___. Now I ___." | E2, E5 | Powerful because they show the upside of going through hardship |
| Us vs Them | Polarize your audience; draw your raving fans closer; give them a rallying cry. "Talkers vs do-ers." | E5, E6 | Draws your raving fans even closer and gives them a rallying cry against the outsiders |
| Before & After | "First I was ___. Now I'm ___." Stories of transformation. | E5 | Often tellable with just pictures; simple but powerful motivators |
| Amazing Discovery | "Oh my gosh, you guys… wait til you hear about this amazing new thing I just discovered!" | E3 | Huge for selling — helps people believe the answer they've been searching for is finally available |
| Secret Telling | "I've got a secret… if you want to find out what it is, you need to do ___." | E3, E4 | The lure of secrets draws the reader into your funnel and up the Value Ladder |
| Third-Person Testimonial | Other people's successes with your products. Sprinkle liberally. | E5 | Provides powerful social proof |

## COPY PERSONA + SCRIPT

- **The WHO:** Attractive Character (DCS #6) — select ONE Identity; deploy all 4 Elements; weave all 6 Storylines across the arc.
- **Story structure:** Epiphany Bridge Script (Expert Secrets, 5 phases / 14 questions): Phase 1 Backstory (desire external + internal, old vehicles) → Phase 2 Journey/Conflict (villain, the wall) → Phase 3 New Opportunity (guide, epiphany) → Phase 4 The Framework/Plan (strategy) → Phase 5 Achievement & Transformation.
- **Delivery mechanic:** Soap Opera Sequence (DCS #7): daily cadence + open loops between emails (each email closes the prior loop and opens a new one).
- **Email mechanics per email:** Copywriting Secrets Email Teaser (Jim Edwards #18): subject = headline (question form); merged-name salutation; shocking statement to break hypnosis; 2–4 curiosity bullets; ONE specific CTA; friendly personal close ("I'll see you over there"). If each email links a video, the email's only job is the click — the video does the bonding.

## FLEXIBILITY — Core Principle

> This template is a **GUIDE and a RESOURCE, never a rule or requirement.** It must not dominate the user's desire.

| Mode | When it applies | What the system does |
|------|----------------|----------------------|
| **1 — Explicit desire** | User states what they want | Do exactly that. This template is an optional reference only — never impose or override. |
| **2 — User is unsure** | User doesn't know what to do | Suggest the 5-phase Epiphany Bridge structure and explain why it works for personal-brand bonding; let the user decide. |
| **3 — Just do it** | User says "handle it" / "build it" | Build the full 5–6 email sequence — AC-01 through AC-06, tags, custom fields, if/else branches, video-engagement fork, and the GHL workflow — exactly from this template. |

Every element (Identity choice, number of emails, inclusion of video, open-loop intensity, which storylines to lead with) is a **recommended default the user can change or skip.** The template assists; it never dominates.
