## Style / Definition
The Competitive buyer decides on **results, advantage, and status.** They ask one question of every offer: *will this make me win — faster, bigger, or more visibly than the people I measure myself against?* They respect confidence, punish fluff, and buy the best version of things. They close the 12-email sequence with **Emails 10–12: E10 = AIDA, E11 = Million Dollar Sales, E12 = Before-After-Bridge.**

## Purpose & Intention
Competitive buyers anchor the end of the sequence because they're often watching the whole campaign, judging whether *you* operate like a winner before they'll buy from you. Their block escalates: a high-velocity persuasion arc (E10), a full-architecture email with disqualification and receipts (E11), and finally the vivid contrast between the version of them that acted and the version that didn't (E12) — the exact contrast this buyer cannot stand to lose.

## How To Write It (voice & tactics)
1. **Lead with the result, stated boldly.** Outcomes, rankings, deltas: "our members outpace their market's average growth 3-to-1." Big claims are fine — as long as step 2 follows.
2. **Back every claim immediately.** Competitive buyers are aggressive skeptics. Claim → receipt, claim → receipt. An unbacked claim doesn't just fail; it recategorizes you as noise.
3. **Frame everything as advantage.** Not "learn marketing" but "gain an edge 97% of your market doesn't have." The words *edge, outpace, ahead, first, top* are this segment's native tongue.
4. **Use exclusivity honestly.** Application-only, capped seats, standards for entry — this buyer WANTS gates, because gates confer status. "Not for everyone" is a feature.
5. **Respect their intelligence and their time.** Confident, compressed, zero begging. Neediness is the one unforgivable tone.
6. **Invoke the competition — carefully.** "While your competitors are still guessing…" motivates; naming or trash-talking specifics reads as insecure.
7. **Give them control of the decision.** "You've seen the evidence — your call" converts better than pressure. Winners choose; they are never herded.

### Subject-Line Guidance (Competitive)
- **Bold claims and results-numbers:** *"3x pipeline in 90 days — the documented path"*
- **Achievement and status framing:** *top 3%, outpace, the operators who win*
- **Challenge energy works:** *"Can you actually execute this, {{contact.first_name}}?"* — they open to prove they can
- **Exclusivity signals:** *invitation, application, not for everyone*
- **No neediness, no begging, no "just checking in"** — ever
- Standard rules apply: 2 A/B subject lines, 8–12 words, key info in first 40 chars, no pricing

## Use Cases
- Emails 10–12 of any 12-email buyer-type sequence
- Premium tiers, masterminds, and application-based offers
- Performance and growth offers with measurable deltas
- Upsells positioned as "what the top performers use"
- Markets with visible rankings and public scoreboards

## Detailed Example
**Framework:** AIDA (Email 10, Competitive slot)

**Subject Line A:** {{contact.first_name}}, your market's top 3% share one habit
**Subject Line B:** The edge is real, {{contact.first_name}} — and it's measurable

**Preview Text:** The documented gap between operators who systemize and those who improvise.

**Email Body:**

{{contact.first_name}},

**The top performers in your market are not out-working you. They're out-systeming you** — and the gap is measurable.

Across the 400+ businesses we've tracked for six years, operators running a documented growth system grew at **3.1x their market's average.** Same markets. Same conditions. Different architecture.

That's the edge on the table: a pipeline that runs while competitors improvise, pricing power that comes from position instead of persuasion, and the standing that follows both. The system that produces it is installable in 90 days — we've documented every install.

While the rest of your market is still guessing, this decision is sitting in front of exactly one person.

**[Review the system — and decide →]**

Doors close Friday; the next cohort starts Monday together. You've seen the evidence. Your call.

[Founder Name]

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Opens with a bold, results-first claim
- [ ] EVERY claim immediately backed by a receipt (number/timeframe/track record)
- [ ] Benefits framed as competitive advantage (edge/outpace/ahead)
- [ ] Exclusivity present and honest (caps, applications, standards)
- [ ] Tone confident and compressed — zero neediness
- [ ] Competition invoked generically, never trash-talked by name
- [ ] Decision handed to the reader ("your call"), not pressured
- [ ] Subject lines carry results-numbers, status, or challenge energy
- [ ] Framework matches slot (E10 AIDA, E11 Million Dollar Sales, E12 BAB)
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
