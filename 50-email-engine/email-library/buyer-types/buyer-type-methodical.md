## Style / Definition
The Methodical buyer decides **slowly, deliberately, and on evidence.** They read the whole email, check the claims, compare the options, and distrust anything that pushes harder than it proves. They receive the largest block of the 12-email sequence — **Emails 3–6: E3 = Features-to-Benefit, E4 = 6 W's, E5 = ACCA, E6 = PASTOR (Solutions)** — because thoroughness takes room.

## Purpose & Intention
Methodical buyers are the most skeptical segment and, once convinced, the most loyal — they did the homework, so they don't churn and they refer with confidence. The four-email block is a deliberate escalation: features grounded in benefits (E3), complete information (E4), mechanism-level understanding (E5), then the full trust-building architecture with proof (E6). You cannot rush this buyer; you can only make every minute they give you productive.

## How To Write It (voice & tactics)
1. **Replace every vague claim with a number, a timeframe, or a source.** "Saves you time" fails; "recovers 8.2 hours/week, measured across 1,100 businesses" passes. This buyer's trust is arithmetic.
2. **Show the mechanism, not just the outcome.** They need to know *how* it works before they believe *that* it works. One clear process explanation outsells five testimonials here.
3. **Answer objections before they form.** FAQs, edge cases, "what if it doesn't work for my situation" — addressing these unprompted signals competence.
4. **De-risk explicitly.** Guarantees, trial periods, refund terms, cancellation policies — stated plainly, not buried. Risk reversal is often the final domino for this segment.
5. **Structure visibly.** Bolded labels, one item per line, logical order. Visual organization tells a methodical reader "this sender thinks the way I do."
6. **Never manufacture urgency.** Real deadlines with stated reasons are fine; pressure tactics read as evidence you can't win on merit — and end the relationship.
7. **Let length serve completeness.** This is the one segment where a longer email outperforms — as long as every line informs.

### Subject-Line Guidance (Methodical)
- **Specific and factual:** numbers, timeframes, concrete outcomes — *"The 4-part system, explained in one email"*
- **Clarity beats curiosity:** they open emails that promise answers, not riddles
- **Questions work when they promise analysis:** *"Is your follow-up losing 30% of leads?"*
- **No hype vocabulary:** avoid *insane, secret, shocking, weird trick* — instant credibility damage
- **`{{contact.first_name}}` reads as data-driven personalization** — use it matter-of-factly
- Standard rules apply: 2 A/B subject lines, 8–12 words, key info in first 40 chars, no pricing

## Use Cases
- Emails 3–6 of any 12-email buyer-type sequence
- High-ticket and considered purchases with real diligence cycles
- Technical, financial, or process-heavy offers
- B2B decision-makers who must justify the purchase to others
- Any audience segment that opens everything but rarely clicks — they're still evaluating

## Detailed Example
**Framework:** ACCA (Email 5, Methodical slot)

**Subject Line A:** {{contact.first_name}}, the exact mechanism — with the numbers
**Subject Line B:** How the system works, step by step, {{contact.first_name}}

**Preview Text:** Cause, effect, evidence, and the guarantee — all in one read.

**Email Body:**

{{contact.first_name}},

You've seen what the program includes (Email 3) and every logistical detail (Email 4). Today: **how it actually works,** because you shouldn't buy a mechanism you haven't seen.

The problem, precisely: growth stalls when acquisition depends on the founder's personal effort. Effort doesn't scale; systems do.

Here's the mechanism. The program installs three documented processes — demand generation, conversion, and delivery — each with a scorecard. **You measure weekly, adjust one variable at a time, and compound.** No motivation required; the scorecard does the pushing.

The evidence: across 400+ implementations over six years, the median business reaches consistent, founder-independent lead flow in 11 weeks. We publish the full data annually — reply "DATA" and we'll send the report.

And the risk sits with us: **30-day full refund, no conditions, plus you keep the templates.**

If the mechanism makes sense to you, the next step is a look at the full curriculum:

**[Review the complete curriculum →]**

Take your time with it. This decision should survive your scrutiny — that's exactly why we built it to.

[Founder Name]

*(Signature rule: replace [Founder Name] with the founder's actual name at generation time.)*

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Every claim carries a number, timeframe, or source
- [ ] Mechanism explained — the reader knows HOW it works
- [ ] At least one objection answered unprompted
- [ ] Risk reversal stated plainly (guarantee/trial/terms)
- [ ] Visible structure: bolded labels, one item per line
- [ ] Zero manufactured urgency; any deadline has a stated reason
- [ ] References prior emails in the block (harmony with E3–E6 arc)
- [ ] Subject lines factual/specific, no hype vocabulary
- [ ] Framework matches slot (E3 F2B, E4 6W's, E5 ACCA, E6 PASTOR-Solutions)
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
