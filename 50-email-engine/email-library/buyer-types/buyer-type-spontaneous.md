## Style / Definition
The Spontaneous buyer decides **fast, on gut feel, in the moment.** They're energized by novelty, immediacy, and momentum — and repelled by friction, length, and anything that smells like homework. In the 12-email buyer-type sequence they own **Emails 1–2: Email 1 = 3-B Plan, Email 2 = Star-Chain-Hook.**

## Purpose & Intention
Spontaneous buyers open the sequence for a reason: they convert first, and a fast, generous opener earns permission for everything that follows. Sell to them at the speed they decide. Every extra paragraph is a chance for their attention — and your sale — to leave. The 3-B Plan matches their pace; Star-Chain-Hook then backs their gut-yes with just enough proof to prevent refund-remorse.

## How To Write It (voice & tactics)
1. **Front-load everything.** Offer, benefit, and CTA in the first three lines. They will not scroll to find your point.
2. **Write at conversation speed.** Short sentences. Active verbs. Now-words: *today, instantly, right now, in 60 seconds.*
3. **Make the action one step.** One click, one reply-word, one button. Every added step halves their follow-through.
4. **Use real urgency and honor it.** Deadlines and limited spots work powerfully — once. Fake scarcity burns this segment permanently.
5. **Keep energy high but claims concrete.** "40% off until midnight" excites; vague hype ("incredible opportunity!") reads as noise.
6. **Design for the phone.** They're reading on a screen in a checkout line. CTA visible without scrolling.

### Subject-Line Guidance (Spontaneous)
- **Short and punchy** — the shortest subjects in the whole system; every word earns its place
- **Urgency/scarcity up front:** *tonight, last call, 6 spots, closes at midnight* — inside the first 40 characters
- **Action verbs and immediate payoff:** grab, unlock, claim, start
- **`{{contact.first_name}}` early** — personal + fast is their language
- A single well-placed emoji can add energy (body limit ≤4 still applies)
- Standard rules apply: 2 A/B subject lines, 8–12 words, no pricing, most compelling info in first 40 characters

## Use Cases
- Emails 1–2 of any 12-email buyer-type sequence
- Flash sales, last-call, and deadline-day sends
- Abandoned-cart first touch (1–3 hour window — speed is the tactic)
- Low-consideration offers and impulse-priced products
- Re-engagement blasts where one bold offer beats five arguments

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Point and CTA within the first three lines
- [ ] Total length appropriate to the buyer: short (3-B under 150 words)
- [ ] Action is exactly one step
- [ ] Urgency is real, specific, and will be honored
- [ ] Now-words present; hedge-words absent
- [ ] Reads fully on one phone screen
- [ ] Subject lines short/punchy with urgency in first 40 chars + `{{contact.first_name}}`
- [ ] Framework matches slot (E1 = 3-B Plan, E2 = Star-Chain-Hook)
- [ ] Energy high, claims concrete
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
