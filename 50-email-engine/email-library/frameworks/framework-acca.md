## Style / Definition
ACCA moves a reader through four cognitive gears: **Awareness (present the situation) → Comprehension (describe its impact — make them truly understand) → Conviction (create desire and certainty that your solution fixes it) → Action (ask for the response).** It resembles AIDA but replaces raw emotion with **clarity and certainty** — comprehension is the beating heart of the formula.

## Purpose & Intention
Some sales are lost not to resistance but to *fog* — the reader never understood the problem well enough to care. ACCA is the teaching-sell: it wins whenever understanding precedes desire, which is exactly how methodical buyers operate (it holds their Email 5 slot in the 12-email sequence). An ACCA email leaves the reader measurably smarter — and buyers trust the person who made things clear.

## How To Write It
1. **Awareness: state the situation as an observation, not an accusation.** "Here's something happening in businesses like yours" — neutral, factual, verifiable. You're a diagnostician at this stage, not a marketer.
2. **Comprehension: explain the mechanism.** This is the step other frameworks skip. Show *how* the problem works — cause, effect, why it persists, why common fixes fail. Use one crisp analogy if the mechanism is abstract. When the reader thinks "OH — that's why," comprehension is done.
3. **Conviction: convert understanding into certainty.** Now connect your solution to the mechanism just explained: "because the problem works like X, the fix must do Y — and that's precisely what [product] does." Add one proof point. Conviction built on comprehension is far sturdier than desire built on hype.
4. **Action: a confident, low-friction ask.** The reader now understands and believes; don't re-sell. State the step, the immediate next thing that happens, and any certainty-boosters (guarantee, trial).
5. **Audit the flow:** could the reader now explain the problem to a colleague? If not, comprehension failed and conviction is standing on sand.

## Use Cases
- Email 5 (methodical block) in the 12-email buyer-type sequence
- Offers solving problems the audience misunderstands or underestimates
- Downsell emails that re-explain value at a new entry point
- Technical, financial, or process-heavy products
- Nurture emails that build authority by making the reader smarter

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Awareness states the situation neutrally — observation, not accusation
- [ ] Comprehension explains the MECHANISM (cause, effect, why it persists)
- [ ] Reader could re-explain the problem to a colleague after one read
- [ ] Conviction ties the solution directly to the explained mechanism
- [ ] At least one proof point supports conviction
- [ ] Action is confident and low-friction, with a certainty-booster
- [ ] Clarity prioritized over cleverness throughout
- [ ] 150–300 words; paragraphs ≤3 sentences; key mechanism bolded
- [ ] Two A/B subject lines; preview <100 chars, no subject repeat
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
