## Style / Definition
AIDA is the oldest conversion formula in advertising, and it still converts: **grab Attention → stimulate Interest → build Desire (unique benefits) → demand Action with a strong, clear CTA.** Four escalating beats that walk a reader from stranger to clicker.

## Purpose & Intention
AIDA's power is momentum. Each stage exists only to earn the next line of reading, so the email accelerates instead of sagging in the middle — where most emails die. Use it when you need a clean, high-velocity persuasion arc: launches, competitive buyers who respect confidence, and Email 9 of the Landing-Page promo set / Email 10 of the 12-email sequence.

## How To Write It
1. **Attention = a pattern interrupt tied to their outcome.** Not a gimmick. The strongest openers break an assumption the reader holds about their own situation ("The reason you're stuck isn't effort. It's sequence."). If your first line could open anyone's email, it opens no one's.
2. **Interest = the mechanism or the surprise.** Give them something they didn't know — a new mechanism, a counterintuitive stat, the "reason why." Interest is intellectual; it answers "wait, how?"
3. **Desire = benefits + proof + future pacing.** Shift from head to gut. Stack 2–3 unique benefits (what only *this* offer does), anchor with one line of proof, then put the reader inside the after-picture: "Imagine opening your laptop Monday and…"
4. **Action = one verb, one link, one reason-to-act-now.** Strong and unmissable. Add a genuine deadline or scarcity element — manufactured urgency reads instantly false to competitive buyers.
5. **Check the handoffs.** The #1 AIDA failure is seams: attention that has nothing to do with the interest, desire that forgets the opening. Each beat must pay off the previous one.

## Use Cases
- Email 9 of the Landing-Page 10-email promo sequence
- Email 10 (competitive-buyer opener) in the 12-email buyer-type sequence
- Launch and cart-open announcements where energy and momentum matter
- Promotional emails to warm lists that need re-ignition
- Any short email that must take a reader from cold interest to a click in one pass

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Opening line breaks an assumption the reader holds (true pattern interrupt, no gimmick)
- [ ] Interest delivers a mechanism or surprise — something the reader didn't know
- [ ] Desire stacks 2–3 *unique* benefits with at least one proof point
- [ ] Future-pacing line puts the reader inside the after-picture
- [ ] One CTA: single verb, single link, on its own line
- [ ] Urgency is real and the reason is stated
- [ ] No seams — each beat pays off the one before it
- [ ] 150–300 words; paragraphs ≤3 sentences; key points bolded
- [ ] Two A/B subject lines (hook in first 40 chars); preview <100 chars, no subject repeat
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
