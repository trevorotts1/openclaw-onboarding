## Style / Definition
Before-After-Bridge paints three pictures in strict order: **Before (the reader's current pain, in vivid detail) → After (the improved future, equally vivid) → Bridge (your solution as the path between them).** It runs on the **contrast principle** — the sharper the gap between the two pictures, the more irresistible the bridge.

## Purpose & Intention
BAB persuades through visualization instead of argument. The reader isn't told the product is good; they *experience* the distance between their Tuesday-as-it-is and their Tuesday-as-it-could-be, and the solution becomes the obvious crossing. Because it compresses an entire persuasion arc into three beats, it excels as a **sequence closer** — it's Email 12 (the final email) of the 12-email buyer-type sequence and Email 6 of the Landing-Page promo set.

## How To Write It
1. **Build the Before from one ordinary moment.** Not a list of problems — one filmable scene: the 6am inbox check, the proposal rewritten for the fourth time, the Sunday dread. Specific beats comprehensive.
2. **Write the After as the same scene, transformed.** Same time of day, same setting, different reality. Mirroring the scene makes the contrast land twice as hard.
3. **Keep both pictures the same size.** A long Before with a rushed After reads as doom; a rushed Before with a long After reads as fantasy. Balance creates believability.
4. **Use present tense in both pictures.** "You open your laptop and…" — present tense puts the reader inside the frame; conditional tense ("you could…") keeps them safely outside it.
5. **Make the Bridge short and load-bearing.** One or two sentences: name the solution, state the mechanism, state the proof. The pictures did the persuading; the bridge just needs to hold weight.
6. **CTA = the first step onto the bridge.** Frame the click as physically crossing: "Start the crossing," "Take the first step."

## Use Cases
- Email 12 — the FINAL email — of the 12-email buyer-type sequence (competitive closer)
- Email 6 of the Landing-Page 10-email promo sequence
- Abandoned-cart recovery (the After they abandoned is still available)
- Downsell emails (a smaller bridge to the same After)
- Short promotional sends where one vivid contrast beats five arguments

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Before is ONE specific, filmable scene (not a problem list)
- [ ] After mirrors the exact same scene, transformed
- [ ] Both pictures roughly equal length (balanced contrast)
- [ ] Present tense throughout both pictures
- [ ] Bridge ≤2 sentences: solution + mechanism + proof
- [ ] CTA framed as crossing / taking the first step
- [ ] Contrast is honest — the After is achievable, not fantasy
- [ ] 150–300 words; paragraphs ≤3 sentences; key contrasts bolded
- [ ] Two A/B subject lines (hook in first 40 chars); preview <100 chars
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
