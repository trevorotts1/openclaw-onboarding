## Style / Definition
Million Dollar Sales is a twelve-part, full-persuasion architecture: **Attention Grabber → Intention Call-Out → Disqualifier → Credibility → Context → CTA #1 → Benefits → Testimonial → CTA #2 → Final Intention Call-Out → Final CTA #3 → Humorous P.S.** It builds relationship *and* gives the reader multiple chances to respond.

## Purpose & Intention
This framework converts through **self-selection**. The Intention Call-Out tells readers exactly who this is for; the Disqualifier tells the rest to leave — which paradoxically makes the qualified reader lean in harder (exclusivity + consistency: "this is for people like me, and I'm still reading"). Three spaced CTAs catch buyers at three different readiness levels, and the humorous P.S. closes on warmth — because the P.S. is the second-most-read line in any email. Built for competitive buyers (Email 11 of the 12-email sequence) and Email 8 of the Landing-Page promo set.

## How To Write It
1. **Attention Grabber:** one bold line that makes a claim you can back up later. Confidence, not clickbait.
2. **Intention Call-Out:** name your exact reader — situation, ambition, stage. "This is for the operator doing six figures who's tired of being the entire engine."
3. **Disqualifier:** name who should close the email — genuinely. A real disqualifier ("If you're looking for a magic button, this isn't it") builds more trust than a page of claims.
4. **Credibility:** 2–3 receipts. Numbers, years, outcomes. State them flat — no bragging tone; let facts flex.
5. **Context:** why this email, why today. A reason-why (new opening, deadline, milestone) makes everything after it feel timely instead of random.
6. **CTA #1:** early exit for the already-convinced.
7. **Benefits:** 3–4 outcome-first bullets, each on its own line. What changes in their life, not what's in the box.
8. **Testimonial:** one specific, results-based quote that answers the reader's biggest doubt.
9. **CTA #2:** mid-email, naturally placed after proof.
10. **Final Intention Call-Out:** re-address your exact reader one last time — now with the shared context of everything above.
11. **Final CTA #3:** the decisive ask, with the reason to act now restated.
12. **Humorous P.S.:** land one genuinely funny, human line that releases the sales tension — and sneak the link in one last time.

## Use Cases
- Email 11 (competitive-buyer middle) in the 12-email buyer-type sequence
- Email 8 of the Landing-Page 10-email promo sequence
- Flagship launches where you have real credibility markers to deploy
- Reactivation of high-value segments who respect directness
- Any promotion where filtering OUT bad-fit buyers protects delivery quality

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] All 12 parts present, in order
- [ ] Intention Call-Out names a specific reader (situation + ambition), not "everyone"
- [ ] Disqualifier is genuine — it would actually turn away a bad-fit buyer
- [ ] Credibility = flat-stated receipts (numbers/years/outcomes), no bragging tone
- [ ] Context gives a real reason-why for today
- [ ] Exactly 3 CTAs, spaced: early / post-proof / decisive close
- [ ] Benefits are outcome-first, each on its own line, bolded
- [ ] Testimonial answers the reader's biggest doubt with specifics
- [ ] P.S. is genuinely humorous AND repeats the link
- [ ] Paragraphs ≤3 sentences; two A/B subject lines; preview <100 chars
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
