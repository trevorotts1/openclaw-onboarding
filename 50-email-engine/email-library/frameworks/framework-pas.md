## Style / Definition
PAS is the three-beat workhorse of direct response: **identify a resonant Problem → Agitate it (deepen it, show it worsening) → present the Solution with a clear CTA.** It runs on pain-avoidance — the strongest motivator in human decision-making — and works at almost any length.

## Purpose & Intention
People move faster to escape pain than to gain pleasure. PAS wins whenever the reader already *feels* a problem but has normalized it. The agitation step de-normalizes the pain — it shows the true cost of leaving it unsolved — so the solution lands as relief, not as a pitch. It's the humanistic-buyer closer (Email 9 of the 12-email sequence) and Email 10 of the Landing-Page promo set.

## How To Write It
1. **Name the problem in the reader's own words.** Use voice-of-customer language — the phrase they'd type at 11pm, not your industry's label for it. If they'd say "I'm invisible online," don't write "suboptimal brand visibility."
2. **Prove you understand before you amplify.** One empathy beat ("if you've ever…then you know") earns you the right to agitate. Skip it and agitation reads as fear-mongering.
3. **Agitate across three dimensions.** (a) The compounding cost — what this problem is quietly costing per week/month; (b) the trajectory — how it gets worse, not better, on its own; (c) the emotional tax — what it does to confidence, sleep, relationships.
4. **Know when to stop.** One paragraph past empathy is persuasion; three is manipulation. Agitate until the reader nods hard, then pivot immediately.
5. **Pivot with relief, not hype.** "It doesn't have to work this way" beats "INTRODUCING…". The solution should feel like an exit sign, and the mechanism gets one clear sentence.
6. **Make the CTA the escape route.** Frame the click as the first step *away from the pain*, on its own line.

## Use Cases
- Email 9 (humanistic closer) in the 12-email buyer-type sequence
- Email 10 of the Landing-Page 10-email promo sequence
- Abandoned-cart emails (the unsolved problem is still sitting in the cart)
- Re-engagement of cold lists — pain recognition cuts through indifference
- Cold traffic that is problem-aware but not solution-aware

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Problem stated in voice-of-customer language (would the reader say it this way?)
- [ ] One empathy beat before any agitation
- [ ] Agitation covers compounding cost, worsening trajectory, and emotional tax
- [ ] Agitation stops at the nod — no dwelling, no fear-mongering
- [ ] Pivot line offers relief ("it doesn't have to be this way"), not hype
- [ ] Solution mechanism explained in one clear sentence
- [ ] CTA framed as the escape route, on its own line
- [ ] 150–300 words; paragraphs ≤3 sentences; key points bolded
- [ ] Two A/B subject lines (hook in first 40 chars); preview <100 chars, no subject repeat
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
