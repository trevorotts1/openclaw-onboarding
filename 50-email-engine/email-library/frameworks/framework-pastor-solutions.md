## Style / Definition
P.A.S.T.O.R. V1 (Solutions Version) is Ray Edwards' six-part persuasion formula with the **product positioned squarely as the solution**: **Problem → Amplify (consequences + aspirations) → Story & Solution → Transformation & Testimony → Offer → Response.** It is the most comprehensive, trust-building framework in the library.

## Purpose & Intention
PASTOR earns the sale the long way — by walking the full emotional journey from pain to proof to purchase. It shines on considered purchases where the reader needs *both* emotional resonance and rational reassurance before acting. In the Landing-Page 10-email promo set it powers Emails 1–3, each targeting a **different pain point** from Section 6 ("Big Bold Who") of the landing page, weaving in a refined version of Section 5 ("Big Bold Why"), and carrying **at least 3 CTAs** — near the beginning, naturally mid-flow, and near the end.

## How To Write It
1. **P — Problem: one problem, precisely named.** Each PASTOR email owns exactly ONE pain point (never recycle a pain point across Emails 1–3). Describe it so specifically the reader thinks "how did you know?"
2. **A — Amplify both directions.** Amplify the consequences of *not* solving it (the compounding cost) AND the aspiration on the other side (what life looks like solved). The gap between those two pictures is your persuasive energy.
3. **Place CTA #1 early.** After Amplify, some readers are already sold — give them the door now rather than making them scroll for it.
4. **S — Story & Solution.** Tell a short story of someone who lived this exact problem, and let the product enter *inside* the story as what changed things. The solution should feel discovered, not announced.
5. **T — Transformation & Testimony.** Show the after-state with specifics, and include real testimonial material — outcomes, not adjectives. "Booked 9 calls in 12 days" beats "amazing program!" (CTA #2 sits naturally here.)
6. **O — Offer, plainly.** State exactly what they get, in benefit-first order. Weave in the refined Big Bold Why here — the mission-level reason this offer exists.
7. **R — Response: ask for it.** Tell the reader exactly what to do, why now, and what happens the moment they act. This is CTA #3 minimum. Never end a PASTOR email politely — end it decisively.

## Use Cases
- Emails 1–3 of the Landing-Page 10-email promo sequence (one distinct pain point each)
- Email 6 (methodical-buyer closer) in the 12-email buyer-type sequence
- High-ticket promotions where trust must be built inside a single email
- Flagship-program launches to warm audiences
- Any email where testimony and mission ("why") are strong assets

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Exactly ONE pain point owned by this email (distinct from sibling PASTOR emails)
- [ ] Amplify covers BOTH the cost of inaction and the aspiration of the solved state
- [ ] Story lets the solution enter naturally — discovered, not announced
- [ ] Testimony is specific and outcome-based, not adjective-based
- [ ] Refined Big Bold Why (Section 5) woven into the Offer
- [ ] ≥3 CTAs: near beginning, natural middle, near end
- [ ] Response section asks decisively — exact action, reason for now
- [ ] Asks for a reply/response, not just a click, where appropriate
- [ ] Paragraphs ≤3 sentences; key points bolded; CTA on own line
- [ ] Two A/B subject lines (hook in first 40 chars); preview <100 chars
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
