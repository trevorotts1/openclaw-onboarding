## Style / Definition
P.A.S.T.O.R. V2 (Story Version) uses the same six parts as V1 — **Problem, Amplify, Story & Solution, Transformation & Testimony, Offer, Response** — but is **narrative-led**: the entire email lives inside a story, and the product is positioned as the solution *inside* that story. Testimonials serve as social proof; **emotional connection is the primary objective.**

## Purpose & Intention
Story disarms sales resistance because the brain processes narrative as experience, not argument — readers lower their guard for a story in a way they never do for a pitch. V2 wins with humanistic buyers (it opens their block as Email 7 of the 12-email sequence), with pitch-fatigued lists, and anywhere identification ("that's me") will convert better than information.

## How To Write It
1. **Open inside a scene, not a setup.** Drop the reader into a specific moment — a time, a place, a feeling ("The third Tuesday in March, she almost quit."). No throat-clearing, no "I want to tell you a story."
2. **Cast a protagonist who is the reader in disguise.** Same problem, same doubts, same words. The reader should recognize themselves within three sentences. (This is the P and A delivered through character instead of lecture.)
3. **Let the low point breathe.** The story's darkest beat IS the Amplify step. Don't rush it — the depth of the low point sets the height of the transformation.
4. **Introduce the solution as the turning point.** The product enters as what the protagonist *found*, not what you're selling. One natural sentence beats a paragraph of product copy.
5. **Show transformation in sensory detail, then confirm with testimony.** What changed in their week, their bank account, their posture. Then one real testimonial that echoes the story — proof the story isn't a unicorn.
6. **Step out of the story for the Offer.** A clean, honest pivot: "That path she took? It's open to you." State what's included, briefly — the story already did the selling.
7. **Make the Response an invitation into the same story.** "Your version of this story starts here." One clear CTA (a second, earlier CTA is optional in longer sends).

## Use Cases
- Email 7 (humanistic-buyer opener) in the 12-email buyer-type sequence
- Warm-list promotions where straight pitches have stopped working
- High-ticket offers needing emotional buy-in before logical justification
- Founder-story and origin-story campaigns
- Re-engagement of long-time subscribers who feel like community, not leads

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Opens inside a specific scene (time/place/feeling) — no preamble
- [ ] Protagonist mirrors the reader's exact problem and self-talk
- [ ] Low point given room to breathe (Amplify delivered through story)
- [ ] Product enters as the discovered turning point, in one natural sentence
- [ ] Transformation shown in concrete, sensory detail
- [ ] At least one real testimonial echoing the story's arc
- [ ] Clean pivot out of story for the Offer; story does the selling
- [ ] Response framed as an invitation into the same story
- [ ] Emotional connection prioritized over information density
- [ ] Paragraphs ≤3 sentences; key beats bolded; CTA on own line
- [ ] Two A/B subject lines; preview <100 chars; founder's actual name signed
