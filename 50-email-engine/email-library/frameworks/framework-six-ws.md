## Style / Definition
The 6 W's answers every question a careful decision-maker will ask — **Who, What, Why, When, Where, and hoW** — inside a single email. It is deliberately comprehensive: when all six are answered, there is no informational gap left for hesitation to live in.

## Purpose & Intention
Methodical buyers don't stall because they're unconvinced — they stall because *one unanswered question* gives them permission to wait. The 6 W's removes every such permission slip. It's the ideal framework for events, webinars, and structured offers, and holds the Email 4 slot (methodical block) of the 12-email sequence and Email 5 of the Landing-Page promo set. Its superpower is **objection prevention**: answering questions before they're asked reads as competence and respect.

## How To Write It
1. **Sequence the W's by persuasion, not habit.** Best-performing order is usually **Why → What → Who → When/Where → hoW**: motivation first, logistics after. Lead with Why and the rest of the email gets read.
2. **Why = the stakes, not the agenda.** Why this matters *now*, in the reader's life. One paragraph of genuine motivation before any detail.
3. **What = the offer in one clean sentence,** then 2–3 concrete specifics of what they'll walk away with.
4. **Who = both directions.** Who it's for (let the reader recognize themselves) and who's delivering it (one line of credibility).
5. **When/Where = zero-ambiguity logistics.** Date, time WITH time zone, duration, location/platform, replay policy. Each on its own line. This section is where methodical trust is won or lost.
6. **hoW = the exact mechanism of saying yes.** What clicking does, what happens next, what it costs, what the guarantee is. Kill every step-two surprise.
7. **Close with Why, compressed.** One sentence that returns to the stakes, then the CTA. Structure opened the loop; emotion closes it.

## Use Cases
- Email 4 (methodical block) in the 12-email buyer-type sequence
- Email 5 of the Landing-Page 10-email promo sequence
- Event, workshop, and webinar invitations
- Launches of structured programs with dates, cohorts, and logistics
- Any offer where confusion — not skepticism — is the real conversion killer

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] All six W's answered — run the checklist literally: Who ✓ What ✓ Why ✓ When ✓ Where ✓ hoW ✓
- [ ] Why leads (stakes before logistics) and returns compressed at the close
- [ ] Who covers both audience fit AND deliverer credibility
- [ ] When includes time zone; Where includes platform/replay policy
- [ ] hoW removes every step-two surprise (cost, process, what happens next)
- [ ] Logistics lines each on their own line, bolded labels
- [ ] No unanswered question a careful reader could stall on
- [ ] 150–300 words; paragraphs ≤3 sentences
- [ ] Two A/B subject lines; preview <100 chars, no subject repeat
