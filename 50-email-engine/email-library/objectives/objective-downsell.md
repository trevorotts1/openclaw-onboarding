## Style / Definition
The Downsell objective converts a **"no" into a smaller "yes"** using five triggers: **risk reduction, incremental commitment (foot-in-the-door), value recalibration, future-opportunity preservation, and face-saving.** It follows a declined or expired offer with a lower-commitment path to the same destination.

## Purpose & Intention
A "no" to your offer is rarely a "no" to your outcome — it's a "no" to the price, size, timing, or risk of this particular package. The downsell honors that distinction. Done well, it starts a relationship at a sustainable entry point, and foot-in-the-door psychology makes today's small yes the reliable ancestor of tomorrow's big one. Done badly, it smells like desperation and devalues the original offer. The difference is *framing*: the downsell must feel like a **considered alternative**, never a panicked discount.

## How To Write It (trigger craft)
1. **Save face first.** Open by making their "no" reasonable: "The full program is a serious commitment, and the timing isn't right for everyone." Never guilt, never "you missed out" — a shamed reader unsubscribes.
2. **Recalibrate value honestly.** The smaller offer is a different, legitimately priced product — the core value with fewer components, and the trade-offs stated plainly. Never reprice the SAME thing (that teaches your list that waiting earns discounts).
3. **Reduce risk to near zero.** Lower price, guarantee, trial, pay-as-you-go. The downsell should feel almost impossible to regret.
4. **Design the foot-in-the-door deliberately.** The smaller offer must genuinely deliver AND naturally lead onward. A downsell that dead-ends is a discount; one that opens a path is a strategy.
5. **Preserve the future opportunity.** Make explicit that the bigger door stays open — and sweeten it: "everything you invest here credits toward the full program."
6. **Keep respect at full volume.** Same tone and craft as the flagship offer received. The moment a downsell sounds like clearance, both offers lose value.

### Per-Buyer-Type Tactics (Downsell)
- **Spontaneous:** a smaller instant win — low price, immediate access, one click. Trigger emphasis: **incremental commitment.**
- **Methodical:** transparent strip-down logic — exactly what's included vs. the full offer, and why the price is what it is. Trigger emphasis: **value recalibration + risk reduction.**
- **Humanistic:** warm no-shame framing — "we'd rather walk with you at your pace than not at all"; door explicitly open. Trigger emphasis: **face-saving + future-opportunity.**
- **Competitive:** strategic entry positioning — "start with the core system, upgrade when you've proven it"; credit-toward-full preserves status. Trigger emphasis: **future-opportunity (advantage-coded).**

**Framework:** ACCA · **Objective:** Downsell (dominant triggers: face-saving + risk reduction)

**Subject Line A:** {{contact.first_name}}, the timing wasn't right — this might be

**Email Body:**

{{contact.first_name}},

Enrollment for the full Intensive closed last week, and you didn't join — which, honestly, is sometimes the right call. **A 12-week commitment has to fit your season, and only you know your season.**

But here's what I'd hate: for the timing of one offer to cost you the outcome entirely. The problem you wanted solved is still there, and it doesn't pause while the calendar sorts itself out.

So we built a right-sized entry: **The Foundations Sprint.** It's the first two modules of the Intensive — the diagnostic and the core system — self-paced, at about a fifth of the investment. It's smaller by design: fewer components, no live coaching, same core engine.

Two things make this an easy decision. It carries the full 30-day guarantee. And **every dollar credits toward the Intensive** if you upgrade when the doors reopen — so this step is never wasted.

**[Start the Foundations Sprint →]**

Same destination, {{contact.first_name}}. Just a gate sized for right now.

[Founder Name]

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Opens by making the reader's "no" reasonable (face-saving) — zero guilt
- [ ] Downsell is a DIFFERENT product (fewer components), not the same one discounted
- [ ] Trade-offs stated honestly (what's not included)
- [ ] Risk reduced to near zero (price/guarantee/trial)
- [ ] Genuine standalone value AND a natural path onward (foot-in-door)
- [ ] Future opportunity explicitly preserved (credit/door-reopens)
- [ ] Tone identical in respect to the flagship offer's emails
- [ ] Tactics tuned to buyer type (see table above)
- [ ] Standard formatting; two A/B subject lines; preview <100 chars
- [ ] Founder's actual name in the signature
