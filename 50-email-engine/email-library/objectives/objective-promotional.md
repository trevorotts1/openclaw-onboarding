## Style / Definition
The Promotional objective drives a **warm audience** toward a specific offer using five psychological triggers: **scarcity/FOMO, exclusivity/belonging, social proof, anticipation/curiosity, and value-driven desire.** Every promotional email targets this ONE objective — never split an email across objectives.

## Purpose & Intention
Warm audiences already know you; promotional emails convert that familiarity into action. The craft challenge is that warmth decays under repetition — a list that's been "promoted at" too hard goes deaf. The five triggers exist so promotion feels like *opportunity arriving*, not pressure applied. The discipline: each email leads with ONE dominant trigger (others may support), keeping every send singular and fresh across a campaign.

## How To Write It (trigger craft)
1. **Scarcity/FOMO — only when true.** Real caps, real deadlines, real reasons ("cohort starts Monday, together"). State the reason WITH the scarcity; unreasoned deadlines read as tactics.
2. **Exclusivity/Belonging — sell the room, not just the offer.** "Founding member," "first 50," "the group doing this together." Belonging converts people that discounts can't reach.
3. **Social proof — specific beats voluminous.** One named, detailed result outperforms "thousands of happy customers." Rotate proof types across the campaign: numbers, quotes, mini-stories.
4. **Anticipation/Curiosity — open loops early in the campaign.** Tease what's coming, reveal in stages, reward the openers. Curiosity is a pre-launch asset; spend it before doors open.
5. **Value-driven desire — demonstrate, don't declare.** Give a genuinely useful piece of the system away in the email itself; the sale becomes the obvious continuation of received value.

### Per-Buyer-Type Tactics (Promotional)
- **Spontaneous:** flash windows and countdowns; instant-benefit framing; one-click CTA; the offer in line one. Trigger emphasis: **scarcity/FOMO.**
- **Methodical:** early-access with complete details; what's-included breakdowns; deadline with stated reason; guarantee up front. Trigger emphasis: **value-driven desire.**
- **Humanistic:** "join us" framing; member stories; founder's personal note on why this launch matters. Trigger emphasis: **exclusivity/belonging + social proof.**
- **Competitive:** capped seats and application framing; first-mover advantage; "not for everyone." Trigger emphasis: **exclusivity + scarcity** (status-coded).

## Use Cases
- Product/program launches and cart-open campaigns
- The Landing-Page 10-email promo sequence (this is its native objective)
- Seasonal and event-driven promotions to the house list
- Waitlist activation and early-bird campaigns
- Evergreen promotional broadcasts between launches

## Detailed Example
**Framework:** AIDA · **Objective:** Promotional (dominant trigger: exclusivity/belonging)

**Subject Line A:** {{contact.first_name}}, the founding 50 starts forming today
**Subject Line B:** You're on the short list for this, {{contact.first_name}}

**Preview Text:** First access goes to this list before anyone else sees it.

**Email Body:**

{{contact.first_name}},

Before this goes public next week, it goes to you — **this list gets the first 50 seats of the new Blueprint Program, at founding-member terms.**

Why founding members matter: the first cohort shapes the program. Your questions become modules. Your results become the case studies everyone after you studies.

Founding members get the full system, direct access for the entire first run, and a rate that never applies again. Sarah — a first-cohort member of our last program — still introduces herself in the community as "founding class." **That's the room you're being invited into.**

**[Claim one of the founding 50 seats →]**

When seat 50 fills, this email's offer is gone and the public version — different terms, bigger room — takes its place.

Come build this with us, {{contact.first_name}}.

[Founder Name]

*(Signature rule: replace [Founder Name] with the founder's actual name at generation time.)*

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Exactly ONE objective (promotional) — no cart/upsell/downsell mixing
- [ ] ONE dominant trigger leads; supporting triggers stay subordinate
- [ ] All scarcity/exclusivity is TRUE, with the reason stated
- [ ] Social proof is specific (named/numbered), not volumetric
- [ ] Email demonstrates value, not just announces the offer
- [ ] Tactics tuned to the target buyer type (see table above)
- [ ] Fresh trigger vs. the previous sends in this campaign
- [ ] Framework chosen per buyer-type/slot mapping
- [ ] Standard formatting: ≤3-sentence paragraphs, bolds, CTA on own line
- [ ] Two A/B subject lines; preview <100 chars; founder's actual name signed
