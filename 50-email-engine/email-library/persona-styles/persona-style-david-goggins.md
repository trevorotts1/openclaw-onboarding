## Style / Definition
The David Goggins style is **confrontational accountability**: no-excuses directness, mental-toughness doctrine, the **40% rule** (when you feel done, you're at forty percent), deliberate **confrontation of comfort zones**, and radical personal ownership. **Hard constraint: no cursing** — the intensity comes from truth-density, not language.

**⛔ Cardinal rule (all 12 persona styles):** NEVER mention the persona's name, never quote them, never reference their books/feats. Patterns only; content 100% original.

## Purpose & Intention
Some readers are drowning in encouragement and starving for confrontation. This style serves the reader who KNOWS they're capable of more and is quietly disgusted with their own comfort — soft selling actually insults them. It works by *respect through hardness*: refusing to accept the reader's excuses signals a higher belief in them than flattery ever could. Deploy on plateau-breaking upsells, discipline-centered offers, and competitive buyers who want a standard, not a hug. Never deploy on fragile or grieving segments — this is a scalpel, not a default.

## How To Write It
1. **Open by refusing the comfortable story.** "Let's skip the version where the timing wasn't right." First line removes the exits.
2. **Put the mirror up, not the product.** The email's antagonist is the reader's own comfort: the snooze button, the safe goal, the recycled excuse. Name the specific behaviors — vagueness lets them off the hook.
3. **Invoke the 40% doctrine in original language.** When they felt "maxed out," they were nowhere near capacity — the mind quits long before the body or business does. The reserve tank is the offer's territory.
4. **Assign total ownership.** No market conditions, no algorithm, no gatekeepers. "Nobody is coming. That's not bad news — it's the best news you've ever gotten, because you're the variable you control."
5. **Frame discomfort as the curriculum.** The program isn't valuable despite being hard; it's valuable BECAUSE it's hard. Callus-building, chosen suffering, earned toughness.
6. **Keep it clean.** Zero profanity. Sharpen with short declaratives and second person. If a sentence needs a curse to hit, the sentence is weak — rewrite it.
7. **CTA as a challenge, with an out that costs pride:** "Or close this email and stay at forty percent. Your call." Acknowledge their previous purchase as proof they've got a base — now build on it (Upsell-2 rule).

## Use Cases
- High-ticket / Upsell-2 emails to competitive, performance-driven segments
- Plateau-breaking offers ("you've stalled and you know it")
- Discipline/accountability programs, challenges, and intensives
- Re-engagement of capable members who went passive
- Deadline-day emails where directness outperforms warmth
