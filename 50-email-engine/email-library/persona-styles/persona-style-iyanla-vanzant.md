## Style / Definition
The Iyanla Vanzant style is **ceremony on the page**: spiritual, rhythmic, poetic prose; direct-soul address ("Beloved," "Dear One"); **worthiness** as the central teaching; affirmation structures the reader can breathe along with; and a **ceremonial** sensibility that treats the reading itself as a small ritual — an anointing, not an announcement.

**⛔ Cardinal rule (all 12 persona styles):** NEVER mention the persona's name, never quote them, never reference their shows/books. Patterns only; content 100% original.

## Purpose & Intention
This style serves the reader whose block is **spiritual exhaustion and unworthiness** — the one who has done everything for everyone and quietly believes abundance is for other people. It converts by consecration: the offer stops being a transaction and becomes a threshold moment, a decision the reader makes *in agreement with their own worth*. The rhythm matters as much as the meaning — repetition, cadence, and blessing-language slow the reader's nervous system until the decision comes from stillness instead of urgency. Reserve it for audiences already comfortable with spiritual register; on the wrong list it reads as theater.

## How To Write It
1. **Open with the sacred address:** "Beloved," or "Dear One," on its own line. This single word re-tunes the entire reading experience.
2. **Name the reader's soul-state, not their business-state.** Not "your revenue plateaued" but "you have been pouring from a vessel no one refills."
3. **Build rhythm through triadic repetition.** Three-beat structures ("You have carried. You have covered. You have kept on.") — read every line aloud; if it doesn't breathe, rewrite it.
4. **Teach worthiness as fact, not aspiration.** The reader doesn't need to *earn* the next level; they need to stop declining what already belongs to them. "Worthy is not a level you reach. It is a truth you remember."
5. **Include one affirmation block** — 2–4 first-person lines the reader can say with you ("I am allowed to receive at the scale I give."). Set it apart visually; it is the email's altar.
6. **Frame the offer as a threshold, the CTA as a crossing.** Ceremonial verbs: step, cross, receive, claim, say yes. Never hustle-verbs at the close.
7. **End with a blessing, not a push.** The reader leaves the email better even if they never click. Honor their previous purchase as a promise they already made to themselves (Upsell-2 rule).

## Use Cases
- High-ticket / Upsell-2 emails for healing, coaching, and inner-work offers
- Audiences of givers: caretakers, healers, teachers, ministry-adjacent communities
- Re-invitation emails after a member's difficult season
- New-year / new-season threshold campaigns
- Any sequence needing one slowed-down, sacred-register email among faster sends

## Detailed Example
**Framework:** PAS carried in this persona style · **Objective:** Upsell

**Subject Line A:** Beloved {{contact.first_name}}, who refills your vessel?
**Subject Line B:** A blessing and a threshold, {{contact.first_name}}

**Preview Text:** Read this slowly. It was written slowly, for you.

**Email Body:**

Beloved,

Sit with me for a moment, {{contact.first_name}}. Take one breath before you read on — this is not an email to skim.

Here is what I know about you: you have been pouring from a vessel that no one refills. You have carried. You have covered. You have kept on. And somewhere in all that giving, you decided — quietly, without ceremony — **that receiving was for other people.**

Dear One, that agreement is expensive. Every season you honor it, the vision entrusted to you waits a little longer. Not because you lack ability — because you keep declining what already belongs to you.

Hear this plainly: **worthy is not a level you reach. It is a truth you remember.**

Say these words with me, out loud if you can:

*I am allowed to receive at the scale I give.*
*My growth is not greed. It is stewardship.*
*I say yes to what has been waiting for me.*

The Sanctuary Program is a threshold, not a transaction — a year of deep work, close guidance, and rooms that pour back. You made a promise to yourself when you first joined this work. This is the keeping of it.

**[Cross the threshold — say yes here →]**

And if today is not your day, Beloved, take the blessing anyway: may your vessel be refilled, running over, beginning now.

[Founder Name]
