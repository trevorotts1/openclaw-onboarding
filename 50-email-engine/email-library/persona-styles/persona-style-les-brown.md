## Style / Definition
The Les Brown style is **high-octane motivational speaking on the page**: passionate, personal storytelling; call-and-response rhythm that makes the reader answer in their head; direct empowerment declarations ("you have the power to…"); and the signature reframe that **challenges are temporary** — circumstances describe where you are, never what you're capable of.

**⛔ Cardinal rule (all 12 persona styles):** NEVER mention the persona's name, never quote them, never reference their books/speeches. Adopt the communication *patterns* — tone, rhythm, rhetoric — while keeping content 100% original.

## Purpose & Intention
Deploy this style when the reader's obstacle is **belief, not information** — they know what the offer is; they doubt they're the kind of person it works for. The style's engine is transferred conviction: the writer believes in the reader harder than the reader currently believes in themselves, and the prose makes that belief contagious. Native habitat: high-ticket / Upsell-2 emails where the upgrade decision is really an identity decision.

## How To Write It
1. **Open with a direct declaration about the reader's capacity.** Not the offer — the reader. "Somewhere in you is a version of this business you haven't met yet."
2. **Use call-and-response rhythm.** Pose short questions the reader must answer internally ("You know that feeling? Of course you do."). The email becomes a dialogue, not a lecture.
3. **Tell one brief personal-struggle story** — the writer's or a member's — where the low point is honest and the climb is earned. Vulnerability first, victory second.
4. **Reframe every challenge as temporary and positional.** "Where you are is an address, not a destiny." Circumstances get downgraded; capacity gets promoted.
5. **Build to a crescendo.** Sentence length shortens as intensity rises. The final third should read like the closing minutes of a keynote.
6. **Land the empowerment phrase near the CTA:** a "you have the power to…" construction, so the click feels like the reader exercising that power.
7. **Acknowledge their previous purchase as evidence** of who they already are (Upsell-2 requirement): "You've already proven you bet on yourself once."

## Use Cases
- High-ticket / Upsell-2 emails where self-doubt blocks the upgrade
- Mid-sequence re-motivation after heavy informational emails
- Enrollment-deadline emails needing emotional lift without pressure
- Win-back emails to members who went quiet after early struggle
- Graduation-to-next-level offers (identity ascension moments)

## Detailed Example
**Framework:** PAS carried in this persona style · **Objective:** Upsell

**Subject Line A:** {{contact.first_name}}, there's another level in you. You know it.
**Subject Line B:** The version of you that's waiting, {{contact.first_name}}

**Preview Text:** Not a pitch. A mirror. Read it standing up.

**Email Body:**

{{contact.first_name}},

Can I tell you what I see? I see somebody who already made one brave decision — you invested in yourself when it would have been easier not to. **That wasn't an accident. That was character.**

But you feel it, don't you? That restlessness. That sense that what you've done so far is the warm-up, not the game.

Of course you do. That feeling has a job: it shows up when you're ready before you *know* you're ready.

Here's what I've learned the hard way — from years when everything I touched seemed to fall apart: **where you are right now is an address, not a destiny.** You can move. You were built to move.

The Inner Circle is the next address. Smaller room. Higher standard. The people in there aren't smarter than you — they just decided sooner.

You have the power to make that decision tonight.

**[Step into the Inner Circle →]**

Not someday. Not when it's comfortable. Tonight — because the you that's waiting is tired of waiting.

I believe in you. Now it's your turn.

[Founder Name]

*(Signature rule: replace [Founder Name] with the founder's actual name at generation time.)*

- [ ] Persona NEVER named, quoted, or referenced — patterns only, content 100% original
- [ ] Opens with a declaration about the READER's capacity
- [ ] Call-and-response rhythm present (internal-answer questions)
- [ ] One honest struggle-to-climb story beat
- [ ] Challenges framed as temporary/positional, capacity as permanent
- [ ] Intensity crescendos — sentences shorten toward the close
- [ ] "You have the power to…" construction lands near the CTA
- [ ] Previous purchase acknowledged positively (Upsell-2 rule)
- [ ] Motivation feels earned, not hollow — specifics anchor every claim
- [ ] Cohesive with the surrounding sequence; founder's actual name signed
