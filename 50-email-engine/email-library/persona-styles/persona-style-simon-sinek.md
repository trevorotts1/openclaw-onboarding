## Style / Definition
The Simon Sinek style **leads with purpose**: the Golden Circle sequence of **Why → How → What.** The email opens with belief and cause, moves to method, and arrives at product last. Its governing ethic is explicit: **inspire, don't manipulate** — no scarcity theater, no fear leverage; alignment of belief is the only pressure applied.

**⛔ Cardinal rule (all 12 persona styles):** NEVER mention the persona's name, never quote them, never reference their talks/books. Patterns only; content 100% original.

## Purpose & Intention
People don't buy what you do; they buy *why* you do it — and they buy hardest when your why matches theirs. This style filters as it attracts: readers aligned with the cause lean in with unusual loyalty; misaligned readers self-select out (a feature, not a bug, for high-ticket rooms that depend on culture). Deploy when the offer genuinely carries a mission, when the audience is fatigued by tactic-speak, or when you're selling belonging-to-a-cause as much as a program. It is the natural voice for weaving a brand's "Big Bold Why" into an upsell.

## How To Write It
1. **Open with "we believe."** The first paragraph contains zero product. State the conviction that makes the company exist: "We believe the people doing meaningful work shouldn't be the worst-paid people in the room."
2. **Invite identification with the belief, not the offer.** "If you've ever felt that too, you're who this was built for." The reader joins a worldview before they evaluate a purchase.
3. **Move to How — the distinctive method** born of the belief. Because we believe X, we build differently: Y. The method is evidence of the conviction's sincerity.
4. **Arrive at What last, almost modestly.** The product is the artifact of the belief: "That belief, made practical, is the Partner Program."
5. **Purge manipulation mechanics.** No countdown clocks, no fear amplification, no false exclusivity. Any urgency must be structural and stated plainly. This style's credibility IS its restraint.
6. **Use calm-conviction cadence:** measured sentences, present tense, "we" for the mission and "you" for the invitation. Inspiration here whispers; it doesn't shout.
7. **Close with alignment as the decision criterion:** "If this is what you believe too, come build it with us." Acknowledge their previous purchase as an act of shared belief (Upsell-2 rule).

## Use Cases
- High-ticket / Upsell-2 emails for mission-driven brands and communities
- Weaving the landing page's "Big Bold Why" (Section 5) into a sequence
- Recruiting founding cohorts, partners, and ambassadors
- Re-anchoring a list after heavy promotional periods (restores trust)
- Offers where culture-fit matters more than volume of buyers

## Detailed Example
**Framework:** ACCA carried in this persona style · **Objective:** Upsell

**Subject Line A:** {{contact.first_name}}, first — here's what we believe
**Subject Line B:** The reason this exists at all, {{contact.first_name}}

**Preview Text:** Why before what. If it resonates, the rest is simple.

**Email Body:**

{{contact.first_name}},

Before anything else, here's what we believe: **the people doing the most meaningful work in their communities should not be the most under-resourced people in the room.**

We believe expertise deserves infrastructure. That serving people and building wealth were never supposed to be opposites. If you've ever felt that in your chest — that quiet insistence that your work should count for more — then you already understand why this company exists.

Because we believe that, we build differently. **We don't sell information; we install systems** — and we stay until they run. That's the How, and it's non-negotiable, because a belief you won't operationalize is just a slogan.

The newest expression of that belief is the Partner Program: our team working directly inside your business for a year. You've already built with us once — that first yes told us your why and ours point the same direction.

There's no countdown clock on this. Just one honest question:

**If you believe what we believe, come build the next level with us.**

**[Read what the Partner Program makes possible →]**

Whatever you decide, keep doing work that means something. That's the whole point.

[Founder Name]

*(Signature rule: replace [Founder Name] with the founder's actual name at generation time.)*

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Persona NEVER named, quoted, or referenced — patterns only, content 100% original
- [ ] Opens with belief ("we believe") — zero product in paragraph one
- [ ] Golden-Circle order held: Why → How → What
- [ ] Reader invited to identify with the belief before the offer
- [ ] How presented as the operational proof of the Why
- [ ] Product arrives last, framed as the belief made practical
- [ ] Zero manipulation mechanics (no countdowns, fear, false scarcity)
- [ ] Calm-conviction cadence; inspiration without shouting
- [ ] Close makes alignment the decision criterion; previous purchase honored (Upsell-2 rule)
- [ ] Cohesive with sequence; founder's actual name signed
