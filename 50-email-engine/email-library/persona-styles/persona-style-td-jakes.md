## Style / Definition
The T.D. Jakes style brings **pulpit architecture to the page**: rhythmic repetition that builds like a drumline; **vivid, extended metaphor** (seeds and seasons, rooms and doors, lions and instinct); the language of **purpose and destiny**; and deliberate **emotional crescendos** — paragraphs that climb, break, and release. The reader shouldn't just be convinced; they should feel *summoned*.

**⛔ Cardinal rule (all 12 persona styles):** NEVER mention the persona's name, never quote them, never reference their sermons/books. Patterns only; content 100% original.

## Purpose & Intention
Deploy when the offer connects to **calling** — when the reader's next level is best understood as stepping into what they were built for, not just buying what they want. The style's engine is elevation: it takes the reader's ordinary business decision and re-stages it inside a account of purpose and season, where hesitation means leaving destiny unclaimed. The crescendo structure does real conversion work: emotional altitude at the CTA moment is the point of every rhetorical device before it.

## How To Write It
1. **Open with the metaphor, already in motion.** "There is a difference between a seed in a packet and a seed in the ground — and only one of them has a future." Choose ONE governing metaphor and commit; mixed metaphors collapse the architecture.
2. **Establish the drumline.** Repetition with escalation: same opening phrase, rising stakes ("You survived the season that was supposed to break you. You survived the door that closed. You survived the delay that made no sense."). Three to five beats, then release.
3. **Speak destiny over the reader.** Purpose-language in second person: what's *in* them, what they were *built* for, what has been *waiting* for them. The reader is cast as someone on the verge, not someone in need.
4. **Reframe their history as setup.** Every delay, every closed door becomes preparation: "the delay wasn't denial — it was development."
5. **Build the crescendo consciously.** Sentences shorten as intensity climbs. Stack the final repetitions tight. Then break the rhythm with one short, quiet line — the release — and place the CTA there, where the reader exhales.
6. **Make the CTA an act of stepping into season:** claim, step, walk through, answer. Never "check out" or "learn more."
7. **Honor their previous purchase as the seed already planted** (Upsell-2 rule) — this upgrade is the harvest posture, not a new gamble.

## Use Cases
- High-ticket / Upsell-2 emails framed around purpose, calling, and legacy
- Faith-comfortable and purpose-driven audiences
- Season-launch campaigns (new year, new cohort, "your season" framing)
- Graduation moments — from student to leader, member to mentor
- The emotional-peak email in a 12-email sequence's late arc

## Detailed Example
**Framework:** PASTOR (Story) carried in this persona style · **Objective:** Upsell

**Subject Line A:** {{contact.first_name}}, the seed in the packet has no future
**Subject Line B:** Your delay was development, {{contact.first_name}}. Now it's time.

**Preview Text:** Some doors don't open twice. This one is open now.

**Email Body:**

{{contact.first_name}},

There is a difference between a seed in a packet and a seed in the ground. Same seed. Same potential. **But only one of them has a future** — because potential doesn't become harvest until it's planted somewhere that costs you something.

You know something about cost. You survived the season that was supposed to stop you. You survived the door that closed with no explanation. You survived the delay that made no sense at the time.

Hear me: **the delay was not denial. The delay was development.** Every closed door was steering you toward the one built with your name on it.

And you've already proven you know how to plant — when you sowed into the first program, that seed produced. But there's a difference between a planted seed and a planted *field*, and something in you has been whispering that it's field season.

The Legacy Program is the field: a year of building at the scale of what's actually in you — mentorship that pulls, rooms that stretch, work that outlives you.

The ground is ready. The season is now. The door is open.

Walk through it.

**[Step into your field season →]**

[Founder Name]

*(Signature rule: replace [Founder Name] with the founder's actual name at generation time.)*

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Persona NEVER named, quoted, or referenced — patterns only, content 100% original
- [ ] ONE governing metaphor, opened in motion and sustained throughout
- [ ] Drumline repetition with escalation (3–5 beats, then release)
- [ ] Destiny/purpose spoken over the reader in second person
- [ ] Reader's history reframed as preparation ("delay was development")
- [ ] Crescendo built consciously; rhythm breaks into a quiet release line
- [ ] CTA placed at the release, using stepping-into-season verbs
- [ ] Previous purchase honored as the seed already planted (Upsell-2 rule)
- [ ] Emotional altitude earned by structure, not by exclamation points
- [ ] Cohesive with sequence; founder's actual name signed
