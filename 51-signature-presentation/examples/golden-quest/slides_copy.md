### Slide 1 — AVATAR — Phase 1 — The Avatar Section
*(phase label slide — carries name + purpose)*

Purpose: before I tell you who I am, I tell you who YOU are. First we tell their story, then we tell ours. #StillCalled

> Suggested image: Full-bleed dawn horizon over a quiet city; a single lit window; cinematic, hopeful, typographic title lockup in warm gold.

### Slide 2 — AVATAR — The Chorus

The world is waiting on the one thing only you can do.

> Suggested image: Pure-typography slide, deep indigo field, the single line set large and centered; no imagery, let the words breathe.

### Slide 3 — AVATAR — Their Current State

#StillCalled — and still standing in the very same spot. You have done everything right and somehow you are still waiting for your life to begin.

> Suggested image: A capable professional at a stoplight at dusk, hands on the wheel, staring at a green light they haven't driven through yet.

### Slide 4 — AVATAR — The Ache

"Is my time ever going to come?" You watch louder, lesser people get chosen, and the quiet question won't leave you alone.

> Suggested image: Close portrait, half in shadow, of someone watching applause across a room they are not standing in.

### Slide 5 — AVATAR — Not Stuck — Unclaimed

You are not stuck — you are unclaimed. Stuck says the road is blocked. Unclaimed says the road is wide open and no one has said your name yet.

> Suggested image: A packed suitcase by an open door, morning light spilling in; the person just out of frame, deciding.

### Slide 6 — AVATAR — The Exhaustion of Proving

"I am so tired of auditioning for a life that is already mine." Competence became a costume. You perform 'fine' so well that no one asks if you are.

> Suggested image: A person removing a stage mask backstage, shoulders finally dropping, exhaustion and relief on the same face.

### Slide 7 — AVATAR — A Riddle

Who am I? I am the room's quiet engine. I make everyone else look ready. I am trusted with everything except the spotlight. Who am I? ... You. And that ends today.

> Suggested image: A hand-lettered riddle card on a table beside a single unlit candle and a match.

### Slide 8 — AVATAR — The Aspirational Avatar

Now imagine waking already chosen — not chasing permission, but doing the work only you can do, and being paid, seen, and thanked for exactly that.

> Suggested image: The same person from the pain slides, now on a sunlit path mid-stride, head up, moving with certainty.

### Slide 9 — AVATAR — What Answering Looks Like

It sounds like your own voice, unhedged. It looks like a calendar you actually chose. It feels like Monday stops being a sentence you serve.

> Suggested image: Split-frame: Monday-as-dread on the left, Monday-as-purpose on the right, same person, different posture.

### Slide 10 — AVATAR — The Whole Picture

Problem, aspiration, and the road between them — this is your story, told back to you so accurately that you lean in. Because I have lived it too.

> Suggested image: A wide watercolor map with a 'you are here' marker and a distant lighthouse.

### Slide 11 — AVATAR — Your Story, Then Mine

You have a story. So do I — and I only get to tell mine because I have already lived yours. So let me show you where the ache in your chest first found its voice.

> Suggested image: A storyteller stepping from shadow into a warm spotlight, turning to face the room.

### Slide 12 — STORY — Phase 2 — Your Signature Story
*(phase label slide — carries name + purpose)*

Purpose: so that you know that I know exactly where you are standing. Not a resume — a journey. #StillCalled

> Suggested image: Sepia-to-color transition of an old family photograph coming alive at the edges.

### Slide 13 — STORY — The Section Hook

I answered the call the day I stopped auditioning for my own life.

> Suggested image: A theatre seat, empty, spotlight fading on an unused stage — the day the auditioning stopped.

### Slide 14 — STORY — Before the Titles

Before the titles, I was a kid who narrated the sky — certain I was here to say something only I could say.

> Suggested image: A child on a rooftop at dusk, arms wide, narrating to the clouds.

### Slide 15 — STORY — People Who Look Like Me

In every hall I walked, I counted the room. "People who look like me" was a refrain I whispered before I learned to say it out loud.

> Suggested image: A lone student in a vast lecture hall, scanning rows of faces, notebook untouched.

### Slide 16 — STORY — First in the Family

I was the first — the first degree, the first office with a door, the first to be 'set for life.' And the first to feel a life set can also be a life stuck.

> Suggested image: Hands framing a diploma on a wall beside a family photo; pride and quiet unease.

### Slide 17 — STORY — The Golden Handcuffs

The career was real, respected, and safe. That was the trap: it was good enough to keep me from the thing I was actually for.

> Suggested image: A polished corner office at night, a beautiful cage of glass and city lights.

### Slide 18 — STORY — The Whisper

#StillCalled — the whisper I kept turning down. Every promotion turned the volume up, not down.

> Suggested image: A person turning down a radio dial that keeps glowing brighter no matter how low it goes.

### Slide 19 — STORY — The Leap

So I traded the title for the calling. I let the old identity go before I had a new one to hold. I told myself the refrain until my feet believed it: I was built for this.

> Suggested image: A figure stepping off a lit dock into fog, one foot already gone, the other lifting.

### Slide 20 — STORY — The Unedited Version

Nobody believes a perfect hero. So here is the unedited version: I doubted, I under-charged, I cried in a parked car. The power of vulnerability is relatability.

> Suggested image: A candid, unposed portrait, tears and a small laugh at once, no retouching.

### Slide 21 — STORY — Scars Into Map

Then the reframe that changed everything: my scars were not damage. They were the map. Every place I got lost is a place I can now guide you through.

> Suggested image: Old scars on a hand slowly rendered as topographic map lines and trail markers.

### Slide 22 — STORY — I Deserve To Win

I stopped asking permission to matter. "I Deserve To Win" — not at anyone's expense, but at last on my own terms.

> Suggested image: A runner at a finish line drawn in chalk that they clearly drew themselves.

### Slide 23 — STORY — The Road Not Taken

"Two roads diverged in a wood, and I— / I took the one less traveled by, / And that has made all the difference." — Robert Frost. The safe road is crowded for a reason.

> Suggested image: A forked forest trail, one path worn and busy, one mossy and quiet and lit.

### Slide 24 — STORY — The Gripping Why

Here is my why, full voice: I build because someone out there is one honest word away from staying — staying in their life, their gift, their calling. That someone is you.

> Suggested image: A single porch light left on against a dark street; someone almost walked away, but didn't.

### Slide 25 — TEACHING — Phase 3 — Transformational Teaching
*(phase label slide — carries name + purpose)*

Purpose: to hand you the map, one step at a time, so a calling stops feeling like a mystery and starts feeling like a method.

> Suggested image: A large blueprint unrolled on a wooden table, weighted at the corners, a compass on top.

### Slide 26 — TEACHING — The Wayfinder Blueprint

A calling is not found. It is followed, one step at a time. The Wayfinder Blueprint is 3 Quests across 5 steps. How do you walk an entire calling? The same way you eat an elephant — bite by bite.

> Suggested image: An elegant hand-drawn blueprint titled 'The Wayfinder Blueprint' with three quest banners and five numbered waypoints.

### Slide 27 — TEACHING — A Definition Pair

Morning vs mourning. You have been spending your mourning — grieving the life you think you missed — in the exact hours meant for your morning. We are going to trade them back.

> Suggested image: Two near-identical dawn windows side by side, one grey with rain, one gold with sun.

### Slide 28 — TEACHING — Quest I — The Purpose Quest

The first Quest answers the oldest question: what is the work only you can do? Its affirmation is Openness — you cannot receive a calling with clenched hands.

> Suggested image: A quest banner reading 'The Purpose Quest' staked at the trailhead of a mountain path.

### Slide 29 — TEACHING — Step 1 — Name the Ache

Step One. The ache is not a malfunction — it is a compass. Where it hurts to stay small is exactly where you are meant to grow large. First we name it precisely.

> Suggested image: A compass whose needle points not north but toward a glowing figure of the reader.

### Slide 30 — TEACHING — Step 1 — The Question

A thought-provoking question: what did you love before the world told you it wasn't practical? Write the first true answer, not the acceptable one.

> Suggested image: A journal open to a blank page, a pen resting, morning coffee steaming beside it.

### Slide 31 — TEACHING — Step 1 — A Story in Three Beats

A woman told me she 'just organizes things.' Beat one: her team fell apart without her. Beat two: she rebuilt it in a week. Beat three: she still called it 'nothing special.'

> Suggested image: A three-panel comic strip of the same calm figure holding chaos together.

### Slide 32 — TEACHING — Step 1 — Core Teaching

Your ache always points at a gift you have been giving away for free. Naming the ache is naming the gift. Unnamed, it leaks. Named, it leads.

> Suggested image: A leaking cupped hand of light beside a lantern that has caught and contained the same light.

### Slide 33 — TEACHING — Step 1 — Do This

Action steps: 1) List three moments you were thanked and shrugged it off. 2) Circle the skill underneath each. 3) The one that repeats is your ache, wearing work clothes.

> Suggested image: A checklist card with three hand-drawn boxes, the third circled in gold.

### Slide 34 — TEACHING — Step 1 — Affirmation: Openness

Say it: 'I open my hands. What I am for is allowed to be bigger than what I have settled for.' Openness is the affirmation of the Purpose Quest.

> Suggested image: Two open palms lifted into a shaft of light, a small seed resting in them.

### Slide 35 — TEACHING — Step 2 — Read the Map You Already Carry

Step Two. You are not missing a map. You are ignoring one. Your history, your wounds, your unreasonable joys — together they are the most detailed map you will ever own.

> Suggested image: A worn map tattooed faintly across an open hand, trails matching old scars.

### Slide 36 — TEACHING — Step 2 — A Media Moment

Play the ninety-second clip here: a child learning to whistle — the frustration, the accidental first note, the face. That face is what following your map feels like.

> Suggested image: A film-strip frame of a delighted child mid-whistle, breath visible in cold air.

### Slide 37 — TEACHING — Step 2 — One-Liner

Write this one down: minimizing yourself is putting yourself on sale. Every time you shrink, someone learns your true value from your discount.

> Suggested image: A luxury item mislabeled with a scrawled clearance sticker, being peeled off.

### Slide 38 — TEACHING — Step 2 — A Simple Formula

The clarity formula: Clarity = Attention x Honesty. Most people have neither the attention to look nor the honesty to admit what they see. You will have both.

> Suggested image: A clean chalkboard with 'Clarity = Attention x Honesty' written in confident script.

### Slide 39 — TEACHING — Step 2 — A Life Re-Read

One learner mapped her 'wasted' years as a caretaker and found the exact empathy her new practice was built on. Nothing was wasted; it was all reconnaissance for the calling.

> Suggested image: A woman tracing a long winding trail on a map and smiling at where it finally leads.

### Slide 40 — TEACHING — Step 2 — Affirmation: Authenticity

Say it: 'I read the map I already carry. My real history is my qualification.' Authenticity is the closing affirmation of the Purpose Quest.

> Suggested image: A hand pressing a wax seal marked 'true' onto a rolled map.

### Slide 41 — TEACHING — Quest II — The Confidence Quest

The second Quest is not about feeling ready — it is about moving while unready. Its affirmation is Revelation: confidence is revealed in motion, never in the mirror.

> Suggested image: A quest banner reading 'The Confidence Quest' planted at a foggy ridge crossing.

### Slide 42 — TEACHING — Step 3 — Silence the Auditor

Step Three. There is a voice that audits every move before you make it — tallying risks, rehearsing the failure. It is not wisdom. It is fear with a clipboard.

> Suggested image: A shadowy figure with a clipboard shrinking as the reader turns on a light.

### Slide 43 — TEACHING — Step 3 — The Question

Powerful question: whose voice is the auditor using? Nine times out of ten it is borrowed. Name the lender, and you can start returning the debt.

> Suggested image: An audio waveform slowly resolving into a familiar silhouette from someone's past.

### Slide 44 — TEACHING — Step 3 — A Story

I once let the auditor talk me out of a talk I was born to give. I sat in the parking lot. Someone else gave a worse version to my exact audience. Never again.

> Suggested image: An empty parking space beside a lit venue, a ticket stub on the ground.

### Slide 45 — TEACHING — Step 3 — Core Teaching

You do not silence the auditor by winning the argument. You silence it by moving before the argument ends. Action is the only mute button that has ever worked.

> Suggested image: A hand pressing a large glowing 'GO' button while alarms fade to grey.

### Slide 46 — TEACHING — Step 3 — Take This With You

Downloadable: the Auditor Rebuttal Card — three sentences to say aloud the next time the clipboard comes out. Keep it in your wallet next to your nerve.

> Suggested image: A pocket-sized printed card titled 'Auditor Rebuttal' tucked into a worn wallet.

### Slide 47 — TEACHING — Step 3 — Affirmation: Revelation

Say it: 'I do not wait to feel ready. I move, and my courage is revealed to me on the way.' Revelation is the opening affirmation of the Confidence Quest.

> Suggested image: A path lighting up one stone at a time only as a foot lands on each.

### Slide 48 — TEACHING — Step 4 — Walk Before You're Ready

Step Four. Readiness is a story you tell to delay the walk. The path reveals itself to those already walking it — never to those still studying the trailhead.

> Suggested image: A hiker three steps onto a path that visibly extends only as far as they've walked.

### Slide 49 — TEACHING — Step 4 — An Anecdote

The learners who transform are not the most prepared. They are the ones who booked the room, made the offer, and hit publish while their hands were still shaking.

> Suggested image: A trembling hand hovering over a 'publish' key, then pressing it, motion-blurred.

### Slide 50 — TEACHING — Step 4 — Borrowed Authority

"Do the thing and you will have the power." — Ralph Waldo Emerson. Power follows the doing. It has never once arrived in advance to reward the hesitant.

> Suggested image: An old library portrait of Emerson beside his quote, gilt frame, warm reading lamp.

### Slide 51 — TEACHING — Step 4 — A Life Changed

A soft-spoken analyst ran one imperfect workshop for six people. Two of them wept. He later said the walk before he felt ready was the readiest he had ever felt.

> Suggested image: Six chairs in a small circle, a nervous facilitator standing, the room leaning in.

### Slide 52 — TEACHING — Step 4 — Do This

Action steps: 1) Pick the smallest real version of the scary thing. 2) Give it a date this week. 3) Tell one witness. Motion plus a witness beats motivation every time.

> Suggested image: A calendar with this week circled and a single name written in the margin.

### Slide 53 — TEACHING — Step 4 — Affirmation: Confidence

Say it: 'I walk before I am ready, and the road rises to meet me.' Confidence is the closing affirmation of the Confidence Quest.

> Suggested image: A road subtly tilting upward to meet a walker's next step, dawn ahead.

### Slide 54 — TEACHING — Quest III — The Value Quest

The final Quest turns your calling outward. Its affirmation is Love — because value, in the end, is love made useful to someone else.

> Suggested image: A quest banner reading 'The Value Quest' at a summit overlooking a valley of lights.

### Slide 55 — TEACHING — Step 5 — Give the Gift Only You Can Give

Step Five. A calling is not a possession; it is a delivery. The last step is to hand your gift to the exact people who have been waiting for your particular hands.

> Suggested image: A wrapped gift being placed into outstretched hands lit by a single warm lamp.

### Slide 56 — TEACHING — Step 5 — Core Teaching

Your gift is not the skill — it is the transformation the skill makes possible. Sell the crossing, not the boat. People do not want the map; they want to arrive.

> Suggested image: A ferryman's hand pointing not at the boat but at the far, glowing shore.

### Slide 57 — TEACHING — Step 5 — Visualization

Close your eyes. See the one person your work is for. Watch their face the moment they understand they were never broken — only unclaimed. Hold that face. That is the point.

> Suggested image: A soft-focus face lighting up with relief and recognition, eyes brimming.

### Slide 58 — TEACHING — Step 5 — Reinforcing Quote

"We make a living by what we get, but we make a life by what we give." The Value Quest is where a living and a life finally become the same act.

> Suggested image: Two cupped hands passing a small flame between them without either being burned.

### Slide 59 — TEACHING — Step 5 — Affirmation: Love

Say it: 'I give the gift only I can give, and in the giving I become who I was called to be.' Love is the crowning affirmation of the Value Quest.

> Suggested image: A single candle lighting a dozen others, its own flame undiminished.

### Slide 60 — TEACHING — The Bridge

So there is the whole map: name the ache, read your map, silence the auditor, walk unready, give your gift. You already have everything you need. What most people miss next is not information — it is company for the walk. In a moment, I will show you exactly what walking it together looks like.

> Suggested image: A trailhead at golden hour where a single path splits into many footprints walking forward side by side.

### Slide 68 — PITCH — Benefit — The Auditor, Quiet

### Slide 70 — PITCH — Benefit — An Offer You Can Make

### Slide 71 — PITCH — Benefit — Motion Over Motivation

### Slide 72 — PITCH — Benefit — Proof You Can Feel

Monthly 'answered' checkpoints where you see, in writing, how far you have actually come — the receipts your doubt keeps hiding.

> Suggested image: A progress journal with checkmarks accumulating down the page.

### Slide 73 — PITCH — Benefit — A Calling That Pays
