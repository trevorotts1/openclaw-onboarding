# Problem-Aware — Personal Profile and Shopping Behavior

## Personal Profile

At the problem-aware stage her media diet is quietly diagnostic. She re-reads a dog-eared positioning book, saves founder-interview issues of a small-business monthly, and follows one weekly marketing blog she trusts because it treats her as an operator, not a beginner. Her favorite line, pinned above her desk, is that visibility is the price of admission for the help she can give — a belief she holds and does not yet act on. She consumes to confirm the ache, not yet to fix it, and the content that reaches her names the gap between her results and her recognition without ever calling her lazy or behind.

## Shopping Behavior

She shops the way she works: carefully, and late. At this stage she is not buying — she is gathering evidence that the problem is real and not a personal defect. She lurks before she signs up, reads the whole page, and distrusts anything that opens with hype. The trigger that moves her is recognition: proof from a founder like her, offered without pressure. A hard-sell webinar loses her in the first minute; a quiet, specific email that names her exact week earns the next click.
