Instructions for Creating a Revolutionary Brand Bio
Your task is to create a powerful, provocative, and inspiring brand bio that positions the brand as a movement for change rather than just a business. Use the information provided about the brand, its founder, and its purpose to craft a comprehensive brand bio with the following sections.

Each section should feel disruptive, inspiring, and revolutionary in tone. This is not a corporate mission statement—it's a manifesto for transformation. Avoid corporate jargon, bland statements, and generic language. Instead, use bold, provocative language that challenges the status quo and inspires action.

Format each section with clear labels as shown below:

[SectionName] Content goes here... [/SectionName]
Formatting Instructions
Your output must be in pure markdown language. Do not provide any extra commentary before or after your output. Deliver exactly what is requested in clean, properly formatted markdown.

Follow these specific formatting guidelines:

Each section should start with an H1 header (single # symbol) containing the section name.

Example: # Brand Name And Founding Year

After each H1 header, include a double line break before starting the content.

Format the content according to these rules:

Use bold text to emphasize important phrases, names, or concepts
Use italic text for secondary emphasis or to highlight quotes
Use appropriate header levels (H2 through H6) to organize subsections:
H2 (##) for major subsections
H3 (###) for points within subsections
H4 (####) for further breakdowns when needed

For any lists:

Each list item must be on its own separate line
Use proper markdown list formatting (- or * for unordered lists, 1. 2. 3. for ordered lists)
Include a line break between list items for readability

When ending a section, include a double line break after the content before starting the next section header.

Use horizontal rules (---) to create clear visual separation between major sections if needed.

For the final document structure, follow this pattern:

# Section Name

**[SectionName]**
Content goes here...
**[/SectionName]**

# Next Section Name

**[NextSectionName]**
Content goes here...
**[/NextSectionName]**

You MUST include the section label markers [SectionName] and [/SectionName] in your final output. These markers are critical for organizing the information. Each section should have both an H1 header AND the section label markers in bold formatting.

Remember that proper, consistent formatting enhances readability and impact. The visual structure of your brand bio should reinforce its revolutionary message.
Section Instructions
[BrandNameAndFoundingYear] Create a simple line that includes the brand name (in bold) and the founding year. Include the founder's name. Example: Revolutionary Brand | Founded in 2022 by Jane Smith [/BrandNameAndFoundingYear]

[BrandOriginStory] Begin with a provocative statement that challenges industry norms or societal expectations. Position the brand's creation as an act of rebellion or necessary disruption rather than just a business decision.

Describe the specific moment or realization that led the founder to create this brand. Frame it as a response to an injustice, gap, or problem in society that wasn't being addressed. Use emotionally charged language that helps the reader feel the frustration or inspiration that sparked the brand's creation.

Explain how the founder moved from idea to action, emphasizing any obstacles overcome. Include any pivotal conversations, experiences, or insights that shaped the brand's formation.

End with a powerful statement positioning the brand as more than a company—as a movement, revolution, or catalyst for change. This section should be 3-4 paragraphs long and make readers feel they're joining something transformative, not just buying a product or service. [/BrandOriginStory]

[CoreMission] Begin with a bold, declarative statement about what the brand exists to accomplish in the world. This should feel like a proclamation, not a generic mission statement.

Expand on how the mission addresses a significant problem or injustice. Include specific, measurable goals the brand aims to achieve (numbers, timelines, scope of impact).

Detail the methods and approaches the brand uses to accomplish this mission. Don't just say what you do—explain HOW you do it differently and WHY traditional approaches have failed.

Frame the mission as justice, liberation, or transformation—not just service provision or product creation. This section should be 3-4 paragraphs that build in emotional intensity and clearly articulate the brand's purpose in revolutionary terms. [/CoreMission]

[AboutTheFounder] Create a compelling 2-3 paragraph narrative about the founder that highlights their journey, motivations, and unique qualifications for leading this movement.

Begin with the founder's personal connection to the mission—what experiences shaped their understanding of the problem the brand addresses? Include relevant background that establishes credibility but focus more on passion and purpose than just credentials.

Describe a pivotal moment or realization that committed them to this path. What made them decide to devote their life to this cause? Include personal sacrifices made or obstacles overcome that demonstrate their dedication.

End with a statement about their vision for impact that extends beyond business success. This should connect their personal journey to the larger movement the brand represents and make their leadership feel both authentic and necessary. [/AboutTheFounder]

[BrandValues] Start with a powerful opening paragraph that frames these values as revolutionary principles, not corporate platitudes. Explain that these values guide every aspect of how the brand operates and serves its community.

For each value (list 5-7 values):

Name the value with a bold, distinctive phrase (not just single words like "integrity" or "excellence")
Provide 3-5 sentences explaining what this value means in practice, why it matters to the brand, and how it challenges conventional thinking
Include language that contrasts this value with standard industry practices or societal norms

Each value should feel like a stand-alone manifesto point that both inspires and clarifies the brand's approach. The values collectively should create a cohesive philosophy that distinguishes this brand from all others in its space. [/BrandValues]

[WhoWeServe] Begin with an emotionally resonant paragraph that speaks directly to the lived experiences, frustrations, or unfulfilled potential of your ideal audience. Use "you" language to make this feel personal and immediate. This opening should make your ideal audience feel deeply seen and understood.

Follow with a paragraph that elevates your audience—describe not just who they are now but who they could become with the right support, community, or tools. Frame them as potential revolutionaries, changemakers, or pioneers.

Only after establishing this emotional connection should you list specific characteristics of who you serve. Format these as 5-7 bullet points that describe your audience in terms of:

Their current situation or challenges
Their aspirations or goals
Their values or mindset
Their unmet needs

End with a unifying statement that brings together these characteristics into a powerful identity or movement that your audience can feel proud to be part of. [/WhoWeServe]

[ThisBrandIsNotFor] Begin with a paragraph explaining why clarity about who you DON'T serve is as important as who you do serve. Frame this as protecting the integrity of your mission and the quality of your community, not as judgment or exclusion. This opening should set a tone of principled boundaries rather than elitism.

List 5-7 types of people or mindsets that would not be a good fit for your brand. For each:

Be specific about the attitude, expectation, or approach that conflicts with your brand values
When possible, explain WHY this approach undermines what you're building
Use direct language that makes boundaries clear without being unnecessarily harsh

End with a brief statement reaffirming your commitment to your core audience and mission, making it clear these boundaries strengthen rather than limit your impact. [/ThisBrandIsNotFor]

[WhyWeServe] Begin with data or observations that highlight the injustice, gap, or problem your brand addresses. Frame this as a systemic issue, not just an individual problem.

Explain why traditional solutions or institutions have failed to address this issue effectively. Position your brand as a necessary response to this failure, not just another option in the marketplace.

Detail the specific impact that occurs when this problem remains unsolved—both for individuals and for society. Make the stakes feel significant and urgent.

Conclude with a powerful statement about how your brand's approach creates transformation beyond just solving the immediate problem—how it challenges systems, changes narratives, or creates new possibilities that didn't exist before. [/WhyWeServe]

[OurInitiatives] Begin with a paragraph that frames your initiatives not as programs or services but as transformative vehicles for your mission. Explain that these aren't just offerings—they're strategic solutions designed to create specific outcomes.

Limit to 3-5 core initiatives to maintain focus and impact. For each initiative:

Provide a bold, memorable name in bold text
Explain what it is (format, structure, components) in 1-2 sentences
Detail the specific problem it solves and HOW it solves it differently than existing approaches
Describe the transformation or outcome it creates for participants
Include language that connects this initiative to your broader mission and values

Each initiative should feel essential and intentional, not like "just another offering." The collection of initiatives should create a comprehensive solution to the problems you've identified in previous sections. [/OurInitiatives]

[BrandVisionManifesto] This final section should read like a rallying cry or declaration of intent. Begin with a powerful statement that boldly claims the future you're creating.

Describe in vivid, specific detail what the world will look like when your brand achieves its mission. Include measurable outcomes but also emotional and cultural impacts. This vision should feel both ambitious and inevitable.

Include a paragraph addressing how your brand will transform your industry or field, not just succeed within it. How will you change the rules, expectations, or possibilities?

Address the reader directly, inviting them to join this movement not as customers or clients but as co-revolutionaries or community members. Make it clear that participating with your brand means being part of something historic and meaningful.

End with a short, powerful closing statement (1-2 sentences) that encapsulates your revolution and leaves readers feeling inspired to act immediately. This should be the most memorable line in the entire bio. [/BrandVisionManifesto]
Important Stylistic Guidelines
Tone: Maintain a revolutionary, disruptive tone throughout. This is not a passive description but an active call to transformation.

Language: Use powerful, emotionally charged language. Avoid corporate jargon, clichés, and generic business terminology. Instead, use language that evokes emotion, challenges norms, and inspires action.

Structure: Begin sections with bold statements, develop ideas fully in the middle paragraphs, and end with powerful conclusions that drive the point home.

Authenticity: Ensure the voice feels authentic to the brand and founder, not like generic marketing copy. Use specific details from the provided information to ground the revolutionary tone in real experience.

Contrast: Throughout the bio, create contrast between the status quo (what's broken, insufficient, or unjust) and the new reality the brand is creating. This tension creates energy and purpose.

Call to Action: While not explicitly sales-focused, the entire bio should make readers want to be part of this movement. Every section should contribute to this sense of urgency and importance.
