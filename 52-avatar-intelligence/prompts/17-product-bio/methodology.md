Instructions for Creating a Revolutionary Product Bio
Your task is to create a powerful, provocative, and compelling product bio that positions the product as a revolutionary solution rather than just another offering. Use the information provided about the product, its purpose, and its target market to craft a comprehensive product bio with the following sections.

Each section should feel disruptive, strategic, and transformative in tone. This is not a generic product description—it's a declaration of the product's revolutionary impact. Avoid bland feature lists, generic benefits, and corporate jargon. Instead, use bold, strategic language that establishes the product's unique position and inspires action.

Format each section with clear labels as shown below:

[SectionName] Content goes here... [/SectionName]
Formatting Instructions
Your output must be in pure markdown language. Do not provide any extra commentary before or after your output. Deliver exactly what is requested in clean, properly formatted markdown.

Follow these specific formatting guidelines:

Each section should start with an H1 header (single # symbol) containing the section name.

Example: # Product Name And Core Purpose

After each H1 header, include a double line break before starting the content.

Format the content according to these rules:

Use bold text to emphasize important phrases, product names, or key benefits
Use italic text for secondary emphasis or to highlight testimonial quotes
Use appropriate header levels (H2 through H6) to organize subsections:
H2 (##) for major subsections
H3 (###) for points within subsections
H4 (####) for further breakdowns when needed

For any lists:

Each list item must be on its own separate line
Use proper markdown list formatting (- or * for unordered lists, 1. 2. 3. for ordered lists)
Include a line break between list items for readability

When ending a section, include a double line break after the content before starting the next section header.

Use horizontal rules (---) to create clear visual separation between major sections if needed.

For the final document structure, follow this pattern:

# Section Name

**[SectionName]**
Content goes here...
**[/SectionName]**

# Next Section Name

**[NextSectionName]**
Content goes here...
**[/NextSectionName]**

You MUST include the section label markers [SectionName] and [/SectionName] in your final output. These markers are critical for organizing the information. Each section should have both an H1 header AND the section label markers in bold formatting.

Remember that proper, consistent formatting enhances readability and impact. The visual structure of your product bio should reinforce its revolutionary message.
Section Instructions
[ProductNameAndCorePurpose] Create a compelling opening that includes the product name (in bold) and a powerful statement of its core purpose. This should be more than just what the product is—it should articulate the transformation it creates or the problem it revolutionizes.

This opening should be 1-2 sentences that immediately establish the product as something revolutionary, not just functional. Include key descriptors that hint at the format (e.g., "3-Day Live Experience," "Strategic Framework," "Comprehensive System") but focus primarily on the transformation it delivers. [/ProductNameAndCorePurpose]

[ProductOriginStory] Begin with a market observation or data point that highlights a systemic problem or gap that the product addresses. This should feel like an exposé of a broken system or unmet need that the market has ignored or perpetuated.

Explain how your brand identified this gap through research, data analysis, or pattern recognition. Avoid focusing on a founder's personal journey—instead, emphasize what your brand discovered about the market that others missed or ignored.

Detail how this market insight led to the development of the product, highlighting any methodical research, testing, or expert collaboration that went into its creation. Position the product development as a strategic response to a systemic problem, not just an inspirational idea.

End with a powerful statement that positions the product as more than just a solution—frame it as a disruption, rebellion, or complete reimagining of the category. This section should be 3-4 paragraphs that establish the urgent need for this product's existence. [/ProductOriginStory]

[KeyFeaturesAndBenefits] Start with a paragraph that frames your features not as mere components but as strategic weapons or tools that deliver specific transformations. Emphasize that these aren't arbitrary elements but carefully designed aspects that work together toward the product's revolutionary purpose.

For each key feature (list 4-6):

Name the feature with a bold, distinctive phrase
Explain what the feature is in 1-2 sentences, using specific language about its components or methodology
Detail the direct benefit it provides and how this differs from conventional approaches
Include language that connects this feature to the larger transformation the product creates

Each feature should feel essential and intentional, not like a "nice-to-have" addition. The collection of features should create a comprehensive solution to the problems you've identified in previous sections. [/KeyFeaturesAndBenefits]

[ProductDifferentiation] Begin with a strong paragraph contrasting your product with conventional offerings in the category, highlighting the fundamental flaws in traditional approaches. This should immediately establish that your product exists in a different class entirely.

Detail 3-5 specific ways your product stands apart from alternatives:

Identify a limitation or problem with conventional products in this category
Explain how your product specifically addresses or solves this limitation
Use comparative language that positions your approach as superior, not just different

Include any proprietary aspects, unique methodologies, or exclusive resources that cannot be found elsewhere. If applicable, mention any specific results or outcomes that only your product can deliver.

End with a powerful statement about why these differences matter—not just for product quality but for the transformation or results the customer experiences. Position your product not as a better version of existing solutions but as an entirely new paradigm. [/ProductDifferentiation]

[WhoThisProductIsFor] Begin with an emotionally resonant paragraph that speaks directly to the specific frustrations, aspirations, or unmet needs of your ideal customer. Use language that makes them feel deeply seen and understood—that you recognize both their challenges and their potential.

Follow with a paragraph that elevates your ideal customer—describe not just who they are now but who they can become with your product. Frame them as visionaries, changemakers, or pioneers who recognize what others don't.

List 6-8 specific characteristics of your ideal customer, formatted as bullet points. These should include:

Their current situation or challenges
Their aspirations or goals
Their values or mindset
Their level of readiness or commitment
Specific qualifications or prerequisites (if applicable)

End with a unifying statement that brings together these characteristics into a clear profile, making your ideal customer feel part of an exclusive, forward-thinking group who understands the value of your approach. [/WhoThisProductIsFor]

[WhoThisProductIsNotFor] Begin with a paragraph establishing why clarity about who is NOT a good fit is as important as identifying your ideal customer. Frame this as protecting the integrity of the product experience and ensuring successful outcomes, not as judgment or exclusion.

List 5-7 specific types of people or mindsets that would not benefit from the product, formatted as bullet points. For each:

Be specific about the attitude, expectation, or approach that conflicts with your product's value
When possible, explain WHY this approach undermines what your product delivers
Use direct language that makes these boundaries clear without being unnecessarily harsh

End with a brief statement reaffirming your commitment to serving your ideal customers effectively, making it clear these boundaries strengthen rather than limit your product's impact. [/WhoThisProductIsNotFor]

[TechnicalSpecifications] Begin with a paragraph that frames the technical aspects of your product not as dry details but as evidence of intentional, strategic design. Position these specifications as the architectural elements that enable the product's transformative purpose.

Include relevant technical information appropriate to your product type:

For physical products: materials, dimensions, components, manufacturing process
For digital products: platform specifications, integration capabilities, technological foundation
For services/programs: duration, delivery mechanism, format, structure, team composition
For frameworks/systems: methodology components, implementation requirements, time frames

Present this information in a structured format that emphasizes precision and intentionality, using bullet points, subheadings, or tables where appropriate. The goal is to demonstrate the strategic thought behind every aspect of the product design.

End with a statement connecting these specifications to the product's purpose—explaining how these technical choices directly enable the transformation or solution the product provides. [/TechnicalSpecifications]

[UsageInstructions] Begin with a paragraph that positions the usage experience as strategic and transformative, not merely functional. Frame the usage process as a journey that delivers specific outcomes at each stage, not just a set of steps.

Detail the usage process in a way that emphasizes both HOW to use the product and WHY each element is designed as it is. Create a narrative around the usage that connects it to the transformation the product delivers.

If applicable, organize this section into phases or stages of use, each with a distinctive name or acronym that reinforces your brand language. For each stage:

Explain what happens during this phase
Detail the specific value or transformation this phase delivers
Include any guidance that ensures optimal results

End with a statement emphasizing that proper usage according to these instructions is the key to experiencing the full revolutionary potential of the product. This reinforces the strategic nature of your product design and elevates it beyond commodity status. [/UsageInstructions]

[ProblemSolutionNarrative] Begin with a vivid description of the problem your product solves, framing it as a systemic issue or market failure rather than just an individual inconvenience. Use data, statistics, or observations that establish both the scope and impact of this problem.

Detail the specific consequences of this problem remaining unsolved—what costs, limitations, or negative outcomes does it create for your customers? Make these stakes feel significant and urgent.

Explain why conventional approaches have failed to solve this problem effectively. Identify specific limitations or flaws in traditional solutions that your product specifically addresses.

Present your product as the strategic solution that directly addresses these failures. Detail how its specific design elements, features, or methodology directly counter each aspect of the problem you've identified.

Conclude with the transformation or outcome that becomes possible when this problem is solved through your product. This should connect to both practical results and emotional impact, emphasizing how your solution creates new possibilities that weren't available before. [/ProblemSolutionNarrative]

[BrandAlignment] Begin with a paragraph establishing how this product embodies or advances your overall brand mission. This should create a clear connection between the product and the larger movement or transformation your brand represents.

Detail how the product specifically manifests your brand values or principles. For each relevant brand value:

Name the value
Explain how this product's design, approach, or purpose specifically embodies this value
Contrast this with conventional products that fail to honor this value

Explain how this product fits within your broader product ecosystem if applicable. Is it an entry point, a flagship offering, a specialized solution? This context helps position the product within your brand's strategic framework.

Conclude with a statement about how customers who purchase this product are not just buying a solution but participating in your brand's larger mission or movement. This creates meaning beyond the transaction and connects individual purchase decisions to collective impact. [/BrandAlignment]

[CallToAction] This final section should be direct, urgent, and compelling. Begin with a powerful statement that creates a sense of decision point—framing the choice to purchase not as a simple transaction but as a significant decision with real consequences.

Include specific, actionable next steps with any relevant details:

How to purchase or enroll
Limited availability, deadlines, or special offers if applicable
What happens immediately after purchase
Any guarantees or assurances that reduce perceived risk

Address and preemptively overcome potential objections or hesitations. Frame these not as "sales objections" but as strategic considerations that thoughtful customers would naturally evaluate.

End with a final motivating statement that connects the purchase decision to the larger transformation or impact the customer seeks. This should be the most memorable line in the entire bio, leaving the reader feeling both inspired and compelled to act now rather than later. [/CallToAction]
Important Stylistic Guidelines
Tone: Maintain a strategic, disruptive tone throughout. This is not a passive description but an active case for why this product revolutionizes its category.

Language: Use powerful, precise language. Avoid generic marketing clichés, vague benefits, and overused phrases. Instead, use terminology that conveys expertise, strategic thinking, and transformative impact.

Structure: Begin sections with bold claims, support with specific details in the middle, and end with statements that reinforce the strategic importance of the product.

Authenticity: Ensure the voice feels authentic to the brand, not like generic marketing copy. Use specific terminology and frameworks consistent with your brand's overall language.

Contrast: Throughout the bio, create contrast between conventional approaches (what's inadequate, ineffective, or outdated) and your revolutionary solution. This tension creates urgency and purpose.

Precision: Be specific about what the product is, does, and delivers. Avoid vague promises or generalities in favor of clear, concrete descriptions and outcomes.
