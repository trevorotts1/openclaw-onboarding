# Book Cover Image Prompt — The Quiet Authority

## Primary generation prompt

A single unlit lamp sitting on a calm, dark wooden desk, photographed in a quiet room at dusk. A soft warm glow is just beginning to bloom at the base of the lamp — not switched fully on, but waking. Minimalist composition, high-contrast, deep charcoal and near-black background with plenty of negative space so the image can breathe. The mood is stillness and earned confidence: quiet, settled authority rather than force or spectacle. Cinematic, restrained studio lighting; the warm glow is the only color in an otherwise cool, muted frame. No people, no text baked into the art, no clutter on the desk. Photorealistic, shallow depth of field, gentle vignette drawing the eye to the base of the lamp where the light is being born.

## Art direction notes

- Concept: the lamp is the book's central motif — quiet, earned light versus forced brightness. The lamp is not blazing; it is *about* to give light. That "just beginning" glow is the whole promise of the book on a shelf.
- Palette: cool dark neutrals (charcoal, ink, deep slate) with a single warm amber source low in the frame. Keep the contrast high and the composition calm.
- Negative space: leave the upper third open and quiet for the title treatment. Room to breathe is a requirement, not a nicety.
- Feeling to evoke: a tired, capable person exhaling. Control loosening into trust. Authority you do not have to assert.
- Avoid: crowds, offices full of props, glossy corporate stock energy, harsh flat overhead light, neon, and any lens flare that reads as "loud."

## Title treatment guidance (for the typesetter, not the image model)

Set the title **The Quiet Authority** in the calm upper negative space, in a restrained serif with generous letter-spacing. Place the subtitle *How the Best New Leaders Trade Control for Trust* beneath it in a lighter weight. Author name — Marcus Halloway — small, at the base. Let the lamp's warm glow do the emotional work; keep the type quiet.
