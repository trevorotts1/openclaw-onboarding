# Back-Cover Blurb — The Quiet Authority

You were the best engineer on the team. Then they made you the manager, and everything you were great at started working against you.

You rewrite people's work at night because you can't stand to see it done differently. You answer every question because answering is faster than teaching. You tell yourself this is what "high standards" look like. But your team has gone quiet. They wait for you. They ask permission. And the harder you push, the more everything depends on you.

**The Quiet Authority: How the Best New Leaders Trade Control for Trust** is the book for the moment you realize your own competence has become your team's ceiling.

Drawing on hard-won lessons from his own first year as a manager — including the 2 a.m. mistake that nearly cost him a teammate — Marcus Halloway shows you how to make the hardest trade in leadership: control for trust. In twelve short, honest chapters, you'll learn to listen louder than you speak, delegate without abandoning, give feedback that lands without fear, and step back so your people can step up.

This is not about becoming a different person. It's about becoming the kind of leader whose authority is felt even when the room is quiet — the kind whose team ships beautifully without them standing in the way.

Stop being the smartest person in the room. Become the leader who multiplies everyone else.
