# The Weight of the Keys

## What a Locked Door Taught Me About Letting Go

For nineteen years I carried the same ring of eleven keys in my right front pocket, and the weight told me who I was. Bell & Son Automotive sat on the corner of Delridge and Orchard for thirty-one years, and I made it better than my father left it. I kept the blue Igloo cooler stocked with cold water for anybody who walked in, customer or not, and word got around that Bell's was a place you could stand in the shade.

Then the padlock the bank sent by certified mail arrived on a Tuesday. Not a suit, not a phone call — a cheap twelve-dollar lock in a box, with a letter saying a representative would secure the premises on Friday. I handed over all eleven keys before he could ask, and I walked to my truck with my pocket empty for the first time in nineteen years.
