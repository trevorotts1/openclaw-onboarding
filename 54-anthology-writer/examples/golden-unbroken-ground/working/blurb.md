# Back-Cover Blurb

When the bank's padlock arrived by certified mail, Marcus Bell still had the keys to a shop that was no longer his. This is the story of the year he spent learning the difference between the building he lost and the man he was afraid he never was without it.

Told in the plain, unhurried voice of someone who worked with his hands, "The Weight of the Keys" follows one mechanic through the shutdown of a family auto-repair shop and the slower, harder work that came after — separating a life's identity from an address, a title, and a set of keys, and rebuilding once there was nothing left to protect.

For anyone who tied their whole sense of self to a business, a role, or a place, this chapter is a hand on the shoulder and an honest map of the ground on the other side.
