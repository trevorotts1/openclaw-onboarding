Note: This is a planning deliverable, not an episode. It runs the research stage and the build gate's document quality control. It skips Steps 6 through 17 of the pipeline. SEASON THESIS
Turn the roastery's regulars into a community and generate warm leads without any episode ever feeling like an advertisement. The season is built on the founder's SHUA principle, so every guest is made to feel Seen, Heard, Understood, and Acknowledged, and every solo episode earns trust before it ever asks for anything.

SEASON ARC (a television-season shape, eight episodes)
The season opens by establishing the owner's point of view, deepens through guest stories that widen the world, turns at the midpoint on a genuinely counter-intuitive operating belief, and lands on a forward-looking invitation. Solo founder episodes carry the spine; guest interviews widen it. The interview slots are the lead-generation engine and each one produces a book teaser as the gift.

CADENCE AND FORMAT
Weekly, one episode per week. Target runtime seven to fifteen minutes, defaulting to the ten-minute sweet spot at one hundred forty words per minute. Solo episodes run Personal mode. Guest episodes run Interview mode and produce the teaser bonus. No episode is padded to hit a runtime; thin material becomes a tight short episode.

EPISODE SLATE

Episode 1. Working title: The First Bag We Ever Sold. Mode: Personal. Style: Vulnerable. Thesis: the founder almost quit before the first wholesale account said yes, and what that near miss taught him about patience. Arc position: establish voice and stakes. Purpose: give the audience a reason to trust the host before any guest arrives.

Episode 2. Working title: The Regular Who Named the Roast. Mode: Interview. Style: Passionate. Guest: a longtime regular who suggested the blend that became the house favorite. Thesis: the best product ideas already sit at your counter if you will listen. Purpose: first lead-generation episode; teaser bonus produced.

Episode 3. Working title: Why We Stopped Chasing New Customers. Mode: Personal. Style: Counter Intuitive. Thesis: the season the shop grew fastest was the season it spent the least on reaching strangers. Arc position: seed the midpoint turn. Note: the Counter Intuitive engine forbids the word paradox, enforced at the episode gate.

Episode 4. Working title: The Farmer Two Time Zones Away. Mode: Interview. Style: Provocative. Guest: a green-coffee importer with a blunt view on fair sourcing. Thesis: convenience is the enemy of a supply chain anyone can be proud of. Purpose: lead-generation; teaser bonus produced. Provocative style uses the Q1 plus Q2 thesis pair.

Episode 5. Working title: What the Morning Rush Taught Me About Silence. Mode: Personal. Style: Vulnerable. Thesis: the founder's burnout and the small ritual that brought the mornings back. Arc position: emotional low before the climb.

Episode 6. Working title: The Barista Who Became a Buyer. Mode: Interview. Style: Passionate. Guest: a former employee who opened a complementary business. Thesis: training people well is a growth strategy, not a cost. Purpose: lead-generation; teaser bonus produced.

Episode 7. Working title: The Loyalty Card We Threw Away. Mode: Personal. Style: Counter Intuitive. Thesis: removing the points program deepened loyalty instead of shrinking it. Arc position: the season's clearest reversal, paying off Episode 3.

Episode 8. Working title: Who We Are Roasting For Next Year. Mode: Personal. Style: Passionate. Thesis: a forward invitation naming the community the shop wants to build. Arc position: finale and call to action into the next season.

STYLE AND MODE DISTRIBUTION
Five Personal episodes carry the spine; three Interview episodes drive leads and produce three book teasers. Styles are spread so no two adjacent episodes share a style: Vulnerable twice, Counter Intuitive twice, Passionate three times, Provocative once. This keeps the emotional texture varied across the run.

QUALITY GATES (from the director-of-podcast doctrine)
Every produced episode passes the four-gate discipline: a content gate at draft, a fidelity gate at improvement, the full episode QC gate before render, and an audio gate after mastering to minus fourteen to minus sixteen LUFS integrated. The season plan itself passes document quality control only, since it produces no audio.

KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS
Return listening rate week over week, guest referral count from each Interview episode, warm leads entering the Convert and Flow pipeline from the teaser bonus, and the ratio of returning to new listeners. Reach is measured but never used as the primary success metric; return and referral are.

DISTRIBUTION
Each episode publishes to the client's own Podbean channel and links back through the Convert and Flow custom fields. Interview episodes additionally enroll the two discovered workflows. Social packaging for any episode is handed to the Skill 57 lane through the Episode Asset Pack preset, never re-produced here.

HANDOFF
On approval, each slot is submitted to the engine as its own intake with the mode, style, and preset named above. Guest slots require a captured guest first name and the transparency answer before production begins. The season plan is stored as the client's running strategy document and updated as slots are produced.
