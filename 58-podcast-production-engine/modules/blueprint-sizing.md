# Module: Sizing and Blueprint (Pipeline Steps 4 and 5)

**Owns:** Step 4 (Size the episode) and Step 5 (Blueprint) of the canonical 18 step pipeline. Both
run after the research package is frozen and before any drafting. Everything this module produces is
internal and never appears in the deliverable script.

**Writing rules binding on everything downstream:** zero em dash characters anywhere; no triple
backtick or code fence markers in any produced output; the spoken word count is counted honestly and
never misreported; the research package is already frozen and this module places only what it
contains, never new fabricated material.

The immediate output of Step 5 is consumed by the draft. The run enters the `writing` state at Step
6 (Draft), so sizing and blueprinting are the last internal work before the first spoken word is
written.

---

## 1. Step 4: Size the episode

Choose the runtime and the total spoken word target before blueprinting, because the arc beat word
budgets are computed against that total.

**The 140 words per minute table (Fish Audio tags excluded from every count):**

| Target runtime | Spoken words | Spoken characters (approximate, including spaces) |
|---|---|---|
| 7 minutes (minimum) | 980 words | 5,900 to 6,400 characters |
| 10 minutes (THE SWEET SPOT) | 1,400 words | 8,400 to 9,100 characters |
| 12 minutes | 1,680 words | 10,100 to 10,900 characters |
| 15 minutes (maximum) | 2,100 words | 12,600 to 13,700 characters |

Character figures assume the English average of roughly six characters per word including spaces.
Words are the primary control metric; characters are the reference metric for cost and text to
speech billing. Both counts exclude every Fish Audio tag.

**Sizing rules:**

- Hard floor 980 spoken words (7 minutes). Hard ceiling 2,100 spoken words (15 minutes).
- The sweet spot is 10 minutes, about 1,400 spoken words. This is the default when no runtime is
  specified. Aim for the sweet spot unless the material clearly justifies going longer or demands
  going shorter. When no runtime is given, target the 1,300 to 1,500 spoken word band.
- Choose the runtime inside the range based on the richness of the respondent's material plus the
  frozen research package. A rich answer full of stories with strong verified case studies supports
  a 13 to 15 minute episode. A thin answer produces a tight 7 to 9 minute episode, never a padded
  15 minute one. Padding is both a quality failure and a cost failure; shorter text to speech
  production is dramatically cheaper.
- Count length by spoken words only. Every bracketed or parenthesized Fish Audio tag is excluded
  from the count because it is never spoken. Any leftover instruction demanding 20,000 or more
  characters is obsolete and is ignored in favor of this table.
- The chosen total is what the Style Engine's proportional word budgets are applied against in Step
  5. Record the chosen runtime and word target in the episode record; they are reported honestly in
  the delivery report.

## 2. Step 5: Blueprint (internal outline)

Privately outline the entire episode before drafting. The blueprint is internal and never appears in
the deliverable. Produce every item below.

### 2.1 The episode title (immutable after this step)

- Create one episode title that is compelling, disruptive, and has an edge that captivates
  attention. It promises the payoff of the thesis without giving the reveal away.
- The word "Title" must never appear before it anywhere, on the page or in the script.
- Inside the Final Draft script the title exists only as spoken language woven naturally into the
  open, for example woven into the show frame in Interview mode. Whether the title is spoken is
  governed by the Style Engine and Mode rules; when in doubt in Interview mode, speak it as part of
  the show frame. Whatever sits on the page gets spoken, so a bare title line is never left in the
  script.
- The title is also reported outside the script in the delivery report for publishing, cover art,
  and metadata.
- The title is IMMUTABLE from this step forward. The Improvement Pass (Step 7) is forbidden from
  changing it, and so is every later step.

### 2.2 The one sentence thesis

Write the thesis in exactly one sentence, traceable to the respondent's Q1 answer (and to Q2 as well
in the Provocative path). This is the respondent's idea, sharpened, never a substitute idea of your
own. Every arc beat serves this thesis.

### 2.3 The style signature line, written verbatim

Write out the chosen style's signature device verbatim now, so the draft has the exact words to hit:

- Counter Intuitive: the reversal line. The single memorable inversion that flips the conventional
  wisdom on its head, the sentence people repeat to a friend. Built from the respondent's core
  insight.
- Vulnerable: the definition drop. One unforgettable, plainspoken sentence that defines the invisible
  thing at the center of the topic. Built from the respondent's Q1 big idea.
- Provocative: the thesis refrain. One line, worded identically every time, deployed at least three
  times at strategic moments so it becomes a drumbeat.
- Passionate: the crescendo command. The final commanding line, or the provocative question that
  echoes the opening question and lingers after the audio stops.

### 2.5 Placement of the transparency beat

The transparency admission (`contact.podcast_interview_smiq`) is mandatory in every episode and is
placed per the chosen Style Engine. Placement guidance (the Style Engine module remains authoritative
on execution):

- Counter Intuitive: woven into a self deprecating aside, commonly inside The Systematic Case or just
  before The Reversal Insight, so it lowers the speaker's status and raises trust.
- Vulnerable: The Breakdown And Surrender beat is built around it. This is where the struggle the
  respondent named lands with real rawness, resistance, and reluctant surrender.
- Provocative: as the humanizing reveal after the evidence waves, entering The New Operating System,
  where hope enters. In Interview mode it is the moment that makes the host respect the guest more.
- Passionate: its own beat, The Transparency Beat, placed right before The Universal Principle, so the
  principle lands as credible.

In Personal mode the transparency answer is a first person vulnerable admission. In Interview mode it
is a humanizing reveal the host delivers about the guest with respect and empathy. Keep the raw
honesty from the improved answer; do not sanitize it.

### 2.6 Placement of case studies, power statements, and humor

- Place the up to three verified case studies from the frozen package at the beats where they carry
  the most weight, entering as spoken story material for the ear, never as recited reports.
- Place the three power statements at high leverage moments (reveals, the reversal or refrain, the
  close).
- Place the humor beats per the style: Counter Intuitive dry deadpan asides, Vulnerable laughter
  within moments after the heaviest lines, Provocative exasperated exaggeration, Passionate callback
  humor near the end.

### 2.7 Opening line and final line, written first

Write the opening line and the final line before anything else in the blueprint body. They anchor the
whole episode. The opening obeys the style and mode rules (no canned intro where forbidden, the
provocative cold open before any show housekeeping in Interview mode). The final line executes the
style's specific closing mechanic and is the line that lingers.

## 3. Handoff and hard rules recap

- The blueprint is internal only and never appears in the Speech Script deliverable.
- The title is fixed; the thesis is fixed; the arc beat budgets sum to the chosen total.
- Sizing counts spoken words only, tags excluded, and the count is reported honestly. Misreporting
  the word count is an absolute failure at episode QC.
- No new research or fabricated material is introduced at blueprint; the research package is frozen
  and this module places only what it holds.
- No em dash characters and no triple backtick or code fence markers in any produced output.
- Convert and Flow is the client facing name; never say GoHighLevel or GHL on a client visible
  surface.
