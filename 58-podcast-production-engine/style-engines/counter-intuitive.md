# Style Engine: Counter Intuitive

The measured academic contrarian engine. Introduces a different way of thinking about something the audience holds so deeply they have never once questioned it.

STYLE ENGINE SET (four siblings, one spine): counter-intuitive.md (this file), vulnerable.md, provocative.md, passionate.md. All four share the length table, the Final Draft format, the Fish Audio tagging discipline, and the episode QC gate. Only the persuasion mechanism, the arc, and the forbidden list differ per engine.

## CORE PERSUASION MECHANISM

Take a belief the audience holds so deeply they have never once questioned it. State that belief fully, fairly, and generously, so the audience nods along and would swear you are on their side. Then dismantle it piece by piece using relatable everyday evidence, escalating from the trivial to the consequential, until the audience realizes the obvious truth has quietly been costing them. The listener should feel the ground shift under a belief they did not know was a belief. The proof is not volume and it is not confrontation. It is the calm, cumulative weight of examples they recognize from their own life, capped by one hard verified finding at the moment casual observation becomes undeniable.

## VOICE DNA

- Professorial but warm. Dry, deadpan wit delivered with a straight face.
- Long, carefully built explanatory sentences punctuated by short, flat punchlines.
- Self-deprecating asides that lower the speaker's status and raise trust.
- Enumeration as a rhetorical weapon: "This has two effects. Effect one. Effect two."
- Everyday domestic imagery (grocery aisles, phone plans, what to cook for dinner) carrying heavyweight ideas, then escalated to serious domains: health, money, family, identity.
- Understated irony. The biggest claims are delivered in the calmest voice.
- The energy never rises to a shout. This engine lands quietly and lets the idea do the shouting.

Beat content:

1. THE OFFICIAL DOGMA. Open by naming the universally accepted belief. State it so fairly that a defender of the belief would applaud. Establish that this belief is so deeply embedded in the water supply that it would not occur to anyone to question it. Do not tip your hand beyond a hint of trouble.
2. THE BELIEF IN THE WILD. Show the belief operating everywhere in the listener's daily life through vivid, escalating, often funny examples. Start trivial and relatable, end consequential. The listener must recognize their own life in these examples.
3. THE TURN. Pose the question directly: is this actually good for us? Deliver the counter thesis plainly. This is the thesis of the entire episode, drawn from the respondent's Q1 answer. One clean, quotable sentence.
4. THE SYSTEMATIC CASE. Enumerate the hidden costs or negative effects, two to four of them, each named crisply, each supported by a relatable scenario, a logical demonstration, or a research finding. Include at least one credible verified study or data point that reinforces the respondent's point of view, represented accurately. Use the enumeration structure out loud ("The first effect. The second effect."). The transparency beat lives inside this section (see below).
5. THE REVERSAL INSIGHT. Deliver the memorable inversion, the line that flips conventional wisdom on its head, the sentence people repeat to a friend. Build it from the respondent's core insight.
6. THE FISHBOWL CLOSE. Zoom out to what this means for how we should live, decide, or build. End on a single extended metaphor or image that reframes the whole episode, followed by a calm, confident final line. No shout. It lands quietly.

## TRANSPARENCY (SMIQ) BEAT PLACEMENT

The respondent's transparency answer (their number-one current struggle, stored in contact.podcast_interview_smiq) is a mandatory beat, woven in as a first person admission. In this engine it lands inside The Systematic Case as a self-deprecating aside: the speaker admits they are the worst offender at the very cost they are describing ("I should confess, I am exhibit A here"). The self-deprecating aside is native to this voice, so the admission raises trust without breaking the calm register. It never becomes a confessional set piece; keep it dry, brief, and honest. In Personal mode it is first person about the speaker. In Interview mode it belongs to the guest, reported by the host in third person with the guest's own framing preserved.

## SIGNATURE DEVICES

- The generous restatement of the opposing view before dismantling it.
- Rhetorical questions answered immediately and flatly ("And do you know what the answer is? The answer is no.").
- The good-news-or-bad-news construction: acknowledge genuine upside before revealing the cost.
- A single hard verified finding, delivered with specificity, positioned as the moment casual observation becomes undeniable.
- Low-expectation humor: joking about one's own credentials, choices, or failures.

## FISH AUDIO TAG TENDENCIES AND MANDATORY LOCATIONS

Palette tendencies for this engine (square-bracket S2.1 Pro syntax; the tagging module owns the canonical palette and density law): favor [dry], [deadpan], [slightly amused], and [pause] before punchlines and before the counter thesis. The respondent's stated speaking tone still governs the overall palette; these are the engine's leanings, not overrides.

Mandatory tag locations for this arc: the cold open (set the measured, warm register explicitly), each transition between the six beats, [pause] immediately before The Turn thesis sentence, [pause] before the single hard research finding in The Systematic Case, a soft register on the transparency admission, and a calm landing tag on the final line of The Fishbowl Close. Density target overall is roughly one tag every two to five sentences, concentrated at these pivots. Tags are excluded from the spoken word count and must stay short.

## MODE INTERACTION

- Personal Podcast Style: first person throughout. The cold open is The Official Dogma stated in the speaker's own voice. No canned self-introduction beyond what the mode module allows.
- Interview Style Podcast: the arc is unchanged, but beat 1 opens on a scroll-stopping cold open built from the guest's Q1 answer before any show housekeeping, then transitions into the show frame, then names the guest as the source of today's insight with a genuine, earned, non-fabricated epic introduction. The host speaks first person; the guest is third person by name; every idea attributed to the guest must come from the guest's own answers. Mode module is canonical for all perspective and framing rules.

## FORBIDDEN LIST (hard)

Behavioral forbiddens for the Counter Intuitive voice:

- Shouting energy. Motivational cliches. Aggressive confrontation of the audience (that belongs to the Provocative engine).
- Ending on a shout or a hard command. This engine ends quietly.

Fabrication boundary (Tier 1 check 12, semantic judge tier): use only the personal stories, quotes, affirmations, and details the respondent actually provided. Never invent a personal story, a childhood memory, a family member, a business failure, or any biographical detail they did not give. If they provided no personal story, build on their ideas, on verified research, and on clearly framed universal or hypothetical scenarios ("imagine a shopper who"), never on fabricated first person biography. Every case study, statistic, institution, and claim must be real and verified.

FORBIDDEN STYLE WORD (Tier 1 check 11): one specific abstract noun is banned from every Counter Intuitive episode, in any casing. It is the one-word noun that titles this archetype's most famous talk about the hidden cost of abundant choice. This file deliberately never spells that token, so it can never ride inside a Counter Intuitive style file and leak into a writer prompt. The canonical literal string lives only in qc-tier1-mechanical.py check 11, which scans the finished deliverable and hard-fails on any hit.

FORBIDDEN NAMES (Tier 1 check 10): this engine is modeled on a real, well-known contrarian speaker and that person's single most famous talk and book. Those identities are deliberately kept out of this file and out of every episode. The writer never names, quotes, attributes to, or alludes to that speaker, book, or talk. The exact forbidden strings for all four archetypes live only in qc-tier1-mechanical.py check 10, which scans the finished deliverable and hard-fails on any hit.

## WORKED-EXAMPLE CALIBRATION

CALIBRATION ONLY. The passage below is a target-voice sample of beat 1 (The Official Dogma) with correct S2.1 Pro tag placement, on an illustrative topic. It exists so the writer can hear the engine before drafting. Never copy its wording, its topic, or its phrasing into a real episode. Every real episode is built fresh from the respondent's own material. Illustrative topic, used for calibration only: the belief that staying constantly informed makes you a better decision maker.

[warm, measured] There is a belief so ordinary that most of us have never once set it down to look at it. It goes like this. The more you know, the better your decisions will be. So we read one more article. We open one more tab. We refresh the feed one more time before we let ourselves choose. [slight pause] We treat this as plain common sense. The responsible thing. The grown-up thing to do.

[dry] So allow me to be the irresponsible one for the next few minutes.

[measured] Because here is what I kept noticing, first in my own week, and then in nearly every sharp, capable person I worked beside. The more they took in, the longer they stalled. Not a little longer. [emphasis] Much longer. Their mornings filled up with input, and the one decision they actually owed themselves kept sliding to tomorrow. [pause] And the part that got me? Every one of them was sure the cure for feeling behind was, and I want you to really sit with this, [slightly amused] a little more reading.
