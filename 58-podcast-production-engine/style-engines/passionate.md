# Style Engine: Passionate

The vulnerable comedic quest engine. Inspirational and motivational. The speaker carries the audience through their own transformation as a series of vivid, funny, cinematic episodes, each ending in a crisp lesson, building to an achievable call to action.

STYLE ENGINE SET (four siblings, one spine): counter-intuitive.md, vulnerable.md, provocative.md, passionate.md (this file). All four share the length table, the Final Draft format, the Fish Audio tagging discipline, and the episode QC gate. Only the persuasion mechanism, the arc, and the forbidden list differ per engine.

## METADATA

- Engine id: passionate
- Intake style label: Passionate (resolved from the style-selection answer by the intake mapper; see the intake questionnaires wiring module).
- Production modes served: Personal Podcast Style and Interview Style Podcast. The mode changes perspective and framing only; the arc and budgets below are identical in both. Mode mechanics are canonical in the Personal and Interview mode modules, not here.
- Default runtime: 10 minutes (about 1,400 spoken words) at 140 words per minute. Hard floor 980 spoken words (7 minutes). Hard ceiling 2,100 spoken words (15 minutes).
- Seeded from: Skill 57 prompt 17 (podcast script writer) scaffolding, per PRD Sections 2 and 7. See LINEAGE below.

Selected when the respondent chose Passionate on the intake form (the inspirational and motivational path). ## CORE PERSUASION MECHANISM

The speaker takes the audience on an emotional journey through their own transformation, structured as a series of episodes, each one funny, vivid, and cinematic, each one ending in a crisply stated lesson. The persuasion comes from lived proof: the speaker did the scary thing, repeatedly, and came back changed. Humor keeps the audience leaning in; vulnerability makes them trust; the escalating episodes make the final call to action feel not just inspiring but achievable. This is a talk with humor in it, never a comedy set; every laugh serves forward motion.

## VOICE DNA

- Warm, self-deprecating, disruptively witty. Dry humor that is unexpected, a little edgy, and emotionally honest. Careful: humor serves the talk, it never becomes a set.
- Cinematic scene-telling: specific places, specific sensory details, dialogue, the pounding heart, the longest walk of your life.
- Emotional whiplash used deliberately: laughter, then a gut punch, then laughter again.
- Crisp lesson statements after each story beat: one or two sentences, quotable, plain.
- Relatable, never overly familiar or slangy in the opening. Polished but human.
- Momentum language: "and then," "so the next day," "and that is when everything flipped."

Beat content:

1. THE PROVOCATIVE QUESTION OPEN. Open with a very powerful, provocative question aimed straight at the listener's private fear or desire, tied to the respondent's key message. No self-introduction, no canned open beyond what the mode module requires. Hook first, always.
2. THE WOUND OR THE DREAM. A formative moment that set up the internal conflict: the old fear versus the big dream, the two selves at war. Built only from material the respondent provided. If they gave no origin story, establish the universal version in second person ("Somewhere along the way, you learned that") and the respondent's stated relationship to it.
3. THE STUCK POINT AND THE CATALYST. Show the cost of staying the same, then the moment of decision: the click, the line in the sand, the experiment begun.
4. THE EPISODES. Two to four escalating story beats or applied examples, each vivid and specific, each funnier or bolder than the last, each closing with a crisply stated lesson drawn from the respondent's insights. Where the respondent's answers are light on stories, use researched real-world examples and clearly framed universal scenarios, never invented personal biography. This section carries the respondent's Q1 message and must deliver the emotions the respondent named they want the audience to feel.
5. THE TRANSPARENCY BEAT. The respondent's stated current struggle, delivered with disarming honesty: the journey is not finished, the speaker is still in it (see below).
6. THE UNIVERSAL PRINCIPLE. Zoom from the personal to the timeless. Connect the lesson to the great pattern: the people who change things are the ones who kept going when it was hardest. State the core principle in one unforgettable sentence.
7. THE CRESCENDO CALL. Build wave upon wave of rising, rhythmic, second person empowerment toward an inspirational crescendo. Give the audience one concrete thing to do differently starting today. End on either a short commanding line ("Don't run.") or a provocative question that echoes the opening question and leaves the listener thinking long after the audio stops.

## TRANSPARENCY (SMIQ) BEAT PLACEMENT

The respondent's transparency answer (their number-one current struggle, stored in contact.podcast_interview_smiq) is its own beat, beat 5, placed deliberately right before The Universal Principle. In this engine the admission makes the principle credible: the speaker states the timeless lesson while openly still living the struggle, so the audience trusts the call to action that follows. Deliver it with disarming honesty, not despair. In Personal mode it is first person about the speaker. In Interview mode it belongs to the guest, carried by the host in third person with the guest's own framing preserved.

## SIGNATURE DEVICES

- Micro-scenes with dialogue and physical detail.
- The lesson stamp: story, then lesson, cleanly separated by rhythm.
- Callback humor: a funny element from early in the episode returning near the end.
- Smooth transitions between points; no abrupt topic jumps.
- The two-selves construction: the fearful self versus the dreaming self.

## FISH AUDIO TAG TENDENCIES AND MANDATORY LOCATIONS

Palette tendencies for this engine (square-bracket S2.1 Pro syntax; the tagging module owns the canonical palette and density law): favor [excited] and [playful], [whispering] for a moment of intimacy, then [powerful and rising] for the crescendo. The respondent's stated speaking tone still governs the overall palette; these are the engine's leanings.

Mandatory tag locations for this arc: the cold open (set an energetic, hooking register on the provocative question), each transition between the seven beats, a lesson-stamp tag after each story beat in The Episodes, the softest and most honest register on The Transparency Beat, a [pause] before the one-sentence Universal Principle, and a [powerful and rising] build across The Crescendo Call that lands hard on the final commanding line or echoing question. Density roughly one tag every two to five sentences, concentrated at these pivots. Tags are excluded from the spoken word count and must stay short.

## MODE INTERACTION

- Personal Podcast Style: first person throughout. The cold open is The Provocative Question Open in the speaker's own voice. No canned self-introduction.
- Interview Style Podcast: the arc is unchanged, but beat 1 opens on a scroll-stopping cold open built from the guest's Q1 answer before any show housekeeping, then transitions into the show frame, then names the guest as the source of today's insight with a genuine, earned, non-fabricated epic introduction. The host speaks first person; the guest is third person by name; every idea attributed to the guest must come from the guest's own answers. Mode module is canonical for all perspective and framing rules.

## FORBIDDEN LIST (hard)

Behavioral forbiddens for the Passionate voice:

- Stiffness. Thesis-paper tone.
- Comedy-set drift: jokes without forward motion.
- Being overly familiar or slangy in the opening.

Fabrication boundary (Tier 1 check 12, semantic judge tier): use only the personal stories, quotes, affirmations, and details the respondent actually provided. Never invent a personal story, a childhood memory, a family member, a business failure, a day-count experiment, or any biographical detail they did not give. Where stories are thin, use researched real-world examples and clearly framed universal scenarios in second person, never fabricated first person biography. Every case study, statistic, institution, and claim must be real and verified.

FORBIDDEN NAMES (Tier 1 check 10): this engine is modeled on a real, well-known comedic-quest speaker and that person's single most famous talk and book, including any day-numbered experiment associated with them. Those identities are deliberately kept out of this file and out of every episode. The writer never names, quotes, attributes to, or alludes to that speaker, book, talk, or experiment. The exact forbidden strings for all four archetypes live only in qc-tier1-mechanical.py check 10, which scans the finished deliverable and hard-fails on any hit.

## WORKED-EXAMPLE CALIBRATION

CALIBRATION ONLY. The passage below is a target-voice sample of beat 1 (The Provocative Question Open) with correct S2.1 Pro tag placement, on an illustrative topic. It exists so the writer can hear the engine before drafting. Never copy its wording, its topic, or its phrasing into a real episode. Every real episode is built fresh from the respondent's own material. Illustrative topic, used for calibration only: starting the thing you keep postponing until conditions feel perfect.

[energetic] Let me ask you one thing, and I want an honest answer, just between you and you. [pause] What would you already be building right now, today, if you knew for a fact you could not fail at it?

[warm, playful] Because here is the secret nobody hands you on the way in. [chuckling] The perfect moment is not on its way. It is not stuck in traffic. It is not going to knock on your door one morning and announce that conditions are finally ideal, go ahead and chase it now.

[building] I gave years of my life to waiting for that moment. I dressed it up as being careful. I called it being smart, being strategic, being patient. [slower] What it really was, was fear in a very nice suit. [pause] And the day my life cracked open was not the day I finally felt sure of anything. [powerful] It was the ordinary Tuesday I decided to move while my hands were still shaking. [pause] And that same decision is sitting right in front of you. Right now. Before you feel one degree more ready than you already do.
