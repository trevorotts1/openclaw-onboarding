# Style Engine: Provocative

The evidence-wielding prosecutor engine. In your face, disruptive, and willing to challenge the norm. A widespread belief is put on trial and the audience is cornered by the verdict.

STYLE ENGINE SET (four siblings, one spine): counter-intuitive.md, vulnerable.md, provocative.md (this file), passionate.md. All four share the length table, the Final Draft format, the Fish Audio tagging discipline, and the episode QC gate. Only the persuasion mechanism, the arc, and the forbidden list differ per engine.

## CORE PERSUASION MECHANISM

The speaker makes a case, openly and unapologetically, like a lawyer in front of a jury. A widespread practice or belief is put on trial. Evidence is marshaled in escalating waves, each wave bigger and harder to dismiss than the last. Objections the audience is silently forming are named out loud and destroyed preemptively. A thesis refrain is hammered again and again until it becomes undeniable. The audience is not invited to consider the idea. They are cornered by it. Certainty is the register; hedging is banned.

## VOICE DNA

- Confident, punchy, rhythmic. Short declarative sentences that hit like a gavel.
- A self-deprecating confession at the open that buys likability before the aggression starts.
- The case frame stated explicitly: "I am not here to tell you a story. I am here to make a case."
- A repeated thesis refrain, deployed at least three times at strategic moments, worded identically each time so it becomes a drumbeat.
- Direct address of the audience as capable adults who have been sold something broken.
- Controlled outrage: "This makes no sense. And it gets worse."
- The rule of three, everywhere.

Beat content:

1. THE CONFESSION OPEN. Open with a disarming, slightly embarrassing admission or an arresting scenario. Then declare the frame: a case is about to be made against something the audience believes. The transparency admission can live here (see below).
2. THE EXHIBIT. Present a concrete demonstration, scenario, or thought experiment that makes the flawed assumption visible and testable. The respondent's Q1 answer is what goes on trial. State the assumption in its strongest form.
3. THE SURPRISING VERDICT. Reveal that the assumption fails, using the respondent's Q2 answer as the spine. Deliver the reveal with genuine shock value ("You would expect one thing. The opposite happened."). Introduce the thesis refrain here for the first time, worded exactly as it will recur.
4. THE ESCALATING EVIDENCE WAVES. Two to three waves of support, each bigger in scope or authority than the last. Conduct verified web research to find real studies, examples, or expert findings that reinforce the respondent's position, represented accurately. Between waves, name the audience's objection out loud and dismantle it ("Now some of you are thinking this only works for certain people. Let me show you why that is wrong."). Repeat the thesis refrain after the final wave.
5. THE NEW OPERATING SYSTEM. Stop demolishing and start building. Present the respondent's alternative, the new way of thinking or operating, broken into two or three named components. Give real or clearly framed examples of the alternative working. This is where hope enters the episode.
6. THE CLOSING ARGUMENT. Summarize the case as numbered findings ("Here is what we now know. One. Two. Three."). Hammer the thesis refrain one final time. Issue a direct challenge to the audience to abandon the broken belief and act. Build to a thrilling final passage that paints what becomes possible, then close on a short, decisive final line. A courtroom-flavored button, in original words, is welcome.

## TRANSPARENCY (SMIQ) BEAT PLACEMENT

The respondent's transparency answer (their number-one current struggle, stored in contact.podcast_interview_smiq) is delivered as the disarming, slightly embarrassing admission inside The Confession Open, beat 1. That admission is native to this engine and it buys the likability the aggression will spend. When the struggle is heavier than an opener can carry, hold it for a brief, controlled beat just before The New Operating System, so the vulnerability humanizes the builder right as hope enters; do not let it soften the case itself. In Personal mode it is first person about the speaker. In Interview mode it belongs to the guest, carried by the host in third person with the guest's own framing preserved.

## SIGNATURE DEVICES

- The preemptive objection kill.
- Authority stacking: attaching evidence to credible institutions and unimpeachable sources so bias accusations die on arrival.
- The refrain as drumbeat, worded identically each time.
- Contrast pairs: "what the evidence says" versus "what everyone keeps doing."
- Humor through exaggerated exasperation, never through meanness.

## FISH AUDIO TAG TENDENCIES AND MANDATORY LOCATIONS

Palette tendencies for this engine (square-bracket S2.1 Pro syntax; the tagging module owns the canonical palette and density law): favor [emphasis], [firm], [building intensity], and [pause] before verdicts. The respondent's stated speaking tone still governs the overall palette; these are the engine's leanings.

Mandatory tag locations for this arc: the cold open (set a firm, building register), each transition between the six beats, [pause] immediately before The Surprising Verdict, an energy shift on each thesis-refrain repetition so the drumbeat reads, a [pause] before each preemptive objection kill in The Escalating Evidence Waves, and a rising build into the final decisive line of The Closing Argument. Density roughly one tag every two to five sentences, concentrated at these pivots. Tags are excluded from the spoken word count and must stay short.

## MODE INTERACTION

- Personal Podcast Style: first person throughout. The cold open is The Confession Open in the speaker's own voice, followed by the case frame. No canned self-introduction.
- Interview Style Podcast: the arc is unchanged, but beat 1 opens on a scroll-stopping cold open built from the guest's Q1 answer before any show housekeeping, then transitions into the show frame, then names the guest as the source of today's insight with a genuine, earned, non-fabricated epic introduction. The host speaks first person; the guest is third person by name; every idea attributed to the guest must come from the guest's own answers. Mode module is canonical for all perspective and framing rules.

## FORBIDDEN LIST (hard)

Behavioral forbiddens for the Provocative voice:

- Hedging language used as thesis softeners ("maybe," "perhaps," "in some cases" attached to the core claim).
- Thesis-paper structure or academic tone.
- Gentle endings. This engine ends decisively.

Fabrication boundary (Tier 1 check 12, semantic judge tier): use only the personal stories, quotes, affirmations, and details the respondent actually provided. Never invent a personal story, a family member, a business failure, or any biographical detail they did not give. If they provided no personal story, build on their ideas, on verified research, and on clearly framed universal scenarios, never on fabricated first person biography. Every case study, statistic, institution, and claim marshaled as evidence must be real, verified, and represented accurately; a prosecutor who fabricates loses the case.

FORBIDDEN NAMES (Tier 1 check 10): this engine is modeled on a real, well-known analytical-argument speaker and that person's single most famous talk and book. Those identities are deliberately kept out of this file and out of every episode. The writer never names, quotes, attributes to, or alludes to that speaker, book, or talk. The exact forbidden strings for all four archetypes live only in qc-tier1-mechanical.py check 10, which scans the finished deliverable and hard-fails on any hit.

## WORKED-EXAMPLE CALIBRATION

CALIBRATION ONLY. The passage below is a target-voice sample of beat 1 (The Confession Open) and the first thesis-refrain seed, with correct S2.1 Pro tag placement, on an illustrative topic. It exists so the writer can hear the engine before drafting. Never copy its wording, its topic, or its phrasing into a real episode. Every real episode is built fresh from the respondent's own material. Illustrative topic, used for calibration only: the assumption that being reachable around the clock is what makes you valuable at work.

[firm, building] Almost everything you have been told about being available is a trap. [pause] And the people who taught it to you are not the ones paying for it. You are.

[direct] I am not here to make you comfortable today. I am here to make a case, and I brought the receipts. [warmer] For years I wore my responsiveness like a badge. Reply in seconds. Never miss a message. Be the one who is always, always on. [firm] I called it dedication. [pause] What it actually was, was fear with a better job title.

[building intensity] So here is the assumption on trial. We believe that the faster you answer, the more valuable you must be. More reachable, more indispensable, more safe. [pause] And the afternoon I finally went dark for three hours, the afternoon I was certain the whole thing would collapse, [emphasis] nothing collapsed. The work got sharper. Now, some of you just tensed up. Good. Stay with me.
