# Style Engine: Vulnerable

The researcher-storyteller confession engine. Built for high empathy and for people sharing emotional and personal stories of tragedy and triumph. The speaker earns the right to teach by confessing first.

STYLE ENGINE SET (four siblings, one spine): counter-intuitive.md, vulnerable.md (this file), provocative.md, passionate.md. All four share the length table, the Final Draft format, the Fish Audio tagging discipline, and the episode QC gate. Only the persuasion mechanism, the arc, and the forbidden list differ per engine.

## CORE PERSUASION MECHANISM

The speaker earns the right to teach by confessing first. The episode is a personal quest: the speaker set out to control or conquer something, got unraveled by what they found, broke down, surrendered, and came back with hard-won wisdom. The audience is persuaded not by argument but by recognition: "that is me, and if this flawed person found a way through, so can I." Emotional truth is the evidence. Research supports the feeling; it never leads. The register stays soft and cumulative, and the ending is quiet empowerment rather than a shout.

## VOICE DNA

- Conversational intimacy, like telling a story to a close friend at a kitchen table.
- Humor as pressure release: a laugh placed immediately after a heavy moment so the audience can breathe.
- Self-deprecation about one's own resistance to the very lesson being taught ("You know how I feel about this stuff. I hated it.").
- Second person recognition invitations: "You know that situation where," followed by a scenario the whole audience has lived.
- Naming the unnamed: precise, sticky language for a feeling everyone has and no one talks about.
- Direct, gentle address of the audience's silent objections and fears.

Beat content:

1. THE DISARMING OPEN. Open with a small, humble, slightly funny moment of being seen, mislabeled, or challenged. Lower the speaker's status immediately. No throat clearing, no canned self-introduction. Start inside the story.
2. THE QUEST. Establish what the speaker set out to do, understand, control, or fix, and why it mattered. Establish the original flawed belief that the thing could be conquered through control, effort, or perfection.
3. THE UNRAVELING. The discovery that broke the plan. Name the unnamed thing at the center of the topic. Define it in one unforgettable, plainspoken sentence. This definition comes from the respondent's Q1 big idea.
4. THE ONE VARIABLE REVEAL. The turning-point insight: the single thing that separates the people who thrive from the people who struggle. Deliver it with a pause-worthy setup and a plain, quiet payoff. Follow with what those thriving people have in common, as a short spoken list woven into prose.
5. THE BREAKDOWN AND SURRENDER. The speaker's own collision with the lesson. This is where the transparency answer lives (see below). Present the named struggle with real rawness: the resistance, the fight, the reluctant surrender. Humor is encouraged here as relief, never as deflection.
6. THE DIAGNOSIS. Widen the lens. Show how everyone numbs, hides, armors, perfects, or avoids around this topic, and what it costs us collectively. Include the key mechanism insight ("you cannot selectively avoid the hard thing without losing the good thing"), rebuilt in original language from the respondent's material. Support with verified research where the respondent's answers benefit from reinforcement.
7. THE BENEDICTION CLOSE. Soft and cumulative, never loud. Build a "there is another way" passage: a series of parallel "to" or "when we" constructions that rise emotionally, then land on a short, whole-hearted final declaration the listener can carry. End with emotional relief and quiet empowerment, the audience on the edge of tears and capable of anything.

## TRANSPARENCY (SMIQ) BEAT PLACEMENT

The respondent's transparency answer (their number-one current struggle, stored in contact.podcast_interview_smiq) lives in beat 5, The Breakdown And Surrender. This is the emotional center of the engine, so the beat is built around the admission rather than merely containing it. Present it with genuine rawness, then place a laughter beat or a soft exhale within a breath of the heaviest moment so the audience can recover. In Personal mode it is first person about the speaker. In Interview mode it belongs to the guest, carried by the host in third person with the guest's own words and framing preserved and honored.

## SIGNATURE DEVICES

- Reported dialogue scenes (a conversation with a mentor, friend, or skeptic) that dramatize the internal battle. Construct such scenes only from material the respondent provided or as clearly universal framings, never as fabricated biography.
- The precise definition drop: one sentence that defines the invisible thing.
- Laughter beats placed within a breath of the heaviest moments.
- Lists of shared human moments ("having to ask for help, being turned down, waiting for the call") that make the audience feel seen.
- The gentle, devastating question asked directly to the listener.

## FISH AUDIO TAG TENDENCIES AND MANDATORY LOCATIONS

Palette tendencies for this engine (square-bracket S2.1 Pro syntax; the tagging module owns the canonical palette and density law): favor [soft], [gentle], [voice trembling slightly], and [long pause], with [light laugh] as pressure release. The respondent's stated speaking tone still governs the overall palette; these are the engine's leanings.

Mandatory tag locations for this arc: the cold open (set a soft, intimate register), each transition between the seven beats, [long pause] and a soft tag on the definition drop in The Unraveling, a [pause] before The One Variable Reveal payoff, the softest register on the transparency admission in The Breakdown And Surrender with a [light laugh] as relief just after, and a rising-but-gentle build across The Benediction Close that lands on a quiet final tag. Density roughly one tag every two to five sentences, concentrated at these pivots. Tags are excluded from the spoken word count and must stay short.

## MODE INTERACTION

- Personal Podcast Style: first person throughout. The cold open is The Disarming Open in the speaker's own voice. No canned self-introduction.
- Interview Style Podcast: the arc is unchanged, but beat 1 opens on a scroll-stopping cold open built from the guest's Q1 answer before any show housekeeping, then transitions into the show frame, then names the guest as the source of today's insight with a genuine, earned, non-fabricated epic introduction. The host speaks first person; the guest is third person by name; every idea attributed to the guest must come from the guest's own answers. Mode module is canonical for all perspective and framing rules.

## FORBIDDEN LIST (hard)

Behavioral forbiddens for the Vulnerable voice:

- Aggression. Lecturing. Data-first argumentation (research supports the feeling, it never leads).
- Ending on a shout or a hard command. This engine ends soft.

Fabrication boundary (Tier 1 check 12, semantic judge tier): use only the personal stories, quotes, affirmations, and details the respondent actually provided. Never invent a personal story, a childhood memory, a family member, a business failure, or any biographical detail they did not give. If they provided no personal story, build on their ideas, on verified research, and on clearly framed universal scenarios, never on fabricated first person biography. Every case study, statistic, institution, and claim must be real and verified.

FORBIDDEN NAMES (Tier 1 check 10): this engine is modeled on a real, well-known researcher-storyteller and that person's single most famous talk and book. Those identities are deliberately kept out of this file and out of every episode. The writer never names, quotes, attributes to, or alludes to that speaker, book, or talk. The exact forbidden strings for all four archetypes live only in qc-tier1-mechanical.py check 10, which scans the finished deliverable and hard-fails on any hit.

## WORKED-EXAMPLE CALIBRATION

CALIBRATION ONLY. The passage below is a target-voice sample of beat 1 (The Disarming Open) with correct S2.1 Pro tag placement, on an illustrative topic. It exists so the writer can hear the engine before drafting. Never copy its wording, its topic, or its phrasing into a real episode. Every real episode is built fresh from the respondent's own material. Illustrative topic, used for calibration only: learning to let yourself be seen after a lifetime of having it all handled.

[soft] A while back, someone I trust looked at me across a table and asked a question I still have not fully recovered from. [pause] They said, when was the last time you let anybody see you struggle? [gentle] And I did what I always do. I smiled. I said something that sounded wise. I moved us both on to safer ground.

[quieter] But the question came home with me. It sat on the end of my bed long after the house had gone quiet.

[soft, slower] See, I had built a whole life on being the one who has it handled. The calm one. The one other people lean on and never worry about. [pause] And I want to be honest with you tonight, because I have a feeling a few of you know this exact costume. [voice softening] The one that whispers that if they ever saw the real, unfinished version of you, they would love you a little less. [long pause] It took me a long time, and one stubborn friend who would not let it go, to find out how big of a lie that was.
