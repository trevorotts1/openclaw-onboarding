# Anthology Engine -- Producer How-To

This is your guide to producing an anthology. You are the producer, the owner of
this Command Center. Your co-authors are your participants. Everything you do
happens on YOUR board and through your forms; you never touch a script.

## What the engine does for you

You collect chapters from many contributors around one theme. For each
participant, the engine turns their form answers into a finished anthology chapter
in their own voice, with a title, a blurb, an outline, a cover, and both a Google
Doc and a designed PDF. When your collection is complete, it assembles the whole
anthology into one manuscript in your editorial voice.

## Where you work: the Anthology board

Open the Anthology tile on your home screen. It takes you to the Anthology
department board. Each participant is one card. A card moves along as their chapter
progresses. When something is ready for your decision, the card lands in the review
column, which is your approval queue.

- To APPROVE a deliverable, open the card and approve it.
- To REQUEST A REWRITE of a chapter, open the card, choose Request rewrite, and add
  your notes. Your notes guide the rewrite. You have up to two rewrites per chapter.
- The card moves to done only after the independent quality check passes. You are
  never asked to green-light something the quality gate already cleared.

## What your participants see

Your participants have no login. When it is their turn, they receive a short,
friendly email with a single link to their own private page. On that page they do
exactly one thing: pick their title and subtitle, approve their outline, or approve
their chapter (or ask for a rewrite with notes). Nothing else. Their place is
always saved, so a busy participant can come back weeks later with nothing lost.

## Assembling the anthology

When every participant is either approved or explicitly excluded, an Assembly card
appears with a readiness report. Assembling is YOUR decision and happens only when
you fire the ready-to-assemble trigger. To protect the edition, the engine asks you
to confirm: you are the producer, every participant is settled, there are at least
the minimum number of finished chapters, and you type the anthology name to
confirm. This is one-way.

The engine then proposes a chapter order (strong opener and closer, long and short
alternation, tone pacing, subtheme grouping), which you can adjust; writes an
editor's introduction in your voice from the inputs you supply; adds front matter,
contributor bios, and back matter; and compiles the full manuscript as a Google Doc
and a designed PDF. You give the final sign-off on the Assembly card, and the
anthology is complete.

## The platform

Every form, link, and field is part of Convert and Flow. Your documents live in
your shared Google Drive tree, organized by producer, then anthology, then
participant, each shared view-only by link.

## Good to know

- There are no deadlines. A participant who pauses for months costs nothing.
- Nothing is ever sent to a participant except the short, friendly nudges above.
- Your deliverables always ship as BOTH a Google Doc and a designed PDF, with a
  comfortable, readable type size throughout.
