## PART B: CONTENT QC GATE SUMMARY (Gate B; full protocol in QC-PROTOCOL-AND-MATRIX.md)

TIER 1 HARD-FAIL CHECKS (binary; any single failure blocks delivery; twelve deterministic checks by qc-tier1-anthology.py): word band measured and honest (chapter 2,000 to 3,500; tone at least 3,000); title lock byte-exact through outline, chapter, every rewrite, and the cover prompt; every personal story placed; zero truncation against the sha256 prompt pins with complete closing structure; zero em dash characters; no markdown artifacts, code fences, stage labels, or system-prompt leakage in prose deliverables; PDF font floor 14 over the RENDERED file; identity integrity (correct contact_id and anthology_id, no cross-participant or cross-anthology bleed); no intake contamination (hidden fields, form mechanics, emails, phones never appear); no fabrication of the participant (no invented biography, quotes, or stories; research claims trace to the search pass); Convert and Flow naming in every client surface with no internal tool or model names; run-ledger cleanliness (zero Anthropic identifiers, model used recorded honestly). Semantic checks 13 to 15 (fabrication nuance, voice fidelity, outline fidelity) run on the JUDGE tier, never the writer tier. ASSEMBLY SCOPE (S9 manuscript) adds: every approved chapter present exactly once and byte-identical; order matches curation; introduction references only real contributors; bios match ledger identities; front and back matter complete; one continuous 14-point-floor PDF.

TIER 2 RUBRIC: ten dimensions, each 8 or higher, no averaging, scored only after Tier 1 fully passes; the judge tier and persona are distinct from the writer tier and persona.

STRIKE CAP (two counters, never confused): participant rewrite budget 2 with gate re-entry (at exhaustion the gate offers Approve as-is or producer escalation, never a silent third rewrite); three internal QC attempts per deliverable, then HOLD plus a single deduped founder alert carrying the failing checks and the best draft. Standards are never relaxed to clear a strike. All attempts share one per-deliverable token budget (anthology-cost-ledger.py).

GATE SEPARATION: Gate B is never conflated with the Gate A build/merge rubric. Gate B's verdict is what the board contract consumes: the independent QC scorer's promotion of a card from review to done IS the Gate B pass surfacing on the board.
