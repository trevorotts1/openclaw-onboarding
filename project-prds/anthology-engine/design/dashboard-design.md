2. THE PRODUCER IS THE BOX OWNER: the client who completed the AI Workforce Interview, passed into every run via the Convert and Flow webhook payload. No separate producer identity system exists or is built.
3. Therefore NO standalone dashboard app is built, no BlackCEO-hosted producer surface exists, and no producer role is added to Command Center code. The v2.0 Mission Control app (Next.js app behind BlackCEO Cloudflare Access, anthology switcher, standalone approval inbox) is NOT BUILT. Its gate semantics live on in Sections 3 to 6 below.
4. The producer experience is a DEPARTMENT/BOARD inside the client's own Command Center (Sections 1 to 4); external co-author PARTICIPANTS (no box, no login, no OpenClaw identity) are served by the NEW token-scoped public page (Section 6); the HOME-SCREEN TILES are a code edit to the hardcoded tile array in command-center src/app/page.tsx (Section 5).
5. Mechanical contracts already in the Command Center, pinned fresh at W0.7 before Wave 3 builds against them: the department seeding path (Skill 32 add-department.sh, equivalently POST /api/departments with create:true); the card ingest endpoint POST /api/tasks/ingest with HMAC plus Bearer auth and mc_board.py as its fail-soft client; the board contract that routes producer-facing output to the REVIEW column and lets ONLY the independent QC scorer at or above 8.5 promote review to done; the hardcoded tile array in src/app/page.tsx; the middleware.ts bypass-list mechanism (the way /api/health is public).
6. HARD LESSON ENCODED: Skill 53 book-writer cards to a books department that was NEVER SEEDED, so its cards fall to the CEO catch-all. The Anthology Engine seeds its own department, first, as a scored build item, and never repeats that defect.

## 1. WHO USES WHAT

- PRODUCER (the box owner): the seeded Anthology department board in their own Command Center: participant cards, the review-column approval queue, the Assembly card, the home tile. Reached through their normal Command Center session on their own box; no new auth surface is created for them.
- PARTICIPANT (external co-author): the single-gate token page ONLY, reached through their nudge deep link: title and subtitle selection (S3), outline approval (S4), chapter Approve-as-is or Request-rewrite-with-notes (S5/S6), with the rendered deliverable inline. No navigation to anything else; foreign, expired, or replayed tokens are refused.
- OPERATOR (BlackCEO): operator-verbose surfaces stay OPERATOR-SIDE (run reports, ledger exports, provisioning and revocation scripts, the cost ledger and hold queue, deduped gateway Telegram alerts). No operator console ships inside the client's Command Center.

## 2. THE SEEDED ANTHOLOGY DEPARTMENT (mandatory, scored; W3.1 with provisioning hooks in W2.6)

- SEEDING PATH: provision-anthology-client.sh seeds an ANTHOLOGY department via Skill 32's add-department.sh (equivalently POST /api/departments with create:true). Seeding is IDEMPOTENT and is verified by reading the department back; provisioning does not pass until the read-back succeeds. Config writes run as the node user.
- REGISTRATION: the seeded department is registered in the fleet floor files (W4.1) and its how-to doc (the per-department owner guide) gains the self-invocation entry: an anthology intake, gate event, or assembly trigger invokes the orchestrator skill through its entry script. On the FLEET side the skill registers under the books/publishing floor grouping for routing (Skill 54's existing role binding); the CLIENT-side board identity is the seeded Anthology department.
- ACCESS SHAPE: marketing and social get READ-ONLY on published links; every other department, no access. Client humans interact only through the board, the forms, the token page, and Convert and Flow.
- ANTI-DEFECT PROOF: the build documents and proves it did not repeat the Skill 53 books-department defect: a provisioned box shows anthology cards landing on the Anthology board, never in the CEO catch-all (acceptance criterion 15).

## 3. THE PARTICIPANT KANBAN: CARDS ON THE BOARD (W3.1 board side, W4.3 engine side)

- ONE CARD PER PARTICIPANT. Cards are created and advanced by mc_board.py posting to POST /api/tasks/ingest (HMAC plus Bearer), keyed by participant_key (the composite contact_id::anthology_id) so ingest is an idempotent upsert; the same human in two anthologies is two cards.
- FAIL-SOFT LAW: board unreachability NEVER blocks the pipeline. The ledger remains the single truth; a failed ingest is logged operator-side and the card reconciles on the daily tick. There is never a second state store; the board is a projection.
- STATUS MAPPING: card status mirrors Participants.stage_cursor through a normative mapping table shipped in W4.3 against the exact board status vocabulary pinned at W0.7. Shape of the mapping: working stages (s0 to s8 in-flight) map to the board's in-progress status; every gate-open state maps to REVIEW (producer-facing deliverables land the card in review; participant-gate states also sit in review so producer visibility rides the review column while the participant acts on the token page); held and exception states surface with the hold or exception reason on the card; approved and delivered map to the board's terminal vocabulary, subject to Section 4's promotion law.
- CARD CONTENT (client-clean serializer, Section 7): participant display name, stage and stage age, gate status badge, rewrite budget remaining (2, 1, 0), quick links to the latest Doc and PDF, a stuck-over-7-days highlight. No internal tool names, no model names, no plumbing, no secrets; Convert and Flow everywhere.
- THE ENGINE ADVANCES CARDS. The board reflects ledger truth via mc_board.py and the transition matrix; dragging a card never writes engine state (acceptance criterion 3).

## 4. THE APPROVAL QUEUE IS THE REVIEW COLUMN (the existing board contract, reused, never forked)

- The Anthology board's REVIEW column IS the chapter-approval queue and the producer gate queue. This rides the EXISTING Command Center board contract: producer-facing output routes to review, and ONLY the independent QC scorer at or above 8.5 promotes review to done. The engine NEVER self-promotes a card (a scored anti-goal; SPEC Section 17).
- GATE B SURFACING: the QC scorer's review-to-done promotion is the content gate (Gate B) verdict surfacing on the board. The two QC gates are never conflated: Gate A (build/merge, 8.5 fleet rubric) scores build units; Gate B (Tier 1 hard fails, then the ten-dimension rubric at 8 or higher per dimension, then the strike cap) decides whether a deliverable ships; only Gate B touches this column.
- GATE ACTIONS ON A CARD (producer decisions): Approve, Request rewrite with notes, Hold, Exclude. Every action shells anthology_state.py record-approval; the board holds NO base credential and never writes state directly (acceptance criterion 13). The chapter gate offers EXACTLY TWO actions: Approve as-is, or Request rewrite with notes; notes append VERBATIM into chapter_updates; the remaining rewrite budget of 2 renders on the card.
- BOTH DOORS, ONE ENDPOINT: a gate decided from the board session and the same gate decided from a nudge deep link hit the SAME endpoint and the SAME writer subcommand; the Approvals row records which door (the Approvals schema's door values per SPEC Section 7.1: dashboard for the board session, nudge_link for the emailed deep link). There is no approve-by-reply, no mail-embedded form, no second approval store.
- NUDGE CADENCE (hybrid Option B, carried over from v2.0 unchanged): a gate opens in the ledger; the approvals steward fires ONE short email nudge to the right actor (producer gates deep-link to the board card; participant gates deep-link to the token page); ONE automatic re-nudge at 7 days stuck, deduped; other repeats are manual and rate-limited. Recipients ALWAYS resolve from the ledger row, never a literal.

## 5. THE ASSEMBLY CARD (S9: the ready-to-assemble trigger and the final sign-off; W3.1)

ONE dedicated card per anthology on the Anthology board, mirroring ANTHOLOGIES.assembly_state through the same status vocabulary. Trigger and sign-off are status transitions on this card PLUS approvals rows in the ledger; the writer, never the UI, enforces every guard.

- STATE 1, COLLECTING (assembly_state not_ready): the card carries the live READINESS REPORT (per participant: approved, in progress, or excluded; emitted read-only by anthology_state.py assembly-readiness-report) and a DISABLED Ready to Assemble action with the blocking list (who is neither approved nor excluded; frozen-chapter count versus min_chapters, floor 2, per-anthology configurable up). The producer can mark a participant EXCLUDED from this edition here (an exclude approvals row).
- STATE 2, ARMED (assembly_state armed): when every participant is approved or explicitly excluded and the chapter minimum is met, the writer arms the trigger and the steward fires ONE readiness nudge to the producer. Firing the trigger, from the Assembly card OR the readiness-nudge deep link (both doors, one endpoint, door recorded), opens the two-step confirmation: the producer TYPES the anthology name, and the action shells anthology_state.py record-approval --gate s9_ready --confirm-name. GUARDS revalidated by the writer on every fire: own-producer auth (the box owner's Command Center session or the producer-scoped token); every participant approved or carrying an explicit exclude row; at least min_chapters frozen approved chapters; confirm-name match (mismatch exits 5); ONE-WAY (re-firing is an acknowledged no-op; reopening collection is a producer-initiated exception that voids in-progress assembly). Assembly NEVER starts without the s9_ready approvals row; an all-approved condition alone never fires it.
- STATE 3, ASSEMBLING AND SIGN-OFF (ready_confirmed through signed_off): the card carries the engine's proposed chapter order with its curation rationale (strong opener and closer, long-short alternation, tone pacing, subtheme grouping); the producer adjusts the order, persisted ONLY via anthology_state.py assembly-set-order; previews of the editor's introduction (producer's voice, producer-supplied inputs only), front and back matter, and bios; the compiled manuscript links; then the FINAL SIGN-OFF, a SEPARATE producer decision recording the s9_producer approvals row that closes the anthology.

## 6. THE PARTICIPANT TOKEN PAGE (new code, Command Center repo; W3.3)

- WHY: participants have no Command Center login and NO public route exists for them today. The build adds exactly ONE token-scoped PUBLIC route (its page plus its decision endpoint), registered in the middleware.ts bypass list exactly the way /api/health is bypassed; the exact path is settled at W3.3 against the middleware shape pinned at W0.7.
- TOKEN/PIN SCHEME (new, single-purpose): minted per participant per gate by gate_engine.py; HMAC-signed over participant_key, gate id, and expiry, keyed by the per-client secret under the label ANTHOLOGY_GATE_TOKEN_SECRET (generated at provisioning, client env store; value never printed). Tokens are SINGLE-GATE-SCOPED and expire on gate closure; the PIN is the token's short human-enterable form under the same mint and scope. Foreign, expired, and replayed tokens are refused with no data rendered.
- WHAT IT SERVES: ONLY that participant's open gate, with the rendered deliverable inline: S3 title and subtitle selection, S4 outline approval, S5/S6 chapter Approve-as-is or Request-rewrite-with-notes (notes verbatim into chapter_updates; remaining budget shown). Nothing else: no navigation, no other participants, no board, no history.
- SAME WRITER, SAME ENDPOINT LAW: decisions from the token page shell the same anthology_state.py record-approval subcommand as every other door; the Approvals row records door nudge_link.
- Every response passes the client-clean serializer (Section 7).

## 7. HOME-SCREEN TILES (code edit; W3.2; Command Center repo's OWN serial train)

- The Command Center home screen is a HARDCODED tile array in command-center src/app/page.tsx. An Opus sub-agent EDITS that array to add THREE tiles matching the existing tile shape: the ANTHOLOGY tile (deep-linking to the Anthology department board), the PODCAST tile, and the INTERVIEW tile (each deep-linking to its own department/spoke surface), so the home screen stays a uniform grid of engine tiles.
- The Anthology tile renders read-only rollup numbers (active anthologies, participants by stage, chapters pending approval, stuck items over 7 days, holds) as a pure projection of the same ledger, plus the deep link. A tile is a WINDOW into the board, never a copy of it, and NEVER carries gate actions.
- The edit contains nothing Anthropic, nothing client-identifying, and no new dependencies; it rides the Command Center repo's own serial merge train (canary-first, single writer, annotated tag, fresh-clone verify), cross-referenced in CHANGE-LOG.md.

CLIENT-CLEAN SERIALIZER (binding on Sections 3 to 7): every client-visible payload (card fields, tile numbers, token page, nudges) strips internal tool names, model names, plumbing, and secrets, and says Convert and Flow everywhere.

## 8. DATA READS AND WRITES (no second store, ever)

- READS: every card, rollup, tile number, readiness report, and token-page render is a projection of the ledger (Airtable-backed base plus the local SQLite mirror per SPEC Section 7; reads come from the mirror, the base wins on conflict, the daily tick reconciles). Doc and PDF links come from Artifact rows. Nothing is recomputed into a second store.
- WRITES: ONLY through anthology_state.py subcommands (gate decisions, exclusions, the s9_ready trigger, order adjustment, exception resolution, nudge log). Neither the board, nor the tiles, nor the token page holds a base credential or writes state directly. Enforcement, not description: the static route audit (acceptance criterion 13) and the repo scan for direct base writes ride Gate A.
- EXCEPTIONS SURFACING: participants in exception state surface as flagged cards; resolution (attach the correct contact_id and anthology_id, replay through S0) shells exceptions.py resolve-and-replay; raw payloads render operator-side, and anything client-visible passes the serializer. Nothing is silently dropped.
- BOARD INGEST CREDENTIALS: the HMAC and Bearer pair is Skill 32's EXISTING provisioned pair, reused, never duplicated; referenced by label only.
